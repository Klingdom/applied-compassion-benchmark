## Wisconsin (12 searches — most complete)

**Likely score-raising**
- **PFAS deadlock broken.** Joint Finance Committee withheld the $125M PFAS trust fund 2023–2025 despite a Legislative Council memo saying it could release it. Evers signed 2025 Acts 200 and 201 early April 2026, releasing $133M (grown by interest): $80M local remediation grants, $35M well compensation. Multi-year correction inside the 12-month weighting window.
- **Youth prison consent decree ended.** Federal judge ended Lincoln Hills / Copper Lake oversight late January 2026 after substantial compliance October 2025. Offset: statutory 2021 closure deadline still unmet as of June 2026.
- **Opioid deaths fell 42.5%** in one year, 1,422 → 817 (2023→2024). Caveat: ~$35M of $90M state-level settlement funds unspent as of September 2025.
- **2025 Act 15 budget** (signed 2025-07-03) raised special education reimbursement 30% → 42% (FY26) → 45% (FY27), ~$500M, bipartisan.
- **Legislative Audit Bureau** publishes unflattering findings: 11 material weaknesses, 49 significant deficiencies, $2.6M questioned costs.

**Likely score-lowering**
- **Worst-in-nation Black incarceration.** 1 in 36 Black adults imprisoned; 6% of population, 42% of prison population; ~11.8–12x disparity.
- **Waupun Correctional.** Four deaths since June 2023; nine staff including Warden Randall Hepp criminally charged 2024-06-05; a fifth death followed.
- **Public records loophole.** Legislators exempt from retention requirement, may delete records. Evers' 2025 budget fix stripped by Joint Finance Committee; open as of March 2026.
- **Medicaid expansion rejected again** May 2025, forgoing projected $1.9B savings. Mitigating: only non-expansion state with no coverage gap.
- **Milwaukee lead crisis.** State DHS noncompliance order to MPS 2025-03-19 — state acted, but only after a poisoned child surfaced it in January.

**Not researched:** July 2025 abortion ruling, 2024 redistricting maps, Act 10 litigation, tribal relations, homelessness/housing, child welfare, B1–B5 (Boundaries almost entirely), I3/I5, S2/S5.

## Virginia (8 searches)

- **JLARC is the standout asset.** Independent legislative audit body with published "Actions Taken on Report Recommendations" series (`jlarc.virginia.gov/pdfs/reports/Rpt603.pdf`, 2025). Dec 2025 reports: community gun violence, K-12 accountability redesign, DOC education unmet demand. Bears on A3, AC3, AB3, AB4.
- **K-12 funding.** JLARC July 2023: divisions get ~14% less than national average (~$1,900/student). Partially acted upon — FY26 budget added $222.9M, lifted the 2009 support-staff cap; recurring K-12 funding up $1.85B (21.4%) FY23→FY26. Documented correction following a self-commissioned unflattering audit.
- **DOJ settlement.** 12-year DBHDS settlement over I/DD services ended 2025-01-15, replaced by permanent injunction retaining independent monitoring. Ambiguous valence — needs Independent Reviewer's final compliance findings.
- **Marcus Alert materially incomplete.** Behavioral Health Commission 2025 report: only 17 of 40 CSBs implemented; 10 more slated 2026-07-01; 13 never received planning funds; six 2024 sites completed planning but implementation funding omitted from budget. 89 of 133 localities "exempt"; one refuses Protocol 1 with no entity able to compel. Hits AC1, AC4, AC5, I1.
- **Corrections oversight new but real.** Ombudsman office created 2023-24 inside Office of the State Inspector General; Andrea Sapone hired late 2024; VADOC access agreement 2025-10-24. Against: Charles Givens death (Marion CTC, 2022-02-05), 13 hypothermia hospitalizations at Marion over three years, VADOC refusing to release facility complaints.
- **FOIA is a documented weakness.** Broad "working papers" exemption shields governor's office and legislators; one of only two states that never defined "personnel records." **Relevant to the Michigan/Massachusetts FOIA consistency question in the runbook's open items.**
- **Medicaid.** Expansion enrollment down to ~595,000 early 2025; uninsured rate rose to 6.9% in 2024; ~500,000 face new work-requirement standards. *Attribution caution: 2025 federal law drives most of this — score only Virginia's implementation discretion.*

**Not researched:** EMP (all 5), BND (all 5), INT (all 5), EQ5 historical harm (eugenics compensation, Massive Resistance), state-employee turnover, eviction/housing, opioid abatement spending, state psychiatric hospital census, **and the entire Spanberger administration from Jan 2026** — the most heavily weighted window, entirely unexamined. Virginia's single non-consecutive term rule makes this the most load-bearing gap.

## New Jersey (4 searches — all verified against publisher URL paths)

- **OPRA rollback, June 2024.** Murphy signed S2930, effective 2024-09-03. Deleted the statutory clause construing OPRA in favor of public access; narrowed attorney-fee recovery to "unreasonable denial, bad faith, or knowing and willful"; extended deadlines to 14/21 business days. Material AB3 transparency regression, and notable because it cuts against New Jersey's policy reputation.
- **Environmental Justice Law.** Signed 2020-09-18, rules effective spring 2023; first US state to mandate permit denial on disproportionate impact in overburdened communities. NJ Appellate Division unanimously upheld January 2026, rejecting economic benefit as a pollution tradeoff. Strong A5/S4.
- **Edna Mahan.** Murphy ordered closure 2021 after officers assaulted incarcerated women; phase one complete, main medium/max building still operating; $310M replacement broke ground October 2025, opening 2027, full completion 2029. 2021 DOJ consent decree jointly terminated. **Six-year gap between order and delivery — the "strong law, weaker delivery" pattern that put Colorado at 52.5.**
- **Office of the State Comptroller.** Active independent oversight; 2026-01-14 PFRS pension report found ~60 members with misconduct records drawing benefits, 21 without required honorable-service review. Countervailing: a November 2025 bill would curtail the Comptroller's corruption-investigation powers.

**Not researched:** school segregation litigation, incarceration and juvenile-justice disparity, affordable housing (2024 A4), ARRIVE Together, Nurture NJ maternal outcomes, NJ Transit, veterans homes, state psychiatric hospitals, school funding formula, Cover All Kids, **January 2026 Murphy-to-Sherrill transition**.

Relevant to AC1, B4 Refusal Ethics, EQ1 Universality — **and the county-level delivery raises the attribution question directly.**

## Process findings from the failure

All six agents independently reached the same two conclusions, which are now reflected in the runbook:

1. **A parent-session probe does not prove a child agent has budget.** The probe must be the child's first action, with an explicit instruction to abort on failure. Wisconsin burned 12 searches and context before discovering the wall; a first-action probe would have failed at zero cost.
2. **WebFetch survives WebSearch exhaustion but is not a substitute.** It requires knowing URLs in advance. Three agents tested it: Virginia got a stale cached JLARC page (nothing after Oct 2023) and undecodable binary; Maryland got HTTP 403; New Jersey confirmed it works but noted that driving an assessment through it means guessing URLs from a January 2026 knowledge cutoff — which is inferred data by another route, in exactly the window the methodology weights most heavily.
