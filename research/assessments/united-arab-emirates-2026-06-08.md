## Methodology Ruling: ACTIVE-COMPLICITY-IN-MASS-ATROCITY-BY-PROXY (NEW)

The published 23.4 profile treats the UAE as an ordinary mid-band autocracy (constrained civic space, migrant-labor inequity, weak accountability) but does NOT price the now well-documented pattern of the UAE actively financing, arming, and supplying mercenaries to the RSF in Sudan — a force the UN Fact-Finding Mission found (Feb 19, 2026) to be committing acts bearing the "hallmarks of genocide."

This is distinct from the absolute 0.0 active-perpetration floor reserved for sovereigns committing or directing in-territory mass atrocity (Sudan, Russia, Israel, Myanmar). The UAE is not perpetrating atrocity on its own territory or against its own population; it is a **complicit external sponsor** of another actor's genocide. The defensible ruling is therefore a scorable downgrade — NOT a floor designation — that pulls the integrity-of-care dimensions (INT, ACC, SYS, EMP) downward to reflect that the state's external conduct directly enables mass atrocity. The BND dimension (3.0) is preserved: the UAE's domestic scope/service-delivery capacity is unchanged. The result (18.4) crosses the Developing→Critical band boundary, exceeds the 5-point proposal threshold, and is the most defensible magnitude given that the complicity is documented and ongoing but adjudication (ICJ case filed March 2025) is pending.
