## Key Findings

- **Tennessee's own auditor is the strongest compassion institution in the state, and its findings mostly do not change what the state does.** The Comptroller of the Treasury published a roughly 200-page audit of the Department of Children's Services on 2025-12-16 and the first evaluation of the school voucher program on 2026-01-05. Six months after the voucher audit found participating students underperforming their public school peers, the state increased the voucher vendor contract by $356 million.

- **A federal court found Tennessee's Medicaid agency broke three separate laws, and no repair has yet reached anyone.** On 2024-08-26 the court held TennCare violated the Medicaid Act, the Due Process Clause and the Americans with Disabilities Act, in a case covering more than 100,000 people whose benefits were terminated. The court singled out an unwritten policy of refusing hearings to people who said they never got notice. The remedy is still in mediation two years later.

- **The state turned down $84 million in federal food aid for 700,000 children, then reversed itself.** Tennessee declined Summer EBT funds in 2024 over administrative costs and substituted a $3 million state pilot reaching 18,000 students — about 2.6 percent of the eligible children. After sustained criticism, the General Assembly allocated $7 million and Tennessee will rejoin the federal program in 2027. This is the clearest correction in the record and it took two years.

- **Tennessee has the worst maternal mortality score in the United States while running some of the country's better maternal benefits.** The March of Dimes 2025 report card, published 2025-11-17, ranked Tennessee last. The same state was among the first to adopt 12-month postpartum Medicaid coverage and added a first-in-nation diaper benefit. Good programs sit on top of a coverage gap the state has chosen not to close.

- **Two of Tennessee's three branches sit largely outside its public records law.** Courts have held the Public Records Act does not reach the House and Senate, and the governor's office uses a judge-made deliberative-process exemption to withhold records. Because the Comptroller is still publishing damaging findings, transparency scores 3 out of 5 under the benchmark's cross-state rule rather than the floor of 2. That single decision is worth 1.3 composite points.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Awareness (2.8) and Systemic Thinking (2.8).** Both rest on structures that outlast any single administration: the Comptroller's recurring adversarial audit cycle, the statutory maternal and infant mortality review committees, the 10-year TennCare III demonstration, the Opioid Abatement Council distributing to all 95 counties, and the 2026 FAIR Rx Act taking on pharmacy benefit manager consolidation.
- **Accountability (2.4)** is third, carried by uncompelled audit publication and by one real correction on child summer nutrition.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Equity (1.6).** Non-expansion excludes a defined population by choice; a federal court found the eligibility system screened people out through design defects; Black infant mortality is more than double white infant mortality; no reparative or historical-harm work was found.
- **Empathy (1.8).** The state's flagship health agency was found to have discriminated against people with disabilities and to have refused hearings to people who said they never received notice. Both score at the floor.
- **Integrity (2.0) and Boundaries (2.0).** The 2023 red-flag commitment collapsed under political pressure within four months. Notices found "misleading and deficient" by a federal court sink both scope clarity and refusal ethics to 1.

## Evidence Gaps

- **E5 Cultural Empathy, B5 Consent Orientation, EQ5 Historical Harm Acknowledgment and I5 Resilience of Care** are scored at or near the lower anchor with low confidence because public evidence is thin, not because harm is established. Per the conservative-scoring rule these were not estimated upward.
- **I5 is structurally untestable.** Tennessee has had one governor since January 2019. No leadership transition has stressed its practices in the assessment window.
- **Two matters are unresolved and are recorded as pending rather than scored as outcomes:** the Tennessee Bureau of Investigation criminal probe into the Erwin flood deaths, and the remedy phase of A.M.C. v. Smith.
- **Erwin recovery outcomes** are documented mainly through national news coverage and federal disaster announcements. No independent state-level evaluation of Tennessee's own Helene recovery execution was located, which limits confidence on AC1 and AC5.

## Recommended Next Steps

Tennessee scores 30.0, in the Developing band. Key structures are emerging but inconsistent or reactive. Consider a [Certified Assessment](/certified-assessments) for a structured improvement roadmap, with priority on Equity and Empathy, where the state's scores are driven by findings a court has already established and the state has not yet remedied.
