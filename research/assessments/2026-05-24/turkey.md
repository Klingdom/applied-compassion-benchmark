# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Turkey (2026-05-24)

**Trigger:** Turkish riot police stormed the CHP (main opposition party) headquarters in Ankara on May 24, firing tear gas and rubber bullets at officials and supporters who had barricaded inside for 3 days. This occurred within 24 hours of the May 23 formal apply (-4.9, Developing → Critical) which was based on the May 21 judicial removal of CHP leadership.

## Triggering Evidence Window

- **2026-05-24:** Turkish riot police physically entered the CHP headquarters in Ankara, firing tear gas and rubber bullets at the party members and supporters inside. Police broke through makeshift barricades. Confirmed by NBC News, Washington Post, PBS News, Al Jazeera, Bloomberg, Euronews.
- **2026-05-24:** Bloomberg reported the event as the violent end to a 3-day standoff between the sitting CHP membership and a court-appointed leadership team — confirming that the police action was direct enforcement of the May 21 judicial annulment.
- **2026-05-24:** EU foreign affairs office issued only a brief "dialogue" statement — no specific condemnation, no Article 7 process, no Council of Europe mechanism triggered. Accountability vacuum confirmed at the supranational level.
- **Carry-forward (24-hour):** The May 23 cycle applied -4.9 (30.8 → 17.6) for the May 21 judicial removal of CHP leadership. That apply established the JUDICIAL-REMOVAL-OF-OPPOSITION-PARTY-LEADERSHIP precedent. The May 24 storming is a distinct enforcement event.

## Methodology-Novel Adjudication: "Physical Seizure of Opposition Party Headquarters"

The scanner flagged this as a methodology-novel event type. I rule as follows.

**Ruling — distinct category established.** Physical state-security enforcement of a judicial party-leadership removal is categorically distinct from the judicial removal itself:

- The judicial removal (May 21) is a legal-institutional act — courts removing leadership.
- The physical storming (May 24) is a state-security enforcement act — riot police entering an opposition political party's own building, firing tear gas and rubber bullets at members who refuse to vacate.

No prior benchmark case has paired a judicial leadership removal with a physical headquarters seizure inside a 72-hour window. The closest analogues are state-on-citizen mass-protest dispersal events, but those occur in public space; an opposition party's own headquarters is constitutionally protected space.

**Weighting convention established — second-event-at-floor:**

When an entity has just received a formal apply at near-floor (Critical-band proximity) and a distinct categorical event occurs in the immediate post-apply window, apply a CONSERVATIVE second delta in the **-2 to -3 range** (not the full -4 to -6 magnitude that would apply absent the prior apply).

Rationale:
1. **Avoids double-counting.** The systemic governance failure was already substantially absorbed in the May 23 apply. The May 24 event is downstream enforcement of the same underlying failure mode.
2. **Preserves accumulation room.** Turkey at 15.1 (post this apply) retains 15.1 points before the absolute floor — important for future further-deterioration signals (mass arrests, election cancellation, etc.).
3. **Floor-proximity discipline.** Scoring near floor must be calibrated to preserve discriminating power across remaining downward space. A -5 apply on top of -4.9 would consume 1/3 of the remaining floor distance for what is effectively one continuous event arc.
4. **Symmetric parallel — Vanuatu.** May 23 applied +5.5 to Vanuatu (band crossing Developing → Functional) at the boundary. The corresponding methodology-novel ruling was: apply once at full magnitude; further-confirming events accumulate conservatively. Turkey's downward second-event apply follows the same logic mirrored.

**Magnitude selected: -2.5.** Midpoint of the -2 to -3 conservative range. Calibration:
- BND -5.0: physical occupation of opposition party's own building is the brightest possible second-order boundary failure on top of the legal-leadership failure already scored. The party has lost both its leadership in law (May 21) and its physical institutional space in fact (May 24).
- ACC -2.5: EU response was minimal; no formal supranational accountability mechanism triggered. Stronger international response would have been a partial offset; its absence is itself an accountability vacuum.
- INT -2.5: state security used as instrument of partisan political enforcement — further values-action inversion.
- AWR / EMP / ACT / SYS -2.5: incremental erosion of detection, recognition, response, and systemic architecture; mostly already absorbed in May 23 apply.
- EQU 0: no new equity-specific signal in the 24-hour window.
