# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Poland (2026-05-24)

**Trigger:** Polish parliament selected 15 new KRS members May 18, defying Constitutional Tribunal injunction — competing positive signal against prior -1.8 sub-threshold downward accumulator. Net direction uncertain at boundary.

## Triggering Evidence Window

- **2026-05-18:** Sejm selected 15 new KRS members; new membership includes 13 judiciary-nominated candidates + 1 PiS + 1 Konfederacja by statutory requirement. PiS boycotted the vote, calling it illegal.
- **2026-05-12 (carry-forward):** KRS four-year terms expired.
- **Prior (carry-forward):** Nawrocki presidential veto of primary KRS reform bill (February 2026); CT injunction ordering parliament not to proceed with alternative-path selection.

## Methodology Note — Competing Signals at Boundary

This is a methodologically complex two-axis event:

**Positive axis (governance restoration):**
- Tusk government used legitimate term-expiry pathway.
- Judiciary-nominated members replace PiS-era political appointees.
- Statutory requirement to include opposition (PiS, Konfederacja) representation honored.

**Negative axis (procedural concern):**
- CT injunction defied.
- PiS boycott; partisan contestation continues.
- Establishes precedent for defying CT orders even when CT itself is contested.

**Adjudication:** The CT that issued the injunction is the PiS-era CT whose legitimacy has been repeatedly questioned by EU institutions (LIBE Committee, Venice Commission). Defying an institutionally-compromised CT to restore independent judicial governance is a defensible action — but it does set a precedent that subsequent governments could weaponize against legitimate constitutional courts.

**Net delta = 0.** The two axes substantially offset each other within the current assessment cycle. The prior -1.8 sub-threshold accumulator was based on the veto-blocked reform pathway; the May 18 KRS reconstitution closes that specific blockage. No apply this cycle.
