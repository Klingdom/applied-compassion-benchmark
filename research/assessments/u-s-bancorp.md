## Scoring Judgment (40 subdimensions condensed to 8 dimensions)

- **AWR 56.3 (Awareness):** Public ESG/CRA reporting acknowledges community impact; the explicit CFPB finding that the bank "knew" of employee misconduct for "over a decade" documents a structural awareness-action gap. Functional.
- **EMP 56.3 (Empathy):** Sales-incentive structure that drove the violations is itself a worker-pressure system; though no documented systemic worker-mistreatment lawsuit in window, the incentive design is a partial EMP signal. Functional.
- **ACT 62.5 (Action):** Genuine product-mission delivery — community banking, small-business credit, affordable mortgage, CRA-rated lending. Functional-plus floor. ([U.S. Bank Community](https://www.usbank.com/about-us-bank/community/community-development.html))
- **EQU 56.3 (Equity):** Sham-accounts harm fell broadly across customer base; credit-profile damage is regressive (lower-income customers more exposed to credit-score impacts). Functional.
- **BND 50.0 (Boundaries):** Population-level boundary issues: unauthorized accounts, PII misuse, credit-profile damage; secondary cash-sweep concern documented in April 2026 sector sweep. Functional minus.
- **ACC 43.8 (Accountability):** Lowest non-INT dimension. Fine paid; bank says processes improved in 2016; but CEO-level shareholder derivative lawsuit remains active in 2026 — accountability cycle not closed. Functional minus.
- **SYS 62.5 (Systems):** Genuine systems-level positive — CRA-rated community lending, small-business banking, mortgage access. Functional-plus floor.
- **INT 43.8 (Integrity):** "Over a decade" of organizational awareness with employee-misappropriation pattern is a multi-year integrity gap. Held at 43.8 rather than lower because documented post-2016 process improvements and the absence of post-settlement repeat-violation findings argue for reform credit. Functional minus.
