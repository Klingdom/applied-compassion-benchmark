## Methodology Adjudication: Is Protest Mobilization a Scored Event?

**Ruling — NO discrete third-event apply this cycle.** Reasoning:

1. **Societal-response signals are captured within the precipitating governance event.** The Compassion Benchmark scores institutional behavior, not civil society response. Mass protest is the populace responding to the institutional acts already scored (May 23 judicial removal, May 24 physical seizure). Scoring the protest as a new event would risk double-counting the same underlying institutional failure.
2. **International characterization shifts qualitative weight but does not add new institutional acts.** Time magazine's "deepening democratic crisis" framing is media interpretation of the arc, not a new state action. The OHCHR / Council of Europe / Article 7 mechanisms remain unfired at supranational level — accountability vacuum persists but is unchanged from May 24.
3. **The nine Sunday-morning Istanbul arrests are continuation of the same prosecution arc** already captured under the "voter influencing" framing that anchored the May 21 judicial decision. Same arc, same legal theory.
4. **DEM Party condemnation is a positive democratic signal (cross-partisan solidarity)** — it counts as a faint accountability counter-signal, not a negative event.

**Threshold for a discrete third event would be:** (a) state security forces using lethal force against the Parliament march or other protesters; (b) mass arrests of protesters (not just nine 2023-congress-related arrests); (c) formal closure of CHP by court order; (d) declaration of emergency / suspension of parliamentary procedure. None of these have occurred in the May 25 24-hour window.

**Per the second-event-at-floor convention (Ruling 3, May 24):** Floor-proximity discipline requires preserving accumulation room for further deterioration signals. A third apply within 72 hours absent a categorically distinct event would consume disproportionate downward space.
