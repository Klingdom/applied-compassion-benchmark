# Assessment: Venezuela — Earthquake Day 12 (Exogenous-Disaster Screen)

**Composite:** 18.0/100 (CONFIRM — only the government's response is scorable)
**Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
The earthquake itself is a natural disaster, not a government act, so only Venezuela's response to it counts toward the score. A rising death toll reflects the scale of the quake and slowly improving disclosure — not a new choice by the state to harm its people.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1))
- Live baseline **18.0**, rank **150**, Critical. Already prices chronic data opacity, weak institutional relief capacity, and thin equitable-access design.

## The scorable question
- Day 12: 3,300+ confirmed dead, 16,400+ injured, 50,000+ unaccounted; UN estimates $4.7–8.7B damage. The government's $200M reconstruction fund covers <1% of estimated need; WFP appealed $50M, UNICEF is supplying 100,000 in-country.
- The quake is **exogenous**; only the state's **response** is scorable. The rising toll is **scale and disclosure**, not fresh state-inflicted harm.
- Chronic data opacity is **already priced**. Any single-source "true toll 3×" or falsification claim is a **forward trigger**, not yet independently verified. Acceptance of WFP/UNICEF aid blocks a downgrade on obstruction grounds.

## §3e-bis screening
- **(3) Exogeneity/directionality:** disaster harm is not own conduct; absence of new own harm is not an upgrade.
- **(4) Double-count:** opacity already reflected in baseline.
- **Forward-trigger:** verified suppression/falsification of death data would be a realized-harm trigger.

## Disposition
**CONFIRM at 18.0. Delta 0.0. No change proposal.** Confidence: medium.
- **Forward trigger (downgrade):** independently verified falsification/suppression of casualty data, or deliberate relief obstruction.
- **Forward trigger (upgrade):** transparent, equitable relief scale-up.
