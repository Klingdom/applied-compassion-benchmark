# Special Upward Assessment: United Kingdom

**Composite:** 65.5/100 (CONFIRM — no upgrade warranted)
**Band:** Established

## Why it matters (plain language)
The UK's Employment Rights Act 2025 is a major worker-rights reform — day-one sick pay and paternity leave, easier union recognition. But it received Royal Assent in December 2025, before this window, and is phasing in through 2027. Within the window there are only consultations and process steps. And the UK's last full assessment found the published 65.5 was, if anything, slightly high — because of the April 2026 disability-benefit cuts pushing on the other side of the ledger.

## Symmetric-rigor screens
- **Window:** The Employment Rights Act's Royal Assent (Dec 18, 2025) and the Fair Work Agency launch (Apr 7, 2026) are both out-of-window. Within-window items are consultations (NDAs to Jul 8; zero-hours to Aug 25) and phased provisions — process, not realized harm-reduction events.
- **Already in the environment / countervailing evidence (DECISIVE):** The most recent full UK baseline (**2026-04-30**) assessed the UK at **60.6** — *below* the published 65.5 — with the Employment Rights Act already in the policy environment, because the Universal Credit health-element cut (~730k disabled claimants, ~400k projected into poverty) pressed EMP/ACT/EQU down. A within-window upgrade would run against the most recent own-conduct evidence.
- **Realization:** Phased day-one rights will become realized as commencement orders land, but not as a single ≥5-point within-window event now.

## Disposition
CONFIRM at 65.5. Delta 0.0. No change proposal. The headline reform is out-of-window and offset by concurrent welfare-contraction evidence. **Watch:** as Employment Rights Act provisions commence through 2026-27, re-test worker-welfare subdimensions; net against UC-cut harm data.
