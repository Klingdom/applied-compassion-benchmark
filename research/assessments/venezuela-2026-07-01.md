# Assessment: Venezuela — Earthquake Day 7 (Disaster-Response Screen)

**Composite:** 18.0/100 (CONFIRM)
**Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
Seven days after Venezuela's worst recorded earthquake, the confirmed death toll rose to 1,943 (from 1,719 on June 30), with 10,500+ injured and 43,200 reported missing — a figure the New York Times warns may be a "substantial undercount." A former civil-defense chief publicly asked how the armed forces could be "a no-show" in the worst quake in the country's history, and Vice President Rodríguez was blamed for a haphazard response. At the same time the government has accepted broad help: 20+ international rescue teams, a US $300M commitment, and a second UNICEF shipment.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1))
- Live baseline **18.0**, rank **151**, Critical. Confirmed at 18.0 across the June cycle on a mixed disaster-response read.

## §3e-bis screening
- **Exogenous cause:** the earthquake is a natural disaster, not own-conduct. Only the government response is scorable.
- **Mixed response nets sub-threshold:** stronger criticism of relief failures (military "no-show," VP blamed) is directionally negative and blocks any upgrade; continued broad acceptance of international aid blocks a downgrade. The rising toll reflects the disaster's scale plus disclosure, not a new government harm act.
- **Double-count:** the response-quality concern was already priced in the June confirmations; incremental day-7 developments do not clear the 5-point threshold.
