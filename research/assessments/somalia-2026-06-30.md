# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Somalia

**Assessment date:** 2026-06-30
**Composite:** 4.7/100 (CONFIRM — near-floor reinforced)
**Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
Buur Hakaba (Burhakaba) district has reached IPC AMN Phase 5 (Extremely Critical), with a Global Acute Malnutrition rate of 37.1% and acute malnutrition already past the famine threshold. Nearly 1.88 million children are projected to need treatment for acute malnutrition in 2026, and roughly 6 million people — about one in three of the analyzed population — face Crisis or worse, with WFP reaching only about one in ten. Deterioration is driven by poor Gu rainfall, food-price spikes linked to the 2026 Middle East conflict, currency depreciation, Al-Shabaab attacks, and declining aid. The scan notes no new formal famine declaration was published June 30. The score holds.

## §3e-bis screening + near-floor limitation
- **Baseline provenance:** 4.7 (rank 179; AWR/EMP/ACT 1.5, EQU/BND/ACC/SYS/INT 1.0); standing critical-band baseline carried through the June series.
- **Near-floor limitation:** Compounding hunger and malnutrition reinforce the near-floor but do not clear a new 5-point threshold toward the floor.
- **(4) Double-count:** The Buur Hakaba Phase 5 / 6M Crisis+ figures continue the already-priced arc; no new formal famine declaration June 30.
- **(3) Attribution / exogeneity:** Drought (poor Gu rains), regional price shocks, and Al-Shabaab attacks are exogenous or non-state drivers; only the federal government's own response capacity is scorable, and it remains consistent with the existing near-floor band. Directionality: surfaced on negative evidence, no positive own-conduct → no upgrade; no new own-harm threshold → no downgrade.
