## Attribution Analysis

The proximate driver of the deepening energy crisis is **externally-attributed** (the US oil blockade cutting off crude supply), compounded by long-standing internal infrastructure decay. Neither factor is a discrete new own-conduct atrocity. The scoreable own-conduct concerns are (a) the state's response and equity in managing the crisis, and (b) its treatment of protesters — Cuba's suppression of dissent is already reflected in the low ACC (1.5) and INT (1.5) subscores. This window shows scattered protest but no dated new evidence of a mass-repression event crossing the threshold. Cuba's relatively higher ACT/SYS (3.0) reflect its historically strong public-health and social-service architecture, now under acute strain. Net: sub-threshold; the crisis pressures the score downward but no discrete new own-conduct failure is evidenced within window.

## Why no proposal

Energy crisis is externally-compounded; own-conduct repression already priced; no new discrete threshold-crossing event. No dimension changes; no sidecar required.
