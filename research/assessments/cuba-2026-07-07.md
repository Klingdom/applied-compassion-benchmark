# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Cuba (Confirmation with Downward Watch)

**Entity type:** Country | **Assessment date:** 2026-07-07
**Composite score:** 32.8/100 | **Band:** Developing

## Score Summary
Published dimensions confirmed, composite 32.8.

## Within-Window Evidence (2026-06-23 to 2026-07-07)
- **Third nationwide blackout in six months (Jul 6):** Total grid collapse left ~10M without power; the Electric Union declared "total disconnection of the national electric power system" (Al Jazeera, CBS, Bloomberg).
- **Healthcare impact (ACT):** Tens of thousands of surgeries canceled nationwide; public transport largely halted. Cuba produces only ~40% of the fuel it needs.
- **Protests (rare):** Daily protests — a rarity for decades — with residents banging pots and blocking roads.
- **State response:** Diaz-Canel blamed US sanctions/fuel blockade; authorities restored "vital services" via microsystems ~2 hours after collapse.

## Threshold Analysis (why confirm, not propose)
Cuba's traditionally higher ACT (3.0) and SYS (3.0) reflect historic service-delivery capacity now under strain. Docking ACT 3.0→2.5 and SYS 3.0→2.5 (healthcare disruption, grid collapse) yields a canonical composite of **29.7**, delta **−3.1** — below the 5-point threshold and no band change (stays Developing). The crisis is (a) largely externally driven (US fuel blockade), (b) ongoing/known (a dedicated "2024-2026 Cuba blackouts" record exists), and (c) partially mitigated (vital services restored in ~2 hours). This is deterioration but sub-threshold. **Confirmation with a downward watch flag.**
