## Assessment Basis

This is an event-driven reassessment triggered by the federal whistleblower lawsuit filed by Robert Gruendel, Figure AI's former head of product safety, in the Northern District of California, alleging wrongful termination after he raised safety alarms. The complaint, as reported by Gizmodo and Notebookcheck, makes specific, score-relevant allegations:

- On joining, Gruendel allegedly found Figure had "no formal safety procedures, incident-reporting systems, or risk-assessment processes for the robots," and leadership expressed disdain for safety documentation.
- Impact testing allegedly showed the robot could strike at "twenty times higher than the threshold of pain" and with "more than twice the force necessary to fracture an adult human skull."
- A malfunctioning robot allegedly punched a refrigerator, leaving "a ¼-inch deep gash in the stainless-steel door" while narrowly missing an employee.
- A home-deployment safety roadmap that investors had reviewed and approved was allegedly downgraded by leadership after the company's ~$39B valuation round — an action Gruendel flagged as potentially "fraudulent." An emergency-stop initiative was allegedly abandoned and a safety feature axed for aesthetic reasons.
- Figure states Gruendel was "terminated for poor performance" and called the claims "falsehoods that Figure will thoroughly discredit in court."

The allegations are pre-adjudication. They are scored as credible, specific, Tier-2/3 evidence (sworn federal complaint reported by multiple outlets) pointing to a gutted internal safety function, retaliation against a safety voice, and a documented anticipatory-harm gap. This drives downgrades concentrated in AWR (anticipatory awareness), BND (consent/scope and refusal ethics around deployment risk), ACC (harm acknowledgment, correction willingness, transparency), and INT (consistency under pressure, internal consistency, values alignment).

### Recommendation
Downgrade from 37.5 to 31.3 (medium confidence). The published flat 2.5 profile predates the Gruendel complaint. The lawsuit provides specific, multiply-reported allegations of a gutted safety function, suppressed near-miss incident, retaliation against the designated safety voice, and a safety roadmap weakened under commercial pressure — concentrated harms to AWR, BND, ACC, and INT. Allegations are pre-adjudication, so the move is calibrated conservatively and confidence is medium. Stays in Developing band.
