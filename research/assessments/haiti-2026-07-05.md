# Assessment: Haiti — SCOTUS TPS Ruling + Gang Crisis (Near-Floor Screen)

**Composite:** 4.7/100 (CONFIRM — near-floor; drivers are US and non-state conduct)

## Why it matters (plain language)
Haiti sits near the bottom because the state can barely protect or provide for its people. This window's headline events are driven by others: a US Supreme Court ruling and armed gangs. Neither is a fresh act of Haitian-state own conduct that would move an already near-floor score.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1))
- Live baseline **4.7**, rank **177**, Critical. This already prices near-total state incapacity, gang control of the capital, mass displacement, and food-insecurity collapse (most dimensions at 1.0–1.5).

## The scorable question: Haiti's own state conduct/capacity

- 1.5M+ internally displaced; 5.8M (52%) at crisis food insecurity, 1.8M at emergency — the ongoing crisis already priced into the near-floor 4.7. No new within-window evidence of a fresh Haitian-state deliberate-harm event, nor of a positive countervailing state-led protection/relief scale-up.
