## Own-conduct evidence (within window)

- **Mixed protection posture.** Lebanon's Ministry of Social Affairs has emerged as a "rights-oriented actor championing a protection-centred approach" to displacement, coordinating aid for displaced Lebanese citizens (The New Humanitarian, Mar 2026). This is a positive own-conduct signal — but it operates against severe state capacity collapse (currency crisis, public-service strain).
- **Differential treatment of non-citizens (EQU/EMP pressure).** Administrative categories such as citizenship status determine access to emergency shelters, food, and cash. Syrian and Palestinian refugees and migrants are separated from Lebanese nationals; some migrant communities were "explicitly turned away, discriminated against, or deprioritized" from collective shelters in favor of Lebanese nationals (The New Humanitarian, Mar 2026). This is own-conduct that constrains the equity and universality subdimensions.
- **State-capacity collapse limits action.** Pre-war, the refugee presence already strained healthcare, education, and electricity; UNHCR's termination of hospitalisation support left tens of thousands without inpatient care (UNHCR / USA for UNHCR, Mar 2026).
