## Cross-State Consistency Flag: AB3 Transparency Rule

**Rule applied.** The adopted cross-state rule caps AB3 at 3 rather than flooring it at 2 where a broad public-records exemption coexists with an independently funded oversight body still publishing damaging, uncompelled findings.

Washington meets both halves. The Court of Appeals upheld a broad legislative privilege in February 2026, letting legislators withhold internal deliberative records despite the 2019 Supreme Court ruling that they are covered by the Public Records Act. Against that, the state funds three oversight bodies that are visibly still producing: the State Auditor published the Digital Navigator finding ($11 million unverifiable) and the childcare finding ($37 million questionable) in 2026; the Office of the Corrections Ombuds published its FY2025 annual report on unexpected deaths with a Department of Corrections response attached; the Office of Family and Children's Ombuds publishes child fatality and near-fatality reports.

The Massachusetts output contingency is satisfied — these bodies are producing current findings, not existing on paper. AB3 is therefore scored 3, not 2.

### Score Difference Analysis

**This is not institutional decline.** The published 94.4 was never measured. It was assigned. A 35-point drop records the replacement of a fabricated number with a researched one, and nothing about Washington's actual conduct in 2021-2026.

**BND (-47.5) is the largest gap and the most substantive finding.** The placeholder implied Washington runs sustainable, boundary-respecting services. The evidence shows the opposite at the frontline. The Legislature itself set a target of 35 clients per developmental disability case manager, then funded only part of it, leaving managers at 75. Behavioral health workforce shortages are the direct mechanism of the Trueblood failures. And when the state cannot help, it often provides no alternative: Apple Health Expansion simply stopped taking applications.

**ACT and EMP (-32.5 each) turn on one fact.** A federal court has fined Washington roughly $400 million for failing to provide competency evaluation and restoration within constitutionally required deadlines, and has found the state in contempt three separate times. Only $100 million had been paid as of the July ruling. No entity scoring 4.5 on Action carries that record.

**SYS (-12.5) is where the placeholder came closest to right.** Washington built the first state-run long-term care insurance program in the United States, which begins paying benefits in July 2026. It runs a cap-and-invest market that has raised $2.9 billion. It capped rent increases statewide for 15 years. It passed a Millionaires Tax in 2026. On root causes, Washington is a genuine national outlier — just not at 4.5.

## Key Findings

- **Washington's published score of 94.4 out of 100 was never measured — it was a bulk-import placeholder.** Its eight numbers are identical to the ones given to Massachusetts. This assessment replaces it with a researched score of 59.4, which is a data correction, not a sign that Washington got worse.

- **A federal court has fined Washington about $400 million for making people wait too long for mental health care in jail.** The court found the state in contempt three separate times in the Trueblood case. Only $100 million of that had been paid as of the July ruling. This single record is what holds down Action, Empathy and Accountability.

- **Washington leads the country on fixing causes rather than symptoms.** It launched the first state-run long-term care insurance program in the United States, paying benefits from July 2026. It raised $2.9 billion through carbon auctions since January 2023. It capped statewide rent increases for 15 years in May 2025. Systemic Thinking, at 4.0 out of 5, is its best dimension.

- **The Covenant Homeownership Program is the strongest single piece of evidence found.** It lent $60 million to more than 500 households in its first year to repair the damage from racially restrictive housing covenants. Washington expanded it in 2025 and kept it running after federal housing officials opened an investigation in March 2026. It earns the only score of 5 in this assessment.

- **The state's care for its own staff is its weakest area, at 2.6 out of 5.** Lawmakers set a caseload target of 35 clients per developmental disability case manager and then funded only part of it, leaving staff at 75. Those same staffing gaps are what keep the state in contempt of court.

## Strongest Dimensions

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Empathy, Action, Accountability and Integrity (3.2/5 each)** — all four are pulled down by the same twelve-year pattern of contesting the Trueblood claims rather than validating them.

This assessment scores Washington state conduct across both political branches, 2021-2026, spanning the Inslee and Ferguson administrations. Federal policy imposed on Washington is excluded. The 2025 federal reconciliation law's Medicaid changes are not scored against the state; what is scored is Washington's own discretionary choice to cap Apple Health Expansion at 13,000 people and to close it in December 2025. The HUD investigation into the Covenant Homeownership Program is federal action; what is scored is the state's decision to continue the program under investigation. Initiative 2113 was a citizen initiative enacted by the Legislature in March 2024 and is scored as state conduct.
