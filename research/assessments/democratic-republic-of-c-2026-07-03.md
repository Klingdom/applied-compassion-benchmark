# Assessment: DR Congo — Ebola in 4 Provinces, International Spread (Near-Floor / Exogenous-Disease Screen)

**Composite:** 2.3/100 (CONFIRM) | **Band:** Critical (near-floor)

## Why it matters (plain language)
The Ebola epidemic in DR Congo has reached 1,406 confirmed cases and 438 deaths across four provinces, using the Bundibugyo strain for which there is no approved vaccine or treatment. Cases have spread to Uganda (Kampala), France (an imported case) and Germany (an evacuated US citizen). A disease outbreak is an outside shock, so the score reads the government's RESPONSE, not the outbreak itself. Surveillance gaps remain, but DR Congo is already near the bottom at 2.3, and the M23 conflict harm is attributed to Rwanda-backed forces. New case counts reinforce but cannot meaningfully lower a near-floor score.
