## Key Findings

- Why it matters: On July 8, 2026 Algeria adopted a new Code of Criminal Procedure that Amnesty International says undermines fair trials — allowing expedited trials without adequate defense time, prosecutorial pretrial detention without judicial review, and travel bans without court oversight.
- Amnesty also documents the summary, collective expulsion of at least 21,948 migrants to Niger and repression of journalists via broad “terrorism” charges — a pattern of due-process and equity collapse.
- The evidence is consistent with the published low Developing score; a first 40-subdimension review confirms it.

## Strongest Dimensions

- Awareness and Action — a functioning, hydrocarbon-funded state administration delivers baseline services.

## Weakest Dimensions

- Equity, Accountability and Integrity — mass migrant expulsions, fair-trial violations, and a new code codifying reduced protections.
