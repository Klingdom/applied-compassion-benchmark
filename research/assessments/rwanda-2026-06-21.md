## Assessment basis & attribution

The window event is the HRW 78-page report "'Death Was Everywhere': Arbitrary Detention, Killings, and Forced Recruitment by the M23 and the Rwanda Defence Force" (Jun 10, 2026), documenting Rwandan military and M23 forced recruitment of thousands (including children as young as 12), torture, executions, sexual exploitation, and child-soldier use at the Rumangabo and Tshanzu camps between mid-2024 and Dec 2025. The Congo War Security Review (Jun 19) confirmed 2,100+ killed since the June 2025 peace deal.

**Attribution:** This is Rwanda's OWN conduct as a perpetrator — the Rwanda Defence Force is directly implicated by name. Under the methodology this scores against Rwanda's treatment of those it affects (it is not harm inflicted ON Rwanda by an external actor). It bears most heavily on ACC (Rwanda continues to deny M23 backing despite Tier-5 documentation), INT (consistency between stated and actual conduct), and EQU (treatment of vulnerable populations including conscripted children).

**Why this is a confirmation, not a downgrade.** Rwanda's ACC (1.5) and INT (1.5) are already near the dimensional floor. A defensible re-score (ACC 1.25, INT 1.25, EQU 1.75) yields composite 25.0 — a delta of -2.3, below the 5-point change-proposal threshold. Per the near-floor limitation, this fresh, high-quality adverse evidence is logged as an evidence-tier upgrade (the HRW report is Tier 5, equivalent to adjudicated-grade documentation of war crimes) and a watch flag, reinforcing rather than moving the composite. The existing 27.3 already reflects a repressive single-party state with documented transnational repression, torture of dissidents, and 99.18% election results.
