## Ruling: SAME-EVENT CONFIRM (military not yet deployed)

Bolivia was downgraded to 6.3 on Jun 9 on the basis of the state-of-exception law (enacted Jun 8/9) authorising military deployment to clear 90+ road blockades, and at least 9 deaths (6 attributed to denied medical access from blockades). The brief asks whether the now-"active" military state of exception is genuinely NEW escalation.

Primary-source verification does NOT support new escalation. JURIST (Jun 8): "Sunday's measure itself does not automatically send troops onto the roads" — President Paz must first issue an executive declaration, and "the next step will depend on whether Paz formally invokes the new powers." Independent reporting (Jun) likewise states the law was promulgated but "no state of exception has been declared, and none of those measures have been implemented so far." US Sec. Def. Hegseth's "attempted coup" framing internationalises the rhetoric but is not a new domestic harm.

The law-enactment + 9-death basis is exactly what set 6.3. Military deployment has not been confirmed as actually executed against protesters within the window. This is a **confirmation**, not new escalation. Bolivia is already near-floor (composite 6.3) with very little downside room; conservative practice is to confirm absent a confirmed mass-casualty deployment event. No scorable movement.
