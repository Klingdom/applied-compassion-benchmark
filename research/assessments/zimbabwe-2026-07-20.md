## Assessment Context

Zimbabwe was surfaced by the 2026-07-20 scan on the Constitution of Zimbabwe Amendment (No. 3) Act, which President Emmerson Mnangagwa signed on 2026-07-07. The Act extends presidential, parliamentary and local terms from five to seven years, postpones the 2028 elections to 2030, and — most significantly — abolishes direct popular election of the president in favour of selection by a joint sitting of Parliament. Zimbabwe was last assessed on 2026-06-16 while the Bill was in Parliament. This assessment tests whether the Act's enactment changes the score.

The assessed composite of 20 is 0.3 points below the published 20.3 — effectively identical. This is the most useful finding for Zimbabwe: the constitutional amendment, severe as it is, does not move the composite, because the published score already sat near the floor on exactly the dimensions the amendment damages. Accountability (published 1.5 raw, assessed 1.2) and Integrity (published 1.5, assessed 1.2) were already scored on the assumption of impunity and personalised rule. Awareness (published 2.0, assessed 2.2) and Equity (published 1.5, assessed 2) moved slightly UP, because the ZimLAC Rural Livelihoods Assessment is a genuine annual, provincially disaggregated needs-detection instrument that the earlier score did not fully credit. The two movements cancel.

## Key Findings

- Zimbabwe just removed its citizens' right to elect their own president. The Constitution of Zimbabwe Amendment (No. 3) Act, signed 2026-07-07, replaces direct popular election with selection by a joint sitting of Parliament.
- People who objected were beaten. Human Rights Watch reports police and unidentified armed men threatened, harassed and beat opposition politicians and civil society leaders, including at the public consultation meetings held about the amendment itself.
- The score does not move, and that is the point. At 20.3 out of 100, Zimbabwe was already scored as an institution without functioning accountability. The amendment confirms that assessment rather than changing it.
- Zimbabwe does measure its own hunger seriously. The ZimLAC Rural Livelihoods Assessment produces annual, province-by-province data disaggregated by female-headed households, disability and informal livelihoods. This is the country's single strongest compassion instrument.
- Nearly half the population lives in extreme poverty, and maternal mortality — while improving, from 357 per 100,000 in 2020 to 323 in 2023 — remains among the world's highest.

## Strongest Dimensions

Awareness (raw 2.2) and Action (raw 2.2), both still weak in absolute terms. The ZimLAC assessment gives Zimbabwe a real, disaggregated picture of rural deprivation, and sustained UN-partnered nutrition and maternal health work has produced measurable improvement in maternal mortality. Coalitional Compassion (S5) is the single highest subdimension at 3 of 5.

## Weakest Dimensions

Accountability (raw 1.2) and Integrity (raw 1.2), both at 1.2. Four of the ten subdimensions in these two dimensions score 1 out of 5. There is no impartial investigation of rights violations, no documented course correction, and the governing party changed the constitution to extend its own tenure and remove the popular vote for the presidency.

## Evidence Gaps

Zimbabwe's Gukurahundi hearings process, led by traditional chiefs, is a potentially significant Historical Harm Acknowledgment (EQ5) development but could not be verified from a primary source within this assessment; EQ5 is therefore scored 2 rather than 3, and this is the most likely source of understatement. Household-level service delivery data for urban populations is thin — ZimLAC is a rural instrument. No independent audit of government social-protection targeting was located. Internal Consistency (I3) is scored on general country-condition sources rather than public-sector workforce evidence.

## Calibration Note

Zimbabwe was assessed on 2026-06-16 and confirmed; this reassessment was triggered by the amendment's enactment. The near-zero delta is a genuine confirmation, not an artifact of reusing prior scores — all 40 subdimensions were scored independently from current sources. Note that a floor effect is operating: when a published score already sits at 20.3 out of 100, further deterioration in governance has very little room to register. Reviewers should read "no change" here as "already scored as failing," not as "nothing happened."

## Recommendation

**CONFIRM** (confidence: medium). Composite delta -0.3 points vs published. Delta is below the 5-point threshold; no change proposal filed.
