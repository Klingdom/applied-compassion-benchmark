# Special Upward Assessment: Ukraine

**Composite:** 50.0/100 (CONFIRM — no upgrade warranted)
**Band:** Functional

## Why it matters (plain language)
Ukraine and Russia swapped 205 prisoners of war each on May 15, 2026. That returned 205 captured Ukrainians home — a genuine relief for them and their families. But it is small against a continuing war, it was brokered by the United States, and it was already inside the window of the assessment that confirmed Ukraine at 50.0.

## Symmetric-rigor screens
- **Anti-double-count:** The May 15 POW swap fell inside the lookback of the **2026-05-15** Ukraine assessment, which confirmed 50.0 and even carried a +3.1 May-11 credit forward. It was reconsidered again on June 21-24. Already priced.
- **Realization:** The 205-for-205 swap is realized. The three-day ceasefire it was tied to collapsed; renewed strikes (24 civilians killed May 14-15) followed.
- **Attribution (partly exogenous):** The swap was US-brokered and bilateral with Russia. Ukraine's own-conduct share is real but partial — the §"own-conduct" bar is only half-met.
- **Magnitude:** The scan itself estimates +1 to +2, below the 5-point threshold, while civilian casualties continue to mount.

## Disposition
CONFIRM at 50.0. Delta 0.0. No change proposal. A realized but small, partly-exogenous, already-priced humanitarian act.
