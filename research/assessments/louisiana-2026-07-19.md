## The short version

Louisiana is two states in one dataset, and averaging them would hide the finding.

On one side, Louisiana runs one of the most sophisticated long-horizon climate adaptation programs of any US state, kept its Medicaid expansion through a change of party control, cut drug overdose deaths 31 percent in a single year, and led the nation in fourth-grade reading growth for two straight testing cycles. Those are real, measured, and unusual.

On the other side, Louisiana jails more of its people than any independent democratic country on earth, repealed its own 2017 sentencing reform, needed two federal court orders in one year to give field workers at Angola shade and water, and cut the number of majority-Black congressional districts from two to one in June 2026.

The strong side lands almost entirely in Awareness, Action and Systemic Thinking. The weak side lands almost entirely in Empathy, Equity, Boundaries and Integrity. That split is the assessment.

## Cross-State Consistency Flag: AB3 Transparency

**Rule considered:** the adopted AB3 cross-state rule, tightened after Idaho, caps AB3 at 3 rather than flooring it at 2 where a branch of government exempts itself from the public records law while an independently funded oversight body still publishes damaging, uncompelled findings.

**Applied, but non-binding.** Louisiana presents a partial executive-branch self-exemption rather than a blanket one. In the 2024 regular session the Legislature enacted a law restricting records requests to the Office of the Governor to Louisiana residents only, shielded portions of the Governor's schedule from disclosure, and removed personal liability from custodians who unreasonably withhold records or fail to respond. That is branch-level self-shielding and does trigger the rule's scope, unlike Idaho's enumerated subject-matter exemptions. Importantly, the broadest measure, Senate Bill 482, which would have exempted deliberative records across state government, was killed by Republicans in May 2024, so the self-exemption is narrower than Virginia's, Massachusetts's or Washington's.

**Output contingency satisfied.** The Massachusetts rider requires the oversight body to still be producing findings. The Louisiana Legislative Auditor released nearly 4,000 reports in calendar year 2025, including findings hostile to state and local management. The cap is earned.

**Scoring effect:** none. AB3 scores 3 whether or not the rule is applied, because the Legislative Auditor's independent output supports a 3 on its own evidence. With AB3 at 3, ACC is 3.0 and the composite is 42.5. If AB3 were floored at 2, ACC would be 2.8 and the composite would be 41.9, verified as `{"composite":41.9,"band":"Functional","integrationPremium":0}`. The band is Functional either way.

## Key Findings

- **Louisiana is not a low-capacity state; it is a state that applies high capacity unevenly.** It runs a 50-year coastal plan with published modeling, cut overdose deaths 31 percent in one year, and led the nation in fourth-grade reading growth twice in a row. It also jails more of its people than any independent democratic country on earth. The composite of 42.5 out of 100 reflects that split, not a middling performance everywhere.

- **The single biggest drag is what the state does to people in its custody.** A federal judge twice in one year ordered heat protections for men working Angola's farm line, finding they faced a "substantial risk of suffering serious harm." Fifteen people died unexpectedly at Elayn Hunt Correctional Center from January 2025 onward, out of 45 deaths there that year. In 2024 Louisiana became the first state in nearly 25 years to abolish discretionary parole.

- **Medicaid expansion is real evidence of compassion holding under political cost.** Louisiana adopted expansion in 2016 and has kept it through a full change of party control. Adult uninsurance fell from 22.7 percent to 8.9 percent. This is why Integrity scores 2.4 rather than near the floor. But more than 200,000 people came off the Medicaid rolls in the year to mid-2026.

- **The state cancelled the centerpiece of its own long-term plan.** In July 2025 Louisiana ended the $2.92 billion Mid-Barataria Sediment Diversion, designed to build up to 21 square miles of land over 50 years, after roughly a decade of work. The reversal cuts both ways: it shows genuine attention to second-order harms to fishing communities, and it shows a state abandoning its own stated priorities.

- **Louisiana's own auditor is one of its strongest compassion assets.** The Legislative Auditor released nearly 4,000 reports in calendar year 2025 and publishes findings that embarrass the state, including roughly $860 million in potential unemployment overpayments and a child welfare audit showing frontline vacancies up 240 percent. That independent output is why Accountability reaches 3.0 despite 2024 laws narrowing access to the Governor's records.
