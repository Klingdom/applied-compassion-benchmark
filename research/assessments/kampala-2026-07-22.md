## Key Findings

- Why it matters: Kampala residents who criticize the government risk military detention. Human Rights Watch reported on July 16, 2026 that Uganda’s military has seized critics and besieged the country’s largest independent media house since late June.
- The city was the sole locus of Uganda’s Ebola outbreak, recording 100+ deaths in the first week of July 2026 — detection came through crisis, not proactive surveillance.
- Kampala Capital City Authority runs real health, education and social-services directorates, lifting it just above a pure floor, but accountability and integrity are near-absent under national repression.

## Strongest Dimensions

- Awareness and Action — KCCA maintains functioning (if under-resourced) health, sanitation, education and social-service directorates.

## Weakest Dimensions

- Accountability and Integrity — military impunity, suppression of critics and media, and no mechanism for harm acknowledgment or repair.

## Evidence Gaps

- Desk assessment relied on HRW reporting and KCCA institutional pages; independent audits of municipal service equity were unavailable.
