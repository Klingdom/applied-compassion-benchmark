### Recommendation

Delta of 3.1 is below the 5-point threshold. Published score broadly confirmed. Ukraine maintains Functional band performance under extreme wartime conditions. The 49% increase in civilian casualties in March 2026 reflects worsening external attack, not deteriorating government performance. Minor downgrades in Empathy and Action reflect documented concerns about language policy and capacity limitations. No change proposal warranted.

## Key Findings
- Civilian casualties increased 49% in March 2026 vs February; 95% from short-range drone attacks
- At least 15,364 civilians killed since full-scale invasion began; 10.6 million displaced
- Ukraine has lost 70% of electricity generation capacity; millions receiving 3-4 hours daily
- Government cooperation with international monitoring continues; OHCHR reporting access maintained
- EU accession process driving genuine institutional reform despite wartime constraints

## Strongest Dimensions
- Awareness (50.0), Action (50.0), Accountability (50.0), Systemic Thinking (50.0), Integrity (50.0): Consistent functional performance maintained under extreme conditions

## Weakest Dimensions
- Equity (37.5): Minority rights concerns, wartime access barriers, front-line communities underserved
- Empathy (43.8): Language policy concerns, some mobilization enforcement issues

## Evidence Gaps
- Occupied territories largely inaccessible to independent monitors
- Full scale of civilian harm likely underreported
- Internal government decision-making during martial law not fully transparent
