## Key Findings

- Why it matters: Beirut is a city in collapse. Roughly 500,000 people remain displaced, the economy has shrunk nearly 40% since 2019, and one in three workers in southern Lebanon has lost their job since fighting resumed.
- The 2020 port blast that killed more than 220 people still has not produced a single convicted official; the World Bank blamed “ad-hoc”, corruption-prone port governance. Accountability is the defining failure.
- Much of the acute July 2026 harm stems from the Israel–Hezbollah–Iran conflict — external shocks the city absorbs — but the institutional collapse and blast impunity are Beirut’s own governance record.

## Strongest Dimensions

- Awareness and Empathy — weak in absolute terms, but civil-society and press keep needs visible.

## Weakest Dimensions

- Accountability and Integrity — five years of impunity for the port blast and obstruction of the investigation.

## Evidence Gaps

- Beirut’s municipality has limited autonomy amid national state collapse; separating city from state is difficult. Conflict conditions limited independent service data.
