# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: India

**Entity type:** Country | **Assessment date:** 2026-06-01 | **Composite:** 15.6/100 | **Band:** Critical
**Published:** 22.7 (rank 136, Developing) | **Proposed movement:** -7.1 (Developing -> Critical, BAND CROSSING)

## Summary of cycle ruling

**SCORABLE DOWNGRADE WITH BAND CROSSING (Developing -> Critical).** This cycle surfaces a corroborated (Tier-5), government-directed pattern of active-state perpetration against Bengali-speaking Muslims and Rohingya refugees that the published uniform-~1.9 baseline (22.7) did not reflect. HRW World Report 2026 (Jan 2026 country chapter) and the Polis Project deportation-regime investigation document: (1) Bangladesh reporting 1,500+ men, women and children expelled in May-June without due process, "several of them turned out to be Indian citizens"; (2) at least 192 UNHCR-registered Rohingya unlawfully expelled (refoulement); (3) 40 Rohingya placed on a navy vessel, beaten, given life jackets, and tossed into the sea near Myanmar; (4) 10,000+ Gujarat structures demolished in violation of the Supreme Court's Nov 2024 ruling; (5) 64 documented hate-speech incidents in 10 days after the April Kashmir attack. The conduct is government-directed: a May 2 SOP eliminated the requirement to notify Bangladesh before deportation; the Union Home Ministry ordered all states to establish Special Task Forces to deport alleged migrants within 30 days; the Immigration and Foreigners Bill 2025 mandates detention centers in every state.

Per the **ACTIVE-STATE-PERPETRATION-REWEIGHT** ruling established 2026-05-31 (Burkina Faso), corroborated independent findings of government-perpetrated deliberate targeting not reflected in the published baseline are reweighted toward — but not necessarily to — the Critical floor. India is scored to **15.6, NOT to the 0.0 absolute floor**, because functioning democratic institutions persist and demonstrably push back: the Calcutta High Court ordered the return of wrongfully deported Indian citizens (Sunali Khatun et al.) in September; courts, opposition states, and a free-ish press continue to contest these acts. This distinguishes India from the sole-perpetrator/uncontested 0.0 floor (Myanmar, Sudan) and even from the multi-sided active-perpetration case (Burkina Faso, 6.3). The downgrade is concentrated in EQU (deliberate religious targeting; refoulement; citizenship-stripping), EMP (life-endangering sea incident; beatings by border guards), ACC (due-process collapse; SOP eliminating notification; no acknowledgment), and INT (defiance of the Supreme Court demolition ruling — rhetoric/conduct contradiction).

### Recommendation
Downgrade to 15.6 (Critical). The published 22.7 uniform baseline predates and does not reflect the HRW WR2026 / Polis Project corroborated, government-directed refoulement-and-expulsion regime. Held above the 0.0 floor and above Burkina Faso (6.3) because functioning courts demonstrably contest and reverse some acts. **Band crossing flagged for human review.** Carry-forward watch: nationwide STF deportation scale-up; any documented mass-casualty refoulement event (would move toward floor); Supreme Court contempt enforcement on demolitions; reversal/halt of the deportation regime (would support re-upgrade).

## Key Findings
- 1,500+ expelled to Bangladesh May-June without due process, "several" confirmed Indian citizens; 192 UNHCR Rohingya refouled.
- 40 Rohingya beaten and tossed into the sea from a navy vessel near Myanmar (UN rapporteur: "blatant disregard for life").
- Government-directed: May 2 SOP removed bilateral notification; Union Home Ministry ordered 30-day STF deportations; detention centers mandated nationwide.
- 10,000+ Gujarat demolitions defy the Supreme Court's Nov 2024 ruling — direct executive defiance of the judiciary.
- Held at 15.6 (not floor) because the Calcutta High Court and other institutions demonstrably reverse some acts — distinguishing from the uncontested 0.0 floor.
