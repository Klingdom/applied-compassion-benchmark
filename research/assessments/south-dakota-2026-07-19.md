## What this score means, in plain terms

South Dakota knows what is wrong and says so out loud. It acts on very little of it.

The state publishes its own evidence of severe racial disparity. It reports that Native American children are more than 70 percent of its foster care population. It reports a Native maternal death rate more than five times the white rate. Its own legislative committee studied why Native people are about 39.7 percent of the prison population while being about 9 percent of adults. Very few states document their own worst outcomes this plainly.

What follows the documentation is thin. The disparities are decades old and are not narrowing. The state's largest single commitment in this five-year window was a $650 million prison. Its most consistent constituency-facing action was a rule change to license more Native kinship foster homes, which worked and which the state measured.

South Dakota scores 35.6 of 100, in the Developing band. That is above Iowa at 30.6 and just below Arizona at 36.2.

## Dimension Details

### AWR: Awareness (Score: 45.0/100, raw 2.8)

South Dakota's strongest dimension. The state operates real detection machinery: a statutory Maternal Mortality Review Committee reviewing every death within a year of pregnancy, annual race-disaggregated foster care reporting, an annual statewide homeless count, a published crisis-care dashboard, and an annual statewide audit. It scores 45 rather than higher because the state's own count concedes it cannot see the most rural areas, which are the reservation counties where need is highest.

## Key Findings

- **South Dakota documents its own failures more honestly than it fixes them.** The state published that 1,201 of its 1,709 foster children were Native American at the end of fiscal year 2025 — the fourth straight year above 70 percent, against a Native share of the child population of about 13 percent. It has known about this overrepresentation for nearly half a century. Detection scores 45 of 100. Follow-through scores 2 of 5.

- **The state spent more on holding people than on any other single commitment in five years.** The Legislature approved a 1,500-bed, $650 million men's prison on 2025-09-23. Its own summer study committee had already flagged a lack of rehabilitation programming and a need to keep people out of prison. Native Americans are about 9 percent of South Dakota adults and about 39.7 percent of its prisoners; Native women are 62.9 percent of women in state prison.

- **South Dakota does almost no repair, and that is the single lowest score here.** Reparative Action scores 1 of 5, the floor anchor. No repair was found for boarding schools, for child-removal practice, or for the 2024 dispute in which all nine tribal nations banished the governor and five asked for an apology that never came. The one civil-rights settlement in the window, on the Riot Boosting Act, covered attorneys' fees only.

- **When South Dakota does act, it acts through rates and rules, and it works.** The state moved long-term care reimbursement to 100 percent of cost, adding roughly $49 million, after years of paying the lowest nursing home Medicaid rate in the country. It rewrote kinship foster licensing in June 2025, and licensed Native American foster homes rose from 93 to 134 in seven months. Both changes were measured and published.

- **The state honoured its voters on Medicaid, then took the fight back to them.** Voters wrote expansion into the constitution in 2022. The state implemented it on 2023-07-01 despite having opposed it. It has since twice asked voters to narrow it — Amendment F passed 56 to 44 in 2024, and Amendment I is on the November 2026 ballot. It used the ballot rather than legislating around the electorate.

- **Compassion in South Dakota depends heavily on who holds office.** Tribal relations collapsed and then recovered within a single change of governor in January 2025, with no change to underlying policy structures. That is the definition of a practice that is not embedded.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Awareness, 45 of 100.** The highest score, and the only dimension in the Functional band. South Dakota runs a statutory maternal mortality review committee, publishes race-disaggregated foster care data annually, commissions comparative incarceration studies, and produces an annual statewide audit with real findings.
- **Systemic Thinking, 40 of 100.** Carried by House Bill 1052, signed 2025-03-06, which banned eminent domain for carbon oxide pipelines and halted a 2,500-mile private project against sustained agribusiness pressure. That is a genuine structural position taken at cost.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Empathy, 30 of 100, and Boundaries, 30 of 100.** Empathy is driven by a documented breakdown with the state's most-affected communities: all nine tribal nations banished the sitting governor in 2024, five requested an apology, and none was given. Boundaries is driven partly by weak practice and partly by absent evidence.
- **Reparative Action within Accountability scores 1 of 5**, the floor. It is the single lowest subdimension in this assessment.

## Evidence Gaps

- **Boundaries is the weakest-evidenced dimension.** B2 Autonomy Preservation, B4 Refusal Ethics and B5 Consent Orientation are all scored on limited public evidence, two at low confidence. South Dakota's small population produces little journalism on intake, referral and consent practice. Under the small-state caution these were marked low-confidence rather than assigned a middling default.
- **E1 Affective Resonance is scored low confidence.** No systematic community testimony on how South Dakotans experience state services was found in either direction.
- **No independent audit of state service equity was found.** The strongest external evidence is the 2021 US Commission on Civil Rights report on Native maternal mortality, now five years old. No independent verification of the state's own disparity reporting exists, which caps EQ3 at 3 rather than higher.
- **Tribal community testimony is largely mediated through tribal government statements and litigation records** rather than direct resident testimony, which limits confidence on A4 and E5.
- **The nine tribal nations are sovereign governments, not units of South Dakota.** Where services on reservations are delivered by the federal Indian Health Service or by tribal governments, outcomes were not scored against the state. Only state conduct and state discretion were scored.

## Recommended Next Steps

South Dakota sits in the **Developing** band at 35.6 of 100.

- Consider a [Certified Assessment](/certified-assessments) for a structured improvement roadmap. The highest-leverage findings are concentrated: Reparative Action at 1 of 5, and the gap between a state that detects disparity well (Awareness 45) and one that corrects it poorly (Follow-Through 2 of 5, Long-Term Impact 2 of 5).
- The single clearest structural opportunity is that South Dakota already has the review machinery — maternal mortality review, legislative summer studies, annual audit, foster care council — and does not connect its findings to resource allocation. Closing that loop would move Action, Equity and Systemic Thinking together.
