- **Limb (a), structural transparency failure:** House Bill 312 (2021) gave the Legislative Research Commission — House and Senate leaders — sole authority over release of General Assembly records, and lawmakers amended KRS 7.119 to remove the courts from appeals. This is a branch of government exempting itself and removing external review. The Idaho scope limit does not apply, because this is not a list of subject-matter carve-outs. The North Dakota scope limit does not apply, because removing judicial appeal is structural, not procedural.
- **Limb (b), surviving output:** the independently elected Auditor of Public Accounts continues to publish damaging, uncompelled findings — the 2024 juvenile justice review, the March 2026 foster care examination, and the April 2026 cabinet audit. The Massachusetts output-contingency rider is satisfied.

**Scoring effect:** with AB3 at 3, ACC is 2.8 and the composite is 47.5 (Functional). With AB3 floored at 2, ACC is 2.6 and the composite is 46.9 (Functional). The band does not change either way.

## Key Findings

- **Kentucky's overdose response is the clearest success in this assessment, and it is measurable.** Drug overdose deaths fell 22.9% in 2025 and 50.8% since 2021, reaching 1,110 deaths — a fourth straight annual decline. The state distributed nearly 183,000 Narcan doses in 2025 and has certified 42 counties as Recovery Ready, covering 2.2 million people. This is the rare case where a state measured an upstream problem, funded a response, and can show the downstream number falling.

- **Kentucky's biggest compassion asset is a 2014 decision it has refused to reverse.** Medicaid expansion cut the uninsured rate from 14.3% in 2013 to 5.4% in 2023 and now covers about 1.5 million people. It survived a repeal attempt, a change of administration, and a work-requirement waiver withdrawn in 2019. That durability is why Kentucky scores 4 out of 5 on resilience of care — one of only three subdimension scores above 3 in the Integrity dimension.

- **The state's independent auditor is doing the work the state's own systems are not.** The independently elected Auditor of Public Accounts found 304 foster children spent 1,577 combined days in offices, hotels and a hospital, and that 162 of 190 nursing home inspections were late — some by 51 months. The state cleared the inspection backlog. It called the foster care report "deceitful." That split response is why Awareness scores 55 while Accountability scores 45.

- **Kentucky's legislature exempted itself from the transparency law it applies to everyone else.** House Bill 312 (2021) gave legislative leaders sole authority over legislative records and removed courts from appeals. Under the benchmark's cross-state transparency rule, this caps the state's transparency score at 3 out of 5 rather than flooring it at 2, because the auditor's independent output survives.

- **When Kentucky cannot help someone, it tends to penalise them rather than refer them.** The Safer Kentucky Act produced at least 425 unlawful-camping charges across 30 counties in its first year, and providers report enforcement pushed people farther from services. This drives Boundaries to 35 out of 100 — Kentucky's joint-weakest dimension.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Systemic Thinking (60/100)** — the strongest. Kentucky does two genuinely long-horizon things: it is moving flood survivors permanently out of the floodplain across eight high-ground communities totalling 500+ homes, and it tracks all pension plans annually against a statutory path to full funding by 2049. It also shares resources widely, with 300 opioid abatement grants worth $86.5 million.
- **Awareness (55/100) and Action (55/100)** — both carried by the overdose programme and by an audit function that reliably surfaces what the state missed.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Empathy (35/100) and Boundaries (35/100)** — joint weakest. Empathy is pulled down by 230+ jail deaths between 2020 and 2024, by children housed in state offices, and by the executive calling an unflattering independent finding "deceitful." Boundaries is pulled down by a foster home shortfall of roughly 4,237 against 4,516 available homes, and by criminal citation as the default response to unsheltered people.
- **Equity (45/100)** — Medicaid expansion is a genuine 4, but the 2025 removal of diversity infrastructure and rising maternal mortality (143.6 per 100,000 live births in 2021, up from 80.8 in 2013) leave the state without a working mechanism to find and correct disparities.

## Evidence Gaps

- **B5 Consent Orientation** is scored at 2 with **low confidence**. No systematic state evidence on informed consent practice was found. The score rests on adjacent evidence and should be treated as provisional.
- **Community testimony (Tier 5) is thin** across Empathy. Scores for E1, E4 and E5 rest mainly on documented outcomes and independent audit rather than on direct testimony from affected Kentuckians.
- **Opioid settlement outcomes are unmeasurable at this stage.** Spending data exists, but with roughly 90% of funds unspent there is no outcome data to assess efficacy of the abatement programme specifically, as distinct from the ODCP grant programme.
- **The federal juvenile justice investigation is unresolved.** The DOJ investigation opened in May 2024 remains open, so its findings could not inform the score. Detention centres under renovation are not due back online until autumn 2026 and spring 2027.
- **Maternal mortality data lags.** The most recent confirmed figure is for 2021. Any 2022–2025 improvement would not yet be visible.

## Recommended Next Steps

Kentucky sits in the **Functional** band: systems exist but have significant gaps.

- Consider [Advisory Support](/advisory) to translate these benchmark findings into strategic action, particularly on the two weakest dimensions — Empathy and Boundaries.
- The single highest-leverage change is structural: the gap between what the Auditor finds and how the executive responds. Kentucky already has a working detection system. It does not yet have a working acknowledgment system.

## Methodology and Attribution Notes

**Attribution.** This assessment scores Kentucky state conduct across both political branches, not a single administration. Kentucky was under divided government for the entire 2021–2026 window: a Democratic governor and a Republican General Assembly with veto-override supermajorities. In the 2026 session the legislature overrode nearly all 32 vetoes, letting only three small line-item budget vetoes stand. Legislative actions taken over a veto — including House Bill 312 (2021) and House Bill 4 (2025) — are scored as state conduct, with the veto recorded as mitigating evidence in the same subdimension. Executive-branch failures found by the independently elected Auditor are likewise scored as state conduct.

**Federal versus state discretion.** Federal policy imposed on Kentucky is excluded. The 2025 federal reconciliation law (H.R. 1), which imposes Medicaid work requirements from 2027 nationally, is not scored against the state. What is scored is state discretion: the 2014 expansion decision, its maintenance through repeal attempts, and House Bill 2 of April 2026 — a state statute adding copays and work verification for roughly 478,900 expansion adults. The federal DOJ investigation into nine juvenile facilities is federal action; what is scored is the state conduct that prompted it and the state reforms that followed. FEMA individual assistance is federal; the state-funded high-ground programme and the Team Eastern Kentucky Flood Relief Fund are state.

**Composite verification.** Composite computed by `computeCompositeFromDimensions` in `site/scripts/lib/scoring.mjs` (v1.2), not by hand. Output: `{"composite":47.5,"band":"Functional","integrationPremium":0}`. Sensitivity cases, also run through the function: AB3 floored at 2 (ACC 2.6) → 46.9 Functional; EMP raised to 3.0 → 49.4 Functional; all dimensions +0.5 → 60.0 Functional. The composite sits 7.5 points from the nearest band boundary, so the band is stable.

**Peer calibration.** Not calibrated to the published US States rank order, which is known to be corrupt. Calibrated to the methodology anchors and to the 17 states assessed at full depth under this methodology (range 26.9–60.6). Kentucky at 47.5 sits level with Alaska and just above North Carolina.
