# Special Upward Assessment: Sweden

**Composite:** 81.3/100 (CONFIRM — no upgrade warranted)
**Band:** Exemplary

## Why it matters (plain language)
Sweden put the EU Gender Pay Transparency rules into force on June 7, 2026. Workers can now ask for pay data and employers must report gender pay gaps. This is a real, enacted right. But it is one narrow improvement at a country that already scores 81.3, and it is the EU-wide minimum every member state had to meet by the same date.
