## Key Findings

- Why it matters: the Philippines is an electoral democracy with courts and press freedom, but its drug war keeps killing people with almost no accountability. That mix keeps it in the middle of the Developing band.
- Human Rights Watch counts 1,273 drug-war killings since Marcos took office in 2022, with only five cases and nine police officers convicted since 2016 (HRW, June 30, 2026).
- Killings continue in plain-clothes 'TokHang' style and illegal arrests have risen, showing the campaign changed form rather than ending (HRW, June 2026).
- The May proposal's upgrade leaned on 2025 ICC cooperation; that is out-of-window and does not offset continuing killings, so the upgrade is dropped and the score confirmed.

## Strongest Dimensions

Awareness and Systemic Thinking subdimensions (2.4) are the least weak: the Philippines has an open press, active courts, congressional hearings and civil-society documentation that surface the killings - even though the state does not act on them.

## Weakest Dimensions

Accountability (ACC, 2.0) and Integrity (INT, 2.0): five convictions in a decade of thousands of killings, and continuing killings under a president who campaigned on reform, show a persistent gap between stated values and conduct.

## Evidence Gaps

The freshest crisp dated primary sources are late June / early July 2026 (HRW, JURIST), just outside the strict 14-day window; no in-window score-moving event or positive countervailing conduct was found despite extensive searching. The government's 2025 surrender of Duterte to the ICC is a genuine accountability positive but is out-of-window and already reflected in the Developing-band baseline.
