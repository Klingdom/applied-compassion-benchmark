## Key Findings

- **Kansas measures suffering well and then does not act on the measurement.** The state runs a strong legislative audit office, a statutory child advocate and a maternal mortality review committee. All three publish unflattering numbers. The nine-year disability waiting list and the fourth straight year of foster care settlement failure are both matters of public record, published by Kansas itself or its monitors. The gap between what Kansas knows and what Kansas does is the single defining feature of this assessment.

- **One coverage decision drives most of the harm.** Kansas is one of ten states that has not expanded Medicaid. About 28,000 people sit in the coverage gap. Kansas now leads the nation with 28 rural hospitals at immediate risk of closing, out of 100 open inpatient hospitals. The state's own governor puts the foregone benefit at $1.2 billion a year and 23,000 jobs. This is state discretion, not federal policy, and it is the main reason Equity and Action score in the Developing range.

- **Kansas ignored its own voters, twice.** Kansans rejected the Value Them Both constitutional amendment by nearly 20 points on 2 August 2022. The Legislature passed abortion restrictions in 2023, overrode vetoes in 2024 and again in April 2026, and has put the question back on the ballot for 4 August 2026. Medicaid expansion is blocked from floor votes despite majority public support. This produced the lowest score in the assessment: 1 of 5 on Consistency Under Pressure.

- **The 2012–2017 tax reversal is a real and rare institutional achievement.** Kansas enacted a large tax cut, watched revenue fall and school and road spending get cut, and then reversed the policy by bipartisan veto override in June 2017. Very few states undo a flagship policy on the evidence. It is the strongest single item in the assessment and is why Systemic Learning scores 4 of 5 — but it happened nine years ago and Kansas has not repeated the trick.

- **Transparency is strong at the audit office and weak at the Legislature.** The Legislative Division of Post Audit issued 18 performance audits in fiscal year 2025 and found that 7 of 15 audited state entities failed IT security standards. At the same time, Kansas allocated $20,000 of public money for outside counsel to defend a state senator against an open records complaint from a newspaper. The watchdog works; the ability to compel the Legislature does not.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Accountability (50.0) and Systemic Thinking (50.0)**, tied. Accountability rests on the tax reversal, court-compelled school funding delivered in full, and an audit body that publishes damaging findings nobody forced it to publish. Systemic Thinking rests almost entirely on water: multi-decade, farmer-written aquifer management plans with modelled targets and published economic analysis, which is genuinely unusual long-horizon state planning.
- **Awareness (50.0)** follows closely, driven by the density and quality of Kansas's own published data on its own failures.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Integrity (30.0)** is the lowest, held down by a score of 1 on Consistency Under Pressure. A state that returns a settled referendum question to the ballot four years after a 20-point defeat is not honouring commitments under pressure.
- **Empathy, Action and Boundaries all score 35.0.** These reflect the same underlying facts from three angles: people wait years, the state disputes independent accounts of that waiting, and there is no concrete alternative offered to those it turns away.

## Evidence Gaps

- **Current corrections staffing.** The best available vacancy figures are from July 2022 (22.6 percent statewide, 31.8 percent at El Dorado). A 2025 legislative committee report states vacancy rates fell in 2023 and 2024 but does not give current facility-level numbers. B1 and I3 are scored at 3 with medium confidence; better 2026 data could move either.
- **Osawatomie State Hospital current status.** Coverage of the 2015 decertification and 2017–2018 partial recovery is solid, but no 2025–2026 reporting on behavioural health capacity was located. This limits confidence in the Action scores for mental health specifically.
- **Consent practice outside abortion regulation.** B5 is scored at 2 largely on state-mandated abortion disclosure requirements. No evidence was found on consent practice in child welfare, guardianship or research contexts.
- **Community testimony.** No Tier 5 community testimony was located for E1 Affective Resonance or B3 Scope Clarity. Both are scored conservatively as a result.

## Recommended Next Steps

Kansas is in the **Functional** band, at the unstable low edge of it. Consider [Advisory Support](/advisory) to translate these findings into strategic action — in particular the Awareness-to-Action gap, where Kansas already produces high-quality data on its own shortfalls but has no reliable mechanism converting those findings into service change. Given the band instability, a [Certified Assessment](/certified-assessments) would also be warranted to resolve the corrections-staffing and behavioural-health evidence gaps that currently sit at the boundary between Developing and Functional.
