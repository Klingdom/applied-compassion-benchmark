# Assessment: China — Ethnic Unity Law in Force (Double-Count + Forward-Trigger Screen)

**Composite:** 19.5/100 (CONFIRM — codifies an already-priced policy)
**Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
China's new Ethnic Unity Law now claims the right to punish people abroad who "undermine ethnic unity." That is alarming, but so far it only writes down and extends a repression policy the score already reflects — no one has yet been prosecuted under the new overseas clause.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1))
- Live baseline **19.5**, rank **147**, Critical. Already prices mass assimilation policy toward Uyghurs, Tibetans and Mongolians, denial of harm, and existing transnational repression. Score reflects China's own conduct; not stale.

## The scorable question
- The Law on Promoting Ethnic Unity and Progress took effect Jul 1; officials confirmed extraterritorial applicability (Article 63). Amnesty, UN OHCHR and the European Parliament condemned it as a "legal blueprint for cultural erasure."
- Article 63 is, as of this window, a **stated legal claim** ("China says it has the right to pursue legal action") — **no documented enforcement action** has been brought against any overseas individual or organisation.
- The assimilation content **codifies already-priced policy**. Germany's and Taiwan's expressions of concern are **third-party** reactions, not new Chinese-state conduct crossing a fresh threshold.

## §3e-bis screening
- **(4) Double-count:** law codifies the already-scored assimilation/transnational-repression arc.
- **Forward-trigger vs realized-harm:** the first documented Article 63 extraterritorial enforcement action would be a realized-harm trigger; none yet.
- **(3) Directionality:** third-party condemnation is not own-conduct evidence and is not an upgrade signal.

## Disposition
**CONFIRM at 19.5. Delta 0.0. No change proposal.** A marginal within-band dock (~-1.5) does not clear the 5-point threshold. Confidence: high.
- **Forward trigger (downgrade):** first documented Article 63 extraterritorial enforcement/detention action.
