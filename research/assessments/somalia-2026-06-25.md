**Assessment date:** 2026-06-25

## Headline (plain language)

Why it matters: A famine is projected in Somalia's Buur Hakaba district by the end of June, and aid is reaching only 1 in 10 people in need. Somalia already scores near the bottom (4.7 of 100), so this reinforces that score.

## §3e-bis Screening
- **Baseline provenance:** Published 4.7 (uniform-seed-adjacent; AWR/EMP/ACT 1.5, EQU/BND/ACC/SYS/INT 1.0). Reconstructs to 4.7.
- **Directionality:** Negative within-window evidence → near-floor reinforcement, consistent.
- **Near-floor limitation:** Evidence-tier reinforcement, not a composite move. No adjudicated-equivalent new scorable severity that crosses threshold; famine is a compounding shock layered on an already-priced crisis.

## Within-window evidence (near-floor reinforcement)
- **Crisis Group / WFP / UN (Jun 2026):** IPC famine projected for Buur Hakaba district by end of June; credible risk in Bay, Bakool, Gedo through September. 6M (1-in-3 Somalis) face life-threatening food insecurity; 1.9M children acutely malnourished.
- WFP reaching only 1-in-10 people needing food (sharp drop year-over-year) due to funding crisis.
- Al-Shabaab taxing/destroying food deliveries (non-state-actor conduct; aid obstruction). Food prices up 70%, fuel up 150% (Hormuz spillover).

## Key Findings
- The Buur Hakaba famine projection and the 1-in-10 WFP coverage collapse reinforce the AWR/ACT/EQU near-floor.
- Near-floor 4.7 sustained. Conversion trigger: confirmed IPC Phase 5 famine declaration with documented state-response failure.
