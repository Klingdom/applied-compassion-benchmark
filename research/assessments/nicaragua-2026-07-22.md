## Key Findings

- Why it matters: Nicaragua is a one-family authoritarian state that on July 20, 2026 abolished elections entirely. On a compassion framework it sits near the absolute floor, and its newest conduct confirms that placement.
- President Ortega declared there will be 'no more elections', cementing indefinite rule by himself and co-president Rosario Murillo (Al Jazeera, July 20, 2026).
- The state has shut roughly 5,500 civil-society organizations, stripped 452 people of nationality, and barred all international rights monitors since 2018 (HRW World Report 2026).
- The May proposal tried to move the score upward on this repression evidence; that is an upgrade on negative evidence and is rejected. The score is confirmed near the floor.

## Strongest Dimensions

None. Every dimension is at or near the floor. The least-low areas (Systemic Thinking, some Awareness subdimensions) reflect only that the state has an elaborate apparatus, not compassion.

## Weakest Dimensions

Accountability (ACC, 1.0) and Integrity (INT, ~1.1): there is no independent oversight, no admitted rights monitors since 2018, and governance is being restructured to remove all electoral accountability.

## Evidence Gaps

No independent monitors have been allowed into Nicaragua since 2018, so all evidence is from exile testimony, UN experts and international NGOs. This limits granularity but uniformly points to floor-level scores; the direction of every gap is downward, not upward.
