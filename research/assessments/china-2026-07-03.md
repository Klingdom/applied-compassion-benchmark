# Assessment: China — Ethnic Unity Law In Force, Day 2-3 (Double-Count / Forward-Trigger Screen)

**Composite:** 19.5/100 (CONFIRM) | **Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
China's Ethnic Unity Law took effect July 1. It compels assimilation across 56 ethnic groups, and Article 63 claims authority over Uyghur, Tibetan and Mongolian people OUTSIDE China. Amnesty International calls it a "legal blueprint for cultural erasure," and Germany's Foreign Ministry voiced "great concern." But on Day 2-3 there is still no documented enforcement action under Article 63. The law codifies an assimilation and transnational-repression policy that China's 19.5 score already prices. Germany's concern is a third-party reaction, not new Chinese conduct.
