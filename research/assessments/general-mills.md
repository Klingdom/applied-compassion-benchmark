## Trigger — Dual Independent Event Evidence

1. **WARN Act investigation (April 2-7, 2026)**: General Mills filed notice April 2 of plant closure at St. Charles, MO eliminating 163 jobs; plant operations will cease on or about June 8. Strauss Borrelli investigation alleges this fails the Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act's 60-day notice requirement ([Strauss Borrelli](https://straussborrelli.com/2026/04/07/general-mills-warn-act-investigation/); [KSDK](https://www.ksdk.com/article/news/local/business-journal/general-mills-eliminate-163-jobs-shutters-missouri-manufacturing-plant/63-0c5788de-992b-4f2d-bd18-50c3794ad104)).
2. **Davis v. General Mills racial-harassment class action (April 22, 2026 ruling)**: Federal judge in N.D. Ga. (case 1:24-cv-02409) gave plaintiffs 'one more chance' to perfect class-action pleadings — case survives dismissal motion ([Law360](https://www.law360.com/articles/2468480/workers-get-one-more-chance-in-general-mills-bias-suit); [Civil Rights Litigation Clearinghouse](https://clearinghouse.net/case/45663/)). Underlying allegations: Black workers at Georgia cereal/trail-mix plant subjected to decades of racial slurs and demotions by 'Good Ole Boy network' of White leadership; allegations covered by [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/general-mills-lawsuit-black-workers-georgia-racism/), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2024/06/07/nx-s1-4995429/black-workers-sue-general-mills-racial-discrimination), and [HR Dive](https://www.hrdive.com/news/general-mills-georgia-plant-fraternity-targeted-black-employees/718252/).

Dual-trigger profile distinguishes General Mills from single-trigger Gen-2 anchors and makes this the second-strongest Night 7 anchor after Danaher.

- **AWR 56.3 (Awareness):** Public sustainability reporting acknowledges DEI and worker-relations programs but does not surface the documented Georgia plant pattern. Functional.
- **EMP 50.0 (Empathy):** Lower bound for Functional. - **ACT 56.3 (Action):** Food-access programs (General Mills Foundation, Box Tops) and product-mission delivery (consumer food at scale) support Functional floor.
- **EQU 43.8 (Equity):** Lowest dimension. Davis class action survived dismissal — federal court has determined the racial-harassment allegations meet plausibility threshold to proceed. Decade-long pattern at Georgia plant + reputational pattern of single-facility racial culture is direct EQU evidence. ([Law360](https://www.law360.com/articles/2468480/workers-get-one-more-chance-in-general-mills-bias-suit); [HR Dive](https://www.hrdive.com/news/general-mills-georgia-plant-fraternity-targeted-black-employees/718252/))
- **BND 56.3 (Boundaries):** No environmental, food-safety, or community-harm boundary violations in 14-day window. Functional.
- **ACC 50.0 (Accountability):** WARN Act investigation is post-hoc accountability mechanism; class-action survival is post-hoc accountability via court oversight. No proactive corporate disclosure of either pattern.
- **SYS 62.5 (Systems):** Genuine consumer-food access-at-scale mission infrastructure (largest cereal manufacturer in U.S.; global supply chain feeding millions); Functional-plus floor preserved.
- **INT 50.0 (Integrity):** Two parallel governance failure patterns (worker notice + race discrimination) without documented executive falsification. Distinguishes from Danaher (securities fraud → INT 37.5).
