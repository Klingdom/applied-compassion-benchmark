# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Anthropic (Confirmation)

**Entity type:** Company (AI lab)
**Assessment date:** 2026-07-12
**Published composite:** 59.1/100 (Functional) — rank 13 of 50, ai-labs index
**Determination:** CONFIRM at 59.1. No score change. Heightened downward watch.

## Within-Window Trigger

On July 8–10, 2026 a cybersecurity threat platform run by China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) / National Vulnerability Database posted an alert warning that Anthropic's Claude Code contains a "backdoor" that can transmit a user's location and identity to a remote server without consent, naming affected versions 2.1.91–2.1.196 and urging users to uninstall or upgrade ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/08/china-anthropic-ai-claude-code-backdoor-security-threat.html); [US News/Reuters](https://money.usnews.com/investing/news/articles/2026-07-08/china-issues-backdoor-security-alert-over-anthropics-claude-code)).

Anthropic responded publicly that the mechanism was an **experiment deployed earlier in 2026 to prevent account abuse by unauthorized resellers and to protect against model distillation** by competitors suspected of training on Claude outputs. Per Anthropic, the mechanism checks a device's time zone and whether a request was routed through a banned/unsupported region or to AI labs suspected of distillation — and the company had already **scheduled its removal in the July 2, 2026 release** ([SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/article/3359901/anthropic-hits-back-after-china-warns-claude-code-backdoor-risks); [GovInfoSecurity](https://www.govinfosecurity.com/anthropic-rejects-chinas-claim-about-claude-code-backdoor-a-32184)).

## Conversion-Trigger Analysis (rigorous)

The pre-defined conversion trigger for Anthropic was: **"a second undisclosed telemetry episode OR a regulator/court deceptive-practice finding."** Both prongs are tested below.

**(a) Is this a genuine SECOND, independently-corroborated undisclosed-telemetry episode, or a re-characterization of the already-scored March tracker?**
It is best read as a **re-characterization / continuation of the same already-priced anti-distillation mechanism**, not a distinct second episode. Anthropic describes it as one experiment ("earlier this year") with a single remediation date (the July 2 release). The conduct class — a distillation/abuse-prevention check that inspects device signals — is the same family of behavior already reflected in the March tracker assessment. There is no independently-corroborated NEW, separate telemetry event beyond the March matter; the only new element is a rival government's re-framing of it as a "backdoor." This does **not** satisfy the "second undisclosed telemetry episode" prong.

**(b) Is an allegation by a rival government's cybersecurity agency an adjudicated finding?**
No. This is an **allegation by the cybersecurity arm of a rival government (China)** — an entity with a clear geopolitical incentive to damage a US AI lab, sharpened by Anthropic's own prior accusation that Alibaba attempted to extract its capabilities. It is not a ruling by a court or a neutral regulator, and it has not been independently corroborated by a security researcher or standards body in the public record. The methodology forbids scoring on allegation, especially where the accuser is geopolitically motivated. This does **not** satisfy the "regulator/court deceptive-practice finding" prong.

**Own-conduct residue (scored on facts, not framing).** Setting the Chinese framing aside, Anthropic did ship released versions containing an undisclosed mechanism that transmits location/identity signals to a remote server. That is a genuine, if modest, consent/transparency negative touching AWR (A5 anticipatory), BND (B5 consent), and INT (I2/I4). But it is (i) largely the same conduct already priced in March, (ii) targeted (banned-region / suspected-distillation checks) rather than blanket surveillance, and (iii) partly mitigated by Anthropic's transparent explanation and pre-existing removal on July 2. This residue is well under a 5-point move and does not cross the Functional/Established or Functional/Developing lines.

## Determination

**CONFIRM at 59.1 (Functional). No dimensional change. Heightened downward watch.** No defensible ≥5pt or band-line move exists on corroborated own-conduct. The conversion trigger is not met: neither a genuine independently-corroborated second undisclosed-telemetry episode nor an adjudicated deceptive-practice finding has occurred. The watch is heightened because a future, independently-verified recurrence (a security-researcher confirmation, a neutral-regulator or court finding, or a distinct new telemetry episode) would convert. Assessors should avoid geopolitically-motivated over-weighting of a rival-state allegation.
