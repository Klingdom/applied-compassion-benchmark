### Score Difference Analysis

The published score pre-dates the 2026 verdict cluster and the April 10, 2026 Massachusetts SJC ruling. Three distinct legal findings in a five-week window — NM $375M consumer protection penalty (Mar 24), LA $3M negligence verdict with 70% Meta responsibility (Mar 25), and the MA SJC Section 230 holding (Apr 10) — together establish a factual record that (a) design features cause measurable child harm, (b) Meta misled consumers about safety, and (c) platform design is not shielded from liability the way user-generated content is. This triad substantively downgrades ACC (from 2.5 to 1.8), EQU (from 2.5 to 2.0), and INT (from 2.5 to 2.0) from the original baseline.

### Recommendation

Downgrade to 29.4 / Developing. Band stays Developing (unchanged from Functional/Developing boundary shift). High confidence given three independent legal findings within the 14-day recency envelope bracket. The MA SJC ruling (April 10) is the anchor in-window event; the NM and LA verdicts are corroborating priors.

## Key Findings

- Three converging child-harm legal events (NM, LA, MA) establish that design features — not content — drive the harm pattern.
- MA SJC (Apr 10, 2026) is the first state supreme court to hold that Section 230 does not shield social-media design features from state consumer-protection claims; materially weakens legal posture.
- ACC is the weakest dimension (20/100); Meta continues to litigate rather than acknowledge.
- Teen Accounts rollout is partial credit on AC5 and AB2, preventing a lower ACT/ACC score.
- No evidence of voluntary reparative fund or structural policy change on engagement-maximization.

## Strongest Dimensions
- SYS (40) — AI research publications and industry coalitions provide upstream presence.

## Weakest Dimensions
- ACC (20) — Legal denial pattern, reactive-only corrections, inadequate repair.
- EQU (25) / AWR (25) / INT (25) — Child-harm equity gap, detection-to-action gap, and values-decision gap all compound.

## Evidence Gaps
- Meta AI-specific (vs. Meta Platforms broadly) safety practices for Llama and AI Studio products are under-disclosed.
- No public independent audit of AI-product harm detection.

## Recommended Next Steps
Developing band — consider a Certified Assessment for structured improvement. Immediate priorities: voluntary reparative framework for documented child-harm cases; pre-launch independent harm review for AI products; acknowledgment-before-litigation protocol for new state-level suits.
