## Trigger for Reassessment

On April 6, 2026, a 46-year-old tote runner collapsed and died on the floor of Amazon's PDX9 warehouse in Troutdale, Oregon. Multiple employees reported that management ordered them to continue loading trucks for more than an hour while the body remained on the floor, with supervisors telling workers to "turn around and not look." Coverage broke April 13–16 across TechCrunch, NewsNation, Novara Media, Yahoo Finance, and WSWS. This incident stacks on top of Amazon's first-ever Teamsters bargaining order (April 3) and the NLRB Palmdale contractor settlement (April 13). Published composite 21.6 sits within 3 points of the Critical/Developing boundary; today's evidence pushes the assessment across that line.
