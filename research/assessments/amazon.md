## Reassessment Context

Amazon was assessed Apr 15 at composite 18.4. Three concurrent April 2026 labor developments warrant review:

1. **Apr 2, 2026: NLRB bargaining order against Amazon at JFK8 Staten Island.** First union to win a bargaining order against Amazon. NLRB found Amazon "illegally and willfully ignored the union's legitimacy" and engaged in coercive conduct against ~5,500 workers over 4 years. Amazon has exhausted appeal options ([Teamsters](https://teamster.org/2026/04/amazon-teamsters-become-first-union-to-win-bargaining-order-against-e-commerce-giant/), [NLRB Edge](https://www.nlrbedge.com/p/04022026-nlrb-orders-amazon-to-bargain)).
2. **Apr 13, 2026: NLRB settles Palmdale delivery-contractor case** before a precedent-setting joint-employer ruling could issue. Cuts off a landmark decision on Amazon's control over DSP drivers ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-13/trump-labor-board-pushes-to-settle-major-amazon-contractor-case), [Palmdale Today](https://nationaltoday.com/us/ca/palmdale/news/2026/04/13/federal-labor-board-settles-amazon-contractor-case/)).
3. **Apr 9, 2026:** NLRB cleared path for Amazon to challenge Staten Island certification in federal court ([Duane Morris](https://blogs.duanemorris.com/laborlaw/2026/04/09/nlrb-clears-path-for-amazon-to-challenge-staten-island-union-certification-in-federal-court/)).

**Net effect:** The NLRB bargaining order is a finding of 4 years of illegal anti-union conduct (negative for AB1, AB2, I1, I4). Amazon continues to litigate rather than accept certification (negative for AB2). The Palmdale settlement is a missed accountability event rather than an Amazon-driven improvement. Composite modest downgrade from 18.4 to 17.2.
