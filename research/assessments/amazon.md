### Score Difference Analysis

**BND (-18.8 points):** The published score of 2.5 raw (37.5 scaled) implied some boundary practices. The extensive evidence of pervasive AI surveillance, automated management systems that remove worker autonomy, and coercive contractor classification significantly lower the boundaries score. Workers have little genuine consent or autonomy.

**EMP (-12.5 points):** The published score implied some empathetic engagement. AI-driven automated management, anti-union surveillance, retaliation against striking workers, and on-site clinics blaming workers for injuries indicate empathy infrastructure is virtually absent for warehouse workers.

**ACT (-12.5 points):** While Amazon shows strong climate action (renewable energy, electric vehicles), the persistent worker safety crisis (7 years of 2x injury rates) drags down the action dimension significantly.

**EQU (-12.5 points):** Delivery driver misclassification, disproportionate injury burden on minority workers, and anticompetitive marketplace practices all lower equity below the published score.

### Recommendation

The published score of 21.6 (Developing) is moderately overstated based on current evidence. The assessed composite of 18.4 represents a -3.2 point delta, which is below the 5-point threshold for a change proposal. However, the band change from Developing to Critical is notable and suggests the score is at the boundary. Worker safety evidence is particularly damning and well-documented.

## Key Findings

- Senate investigation found Amazon warehouse injury rates are twice the industry average for 7 consecutive years; US Attorney investigating potential fraud in injury data reporting
- NLRB settlement confirmed Amazon retaliated against striking Teamsters warehouse workers; separate delivery driver joint-employer case settled to avoid precedent-setting ruling
- Pervasive AI surveillance system monitors workers' every move, automatically generates warnings and terminations without human supervisor input
- Strong environmental sustainability program (Climate Pledge, net-zero by 2040, 100% renewable energy match) contrasts sharply with worker safety failures
- FTC antitrust lawsuit alleging 82% marketplace monopoly and anticompetitive practices headed to trial in late 2026 or early 2027

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Action (25.0) and Systemic Thinking (25.0):** Environmental sustainability program is substantive with measurable outcomes, but worker safety dimension of both is failing
- **Integrity (21.9):** Environmental commitments have survived leadership transitions; some genuine values alignment on climate

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Empathy (12.5), Equity (12.5), and Accountability (12.5):** AI-driven management, anti-union surveillance, contractor misclassification, and alleged injury data manipulation represent near-total absence of empathy, equity, and accountability for workers

## Evidence Gaps

- Detailed internal injury data (beyond what Senate investigation accessed) not publicly available
- Worker satisfaction surveys not independently published
- Delivery driver injury and working condition data limited
- Marketplace seller impact data from FTC case still under seal
- Full scope of AI surveillance capabilities not publicly documented
