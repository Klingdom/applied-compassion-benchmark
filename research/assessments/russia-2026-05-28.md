**Trigger:** State Duma passed Civic Death Law in second and third readings May 26. Law enables asset seizure, passport denial, digital service exclusion, and consular-services denial for emigrated critics convicted of "offenses against Russia's interests." Effective September 1, 2026. Scale-floor annotation filed May 26 now promoted to formal methodology category.

## Triggering Evidence Window

**Primary event (2026-05-26, Civic Death Law passed):**
- State Duma adopted in second and third readings a law allowing seizure of property of citizens who have committed offenses against Russia's interests abroad.
- Restrictions on convicted Russian emigrants:
  - Prohibition on consular support (passport renewal, notary services).

**Decision: Promote CIVIC-DEATH-LAW-FOR-DIASPORA from May 26 annotation status to formal benchmark methodology category.**

The Civic Death Law meets formal category criteria because it is:
1. **Codified state instrument** (legislation, not informal practice).
2. **Targeting legal personhood of extraterritorial citizens** (a novel state-power exercise).
3. **Multi-dimensional harm vector**: economic (asset seizure), administrative (passport/services denial), digital (service exclusion), social (effective citizenship revocation).
4. **Independently classified as benchmark-novel** by HRW and Meduza.

This is structurally distinct from prior Russia floor anchors:

- The CIVIC-DEATH-LAW-FOR-DIASPORA category targets the legal status of citizens abroad whose offense is post-emigration political speech.

**Promoted methodology category criteria** (for future entity matching):

2. Conviction-triggered deprivation across ≥3 dimensions (e.g., consular + financial + digital).
