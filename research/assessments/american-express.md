> This assessment is based on publicly available information and does not constitute a formal Compassion Benchmark Certified Assessment.

## Trigger — DOJ Combined Settlement + Antitrust Settlement + Cluster Invalidation

1. **Tier-1 DOJ record**: January 2025 — AmEx paid ~$230M to settle DOJ civil deceptive-marketing allegations and a parallel E.D.N.Y. wire-fraud investigation. Civil $108.7M; criminal-side $138.4M (non-prosecution agreement plus criminal fine and forfeiture). ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/01/16/american-express-doj-fraud-credit-cards-marketing-settlement.html); [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-01-16/amex-to-pay-230-million-over-misleading-sales-practices); [Payments Dive](https://www.paymentsdive.com/news/amex-federal-government-doj-allegations-credit-cards-small-business/737635/)).
2. **Conduct 2014-2017**: dummy EINs ("123456788"), falsified income data, unauthorized credit checks, misrepresented rewards/fees, inaccurate tax advice on Payroll Rewards/Premium Wire products. AmEx fired ~200 employees post-internal investigation.
3. **In-window event**: $17.5M antitrust class settlement (anti-steering); claim deadline April 29, 2026; final approval hearing June 17, 2026. ([Top Class Actions](https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/open-lawsuit-settlements/17-5m-american-express-antitrust-class-action-settlement/))
4. **Gen-2 cluster artifact**: Published 81.4 is uniform 4×7+3.5×1 Gen-2 cluster profile, seventh consecutive forensic anchor.

## Scoring Judgment (40 subdimensions condensed to 8 dimensions)

- **AWR 56.3 (Awareness):** Public ESG/corporate-responsibility reporting acknowledges customer-experience and small-business impact; the 3-year deceptive-sales episode documents a structural awareness-disclosure gap on small-business sales practices through 2017. Functional.
- **EMP 62.5 (Empathy):** No documented systemic worker-mistreatment in window; 200-employee firing post-investigation is a complicated signal — proper consequence but also documentation that a sizeable portion of the salesforce was implicated. Functional-plus.
- **ACT 62.5 (Action):** Genuine product-mission delivery: payments network, legitimate small-business credit channels, traveler protections. Functional-plus floor. ([AmEx CSR](https://www.americanexpress.com/en-us/about/corporate-social-responsibility/))
- **EQU 56.3 (Equity):** Small-business customers — disproportionately women- and minority-owned in many product segments — bore the brunt of unauthorized credit checks and falsified income entries. Anti-steering practices raised end-prices for all merchants. Functional.
- **BND 50.0 (Boundaries):** Population-level boundary issues: unauthorized credit checks (credit-score harm), inaccurate tax advice on wire products causing potential downstream tax exposure, dummy-EIN enrollment without proper consent. Functional minus.
- **ACC 43.8 (Accountability):** Lowest non-INT dimension. Settlement paid in full; 200 firings documented; criminal-side NPA accepts factual basis without admission of liability in civil component. Post-hoc accountability via fines without proactive disclosure. ([The Hill](https://thehill.com/business/5090091-american-express-to-pay-230m-to-settle-deceptive-marketing-fraud-probe/))
- **SYS 62.5 (Systems):** Genuine systems-level positive — global payments network and small-business credit infrastructure supports legitimate commerce. Functional-plus floor.
- **INT 50.0 (Integrity):** 3-year sustained deceptive-marketing pattern. Held at 50.0 rather than lower because (a) self-remediation (200 firings) is genuine, (b) deceptive period ended ~2017 with reported governance reforms, and (c) civil settlement is without admission though criminal-side NPA acknowledges factual basis. Functional.
