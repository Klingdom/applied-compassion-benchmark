# Special Event Assessment: Kuwait — Iran Missile/Drone Attack (Attribution Screen)

**Composite:** 25.0/100 (CONFIRM — sub-threshold; attribution to Iran)
**Band:** Developing

## Why it matters (plain language)
Iran fired ballistic missiles and drones at the US-run Ali Al Salem Air Base in Kuwait early on June 28, 2026. Kuwait's air defenses shot down two ballistic missiles. There were no deaths and only two minor injuries from falling shrapnel. The important point for scoring is this: the attack was Iran's act, not Kuwait's. We score a government on its own conduct toward its own people. Kuwait protected its civilians effectively and lost no one. Being a target does not lower a country's compassion score. So Kuwait's score does not move.

## The scorable question: Kuwait's own conduct, not the attack
A missile attack BY Iran is Iran's conduct, scored against Iran. Only Kuwait's OWN response conduct — civilian protection, treatment of its population, transparency — is scorable here.

### Within-window own-conduct (Jun 28, Tier 2-4)
- **Effective civilian protection.** Kuwait intercepted two ballistic missiles over its airspace; no fatalities, two minor shrapnel injuries. Competent air-defense and emergency response. (Al Jazeera, Stripes, Middle East Eye.)
- **Sovereignty condemnation, no retaliation against population.** Kuwait called the strikes a "flagrant violation of its sovereignty" and pursued de-escalation (Doha talks June 30). No internal crackdown, mass detention, or scapegoating reported.
- **Standing risk (not own-conduct harm):** desalination plants supplying ~99% of drinking water remain exposed to the wider war — a *threat from Iran's conflict*, not a harm Kuwait inflicted.

## §3e-bis screening (both directions blocked)
- **Attribution (decisive):** The missile/drone harm is Iran's conduct. It is scored against Iran, not Kuwait. Kuwait being attacked is not negative Kuwaiti own-conduct.
- **(3) Directionality — NO upgrade:** Kuwait surfaced on a negative-theater event. Effective interception is competent defense, not strong countervailing compassion conduct toward its own people; it does not clear the bar for an upgrade.
- **(4) Double-count / threshold — NO downgrade:** No new deliberate Kuwaiti harm. The chronic *bidoon* and migrant-labor limitations are already priced in the 25.0. Nothing new clears the 5-point threshold.

## Disposition
**CONFIRM at 25.0. Delta 0.0. No change proposal.** Kuwait's own conduct during the attack — effective civilian protection, no internal repression — is consistent with its existing Developing band. Confidence: medium (fast-moving event).
- **Watch flag — ACT (2.0):** sustained protection of civilian water/infrastructure if the war re-escalates.
- **Watch flag — EQU (1.5):** whether *bidoon* and migrant workers receive equal emergency protection.
- **Forward trigger (downgrade):** documented internal crackdown, discriminatory denial of emergency protection, or suppression of casualty information.
- **Forward trigger (upgrade):** durable, independently verified expansion of rights/protection to the *bidoon* and migrant population.
