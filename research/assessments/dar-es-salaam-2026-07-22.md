## Key Findings

- Why it matters: On July 7–8, 2026, Tanzanian police and soldiers flooded Dar es Salaam to crush pro-democracy protests demanding the release of jailed opposition leader Tundu Lissu — part of a crackdown rights groups say has killed hundreds.
- The European Parliament has moved to block an EU funding package over the violence, an external signal of how far accountability has collapsed.
- Municipal services function at a basic level, but the dominant in-window signal is state repression administered in the city, keeping Dar es Salaam in the low Developing / Critical border zone.

## Strongest Dimensions

## Weakest Dimensions

- Equity, Boundaries and Integrity — lethal suppression of dissent and no repair for victims.

## Evidence Gaps

- Crackdown is national-government conduct enacted in the city; separating municipal administration from national security policy is difficult. No independent municipal audit available.
