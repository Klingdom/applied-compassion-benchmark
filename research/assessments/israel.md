### Score Difference Analysis

The published score of 27.8 (Developing band) appears to have partially credited Israel's domestic governance institutions without fully accounting for the impact of the occupation and military operations on the total population under its control. The Compassion Benchmark methodology assesses an entity's compassion toward ALL populations it serves or controls — including occupied populations. Since October 2023, the scale of harm has escalated dramatically: 72,289 deaths, 172,040 injuries, UNRWA operations blocked, ICC arrest warrants issued, and the ICJ has declared the occupation unlawful. The Accountability dimension shows the largest gap (-35.0 points) because the published score of 2.5 implied some correction willingness, while the evidence shows active resistance to all international accountability mechanisms.

### Recommendation

The published score is significantly **overstated** based on current evidence. A downward revision of approximately 19 points is warranted, with a band change from Developing to Critical. The scale and documentation of harm since October 2023 — combined with rejection of ICJ and ICC findings and acceleration of settlement expansion — places Israel in the Critical band.

## Key Findings

- As of April 2026: 72,289 Palestinians killed in Gaza since October 7, 2023; 172,040 injured; 1,079 killed in West Bank including 235 children
- UNRWA banned from bringing international staff or aid into Gaza since March 2025; 127 facilities within Israeli-militarized zones
- ICJ advisory opinion (July 2024) declared occupation, settlement regime, and annexation unlawful; ICC issued arrest warrants for PM Netanyahu on war crimes and crimes against humanity charges (Nov 2024)
- West Bank displacement accelerating: 1,697 Palestinians displaced in Q1 2026 alone — more than all of 2025 — with 38 communities emptied by settler violence
- Security cabinet ratified measures facilitating expanded settlement land registration and property acquisition in occupied territory (Feb 2026)

## Strongest Dimensions

- Boundaries (20.0/100) — domestic institutional framework provides some sustainable governance infrastructure
- Action (17.5/100) — domestic services and programs function; partial credit for state capacity even though misdirected

## Weakest Dimensions

- Empathy, Equity, Accountability (all 2.5/100) — fundamental failure to empathize with, provide equity to, or accept accountability for the occupied population
