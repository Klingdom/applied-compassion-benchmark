### Recommendation

The published score of 0/100 (all dimensions at floor) is **confirmed** by current evidence. The humanitarian catastrophe has worsened since publication — entering the fourth year of war with famine confirmed, up to 400,000 dead, and 34 million needing aid — but the scoring methodology already places Sudan at the absolute floor. No change proposal warranted.

## Key Findings

- Sudan has entered its fourth year of war (April 15, 2026), with the world's worst humanitarian crisis: 34 million people (65% of population) need urgent humanitarian assistance
- Up to 400,000 estimated dead; 14 million displaced; famine confirmed in Al Fasher and Kadugli with 20 additional areas at risk
- 130 humanitarian workers killed in Sudan in 2025 alone; aid convoys ambushed; access systematically blocked by both warring parties
- International humanitarian response plan receiving only 5.5% of the needed $2.9 billion in funding
- RSF captured El Fasher (Oct 2025) with reports of mass killings, sexual violence, and aid blockade; Darfur genocide patterns repeating with impunity

## Strongest Dimensions

- None. All dimensions score at the absolute floor (0/100).

## Weakest Dimensions

- All dimensions equally at floor. Sudan represents a total collapse of institutional compassion infrastructure — the state itself is the primary driver of suffering.

## Evidence Gaps

- Direct ground-level reporting severely limited due to aid worker killings and access restrictions
- Death toll estimates vary widely (40,000 UN confirmed to 400,000 estimated) due to inability to count in active conflict zones
- Information from RSF-controlled areas extremely limited
