### Score Difference Analysis

The published 65.5 (Established) is approximately consistent with current evidence. EMP and ACT scores soften by ~7.5 each because of the April 2026 Universal Credit health-element cut, which is documented to reduce support for ~730,000 new disabled claimants by ~£3,000/year and is projected by independent analysts to push ~400,000 into poverty. This is precisely the kind of decision that erodes E4 (Validation) and AC2 (Proportionality) — the government framed disabled claimants as responsive to "perverse incentives" rather than affirming the legitimacy of the disability experience, and it scaled help inversely to the marginal-need cohort.

Offsetting positives include strong statutory accountability infrastructure (NAO, PHSO, FOI), inquiry-driven harm acknowledgment, and long-horizon planning capacity that distinguishes the UK from peers in the developing band.

### Recommendation

Composite delta is **-4.9 points**, below the 5-point change-proposal threshold. Confirmation recommended. Flag UC health-element implementation effects for monitoring in subsequent cycles — if poverty projections materialize, EMP/EQU/INT may compound downward over the next 6-12 months.

## Key Findings

1. **Welfare contraction during cost-of-living period** is the most significant in-window compassion-relevant event. UC health-element cut takes effect April 6 2026; 730,000 new disabled claimants affected, ~400,000 projected into poverty.
2. **Statutory accountability infrastructure remains strong** — NAO, PHSO, FOI, statutory inquiries — providing high baseline ACC and AWR.
3. **NHS performance recovery is partial** — waiting list down to 7.3M but median wait double pre-COVID; targets being narrowly missed.
4. **Inquiries produce delayed but real reparative outcomes** (Post Office Horizon, Infected Blood); pattern is acknowledgment-after-pressure rather than self-initiated.
5. **Pattern consistent with Established/Functional boundary** — strong infrastructure, weakening on equity for vulnerable claimants under fiscal pressure.

## Strongest Dimensions
- **BND (70)** — robust consent, scope clarity, refusal-with-alternatives via tribunal/judicial review.
- **AWR (65)** — MOSS, PHSO complaint volume capture, statutory equality impact assessments.
- **ACC (65)** — Statutory transparency baseline (FOI, NAO, parliamentary scrutiny).

## Weakest Dimensions
- **EQU (50)** — UC reform inverts proportionality for new disabled claimants; Windrush, Roma/Traveller and asylum-seeker access gaps persist.
- **ACT (55)** and **EMP (55)** — Welfare reform does not scale help to severity; framing characterizes rather than validates claimant experience.

## Evidence Gaps
- Direct community testimony from affected disabled claimants on UC health-element cut is anticipated rather than retrospective.
- Cross-departmental implementation data on equality impact assessment usage is partial.

## Recommended Next Steps

Functional/Established boundary entity. Monitor UC health-element implementation in Q2-Q3 2026 — material harm data could push composite down by 5-10 points.
