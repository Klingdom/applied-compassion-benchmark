## Assessment basis & attribution

Dual window event:
1. **Ebola PHEIC (Tier 5):** 896 confirmed cases / 232 deaths as of Jun 17, spreading to Uganda (19 cases); no vaccine; contact tracing at 45% (needs 90%). WHO chief called for a ceasefire. Outbreak spreading amid active conflict.
2. **M23/Rwanda (Tier 5):** HRW (Jun 10) documented Rwanda/M23 forced recruitment of thousands including children, with beatings, sexual exploitation, and executions in detention camps; 7M+ internally displaced.

**Attribution:** The M23/RDF abuses are inflicted BY an external actor (Rwanda) and a non-state armed group ON Congolese civilians and territory — attributed to the perpetrators (see Rwanda assessment), NOT scored against DRC. DRC is the victim entity here. What is scorable against DRC is its OWN conduct toward those it serves: the state's capacity to detect, respond to, and protect its population (Ebola response, IDP protection, healthcare). That capacity is near-total collapse in the affected east, hence the near-floor baseline.

**Why confirmation, not movement.** DRC is at 2.3 (near-floor, all dimensions 1.0-1.2). The Ebola response shows minimal functioning state capacity (some contact tracing, WHO coordination) which keeps it marginally above the absolute floor. The new evidence is consistent with the existing near-floor score. A defensible re-score does not move the composite by ≥5 points; logged as evidence-tier upgrade and watch flag.
