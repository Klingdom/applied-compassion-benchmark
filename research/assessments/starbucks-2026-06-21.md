## Assessment basis & attribution

Window context (Tier 2-4): The US House passed the Faster Labor Contracts Act (Jun 9) — directed at delay tactics like those used against unionized workers including Starbucks. No ratified first contract exists at any Starbucks location 4+ years after the first election; the two sides have not met since December 2024. The Nov 2025 "Red Cup Rebellion" open-ended strike (peaking ~4,500 baristas at 230 stores in December) was the longest in company history. In March 2026, shareholders were urged to vote against directors Knudstorp and Ford over labor-relations oversight failures (board quietly eliminated the EPCI committee in Nov 2025).

**Attribution:** This is Starbucks' OWN conduct — its bargaining posture and labor-governance choices toward its internal stakeholders (partners). Directly scorable, bearing primarily on INT (Internal Consistency, I3) and ACC.

**Why confirmation, not a change proposal.** The labor conduct reinforces the already-discounted INT (2.5) and ACC (2.5). A defensible re-score (ACC 2.25, INT 2.25) yields composite 46.9 — a delta of -1.5, below the 5-point threshold. The conduct is a continuation of a documented pattern already reflected in the published score, not a new adjudicated finding (the strike and stalled bargaining are ongoing; no NLRB final order moving the needle this cycle). Score confirmed at 48.4; watch flag set for first-contract ratification or NLRB findings.
