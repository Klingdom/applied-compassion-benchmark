## Assessment Type: Confirmation

Turkey appeared as a flagged NEW-attention entrant this cycle on strong within-window negative evidence (the NATO-summit mass crackdown). This is a targeted confirmation assessment against the published Critical-band baseline of 10.3.

## Within-Window Evidence (2026-06-24 to 2026-07-08)

- **Mass crackdown ahead of/during the Ankara NATO summit (Jun 23–Jul 8).** Human Rights Watch reports at least 209 people arrested in Ankara ahead of the July 7–8 summit; of 225 arrested June 23, courts sent 178 to pretrial detention and placed 34 under house arrest on "membership of armed terrorist organizations" grounds. Detainees include journalist/LGBT-rights activist Yıldız Tar, two lawyers, an academic, and 14 members of the TEMA reforestation foundation. Dawn raids spanned Istanbul, Ankara, Antalya, Kocaeli, Izmir and other provinces, sweeping up lawyers, writers, students, and editors from independent outlets T24 and Odatv. (HRW, 2026-06-25; Japan Times, 2026-07-06; Turkish Minute, 2026-07-06)
- The Ankara governor banned all public assemblies June 28–July 10; independent-outlet journalists (Cumhuriyet, Sözcü, Anka, T24, Medyascope) were denied summit press accreditation; 100+ additional anti-NATO protesters were detained. (Sunday Guardian, 2026-07-06)
- HRW characterized the arrests as evidence of "ruthless intolerance of freedom of speech and assembly."

## Anti-False-Positive Screening

1. **Baseline provenance.** Turkey's current 10.3 was set 2026-05-29 (arc: 22.5 → 17.6 → 15.1 → 10.3 across May 23–29) by Turkey's OWN documented conduct — judicial removal of CHP opposition leadership, physical seizure of CHP HQ, and opposition suppression. The low baseline is recent and evidence-driven, not stale.
2. **Directionality.** Negative within-window evidence; any move would be downward. No upgrade risk.
3. **Materiality.** The NATO-summit crackdown is a continuation of the same authoritarian pattern (suppression of assembly, press, and civil society) already priced into the 10.3 baseline. From an already-Critical 10.3 floor, the evidenced marginal move is well below the 5-point proposal threshold: ACC (1.15) and INT (1.15) are already near the floor; the arrests reinforce rather than newly establish the deficit.
4. **Calibration.** Not a calibration outlier requiring flag-for-review; the score sits appropriately among authoritarian-crackdown peers.
