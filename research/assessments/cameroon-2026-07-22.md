## Key Findings

- Why it matters: Human Rights Watch (July 13, 2026) found that poor coordination among Cameroon’s government agencies, police, courts and social services leaves survivors of gender-based violence without protection or justice.
- Cameroon missed its 2026 target to halve gender-based violence; HRW says “awareness campaigns and rhetoric are not enough” and the state has failed to reform discriminatory laws or fund protective services.
- This documented governance failure toward half the population is consistent with the published low Developing score; a first 40-subdimension review confirms it.

## Strongest Dimensions

- Awareness — only relatively: the state runs awareness campaigns, though HRW calls them insufficient.

## Weakest Dimensions

- Equity, Accountability and Integrity — systemic failure to protect women survivors and unmet GBV commitments.

## Evidence Gaps

- HRW is the primary within-window source, focused on GBV; broader service-delivery data across other populations was limited.
