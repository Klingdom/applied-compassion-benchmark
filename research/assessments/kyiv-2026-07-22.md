## Key Findings

- Why it matters: For three nights (July 16–18, 2026), more than 5,000 people protested in Kyiv against the sacking of a reform-minded military/anti-corruption official — with solidarity rallies in Lviv, Kharkiv, Odesa and Dnipro.
- Unlike several peer cities in this batch, Kyiv tolerated mass protest without lethal suppression — a sign that civic space, press and accountability institutions still function under wartime stress.
- The dismissal raises real fears of governance backsliding toward an “entrenched command culture,” but it is being publicly contested, not silenced. A full review confirms the Functional score.
