## Assessment Basis — UNSC Demand to Release 73 Detained UN Staff

On 6 June 2026 the UN Security Council, in a unanimous statement marking ~2 years of detentions, demanded the immediate and unconditional release of 73 UN personnel detained by Yemen's **Houthi movement**, warning the arrests are worsening a humanitarian crisis affecting 22.3M+ people. HRW and CIHRS issued parallel demands (June 7).

### Methodology ruling: ATTRIBUTION (Houthi non-state actor) + ALREADY-AT-FLOOR

1. **Attribution — Houthi vs. state.** The detentions are perpetrated by the Houthi (Ansar Allah) movement, a non-state armed group controlling territory in northern Yemen, not by the internationally-recognized government. The Compassion Benchmark "Yemen" country entity is published at the absolute 0.0 floor reflecting the collapse of any unitary state capacity for compassion amid a multi-party civil war.
