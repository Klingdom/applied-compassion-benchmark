## Assessment basis & attribution

Window event (Tier 4-5): WFP and the Cadre Harmonisé project 34.7M Nigerians at IPC Phase 3+ during the Jun-Aug 2026 lean season, with ~15,000 in Borno at Phase 5 (Catastrophe). WFP warned it would exhaust emergency resources, leaving 1.3M without support. Drivers: ISWAP/Boko Haram insurgency, climate shocks, high food prices, and global aid-funding cuts.

**Attribution:** Much of the acute harm is driven by (a) ISWAP insurgent attacks (external/non-state perpetrator) and (b) donor aid-funding cuts (external). These are not scored against Nigeria's own conduct. What is scorable is the Nigerian state's own protective and response capacity — chronically weak governance, limited social protection, and slow security response in the northeast — which is already reflected in the Critical baseline (18).

**Why confirmation.** No new adjudicated change in state conduct within the window; the crisis is a continuation/intensification of documented structural weakness plus external drivers. Score confirmed at 18; watch flag for famine declaration in Borno.
