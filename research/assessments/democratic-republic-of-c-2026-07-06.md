# Assessment: Democratic Republic of Congo — Ebola Accelerating (Exogenous-Disease + Near-Floor Screen)

**Composite:** 2.3/100 (CONFIRM — exogenous outbreak scored on response; near the floor)

## Why it matters (plain language)
The Bundibugyo Ebola outbreak is a disease event, not a government act, so only the DRC's response to it is scorable. The case count is rising fast, but that is the outbreak's trajectory — and the score already sits near the bottom.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1))
- Live baseline **2.3**, rank **181**, Critical (near floor). Already prices near-collapsed public health capacity, mass displacement, and near-absent civilian protection.

## The scorable question
- WHO (Jul 6): 1,561 confirmed Ebola cases, 506 deaths — up from 1,406/438 on Jul 3 (~52 new/day, accelerating); Ituri most affected. Limited international spread (France 1 imported case; Uganda 20, contained). No approved vaccine/treatment for the Bundibugyo strain; PHEIC active.
- The outbreak is **exogenous**; only the **response** is scorable. Rising counts reflect the outbreak's trajectory. M23/FARDC conflict harm attributes substantially to **Rwanda-backed forces**, not solely the DRC state.
- Reinforces but **cannot meaningfully lower** an already near-floor 2.3.
