## Refinement basis (what changed in the window)

This is a REFINEMENT of the index-assembly baseline (52.3), not a first baseline. The June 2026 news cluster comprises:

1. **DOJ lawsuit to recoup grants and bar future federal access** — refiled around Mar 20 2026; Harvard rejected a $1B settlement demand; a federal court blocked the ~$2.2B+ funding freeze; Harvard reported blacklisted from federal contracts. This is federal action INFLICTED ON Harvard. Under the methodology's victim/perpetrator attribution rule, harm imposed by an external actor is attributed to the perpetrator (the federal government), not scored as a compassion failure of Harvard-as-subject. Notably, a federal judge previously ruled the administration acted unlawfully in cutting Harvard's funding — Harvard is the prevailing/contesting party, not the wrongdoer, in this dispute.

2. **Continued layoffs, salary freeze, hiring moratorium; Broad Institute cut 75 staff** — this IS a genuine internal-consistency (I3) and self-sustainability (B1) signal: research and administrative workers bearing the cost of the funding fight. However, much of this is a downstream consequence of the external funding attack, and Harvard's published INT is already low (2.5) and ACC low (2.75), reflecting prior internal-culture and slow-harm-acknowledgment findings (e.g., the OCR Title VI/Title IX issues and the Comaroff case cited in the assembly rationale). The new layoffs are consistent with — and do not push below — these already-conservative scores.

Neither category constitutes a new adjudicated conduct-toward-students harm of a magnitude that would move the composite by ≥5 points or change the band.
