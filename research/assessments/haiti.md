## Context

First full assessment. Haiti's published score (3.1) reflects a Critical-band floor set without a prior nightly assessment file. April 2026 evidence confirms the score: gangs control 90% of Port-au-Prince and are expanding into Artibonite, Centre, and Grand Nord; 6.4M need humanitarian assistance (>50% of population); 1.45M internally displaced; 5.7M acutely hungry; GBV cases rose 25% YoY in 2025 (8,000 recorded); 1,000% rise in child sexual violence since 2023; humanitarian response plan only 24% funded.

Assessment focus: distinguish between the legitimate transitional government's structural inability to provide basic protection (which is the compassion-relevant failure) vs. gang atrocities (which are attributable to non-state actors). For the purposes of a state-level benchmark, the inability to protect is itself the failure.
