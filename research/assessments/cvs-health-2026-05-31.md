## Summary of cycle ruling

CVS Health was flagged for a full assessment on two genuinely new litigation events. The ruling is **confirm at 25.6**: both events are **filed-but-unadjudicated civil complaints** that *reinforce* — rather than reveal something not already priced into — CVS's deeply compromised baseline (ACC 1.75, EQU 1.75). Under the evidence hierarchy and the ALGORITHM-BASED-WORKER-CLASSIFICATION/Oracle precedent (unadjudicated allegations are not yet scorable as dimension moves), neither event crosses the threshold for a sub-threshold downgrade on a baseline that already sits near the Developing/Critical floor.

**Event 1 — 340B diversion suit (NEW, May 21, 2026):** Mount Sinai, Michigan Medicine, and University of Kansas Health System sued CVS and affiliates (CaremarkPCS, CVS Specialty, WellPartner) alleging a "secret pricing scheme" that diverted ~$250M in 340B savings (2020–2025) intended for indigent/underserved care. Mechanism: specialty claims processed at standard rates, then retroactively repriced downward once flagged 340B-eligible, with CVS pocketing the spread. This directly implicates EQU (funds earmarked for vulnerable patients) and ACC.

**Event 2 — Tennessee FAIR Rx suit (NEW, May 22, 2026):** CVS filed a federal challenge to Tennessee's bipartisan PBM-pharmacy-ownership-divestiture law, arguing Dormant Commerce Clause violation. A Tennessee state audit found Caremark reimbursed affiliated pharmacies up to 16,000% more than non-affiliated pharmacies. Implicates S4 (structural critique) and I4 (values alignment) negatively.

Both are allegations/litigation postures, not findings. The prior Louisiana $45M settlement (Feb 2026) is already-priced pattern context. **No dimension moves; logged as carry-forward watch pending adjudication.**
