## Assessment Basis

Event-driven reassessment triggered by the June 2026 FAO-WFP hunger-hotspot report and corroborating IPC/IRC/WFP findings:

- ~15,000 people in Borno State (Dikwa, Kaga, Kalabalge) at risk of slipping into **Catastrophe (CH/IPC Phase 5)** during the June-August 2026 lean season — the most severe classification, denoting starvation, death, and extremely critical malnutrition. (FAO-WFP)
- ~34.8 million people in Nigeria projected at high acute food insecurity (CH Phase 3+), including ~1.8 million in Emergency (Phase 4). (WFP)
- Nigeria added to the highest-concern FAO-WFP hunger-hotspot tier.
- The IRC characterizes the famine risk as "entirely man-made" and "entirely preventable," with children starving "because they were given no other option than to be caught in the middle of this war."
- Conflict (Boko Haram/ISWAP) is the primary driver — armed groups block farming, sever supply routes, and collapse humanitarian pipelines — but the famine is preventable with adequate state and international response, and access remains the central barrier.

Distinct from Mali/Burkina Faso, the Nigerian state is not documented as the primary perpetrator of the civilian harm; the failure is one of prevention, protection, and prioritized response to catastrophic, foreseeable need. The downgrade is therefore concentrated in ACT (responsiveness/proportionality to catastrophe-level need), EQU (priority for the most vulnerable, who are slipping into Phase 5), and SYS (root-cause/structural failure), and is calibrated conservatively. The result, 18.0, crosses the Developing→Critical band boundary.

### Recommendation
Downgrade from 21.9 to 18.0 (medium confidence). The composite delta (-3.9) is below the 5-point change threshold, BUT the move crosses the Developing→Critical band boundary, which is a designation change warranting a proposal. The trigger is a Tier-4 FAO-WFP / Tier-3 IRC documented escalation to IPC/CH Phase 5 Catastrophe for ~15,000 Borno residents amid a "man-made, entirely preventable" famine and 34.8M at Phase 3+. Nigeria is scored as failing to prevent and prioritize response to foreseeable catastrophic need, not as the primary perpetrator — hence the measured magnitude.

## Key Findings
- ~15,000 in Borno State at risk of IPC/CH Phase 5 Catastrophe during the June-August 2026 lean season.
- ~34.8M Nigerians at CH Phase 3+, including ~1.8M in Emergency (Phase 4); Nigeria added to the highest-concern hotspot tier.
- IRC: the famine is "entirely man-made" and "entirely preventable"; access is the central barrier.
- Recurring decade-plus northeast crisis indicates structural (SYS) and accountability (ACC) failure, not a one-off shock.

## Strongest Dimensions

## Weakest Dimensions

## Evidence Gaps
