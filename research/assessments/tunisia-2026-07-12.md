## Fresh Within-Window Evidence (July 8, 2026)

A Tunis court sentenced **21 people — including Ennahda party leaders, former officials and lawyers — to prison terms of 12 to 35 years** on vague terrorism/state-security charges. Rached Ghannouchi (former Ennahda president, detained since April 2023) received a **14-year sentence in absentia**, the latest of several convictions against him. This followed the July 8 close of the 62nd UN Human Rights Council session, where UN experts and civil society warned that continued member-state silence emboldens Tunisia's crackdown.

The existing proposal (23.8) already rests on: the criminalization of Tunisia's transitional-justice legacy (Sihem Bensedrine 25-yr + heavy fine), mass "conspiracy" trials with sentences up to 66 years, Ghannouchi's prior 20-year sentence, the shutdown of the independent information-access authority, and racially-targeted collective expulsions of sub-Saharan migrants ([HRW, Jul 8](https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/07/08/sounding-the-alarm-over-tunisias-crackdown); [Freedom House 2026](https://freedomhouse.org/country/tunisia/freedom-world/2026)).

The July 8 mass sentencing of 21 opposition figures is **fully consistent with and corroborative of** that basis (ACC/INT/EQU institutional collapse). It reinforces — but does not materially change — the proposed figure. The 23.8 already sits in Developing, above Critical-band peers, and the new evidence does not push the derived composite to a different point or band.
