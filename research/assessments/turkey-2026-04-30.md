### Score Difference Analysis

The published 32.8 appears to under-weight the cumulative pattern of ECtHR non-compliance (432 Article 10 violations between 2002 and 2025, per April 29 2026 Stockholm Center for Freedom and Turkish Minute reporting). This is structurally distinct from a one-off rights violation: it is a sustained, decade-plus pattern of refusing to honor binding judgments, which is the textbook ACC/INT failure profile.

In-window evidence (April 14-29 2026) compounds the picture:
- Five named journalists sentenced April 14-15 (Soykan, Pehlivan, Ağırel, Boltan, Arapkirli)
- Mehmet Yetim arrested April 18-19 under "disinformation law"
- EFJ joint statement April 28 condemning continued use of Article 217/A
- ECtHR cumulative ruling tally published April 29

The pattern suggests the published 32.8 reflects pre-2025 evidence and that the entity has structurally degraded since. Proposed composite 22.5 better reflects the current trajectory.

### Recommendation

Composite delta is **-8.75 points** (medium magnitude). Generate change proposal: **downgrade**. Confidence: medium-high (multiple T1 sources: ECtHR rulings, CPJ, EFJ, HRW). Recommended for human review given Turkey's geopolitical sensitivity and the magnitude of the move.

## Key Findings

1. **ECtHR non-compliance pattern is the dominant compassion-relevant feature** — 432 freedom-of-expression violations cumulative; the published score does not fully reflect this.
2. **April 2026 journalist sentencings (5 named, 1 arrested)** confirm continuation rather than reform.
3. **Article 217/A "disinformation law" usage compounds Article 299** in producing chilling effects on amplification, validation, and structural critique.
4. **Earthquake response criticism criminalization** is a uniquely diagnostic feature (rights restriction overlaying disaster response).
5. **Strongest residual compassion infrastructure: refugee-hosting at scale** (~3M Syrians) and basic universal services provide partial floor.
