# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Venezuela (Confirmation — Threshold Re-check)

**Entity type:** Country
**Assessment date:** 2026-07-12
**Published composite:** 18.0/100 (Critical) — rank 150 of 193, countries index
**Determination:** CONFIRM at 18.0. The cumulative evidenced own-conduct response-failure move does NOT cross the 5-point threshold. Strong downward watch sustained.

## Within-Window Evidence (June 24 twin earthquakes)

- Death toll climbed to **4,333** as of July 11 (up from ~3,889 on July 10 and 3,535 on July 6), with 16,740 injured and ~17,854 left without housing; National Assembly President Jorge Rodríguez said the count is not final ([TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320189/20260711/venezuela-earthquake-toll-climbs-4333-navy-warship-built-broken-ports-takes-over.htm); [US News](https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2026-07-06/death-toll-from-venezuela-quakes-rises-to-3-535-as-thousands-remain-displaced)).
- USGS estimates the final toll will most likely fall between 10,000 and 100,000 given the devastation (La Guaira ~80% of buildings collapsed).
- Response criticism intensifying: Plan International reports some shelters lack basic hygiene facilities or amenities for women and children; labor unions and civil-society groups marched in Caracas demanding new presidential elections and criticizing the quake response; disapproval of the government's handling reached 63.3% in June polling.
- Countervailing response capacity: a navy warship was deployed for broken-port logistics; ~87 makeshift camps established; external aid inflows referenced in prior cycles (US ~$300M, government relief fund, IMF request).

## Threshold Re-check (attribution discipline)

The prior cycles held the evidenced **own-conduct response-failure** move at approximately **−2.7** (sub-threshold). The re-check asks whether the new evidence produces an additional discrete own-conduct step-change large enough to reach ≥−5.0 (i.e., to ~13.0).

- The toll increase from ~3,899 to 4,333 is predominantly **continued body recovery from collapsed buildings** — attributable to the **scale of the natural disaster**, not to a new state-conduct failure. Under attribution rules, natural-disaster deaths are not scored as Venezuela's own conduct.
- The genuinely own-conduct signals (shelter inadequacy, high disapproval, protests) are **intensifications of already-counted response gaps**, not a discrete new failure event.
- Some countervailing state response capacity (naval logistics, shelter expansion) and external aid partly offset.

**Result:** the cumulative own-conduct response-failure move deepens modestly (toward the low −3s) but does **NOT** cross the −5-point threshold. Conservative, attribution-disciplined scoring forecloses a downgrade this cycle.

## Determination

**CONFIRM at 18.0 (Critical). No proposal.** Strong downward watch sustained. A discrete, evidenced own-conduct failure (e.g., documented aid diversion, denial of access to responders, or a state action that measurably worsens survivor outcomes) — as opposed to further disaster-scale toll growth — would be required to cross the threshold and warrant a proposal.
