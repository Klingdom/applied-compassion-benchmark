## Key Evidence (within-window)

- HRW documents that more than 17 million Afghans face severe food insecurity in 2026 and about 22 million require urgent food and health assistance. Only 20% of Afghan households can afford sufficient food without aid, down from 28% before October 2025. Source: https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/07/17/i-cant-feed-my-children

- OWN-CONDUCT COMPONENT: HRW attributes part of the access failure directly to Taliban policy — restrictions on women working in humanitarian and UN roles materially impede aid delivery to vulnerable households. This is distinct from the external funding shortfall. Source: https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/07/17/i-cant-feed-my-children

- EXTERNAL COMPONENT, NOT SCORED AS TALIBAN CONDUCT: the UN 2026 appeal of $1.71 billion is under 20% funded at midyear, compounded by a sharp cut in US humanitarian financing which previously supplied about 40% of Afghanistan aid. Source: https://kabulnow.com/2026/07/human-rights-watch-warns-millions-in-afghanistan-face-imminent-hunger-crisis/
