## Within-Window Evidence (2026-06-24 to 2026-07-08)

- **UN permanently closes its Burkina Faso human-rights office (MAJOR new event, Jun 30 announcement / Jul 2 reported).** The UN Human Rights Office (OHCHR) announced it will permanently close its country office by 30 November 2026, following the junta's February suspension of its local operations. OHCHR sought clarification on the suspension's duration multiple times and received no response. Foreign Minister Karamoko Jean Marie Traoré accused international organizations of behaving like "super police" and overstepping national sovereignty. HRW: the closure "ends its ability to monitor, document, and report on human rights abuses at a time when conflict continues and violations are rampant." (HRW, 2026-07-02; OHCHR press release, 2026-07; Euronews, 2026-07-02)
- Context (HRW): since the 2022 coup the junta has suspended media outlets, unions, and hundreds of CSOs, dismantled multiparty politics, and subjected critics to arbitrary detention, enforced disappearances, unlawful conscription, and torture.
