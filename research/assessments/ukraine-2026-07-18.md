### ADJUDICATION: Ukrainian own-conduct civilian harm at Kotovsk

**The attribution question is settled, and it goes against Ukraine.** The scanner flagged this without adjudicating it. I adjudicate it here.

Under the acting-party rule this is Ukraine's own conduct. Ukraine acknowledged the strikes at head-of-state level — President Zelensky publicly confirmed that two major logistics facilities were hit. Seven of the nine dead were civilian night-shift workers at a retail distribution centre 500 to 700 kilometres from the front line. This is materially different from every prior Ukraine cycle, all of which recorded harm inflicted ON Ukraine BY Russia and correctly attributed it to Russia. **This is the first entry of Ukrainian own-conduct civilian harm in the ledger.** That is a real finding and it should not be softened.

**Three factors bear on magnitude, and they cut in both directions.**

*Against Ukraine:* the targets were retail warehouses struck at night, when night-shift workers are predictably present. Ukraine's dual-use justification — that the facilities were used in drone manufacture — is an assertion for which no supporting evidence has been offered publicly. Whether or not it is true, the timing choice bears on A5 anticipatory harm assessment, and Ukraine has not acknowledged the civilian deaths, which bears on AB1 harm acknowledgment.

*For Ukraine:* this is one dated incident, not an established pattern. The benchmark does not convert single incidents into large score moves for any entity, and it should not start doing so here.

*The arithmetic:* all 8 Ukrainian dimensions sit below 4.0, so weaknessFactor is 0, the integration premium is 0, and composite equals baseComposite exactly. A 5-point move therefore requires the 8-dimension mean to shift by 0.2 — **1.6 raw dimension points in total**. Scoring this incident honestly against the anchors yields approximately: ACC 3.0 to 2.75 (no acknowledgment of the civilian deaths, unevidenced dual-use assertion), AWR 3.0 to 2.9 (A5 anticipatory assessment for night-occupied logistics targets), INT 3.0 to 2.9. That is about -0.45 total, a composite move of roughly **-1.4 points**. Even a deliberately aggressive reading reaches only about -3.1.

**Ruling: CONFIRM at 50.0 with a downward watch flag.** The evidence is real, new, and correctly attributed to Ukraine, but it is roughly a third of what the 5-point threshold requires. Manufacturing a proposal from it would be precisely the false positive the screening rules exist to prevent.

**Conversion trigger:** a second dated incident of Ukrainian deep-strike civilian casualties, or an institutional finding (OHCHR, HRW, Amnesty) characterising the Kotovsk strike as unlawful, converts this to a proposal.

Those dimensions have never been independently re-derived. That is a scope gap requiring a scheduled deep assessment, not a one-off adjustment, and per the anti-false-positive rules it must not be encoded as a score proposal.
