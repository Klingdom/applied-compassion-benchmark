# Special Upward Assessment: South Korea

**Composite:** 62.5/100 (CONFIRM — no upgrade warranted)
**Band:** Established

## Why it matters (plain language)
South Korea's democratic recovery from the December 2024 martial-law crisis is genuinely impressive — parliament resisted within hours, the court upheld the impeachment, and lawmakers passed safeguards against future martial law. But almost all of that happened in 2025, outside this 30-day window, and is already reflected in the 62.5 score. Within the window, the only new action is routine staff appointments.

## Symmetric-rigor screens
- **Window (DECISIVE):** The martial-law safeguard amendments were enacted in **July 2025** (stricter declaration parameters, ban on military entering the Assembly, Assembly lift-authority). That is ~11 months before this window opened. The constitutional amendment is a notice/proposal, not enacted.
- **Anti-double-count:** The 62.5 baseline post-dates the 2025 restoration and already prices the democratic-resilience story. The scan concedes "core realizations occurred before the window."
- **Within-window own-conduct:** Only President Lee's June 21 appointment of senior aides — administrative, not a realized structural compassion change.
- **Realization vs announcement:** Prosecutorial-reform agenda is in-progress advocacy, not enacted within window.

## Disposition
CONFIRM at 62.5. Delta 0.0. No change proposal. The structural positives are real but out-of-window and already priced; the within-window evidence is administrative.
