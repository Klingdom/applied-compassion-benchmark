# Assessment: El Salvador — First Formal Baseline (Codified Authoritarian Consolidation)

**Composite:** 15.0/100 (DOWNGRADE from 20.3)
**Band:** Critical (crossing from Developing)

## Why it matters (plain language)
El Salvador has never had a full Compassion Benchmark assessment. Its published score of 20.3 (Developing) sits just above the Critical line and predates a wave of documented, in-force events: a constitutional amendment allowing indefinite presidential re-election (approved 57-3 on July 31, 2025), a May 2025 "foreign agents" law, the arrest of anti-corruption chief Ruth Lopez, and the forced exile of Cristosal, the country's leading human rights group, after 25 years. These are the government's own actions, not exogenous shocks. On the evidence they warrant a Critical-band baseline.

### Recommendation
Published 20.3 is understated in severity for a government that in 2025 codified indefinite re-election, expelled its leading human rights organization, and jailed its anti-corruption lead while running a 3-year mass-detention regime. A downgrade to **15.0 (Critical)** is warranted and conservative — it stays above Bahrain (9.4) and Mali (12.5) and below Venezuela (18.0)/China (19.5), reflecting a functioning state that nonetheless perpetrates large-scale rights harm. ACT is held above baseline to credit the real homicide reduction.

## Key Findings
- **Codified democratic dismantling.** Indefinite presidential re-election approved 57-3 (July 31, 2025); term extended to 6 years; runoff elections removed. Takes effect 2027.
- **Civic space closed.** May 2025 "foreign agents" law; Cristosal forced into exile after 25 years ("prison or exile"); anti-corruption chief Ruth Lopez arrested; 140+ defenders/journalists in exile.
- **Mass detention without due process.** State of exception since 2022 (~87,000 detained), documented custodial deaths, profiling of poor young men.
- **Genuine countervailing security outcome.** Homicide rate fell dramatically — priced into ACT (2.4), the one dimension above published baseline.

## Strongest Dimension
- ACT (2.4): decisive, measurably effective crime reduction, though achieved at grave rights cost.

## Weakest Dimensions
- EQU (1.2), ACC (1.2): entire populations excluded from rights; harms denied, watchdogs expelled.

## Evidence Gaps
- Independent in-country monitoring has thinned as NGOs exit; custodial-death totals are contested and likely undercounted.
