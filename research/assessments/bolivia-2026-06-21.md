## Assessment basis & attribution

Window event (Tier 2-4): President Rodrigo Paz declared a 90-day nationwide state of emergency (Jun 20), deploying the military to clear 103 blockade points after ~50 days of protests. The Ombudsman attributes at least 17 deaths to disrupted medical access (7 after supplies failed to reach a blockaded hospital; a cancer patient died unable to reach radiotherapy; a truck driver died after 32 days stranded); at least one death in clashes with police; 365 arrests.

**Attribution (nuanced):**
- The blockade deaths are caused principally by **protesters** erecting barricades (external to state conduct) — attributed to the protest actors, not the state.
- The protests themselves were triggered by the state's **fuel-subsidy cut** (state conduct, but a fiscal-policy decision with contested compassion valence).
- The state's OWN scorable conduct is its **response**: declaring emergency and using the military to restore hospital/food/medicine access. At least one death in clashes with police is a state-conduct harm.

**Why confirmation, not movement.** Bolivia is at 6.3 (near-floor; ACC and INT already at 1.0). The state's emergency response is a (constitutionally escalatory) attempt to restore access — not obviously a compassion improvement nor a clear new atrocity. The dominant harm vector (blockade deaths) is attributable to protesters. Per near-floor limitation and attribution rules, no scorable ≥5-point movement; logged as watch flag.
