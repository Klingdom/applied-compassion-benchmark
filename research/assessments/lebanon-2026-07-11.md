# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Lebanon (Confirmation)

**Assessment date:** 2026-07-11 | **Published composite:** 17.2/100 (Critical) | **Recommendation:** confirm

## Within-window signal
The Israel–Hezbollah ceasefire remains unstable, with continued cross-border incidents. The dominant harm affecting Lebanese civilians this window is attributable to Israeli strikes and Hezbollah (a non-state actor) — not to the conduct of the Lebanese state, which remains institutionally weak but is not the perpetrator of the primary within-window harm.

## Threshold determination
Attribution controls: externally-inflicted harm and non-state-actor harm are not scored against the Lebanese state. Lebanese state own-conduct (chronic governance paralysis, economic collapse, weak service delivery) is unchanged and already priced. Sub-threshold.

## Outcome
**Confirm at 17.2 (Critical). Downward watch sustained.** Conversion trigger: a documented Lebanese-state own-conduct failure of new scale (e.g., deliberate obstruction of aid or a state-attributable atrocity).
