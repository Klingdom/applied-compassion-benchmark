The assessed composite of 43.8 is 4.3 points below the published 48.1 — inside the confirmation threshold, so no change proposal is filed. The interesting movement is internal and offsetting. Equity RISES relative to the published baseline: Access Design now scores 4 of 5 because the FTC consent order removes a concrete, decade-long barrier that had prevented farmers from fixing equipment they own. Boundaries Autonomy Preservation also scores 4 of 5, since restoring the ability to repair your own machine is close to a textbook case of building capacity rather than dependency. These gains are offset by Integrity and Empathy, where the 2024-2025 pattern of US layoffs alongside expanded Mexican production, and Deere's insistence that the two were unrelated, keeps Perspective-Taking and Internal Consistency at 2 of 5.

## Key Findings

- Farmers can now legally fix their own tractors, and Deere is bound to help for ten years. The FTC settlement requires Deere to give farmers and independent shops repair resources "equivalent to those Deere now makes available to Deere dealers."
- This is the second repair settlement this year. Deere paid $99 million to farmers in an April 2026 class action before the FTC deal in July, so actual money has reached the people who were harmed.
- Deere only fixed this under pressure. The FTC and five state attorneys general sued in January 2025; the changes are compelled, which is why Harm Acknowledgment still scores 2 out of 5.
- Deere treats its customers better than its workers. It cut hundreds of Iowa and Illinois jobs while building capacity in Mexico, and the UAW called the cuts "an insult to the working class people of Iowa and Illinois."
- The score does not change. At 43.8 against a published 48.1, the 4.3 point gap is within normal assessment variation and no change proposal is warranted.

## Strongest Dimensions

Action (raw 3) and Boundaries (raw 3). Autonomy Preservation and Access Design both score 4 of 5 — the highest subdimension scores Deere records — because the right-to-repair remedy directly restores customer self-determination rather than deepening reliance on the manufacturer. Reparative Action also reaches 3 of 5 on the $99 million class settlement.

## Weakest Dimensions

Integrity (raw 2.4) and Empathy (raw 2.6). Consistency Under Pressure, Internal Consistency, Values Alignment and Perspective-Taking all score 2 of 5. Deere attributed job cuts to a weakened farm economy while simultaneously expanding Mexican operations, and workers had to take plant-closure fights to Congress to be heard.

## Evidence Gaps

Compliance with the FTC consent order has not yet begun in earnest — the order was announced on 2026-07-08, so there is no evidence on whether farmers actually receive equivalent resources in practice. Efficacy (AC3) and Follow-Through (AC5) are therefore scored on the commitment, not on outcomes, and should be re-tested in 6 to 12 months. Farmer testimony about whether the $99 million settlement was considered adequate was not located, which constrains Reparative Action (AB5). Deere's dealer network relations and its treatment of seasonal and migrant labour in its supply chain were not examined.
