# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Humana (Reassessment)

**Entity type:** Company | **Sector:** Health Insurance / Medicare Advantage | **Assessment date:** 2026-06-18
**Composite score:** 40.6/100 | **Band:** Functional (lower boundary) | **Published:** 51.6 (Functional, rank #83) | **Delta:** -11.0

## Ruling: DOWNGRADE — Tier-5 federal watchdog finding of systematic care denial

The trigger is a **new HHS Office of Inspector General (OIG) report**, surfaced June 12-15 2026, examining prior-authorization denials for post-acute care across the three largest Medicare Advantage insurers. The OIG found that **Humana denied 72% of long-term care hospital (LTCH) admission requests and 54% of inpatient rehabilitation facility (IRF) requests** — far above the ~42% peer average of the 16 smaller MA organizations studied. Across the three largest insurers, roughly **95% of appealed skilled-nursing denials were overturned**, indicating that a large share of initial denials were not clinically warranted. Coverage characterized the pattern as insurers appearing to "deny care for profit."

This is a Tier-5 government watchdog finding (not a pre-adjudication allegation), bearing directly on whether Humana's help is calibrated to need (ACT), prioritizes the most vulnerable post-acute patients (EQU), validates the experience of those harmed (EMP), and owns the pattern (ACC/INT). Post-acute LTCH/IRF patients are among the highest-need, lowest-power beneficiaries — disproportionately elderly, disabled, and recovering from serious illness — making the equity dimension especially load-bearing.

A partial mitigant: Humana publicly committed in 2025 to **reduce prior authorizations for Medicare Advantage in 2026**, which is a documented (if unproven) correction signal that prevents ACC from collapsing toward the floor.
