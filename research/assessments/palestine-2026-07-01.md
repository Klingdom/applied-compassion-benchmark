# Assessment: Palestine — Gaza Food Crisis + Aid Restrictions (Attribution Screen)

**Composite:** 25.0/100 (CONFIRM)
**Band:** Developing

## Why it matters (plain language)
The humanitarian crisis in Gaza keeps deepening: 77% of the population faces acute food insecurity, and aid trucks fell from 4,200 per week to 590 per week in February 2026. OCHA's June 19 report says aid is far below required levels and access routes are repeatedly obstructed. Israel banned 37 humanitarian organizations. MSF says "this is not a ceasefire." Almost all of this harm is inflicted through Israel's blockade and aid restrictions — which is scored against Israel, not Palestine.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1))
- Live baseline **25.0**, rank **123**, Developing. Prices Palestinian governance capacity limits (fragmented PA/Hamas authority, weak institutions) independent of the externally-imposed humanitarian harm.

## §3e-bis screening
- **Attribution (§3e-bis(3)):** the food crisis, aid-truck collapse, access obstruction, and 37-org ban are Israel's conduct (blockade/restrictions), scored against Israel. They are not Palestine's own conduct and do not lower Palestine's own score.
- **Directionality:** surfaced on negative humanitarian evidence whose cause is external — this cannot generate a Palestine downgrade, and the absence of new positive Palestinian own-conduct blocks an upgrade.
- **No new own-conduct:** no documented within-window change in Palestinian governance conduct (either direction) that clears the threshold.

## Disposition
**CONFIRM at 25.0. Delta 0.0. No change proposal.** Confidence: medium.
- **Forward trigger (downgrade):** documented diversion/obstruction of aid or rights abuses by Palestinian governing authorities.
- **Forward trigger (upgrade):** documented effective, equitable governance/relief delivery by Palestinian authorities under constraint.
