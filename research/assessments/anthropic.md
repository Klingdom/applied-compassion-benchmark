### Score Difference Analysis

The 22.1-point downgrade is driven by three major developments in early 2026:

1. **Safety pledge rollback (Feb 24, 2026)**: Anthropic removed the commitment to pause model development if capabilities outpaced safety measures. This was the cornerstone of their safety identity and directly undermines Exemplary-band scoring across Action, Accountability, and Integrity.

2. **Mrinank Sharma resignation (Feb 9, 2026)**: The head of safeguards research resigned publicly, stating "I've repeatedly seen how hard it is to truly let our values govern our actions" and citing "constant pressures to set aside what matters most." This is Tier 5 evidence (independent testimony from a senior insider) that directly contradicts Exemplary-band scoring across Awareness, Empathy, and Integrity.

3. **Pentagon pressure (Feb 2026)**: Defense Secretary Hegseth gave Anthropic a Friday deadline to roll back safeguards or risk losing a $200M Pentagon contract and government blacklisting. Anthropic complied within days. This demonstrates that safety commitments did not hold under financial/political pressure — directly contradicting the I1 (Consistency Under Pressure) anchor for scores above 2.

The published Exemplary score was earned based on Anthropic's industry-leading safety framework and Constitutional AI approach. These achievements remain real, but the February 2026 events represent a fundamental shift from "will pause if safety requires it" to "will compete even if safety is uncertain." This moves Anthropic from Exemplary to Established band.

### Recommendation

Published score of 90.9 is significantly overstated given February 2026 developments. Current evidence supports 68.8 (Established). Recommend downgrade from Exemplary to Established. Anthropic retains strong safety infrastructure relative to peers but can no longer claim the highest safety commitment standard it previously set for itself.
