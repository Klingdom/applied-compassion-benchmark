# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Bolivia (2026-05-28)

**Trigger:** Crisis escalation in 48 hours since May 26 first-baseline application (30.9). President Paz enacted a law restoring executive authority to deploy military in streets (May 27); Congress lifted state-of-emergency restrictions. Four deaths confirmed (three from blocked emergency/oxygen vehicles, one in police clashes). La Paz hospitals rationing oxygen. President publicly declared country "at breaking point."

## Drift-Guard Check

Published index: 30.9 (first-baseline applied 2026-05-26). Working baseline: 30.9. Drift = 0 → **ACCEPT** band.

## Triggering Evidence Window

**Primary new event (2026-05-27, military deployment law enacted):**
- President Rodrigo Paz signed law restoring executive authority to deploy armed forces in streets and simplifying state-of-emergency procedures.
- Law took effect upon publication in Official Gazette; armed forces now fully authorized for joint operations with National Police.
- Fast-tracked through Plurinational Legislative Assembly. **Humanitarian-impact escalation:**
- Government confirms ≥4 deaths from blockade-related lack of medical care (oxygen, supplies).
- Clinicas de La Paz oxygen reserves depleted; hospitals rationing critical supplies.
- 1,200+ truck drivers stranded 12+ days; blockades now in fourth week.
- Paz declared country "at breaking point" (May 27). Source: https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20260527-bolivia-at-breaking-point-president-warns-protesters

**Carry-forward context:**
- May 13 land-mortgage-law annulment failed to defuse crisis.
- Evo Morales-led 190km march reached La Paz May 19; blockades spread to El Alto May 24.
- Sovereign bonds down 10 consecutive sessions; economy losing ~$50M/day.

## Methodology Adjudication — Two-Phase Escalation

The May 26 first-baseline (30.9) was applied under HUMANITARIAN-BLOCKADE-FIRST-BASELINE-PROTOCOL with a reassess-within-30-days-of-crisis-resolution trigger. The crisis has not resolved; the state has escalated its response. Two distinct new events warrant interim re-assessment:

1. **STATE-DEPLOYING-MILITARY-FOR-PROTEST-SUPPRESSION-AMID-HUMANITARIAN-EMERGENCY** is a benchmark-novel sub-category combining (a) authorized military deployment against domestic civilian protest, (b) concurrent unaddressed humanitarian emergency (hospital oxygen depletion, ≥4 deaths from blocked medical access). This is methodologically distinct from the first-baseline blockade event itself.

2. **Confirmed-deaths-attributable-to-state-failure** is a new factual update: the first-baseline absorbed the *risk* of deaths from blocked emergency vehicles; the four confirmed deaths plus depleted hospital oxygen confirm the worst-case predicted in that assessment.

**Apply-threshold test:** Combined, these constitute a discrete, dated, quantified deterioration on top of an already-active humanitarian arc. The state escalation (military authorization) is the cleaner scoring trigger because it is a deliberate institutional decision; the deaths are a confirmation of predicted harm. Apply -2.5 composite (within Tier B sub-apply-threshold range, justified by combined dimension specificity).

## Dimension-Level Reasoning

- **ACT -2.5 (25.0→22.5):** Action dimension is the cleanest hit. Government has authorized military force as response to protest while failing to negotiate corridor for medical supplies. Action calibration favors coercion over need-relief.
- **ACC -5.0 (25.0→20.0):** Accountability dimension hit twice: (a) confirmed deaths previously framed as risk now realized without state acknowledgment of causation; (b) military-deployment law lifts state-of-emergency procedural constraints intended to check executive power. Drops to Developing-floor.
- **EQU -2.5 (25.0→22.5):** Equity dimension hit: oxygen rationing concentrates harm on lowest-acuity-tolerance patients (chronically ill, elderly). Blockade impact is geographically concentrated on La Paz/El Alto residents.
- **EMP -2.5 (37.5→35.0):** "At breaking point" rhetoric reads as adversarial framing of citizens rather than empathic acknowledgment of suffering on multiple sides. Partial half-magnitude hit.
- **AWR -2.5 (37.5→35.0):** Awareness dimension hit modestly: scale of suffering is publicly named ("breaking point") but state response indicates the framing is rhetorical rather than action-orienting.
- **BND -5.0 (37.5→32.5):** Boundaries dimension hit by simplified state-of-emergency declaration procedure (institutional self-restraint reduced). Military-deployment authorization expands coercive scope.
- **SYS -2.5 (37.5→35.0):** Systemic dimension: military-deployment law addresses symptom (protest visibility) rather than root cause (land-rights/economic-policy dispute). Earlier annulment of the May 13 land-mortgage law was a positive root-cause response; new law inverts direction.
- **INT 0.0 (25.0→25.0):** Hold. Integrity dimension already at Developing-floor 25.0 from first-baseline.
