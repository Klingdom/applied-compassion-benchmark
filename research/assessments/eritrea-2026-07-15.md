## Own-Conduct Evidence Base (the score driver)

- **Ongoing crimes against humanity.** The 2016 UN Commission of Inquiry found the government and ruling party perpetrated enslavement, imprisonment, enforced disappearance, torture, persecution and murder as part of a campaign to control the civilian population since 1991. For April 2025-April 2026, the UN Special Rapporteur determined there remain **reasonable grounds to believe crimes against humanity continue**. Source: https://www.ungeneva.org/en/news-media/press-release/2016/06/un-inquiry-finds-crimes-against-humanity-eritrea ; https://www.ohchr.org/en/countries/eritrea ; https://www.hrw.org/news/2025/07/04/un-rights-council-rejects-bad-faith-bid-to-end-eritrea-scrutiny
- **Indefinite national service as enslavement.** Compulsory, open-ended national service (ages 18-40) functions as a state policy of enslavement enforced through coercion, collective punishment and violence — an active, ongoing own-conduct harm affecting a large share of the adult population.
- **Total suppression of independent voice.** In the 2026 RSF World Press Freedom Index, Eritrea ranked **180 of 180 — last for the third consecutive year**. No independent domestic media are permitted. Journalist Dawit Isaak has been jailed without trial for 25 years. Source: https://rsf.org/en/country/eritrea
- **No electoral or constitutional accountability.** No national elections have been held since independence in 1993; the ratified constitution has never been implemented; the national assembly has not convened in over two decades.
