# Assessment: Nigeria — Worst Hunger in a Decade (Double-Count / Non-State Screen)

**Composite:** 18.0/100 (CONFIRM) | **Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
The WFP's July 2 report records 36.2 million people food insecure in Nigeria — the highest ever measured — with 5.5 million of 6.2 million in need going without lifesaving food aid, and 10,000+ in Borno at catastrophe level. This is a real deterioration, but it continues the same crisis that already drove Nigeria's June crossing to 18.0. The new drivers are increased insurgent (JNIM/ISWAP) attacks (non-state conduct) and a WFP funding shortfall (not government obstruction). A formal IPC Phase 5 famine declaration in Borno would be a new scoring event; it has not been made.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1))
Live baseline **18.0**, rank **150**, Critical. Set/priced on the June 19 food-insecurity crossing; confirmed 2026-07-03. Not stale.

## §3e-bis screening
- **(4) Double-count:** the record 36.2M / 15,000 Phase 5 figures continue the already-priced arc; they reinforce but do not clear a fresh -5 dock.
- **(3) Non-state attribution:** insurgent attacks driving displacement/hunger are non-state actors, not Nigerian-state own conduct.
- **Funding gap ≠ obstruction:** the WFP shortfall reflects donor funding, not deliberate government relief-blocking.
- **Forward trigger vs realized harm:** a formal Phase 5 famine declaration or documented state aid obstruction remains a forward trigger.

## Disposition
**CONFIRM at 18.0. Delta 0.0.** Confidence: high.
- **Forward trigger (downgrade):** formal IPC Phase 5 famine declaration in Borno, or documented deliberate aid obstruction.
