## What this assessment replaces

Missouri's published score of 25.0 was a placeholder. It was loaded in bulk with no evidence behind it and no assessment date. Four other states — Idaho, Indiana, North Dakota and South Dakota — carry the exact same number and the exact same eight dimension values. One invented figure was copied across five states. It says nothing about Missouri.

This assessment is Missouri's first score built from evidence. The move from 25.0 to 27.5 is a data repair. It is not a sign that Missouri got better or worse.

### Score Difference Analysis

The published composite of 25.0 is not a Missouri finding. It is one number copied across Idaho, Indiana, Missouri, North Dakota and South Dakota, with `last_assessed: null`. The close final agreement between 25.0 and 27.5 is a coincidence produced by offsetting errors, not a validation.

The two largest gaps run in opposite directions. **Equity** was understated by 17.5 points because the placeholder gave Missouri no credit for Medicaid expansion, which is now the state's single largest compassion asset: 355,465 low-income adults in the expansion group as of August 2025, inside a program covering more than 1.2 million people. **Boundaries** was overstated by 17.5 points. The placeholder's 2.5 implied above-average practice. The record shows a federal court finding that Missouri illegally denied benefits, rejected 48.22% of food-benefit applications on a procedural technicality, and failed to accommodate disabled applicants under the Americans with Disabilities Act.

The published rank of #11 should be disregarded regardless. The index holds only 21 of 51 jurisdictions.

### Recommendation

Replace the placeholder with the evidence-based dimensions. The composite moves from 25.0 to 27.5, and the band stays Developing. Record the change as a data-defect correction, not as institutional improvement or decline.

## Key Findings

- **Missouri overrode its own voters twice in five years, and that is the defining pattern in this assessment.** Voters approved Medicaid expansion in 2020; the legislature refused to fund it and the Missouri Supreme Court had to order it in July 2021. Voters approved paid sick leave in November 2024 with nearly 1.7 million yes votes; the legislature repealed it in May 2025 and the repeal took effect on 2025-08-28. One event is an incident. Two is a pattern, and it drives Integrity to 25 of 100.

- **Getting help in Missouri is the hardest part of getting help.** A federal court found the state rejected 48.22% of food-benefit applications only because applicants could not complete a phone interview, with average waits of 37 minutes and 50,488 dropped calls. Separately, Missouri had the worst Medicaid application delays in the United States — 72% past the federal deadline in February 2024, with a median wait of 77 days. Access failure, not policy design, is Missouri's largest source of avoidable suffering.

- **Missouri contests innocence rather than repairing it.** The state executed Marcellus Williams on 2024-09-24 despite objections from both the prosecuting attorney's office and the victim's family. The attorney general's office also fought the exonerations of Sandra Hemme, Christopher Dunn, Lamar Johnson and Kevin Strickland, and in one case a prison ignored a court order to release a man. This produces the lowest scores in the assessment: Validation 1 of 5, Harm Acknowledgment 1 of 5, Reparative Action 1 of 5.

- **The strongest real asset is health data and the maternal mortality review.** Missouri publishes disaggregated health data through MICA and MOPHIMS, runs an annual Pregnancy Associated Mortality Review that names a 2.5-times racial gap in pregnancy-related deaths, and extended postpartum Medicaid coverage to one year as a result. This is a working detect-investigate-correct loop, and it is why Awareness and Equity score above the state's own average.

- **Missouri's independent auditor keeps finding problems the state does not fix.** The 2025 federal-funds audit contained 17 findings, 13 of them repeats from earlier audits spanning one to five years. The auditor said agencies "have yet to put our previous recommendations into place." Missouri detects better than it corrects.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Awareness (35), Action (35) and Systemic Thinking (35)** are the top three, all in the Developing band. Awareness rests on genuine public health data infrastructure and an independently elected auditor who still publishes damaging findings. Action rests on a fast $100 million tornado response in June 2025 and a $216 million rural health award. Systemic Thinking rests on a 20-center behavioral health crisis network that moves mental health calls away from police and jails.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Empathy (20), Boundaries (20) and Accountability (20)** all sit in the Critical band. All three are driven by the same two clusters: the benefit-access failures a federal court ruled illegal, and the state's posture toward people claiming wrongful conviction. Boundaries in particular carries two scores of 1 out of 5 — Scope Clarity and Refusal Ethics — because people were denied without explanation and without an alternative.

## Evidence Gaps

- **Cultural empathy (E5) and consent orientation (B5) are low-confidence.** No evidence was found of Missouri co-designing core practices with non-dominant cultural communities, and institutional consent practice outside the reproductive health context is not documented publicly. Both were scored at the lower anchor as the methodology requires.
- **No independent, statewide assessment of frontline staff sustainability exists.** Turnover figures come from agency reporting rather than external audit, so B1 was capped at 3.
- **Prison conditions evidence is largely journalistic rather than official**, precisely because Missouri does not require its corrections department to track and report deaths. That absence itself scored against the state under A1 and A4.

## Recommended Next Steps

Missouri lands in the **Developing** band at 27.5 of 100. The fastest available gains are administrative, not political: meeting the federal court's benefit-processing orders would lift Boundaries and Equity together, since four separate subdimension scores rest on the same call-center and application-processing evidence.
