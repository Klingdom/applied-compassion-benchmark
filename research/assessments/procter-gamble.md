## Context for this reassessment

P&G was flagged by the scanner (priority score 80) following its June 5, 2026 announcement of 7,000 job cuts — roughly 15% of its non-manufacturing workforce and ~6% of its global workforce — over two years. Coverage emphasized that the cuts come "despite increasing profits," with the restructuring framed as tariff-driven ($600M pre-tax headwind) and accompanied by $1B–$1.6B in restructuring charges. This event primarily affects Integrity (internal consistency between external compassion messaging and treatment of staff) and Boundaries (workforce sustainability), and modestly Equity. It does not materially change the dimensions tied to product safety, consumer awareness, supply-chain systemic work, or accountability infrastructure, which retain their published levels.

### Score Difference Analysis

The published composite of 86.1 reconstructs exactly under the canonical formula (base 78.1 + integration premium 8.0), so there is no math-hygiene issue. The change is substantive, driven by the June 5, 2026 restructuring. Cutting 7,000 jobs while the company is profitable and projecting profits — with the stated driver being tariff cost protection rather than financial distress — is direct evidence against B1 (Self-Sustainability: workforce as adjustable cost) and I1/I3 (Consistency Under Pressure and Internal Consistency). I conservatively moved only BND and INT from 4.0 to 3.5 and left the seven other dimensions unchanged, since this single event does not bear on product safety, consumer awareness, or systemic sustainability work.

### Recommendation

Downgrade composite from 86.1 (Exemplary) to 79.0 (Established). The downgrade reflects a single but material event; if P&G manages the reduction with strong transition support, follow-through could restore the prior level. Confidence: medium.
