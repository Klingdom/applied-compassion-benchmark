## Key Findings

- **Illinois builds compassion into permanent law more than most states, and that is what carries its score.** Systemic Thinking is its only dimension at 4.0 out of 5. The Climate and Equitable Jobs Act sends more than $80 million a year to clean-energy jobs in Black and Brown communities. The R3 program permanently dedicates 25% of cannabis tax revenue to communities harmed by drug enforcement — $330 million and counting to over 300 organizations.

- **The state cleared over 780,000 cannabis records and left only 94 people in prison for cannabis offenses.** This is one of the clearest examples in the benchmark of a government dismantling a harm it created rather than managing its symptoms. It is paired with the first state-commissioned account of Illinois's own racial harms, published February 27, 2026.

- **Illinois cut health coverage for 30,000 immigrants in the same year it went to court to protect immigrants.** The Health Benefits for Immigrant Adults program closed on July 1, 2025 for budget reasons. Meanwhile the Attorney General filed more than 50 suits against federal action and the state sued over immigration raids in Chicago. That contradiction is why Integrity scores 3.2, not higher.

- **The state's oldest promises to its most vulnerable residents remain unkept.** The B.H. child welfare consent decree has run for more than three decades. The Lippert prison health care decree has run seven years without compliance, with 16 full-time doctors covering 29 facilities holding 30,000 people. Accountability scores 3.2 largely because of this.

- **Corruption is the single heaviest drag on Illinois's score.** Former House Speaker Michael Madigan was sentenced to 7.5 years in prison on June 13, 2025. More than 2,310 Illinois public officials were convicted of corruption between 1983 and 2023. The legislature has passed no major ethics bill in over four years. Values Alignment scores 2 of 5 — the lowest subdimension in this assessment alongside Refusal Ethics.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Systemic Thinking (4.0/5, 75/100)** — recurring statutory funding aimed at root causes, 2050-horizon planning with tracked milestones, and coalition leadership across 17-18 state alliances.
- **Equity (3.8/5, 70/100)** — a prioritization formula that sends new school money to the least adequately funded districts first, large-scale barrier removal through expungement and bail reform, and a state-commissioned historical harms accounting.
- **Awareness (3.6/5, 65/100)** — standing disaggregated data systems in health, overdose and housing, plus a commission that converted resident testimony into official action.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Boundaries (2.8/5, 45/100)** — the lowest dimension. When Illinois cannot serve someone it offers a queue rather than an alternative: 1,108 of 1,342 people selected from the disability waiting list were still unserved eight months later, and shelter capacity falls thousands of beds short of documented need.
- **Action, Accountability and Integrity (all 3.2/5, 55/100)** — delivery, repair and consistency all lag the policy architecture. Decades-old consent decrees, an entrenched corruption record, and a stalled ethics agenda hold all three at the middle of the scale.

## Evidence Gaps

- **Affective Resonance (E1) and Consent Orientation (B5) are scored at low confidence.** No systematic study of how Illinois residents experience state services was found. Both are scored at 3 from structural evidence alone.
- **No independent audit of disparity corrections.** Illinois publishes disparity data extensively but no third-party verification that its corrective programs are narrowing gaps was located. This capped EQ3 at 4.
- **No documented program discontinuation on efficacy grounds.** A targeted search found no case of Illinois ending a program because evaluation showed it failed. This capped AC3 at 3.
- **No published state-employee culture assessment.** The Office of the Executive Inspector General publishes a complaint process but no aggregate workplace-culture findings were located, limiting I3 to structural evidence.
- **Search budget:** 28 web searches were used against a target of approximately 30. No search cap or error degradation occurred.

## Recommended Next Steps

Illinois lands in the **Functional** band at 60.0, one tenth of a point below Established. The gap is structural, not marginal: seven of eight dimensions sit below 4.0, so the integration premium — the bonus for being strong across all eight areas — is zero. Illinois would gain up to 10 points at once if it lifted its weakest areas rather than its strongest.

- **Functional/Established**: Consider [Advisory Support](/advisory) to translate benchmark insights into strategic action. The highest-leverage moves are (1) closing out the B.H. and Lippert consent decrees, which alone would move Accountability and Action, and (2) passing the ethics reforms that would lift Values Alignment off 2 of 5.
