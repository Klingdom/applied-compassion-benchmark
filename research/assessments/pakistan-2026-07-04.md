# Assessment: Pakistan — June 28-29 Cross-Border Strikes (Disputed-Attribution / Double-Count Screen)

**Composite:** 17.2/100 (CONFIRM) | **Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
Pakistan launched ground operations and airstrikes on June 28-29 against Pakistani Taliban (TTP) targets inside Afghanistan (Paktia, Paktika, Kunar). The Afghan Taliban reported 36 civilians killed and 163 injured; Pakistan disputes those figures. Pakistan's security forces reported killing 29 fighters. This is Pakistan's own conduct, but three screens apply: the civilian toll is disputed and single-sourced (Taliban claim vs Pakistani denial); the harm falls on Afghan civilians in Afghan territory (relevant primarily to Afghanistan's own protection duty); and the strikes target the TTP, a non-state group. No independently verified within-window mass-casualty event attributable to deliberate Pakistani targeting is established.
