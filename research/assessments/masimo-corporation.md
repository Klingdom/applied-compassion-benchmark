# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Masimo Corporation (Forensic Re-Assessment)

**Entity type:** Company
**Sector/Domain:** Medical Devices / Pulse oximetry
**Assessment date:** 2026-04-23 (forensic first-ever baseline; Gen-2 anchor case)
**Composite score:** 48.4/100
**Band:** Functional

**Trigger:** Second forensic anchor for F500 Gen-2 audit. Published composite 81.4 exemplary is near-uniform 4×7 + 3.5×1 profile shared with 7 other F500 entities at ranks 10-17. Real record includes multiple DOJ/SEC actions and significant governance disputes.

**Material evidence:**
- **DOJ subpoena (Feb 2024)** — documents on Rad-G/Rad-97 pulse oximeters, complaints, and Rad-G recall decision ([MedTech Dive](https://www.medtechdive.com/news/masimo-dual-doj-sec-probes/718870/))
- **DOJ civil investigative demand (March 2024)** — customer returns of Rad-G/Rad-97 devices
- **SEC subpoena (March 2024)** — potential accounting irregularities and internal control deficiencies
- **Whistleblower lawsuit involving 16 former employees** — referenced in SEC 14A filings re: Board oversight failure ([SEC 14A](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/937556/000110465924100366/tm2424200d1_dfan14a.htm))
- **CEO Kiani 75% stock-pledge as personal-loan collateral** — undisclosed to Board material risk
- **Politan proxy contest (2024)** — ultimately resolved with Politan obtaining disclosures correcting false statements; Kiani departed
- **April 27, 2025 cyber incident** — unauthorized activity in on-premise network impacted manufacturing and order fulfillment ([Strauss Borrelli](https://straussborrelli.com/2025/05/12/masimo-corporation-data-breach-investigation/))
- **Shareholder litigation June 2025** — Johnson Fistel + Robbins LLP investigations into fiduciary duty breaches ([GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/06/27/3106501/0/en/MASIMO-CORPORATION-INVESTIGATION-ALERT-Johnson-Fistel-PLLP-Investigates-Potential-Breach-of-Fiduciary-Duties-by-Masimo-Officers-and-Directors.html))
- **March 2026 Portnoy Law Firm investor investigation** ([GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/03/11/3253810/0/en/Masimo-Corporation-Investigated-by-the-Portnoy-Law-Firm.html))

**Offsetting positive evidence:**
- Core pulse-oximetry technology has genuine patient-safety value
- SET technology reduces neonatal retinopathy; documented clinical outcomes
- Post-Kiani board governance reforms
- Cooperation with regulators

**Scoring judgment:** Masimo's core medical technology genuinely saves lives. That supports moderate ACT/SYS scoring. But the dual-DOJ-investigation + SEC-subpoena + 16-employee whistleblower + CEO undisclosed-pledge cluster is a clear ACC anchor 2 ("acknowledged only after external establishment") and I2 anchor 2 ("some genuine practice, primarily reputation-motivated"). Ownership-governance chaos (Kiani v. Politan proxy war, ultimate Kiani departure) affects I5 resilience. **Composite 48.4 vs. published 81.4 = -33.0 delta; band shift: exemplary → functional.**
