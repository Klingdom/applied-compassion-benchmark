## Trigger for Reassessment

On April 14, 2026, Ford filed an NHTSA-triggered safety recall affecting 1,392,935 F-150 trucks (2015–2017 model years with 6R80 transmission). Root cause: degraded electrical connections in the transmission lead frame cause the PCM to receive incorrect signals from the Transmission Range Sensor, triggering unintended downshifts — in the worst cases, from 6th gear to 2nd gear at highway speeds. Two injuries and one accident confirmed. NHTSA flagged the issue in October 2024; Ford's internal review opened immediately but the formal recall was only filed April 14, 2026 — roughly 18 months after regulator flag. Owner notification due July 13–17, 2026.
