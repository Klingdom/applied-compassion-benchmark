## Why it matters (plain language)
In South Sudan, 7.8 million people — about 56% of the population — face acute food insecurity, 2.2 million children are acutely malnourished, and four counties are at famine risk, while the WFP reaches roughly 1.5 million fewer people than planned. A new factor surfaced June 30: the DRC Ebola outbreak has spread to Haut-Uele province bordering South Sudan, creating a cross-border disease risk. This continues the already-priced catastrophe. South Sudan is already at the 0.0 floor — no lower score exists.
