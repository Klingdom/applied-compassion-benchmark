# Assessment: China — Ethnic Unity and Progress Law In Force (Codified-Policy Screen)

**Composite:** 19.5/100 (CONFIRM — research finds sub-threshold downward pressure to ~18.0)
**Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
On July 1, 2026, China's Ethnic Unity and Progress Law took effect. It turns decades of forced-assimilation practice into enforceable national law. It reaches into family, religion, education, language, and media, and brings Buddhism and Islam under tighter state control. Article 63 says people and groups outside China can face legal liability for acts that "undermine ethnic unity" — a legal cover for reaching diaspora Uyghurs and Tibetans abroad. In June 2026, Hong Kong's last active pro-democracy party, the League of Social Democrats, disbanded under "tremendous political pressure." These are China's own actions and push its already-Critical score lower.

## §3e-bis screening
- **Realized vs codification (double-count, §3e-bis(4)):** Both Amnesty International and HRW characterise the new law as **codifying existing practice** — "existing practices now embedded in national law rather than primarily driven by policy campaigns." The underlying suffering (cultural erasure, boarding-school separation, religious control, transnational harassment) is already documented and already priced into the 19.5. The law's enactment is largely an enabling instrument; no NEW enforcement action (prosecutions, renditions, family detentions) under the law is yet documented as of day one.
- **Directionality:** surfaced on negative own-conduct evidence — an upgrade is blocked. The codification is genuine negative own-conduct that directionally lowers EQU (codified ethnic-discrimination framework) and EMP (codified religious/cultural suppression). Research dock: EQU 1.5 → 1.25, EMP 2.0 → 1.75.
- **Marginal-delta / threshold:** the defensible dock reconstructs to composite **~18.0** — a **-1.5** move, **below the 5-point substantive-change threshold**. Precedent: a single quarter-step dock on China moved the composite ~0.8 points (2026-05-18). Two quarter-step docks (~1.5 points) do not clear the threshold.
- **Not pre-adjudication:** the law is in force (realized), not a forward legal risk. It is correctly treated as own-conduct — but its marginal realized-harm delta over already-priced practice is sub-threshold.
