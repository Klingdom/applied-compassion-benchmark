## Key Findings

- Why it matters: On July 15, 2026 the FDA classified Baxter’s recall of 10,540 Hillrom VOLARA respiratory-circuit kits as Class I — its most serious tier — because a leak can cut patients’ oxygen and, per the FDA, lead to injuries “including brain injury, heart attack, respiratory failure and death.”
- This is a pattern, not a one-off: the FDA labeled a prior VOLARA recall Class I in May 2022, and Baxter’s Novum IQ infusion pumps drew litigation tied to 79 injuries and 2 deaths. That recurrence is what pulls Accountability below the rest of the profile.
- Baxter also runs genuine programs — ~$31.5 million in community and disaster investment in 2024, product donations reaching 100+ countries, and SASB/TCFD-indexed reporting — which keep most dimensions solidly mid-tier.

## Strongest Dimensions

- Action, Equity and Boundaries — resourced access-to-healthcare and disaster programs with structured public disclosure.

## Weakest Dimensions

- Accountability — a recurring pattern of Class I device recalls and injury/death-linked litigation with reactive rather than pre-emptive correction.

## Evidence Gaps

- Recall databases confirm the events but not internal root-cause remediation quality; the two reported Novum IQ deaths are from litigation filings, not adjudicated findings.
