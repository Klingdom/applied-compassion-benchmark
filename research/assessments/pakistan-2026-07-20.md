Accountability (raw 1.2) and Equity (raw 1.4) are the lowest dimensions, matching the published profile. ## Key Findings

- Pakistan is fighting an insurgency and the civilian cost cannot be verified. Coordinated attacks killed 42 people in early July, and the state's counter-operation reports 129 militants killed — a figure sourced only to the military.
- The casualty numbers are contested by the people they describe. Baloch groups allege that extrajudicial killings and the bodies of disappeared civilians are being presented as militant deaths.
- Enforced disappearances continued during the operation. Three young Baloch men, including a minor and a student, were reportedly taken during overnight raids in Kech and Gwadar in early July.
- The score does not move. At 15.6 against a published 17.2, and following a confirmation only two days earlier, the evidence supports holding Pakistan at 17.2 in the Critical band.
- The downward watch flag should stay open. Casualty reporting is now unverified into a fifth week, and if independent verification later contradicts the official toll, that would be new evidence warranting reassessment.

## Strongest Dimensions

Action (raw 2) at 2.0 is nominally the highest dimension. Pakistan mobilised a large, coordinated multi-agency response quickly after the July attacks. That is genuine institutional capacity, though it is capacity directed at security rather than at the civilian population's suffering, which is why it does not lift the composite.

## Weakest Dimensions

Accountability (raw 1.2) and Equity (raw 1.4). Harm Acknowledgment, Correction Willingness, Systemic Learning and Reparative Action all score 1 of 5. Universality also scores 1: Baloch civilians are effectively outside the protection the state extends to others, and disappearances continue without acknowledgment or investigation.

## Evidence Gaps

The single largest gap is that every casualty figure in this assessment originates from Pakistani security sources and has not been independently verified. No UN body, treaty body or international NGO has published a verified toll for Operation Shaban. Counter-claims come from Baloch advocacy organisations, which are tier-1 sources with an interest in the outcome. This assessment therefore weights BOTH the official toll and the counter-claims cautiously and scores the dimensions they touch conservatively rather than at the floor. HRCP's most recent reporting and current Afghan refugee deportation figures were not obtainable in this cycle.
