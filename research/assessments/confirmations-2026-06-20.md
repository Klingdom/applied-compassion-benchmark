## Methodology framing for this batch

**University Index entities** were desk-baselined at index assembly (research/university-index/scores-batch-*.json). Each university assessed tonight is treated as a REFINEMENT of its published composite, not a first baseline. The June 2026 news cluster for these entities is overwhelmingly:

1. **Federal action AGAINST the university** (DOJ funding suits, grant freezes, EEOC settlements, admissions compliance reviews). Under the methodology, harm INFLICTED ON an entity by an external actor is attributed to the perpetrator (here, the federal government), not scored as a compassion failure of the university-as-victim — the same victim/perpetrator attribution applied to Ukraine on 2026-06-19.
2. **Labor organizing wins/settlements** (NYU grad-worker contract, USC RTPC faculty unionization, Columbia UAW). A union election win is not in itself a compassion failure; the relevant signal (worker voice/internal consistency, I3) was already priced into each baseline.
3. **Protest discipline and Title IX / OCR history** (Columbia, UCLA, USC-Tyndall, Yale-leave practices) — these conduct-toward-students harms are ADJUDICATED or well-documented and were already explicitly cited in the assembly rationales and reflected in the published EMP/ACC/INT scores.

The net result: no university assessed tonight crosses the 5-point threshold. The published composites hold. The one genuinely new conduct signal — continued Harvard layoffs/salary-freeze (internal-consistency, I3) — is consistent with Harvard's already-low published INT (2.5) and ACC (2.75) and does not move the composite.
