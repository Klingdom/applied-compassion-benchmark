# Assessment: Venezuela — Earthquake Day 9 (Exogenous-Disaster Screen)

**Composite:** 18.0/100 (CONFIRM) | **Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
The June 24 twin earthquakes are a natural disaster — an outside shock, not a government harm event. So the score reads only the government's RESPONSE, not the death toll itself. On Day 9 the official toll reached 2,595, but a forensic pathologist told CNN the real number is "not even a third" of that, with morgues processing about 400 bodies a day and roughly 50,000 people still unaccounted for. That undercount concern is real, but it is a single-source estimate amid chaos, and Venezuela is accepting large-scale foreign aid (4,000+ international rescuers, 20+ countries). Aid acceptance blocks a downgrade; documented relief-failure blocks an upgrade.
