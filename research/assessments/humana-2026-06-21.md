## Assessment basis

Humana was flagged on the band-boundary watch (published 40.6, only 0.6 above the Functional/Developing line) with two material adverse developments inside the lookback window:

1. **DOJ False Claims Act complaint (Tier 5 — government):** The United States filed a False Claims Act complaint against Aetna, Elevance, and Humana, and three brokers (eHealth, GoHealth, SelectQuote), alleging hundreds of millions in illegal kickbacks for Medicare Advantage enrollments (2016–at least 2021). Critically, the complaint alleges **Aetna and Humana each conspired with brokers to discriminate against Medicare beneficiaries with disabilities** whom they perceived as less profitable — threatening to withhold kickbacks to pressure brokers to enroll fewer disabled beneficiaries. This is a direct, government-pleaded allegation of deliberate equity harm to the most vulnerable population Humana serves.

2. **Barrows v. Humana (Tier 4 — active litigation):** Class action over the **nH Predict AI algorithm** used to deny post-acute care to elderly Medicare Advantage members. The court declined to dismiss, and the case alleges Humana knowingly used a model it understood to be highly inaccurate to override live physicians' determinations of medically necessary care.

Note on adjudication discipline: the DOJ complaint is a civil filing (pleaded, not yet adjudicated) and Barrows is unresolved. Under pre-adjudication discipline these allegations do not by themselves drive a score to the floor. However, the DOJ complaint is a sovereign enforcement pleading from a Tier-5 source, and the conduct pattern (AI-driven denial + disability discrimination) is corroborated across two independent federal/judicial channels and is consistent with the broader documented Medicare Advantage denial-and-coding pattern in the sector (CVS/Aetna's $117.7M settlement this cycle). Together these justify a measured downward revision of EQU, EMP, ACT, and ACC — not a floor designation.

### Recommendation
Downgrade to 35.2 (Developing). The two largest moves (EQU, ACC) rest on a Tier-5 government FCA pleading and an active class action that survived a dismissal motion. Confidence: medium (allegations are pleaded/unresolved, but the conduct pattern is corroborated across independent federal and judicial channels and is sector-consistent). Set watch flag for DOJ FCA resolution and Barrows ruling.

## Key Findings
- DOJ FCA complaint alleges Humana conspired to discriminate against disabled Medicare beneficiaries — a direct equity harm to its most vulnerable members.
- nH Predict AI denial litigation survived dismissal; alleged knowing use of an inaccurate model to override physicians.
- Humana is contesting both actions; no acknowledgment or reparative action observed.
