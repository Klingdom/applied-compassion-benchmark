## Assessment Context

Egypt was surfaced by the 2026-07-20 scan on Human Rights Watch reporting of 2026-07-07 that Egyptian authorities are arbitrarily arresting, detaining and deporting refugees and asylum seekers — mostly Sudanese and South Sudanese — whose residency permits lapsed because of the government's own administrative backlogs. This is Egypt's first individual assessment. The assessment sets that finding against Egypt's large national social protection system, which the published placeholder score does not appear to have considered.

The assessed composite of 21.9 is 7.8 points ABOVE the published 14.1. The gap is concentrated in Action (published 1.5 raw, assessed 2.8) and Systemic Thinking (published 1.5, assessed 2.4). The placeholder implied a state with essentially no functioning system for reaching people in need. That is not accurate: Takaful and Karama reaches 4.66 million households, is independently financed and evaluated by the World Bank, and deliberately targets poor households with children, the elderly, people with disabilities and orphans. Accountability (assessed 1.2) and Integrity (assessed 1.4) sit at or below the placeholder, driven by the refugee deportations and by Egypt's omission of nonrefoulement from its own 2024 Asylum Law.

## Key Findings

- Egypt is jailing refugees for paperwork Egypt failed to issue. Human Rights Watch found people are "being jailed and deported for lacking documents the government has failed to provide," including people holding valid UNHCR cards.
- Egypt wrote a refugee law that leaves out the core protection. Although it is party to the 1951 Refugee Convention, its 2024 Asylum Law does not include the principle of nonrefoulement — the rule against returning people to danger.
- Egypt also runs one of the region's largest cash transfer systems. Takaful and Karama reached 4.66 million households as of December 2025, targeting poor families with children, the elderly, people with disabilities and orphans.
- Egypt hosts 1.1 million registered refugees while deporting thousands of them. Both facts are true at once, and the assessment scores both rather than choosing the more convenient one.
- The published score of 14.1 out of 100 looks too low, but tonight is not the night to raise it. Egypt was flagged for deporting refugees; raising a score on negative evidence would be a false positive, so this goes to calibration review instead.

## Strongest Dimensions

Action (raw 2.8) and Systemic Thinking (raw 2.4). Proportionality, Efficacy, Resource Mobilization and Follow-Through all score 3 of 5, supported by a decade of Takaful and Karama operation, World Bank results evaluations, and integration with the Hayah Karima rural development initiative. Autonomy Preservation also reaches 3 of 5 through the programme's economic empowerment and microcredit components.

## Weakest Dimensions

Accountability (raw 1.2) and Integrity (raw 1.4). Harm Acknowledgment, Correction Willingness, Systemic Learning and Reparative Action all score 1 of 5, as do Consistency Under Pressure and Values Alignment. Human Rights Watch received no response to detailed questions sent to the Egyptian Foreign Ministry on 2026-06-23, which is the behavioural marker of an institution that does not answer for harm.

## Evidence Gaps

Egypt's treatment of political prisoners and domestic civil society — a very large component of any full compassion assessment of the state — was not examined in this cycle and is a material omission that likely biases this assessment upward. The Takaful and Karama beneficiary figures come from government and World Bank sources; the Arab Reform Initiative analysis suggesting the programme is "caught between" competing objectives was located but not read in full, so targeting accuracy and exclusion errors are unverified. No independent audit of deportation numbers exists; HRW reports "thousands" without a verified count. Internal Consistency (I3) is scored 2 on absence of evidence about Egypt's own public-sector workforce.

## Calibration Note

DO NOT APPLY AS A ONE-OFF UPGRADE. Egypt has never been individually assessed; the published 14.1 is a bulk-import placeholder. This first 40-subdimension review lands at 21.9 — above the placeholder — because the placeholder never accounted for Takaful and Karama, a targeted cash transfer system reaching 4.66 million households with sustained World Bank financing and published results evaluations. That is a large, real, measurable compassion instrument and Action and Systemic Thinking score accordingly. However, the anti-false-positive screen governs. Egypt was surfaced on NEGATIVE within-window evidence: Human Rights Watch's 2026-07-07 finding that thousands of refugees and asylum seekers have been arrested or deported in 2026. There is no new within-window evidence of positive countervailing conduct. Under screening rule 3, negative-trigger evidence must not produce an upgrade, and under rule 5, a belief that a placeholder is mis-calibrated must be routed to calibration review rather than encoded as a one-off reset. Recommendation is flag-for-review. The correct remedy is a calibration review of never-assessed countries in the Critical band, several of which likely have unaccounted social-protection infrastructure, not an isolated upward move on Egypt while it is actively deporting refugees in breach of the Refugee Convention.
