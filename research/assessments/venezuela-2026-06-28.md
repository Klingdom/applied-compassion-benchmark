# Special Event Assessment: Venezuela — Earthquake Government Response

**Composite:** 18.0/100 (CONFIRM — sub-threshold in both directions)
**Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
Two huge earthquakes hit Venezuela on June 24, 2026 — magnitude 7.2 and 7.5, the strongest since 1900. More than 1,430 people are dead, over 3,200 are hurt, and families have reported about 68,900 people missing. About 60,000 buildings were destroyed. The earthquake itself is a natural disaster, not the government's doing, so it does not move the score by itself. What we score is how the government responded. That record is mixed: the interim government accepted broad international help, but its own relief effort has been slow, disorganized, and not transparent. On balance the new evidence is not strong enough to move the score in either direction.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1) — verified, not assumed)
- Published composite is **18.0**, set **2026-04-19** as a regime-change reset (+13.6 from the old Maduro-era 4.4), crediting the interim Rodriguez government's amnesty law, 659+ political-prisoner releases, and the ordered closure of El Helicoide. (research/change-proposals/history/venezuela.json; APPLIED_CHANGES.md.)
- Reconfirmed multiple times since (Jun 6: "priced-chronic-repression + accountability-documentation-of-past-harm"; Jun 22: assessed 18.4 vs 18.0, sub-threshold). Last assessed **2026-06-22, pre-earthquake.**
- The 18.0 already prices weak institutional capacity, poor accountability, and a fragile transition with 400+ political prisoners still held. The "stale 4.4" figure in some scan caches is wrong; the authoritative baseline is 18.0.

## The scorable question: government conduct, not the disaster
A natural disaster is exogenous (cf. the Jun 6 Uganda "natural-disaster-overlay-non-double-count" precedent). Only the government's OWN response conduct — relief, transparency, acceptance/blocking of aid, treatment of victims — is scorable.

### Positive own-conduct (cuts against a downgrade)
- **Aid accepted broadly, not blocked.** 17 flights carrying 1,600+ rescuers landed by Jun 27; assistance came from the US, Cuba, Mexico, Colombia, Brazil, El Salvador, France, and UN agencies (WFP, OCHA). This is the decisive differentiator from floor-cluster aid-blockers (e.g., Yemen/Houthis forcing out WFP). (Al Jazeera, PBS, State Dept — Tier 2-5.)
- **State of emergency declared; $200M reconstruction fund created** for hospitals, housing, and infrastructure. (Al Jazeera / NBC.)
- Official casualty figures published, including the 68,900 family-reported-missing figure (transparency on the headline numbers).

### Negative own-conduct (cuts toward a downgrade)
- **Slow, disjointed relief.** "Tensions peaked over what many Venezuelans viewed as an inadequate response"; government "appeared underprepared." Residents dug through rubble by hand; "at 8 p.m. there were people alive down there, and they haven't bothered to rescue them." (PBS, Jun 27.)
- **Aid-delivery opacity.** Medical staff: "We don't know what arrived to Venezuela. We don't know where are those things that are coming." (NPR/NBC.)
- **Heavy access restriction.** 14,000+ military/police patrolling; access blocked, special permits required (defended by the Justice/Peace Minister as clearing roads for ambulances "and for health reasons," not elaborated). (PBS.)
- **Performative officials:** "They came to eat arepas and take pictures... They didn't even get their uniforms dirty." (PBS.)

## §3e-bis screening (both directions blocked)
- **(3) Directionality — NO upgrade:** Venezuela surfaced on a negative within-window event. Aid acceptance is mild positive conduct, but the absence of obstruction is not strong countervailing positive conduct. A negative-surfaced entity must not yield an upgrade proposal.
- **(4) Double-count + threshold — NO downgrade:** The 18.0 already prices weak capacity and poor accountability. A slow, opaque, capacity-limited response to the strongest quake in 126 years is severe but consistent with the existing Critical band; it does not establish NEW deliberate state-perpetrated harm clearing the 5-point composite threshold.
- **Pre-documentation:** Aid-transparency concerns are early allegations from medical staff and residents, not documented diversion, theft, or obstruction. The methodology scores documented conduct, not yet-unverified allegations.
- **Attribution:** The ~1,430 deaths and 68,900 missing are overwhelmingly disaster harm, not state conduct, and are not scored against the government.

## Disposition
**CONFIRM at 18.0. Delta 0.0. No change proposal.** The response is genuinely mixed — broad aid acceptance (positive) against a slow, opaque, access-restricted relief effort (negative) — and nets out below the 5-point threshold in both directions. Confidence: medium (fast-moving event, evidence still developing).

- **Watch flag — ACT (1.6):** relief responsiveness; sustained failure to mount effective rescue/relief as the disaster matures.
- **Watch flag — ACC (1.9):** aid-delivery transparency; the "we don't know what arrived" concern.
- **Forward trigger (downgrade re-assessment):** documented deliberate aid obstruction, diversion/theft of relief, suppression of casualty data, or use of access restrictions to conceal conditions would be scorable negative own-conduct.
- **Forward trigger (upgrade re-assessment):** a demonstrably effective, transparent, sustained relief and reconstruction effort would be scorable positive own-conduct.
