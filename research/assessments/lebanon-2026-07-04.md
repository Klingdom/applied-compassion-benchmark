# Assessment: Lebanon — IDF "No Ceasefire" in the South (Attribution / Non-State Screen)

**Composite:** 17.2/100 (CONFIRM) | **Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
Fighting continues in south Lebanon after the IDF chief declared "no ceasefire," and a June 28 US-brokered framework has not held. More than 4,000 people have been killed since the March 2 resumption and over a million displaced. But this harm is inflicted BY Israel (a foreign state) and Hezbollah (a non-state armed group) — not by the Lebanese state itself. Under the attribution rule, war harm inflicted by another state cannot lower Lebanon's own compassion score, and Hezbollah's rejection of the ceasefire is non-state conduct. The unrealized ceasefire is not positive evidence either.
