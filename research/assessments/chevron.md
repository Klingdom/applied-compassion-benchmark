## Context

First full assessment. Chevron's published composite is 9.1 (Critical). Two material April 2026 events anchor this assessment:

1. **Colorado $1.53M penalty (Feb 24, 2026):** Colorado ECMC 5-0 vote against Chevron subsidiary Noble Energy for April 2025 Galeton well blowout — 4-day blowout spewed 20,000 barrels produced water/sand/salts + 5,000 barrels oil and condensates over 7 square miles of Weld County, with contractor human error as cause ([CPR](https://www.cpr.org/2026/02/24/chevron-noble-energy-weld-county-oil-spill-fine-penalty/), [Colorado Sun](https://coloradosun.com/2026/02/24/colorado-noble-energy-chevron-fine-well-blowout/)).
2. **California climate-disclosure litigation (ongoing):** Chevron (with other majors) sued by CA AG Bonta for deceiving consumers about fossil fuels' climate role; California's SB 253/SB 261 disclosure laws also being challenged by Chevron ([CA AG](https://oag.ca.gov/news/press-releases/attorney-general-bonta-announces-lawsuit-against-oil-and-gas-companies)).
