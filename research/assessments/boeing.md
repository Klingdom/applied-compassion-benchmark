### Recommendation

The published score and the assessed score are both firmly in the Critical band. The delta of -4.1 is below the 5-point threshold for a change proposal. The published score of 9.1 is broadly consistent with current evidence, though the research suggests it may be slightly overstated. **No change proposal warranted.**

## Key Findings

- DOJ non-prosecution agreement (May 2025) allowed Boeing to avoid criminal prosecution for 737 Max crashes killing 346 people; Fifth Circuit sealed this outcome on March 31, 2026, denying families' petition to reopen
- NTSB determined probable cause of 2024 door plug blowout was Boeing's failure to provide "adequate training, guidance and oversight" — the same category of failure as the 737 Max crashes
- 32 OSHA whistleblower complaints since 2020; FAA panel found employees fear retaliation and culture "still allows opportunities for retaliation to occur"
- 2025 employee survey showed mixed results with middle management and officers remaining "key obstacles" to safety culture change
- Whistleblower John Barnett died during legal proceedings; case underscores systemic retaliation risk

## Strongest Dimensions

- Boundaries (17.5/100) — standard large employer HR infrastructure provides partial foundation, though undermined by retaliation culture

## Weakest Dimensions

- Awareness, Empathy, Integrity (all 0/100) — complete failure to detect, empathize with, or maintain genuine commitment to the safety of those served

- Limited access to internal safety audit results
- 2025 annual safety report referenced but full details not publicly accessible
- Supply chain safety data (Spirit AeroSystems) partially opaque
- Victim compensation fund distribution details not fully public
