At April 24 international press conference, Magyar pledged to reverse Hungary's ICC withdrawal. HRW April 14 issued public agenda for incoming government. **The score reflects current state at end-of-Orbán-era; positive trajectory signals exist but have not yet produced structural change.**

### Score Difference Analysis

The published 28.1 captures the deeply degraded rule-of-law profile correctly but appears to score EQU and INT slightly below the floor-anchor descriptions warrant. The agent-baseline 31.3 brings these dimensions into alignment with anchor 2/5 ("Some disaggregation, disparities not investigated" / "Pressure occasionally causes unacknowledged compromises") rather than 1/5 (floor-level absence).

The April 2026 election outcome introduces upside trajectory: Magyar's stated ICC reversal, Pride decriminalization commitment, and Sovereignty Protection Office dissolution would constitute structural ACC/INT positives if implemented. **None of these have happened yet** — the agent must score current state, not promised state.

### Recommendation

Composite delta is **+3.2 points**, below the 5-point threshold. Confirmation recommended. Flag for re-queue once incoming Magyar government takes office and structural commitments materialize or fail.

## Key Findings

1. **Election outcome (April 12 2026, Magyar landslide) is a structural transition signal** but pre-implementation; effects not yet scoreable.
2. **Sovereignty Protection Office and Pride prosecutions** are the most-acute in-window negative signals.
3. **EU Article 7 process and ~€21B funding freeze** provide external accountability infrastructure that prevents floor-level outcomes.
4. **Ukraine refugee response** since 2022 is a substantive coalitional-compassion positive offsetting other patterns.
5. **Magyar's ICC-reversal pledge (April 24)** is a high-signal commitment to monitor.
