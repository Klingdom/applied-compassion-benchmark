## Cycle Context & Ruling — MILITARY-AI-BY-CONTRACT-GOVERNANCE (net-neutral)

Two offsetting in-progress signals:
- **NEGATIVE/governance:** OpenAI's Department of War (DoD) classified-deployment deal defines guardrails by reference to existing legal authorities and DoD policy, with *interpretive discretion resting with the government* — weaker than Anthropic's sought explicit contractual prohibitions with vendor enforcement (Lawfare; TechPolicy.Press "Five Unresolved Issues"). The deal was adjusted days after signing. This is a candidate INT/BND concern but is procurement-governance, not an adjudicated harm.
- **POSITIVE/in-progress:** OpenAI published its **Frontier Governance Framework (May 28, 2026)** aligning its Preparedness Framework with the EU AI Act GPAI Code of Practice and California's frontier-AI law; OpenAI is among ~two dozen GPAI Code signatories. Stated DoD "red lines" (no mass domestic surveillance; no autonomous-weapons direction; no high-stakes automated decisions).

Under the established MILITARY-AI-BY-CONTRACT-GOVERNANCE ruling, procurement terms are **not operationalized** into a standing scorable rule; the red-lines-vs-government-discretion tension and the EU-alignment publication are both in-progress and offset. The near-floor INT 1.7 already prices a weak consistency/values posture. **Net-neutral; confirm at 27.5.** EU AI Act enforcement (Aug 2) is a forward watch.
