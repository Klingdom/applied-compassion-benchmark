## Key Findings

- **Iowa is the first US state to delete a protected class from its civil rights code, and that single act sets a ceiling on its equity score.** Senate File 418, signed 28 February 2025 and effective 1 July 2025, removed gender identity from the Iowa Civil Rights Act. That law covers employment, wages, housing, education, public accommodations, and credit. This drove Equity to 25 of 100 and Empathy to 25 of 100 — the two lowest of Iowa's eight dimensions.

- **Iowa reversed one major policy on its own evidence, which is the strongest single signal in this assessment.** The state rejected federal Summer EBT for 2024 and 2025 and ran food boxes instead. Healthy Kids Iowa served about 47,000 children in July 2025, roughly one-fifth of the estimated 240,000 Summer EBT would have reached. Iowa then announced it will join the federal program for 2026. Few states reverse a governor's signature program on published reach data.

- **Iowa knows why its cancer rate is high and has not acted on the cause.** Iowa has the second-highest cancer rate in the country and is one of only three states where rates are rising. A March 2026 report tied that to nitrate, pesticides, PFAS, and radon, and agricultural sources drive up to 80% of nitrate contamination in some areas. Every nitrate and water-quality bill died in the 2026 legislative funnel. This is why Structural Critique scored 1 of 5, the lowest subdimension in the assessment.

- **Iowa cut the power of the office whose job is finding what the state is missing.** Senate File 478 (2023) removed the State Auditor's ability to subpoena disputed records or sue agencies that refuse them, sending disputes instead to an arbitration board whose deciding member the Governor appoints. In 2025 the Governor's office also claimed executive privilege to withhold records and sued the Des Moines Register over four emails.

- **Iowa cites problems and then does not penalize them.** In 2025, 60 of Iowa's 397 nursing homes — 15% — were cited for insufficient staffing, a rate two to five times higher than most nearby states. No fines or penalties were imposed for those violations. Inspectors also missed the 15.9-month inspection deadline at least once for 85.4% of Iowa's 403 homes.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Systemic Thinking (40 of 100)** is Iowa's highest score, and it is still Developing. Iowa did two things that count as structural: House File 972 draws down $150 million in federal funds for about 115 new medical residency slots, addressing the root cause of the nation's worst OB-GYN-per-capita ratio; and House File 2673 merged 13 mental health regions and 19 substance use networks into 7 behavioral health districts, launched 1 July 2025.
- **Awareness (35) and Accountability (35)** are lifted by genuine infrastructure: the Iowa Cancer Registry, a SEER member since 1973; Iowa HHS public dashboards and overdose data; an Ombudsman for managed Medicaid and a State Long-Term Care Ombudsman; and one documented, evidence-driven policy reversal.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Equity (25), Empathy (25), and Integrity (25)** are Iowa's floor. All three are driven by the same pattern: the state narrowed who is covered (civil rights code), narrowed who can raise disparities (2025 DEI ban across state agencies and local governments), and narrowed enforcement against those who harm the vulnerable (child labor penalties capped at $2,500 with a mandatory 15-day grace period; nursing home staffing citations without fines).
- **Structural Critique (S4) at 1 of 5** is the single lowest subdimension, followed by Universality (EQ1), Non-Judgment (E3), and Scope Clarity (B3), all at 1 of 5.

## Evidence Gaps

- **State workforce sustainability (B1) is the largest gap.** No Iowa turnover, vacancy, or burnout reporting for child protection workers or other frontline state staff was located. B1 was scored 2 on adjacent evidence (AEA staffing down ~200; nursing home staffing citations) and marked low confidence. A state that published this data could plausibly score higher.
- **Community testimony is thin across Empathy.** Iowa's E1 score rests on program-reach and journalism rather than on structured recipient surveys. No independent statewide survey of how people experience Iowa state services was found.
- **Racial disparity data in corrections is 2021–2023 vintage.** The 9.1x figure and the 7th-worst national ranking come from Prison Policy Initiative and ACLU of Iowa analysis of Bureau of Justice Statistics data. No more recent state-published disparity analysis was located, which is itself an EQ3 finding.
- **Child welfare federal review is stale.** Iowa's most recent completed Child and Family Services Review is Round 3, with results issued February 2019. No Round 4 final report was located, so child welfare evidence carries less weight than it does for peer states with current federal reviews.
- **Long-term outcome data for the July 2025 behavioral health redesign does not yet exist.** S2 and AB4 are scored on design quality, not results.

## Federal Interaction Note

Per scope, this assessment scores Iowa's own conduct. Two areas involved federal policy where Iowa exercised genuine discretion and is therefore scored:

- **Immigration enforcement.** In March 2025 the Iowa Department of Public Safety voluntarily signed a 287(g) task force agreement with ICE, giving three Iowa State Patrol special agents authority to make immigration arrests and issue detainers. In August 2025 the Governor directed 20 Iowa National Guard members to support ICE under Title 32 with the state retaining control. Both were state choices, not federal mandates.
- **Summer nutrition.** Federal Summer EBT is opt-in. Iowa opted out for 2024 and 2025 and opted in for 2026. Each decision was Iowa's.

Conversely, the federal court ruling of 23 June 2026 striking Iowa's SNAP purchase restrictions turned on USDA exceeding its authority. Iowa's scored conduct is the design and pursuit of the restriction, not the federal approval.

## Recommended Next Steps

Iowa's composite of 30.6 places it in the **Developing** band — key structures exist but are inconsistent or reactive. Based on this profile:

- **Consider a [Certified Assessment](/certified-assessments)** for a structured improvement roadmap. Iowa's flat profile (all eight dimensions between 25 and 40) means there is no single fix; it needs sequenced work.
- Three changes would move the composite fastest, in order of leverage: (1) act on the state's own cancer-cause findings, which alone would lift the lowest subdimension in the assessment; (2) restore the State Auditor's access powers, which would raise both Transparency and Blind Spot Mitigation; (3) publish frontline workforce data, which would convert a low-confidence score into a measurable one.
- Iowa should also be credited for what already works. The Summer EBT reversal is a model of evidence-driven correction, and the state should be encouraged to apply the same published-reach test to Medicaid work requirements before the December 2026 implementation date.
