# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: IBM (First-Ever Baseline)

**Entity type:** Company
**Sector/Domain:** Technology (enterprise, federal contractor)
**Assessment date:** 2026-04-22

**Triggering evidence:** April 10, 2026 DOJ-announced **$17,077,043 False Claims Act settlement** — the *first-ever* FCA resolution under the Civil Rights Fraud Initiative. IBM certified compliance with federal anti-discrimination contract requirements while (per DOJ allegations) setting workforce representation goals by race/sex, tying incentive compensation to DEI achievement, using diverse interview slates, and restricting access to employment/educational programming on race/sex basis ([DOJ](https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/ibm-pays-17-million-resolve-allegations-discrimination-through-illegal-dei-practices); [Latham & Watkins](https://www.lw.com/en/insights/ibm-pays-in-first-dei-related-false-claims-act-resolution); [Foley Hoag](https://foleyhoag.com/news-and-insights/publications/alerts-and-updates/2026/april/doj-announces-first-false-claims-act-settlement-under-civil-rights-fraud-initiative-ibm-to-pay-$17/)).

**Scoring judgment:** The settlement is simultaneously (a) a hard-evidence EQU/E3 floor (pattern of documented race- and sex-based differential treatment, regardless of which direction) and ACC/A3 anchor (certification-compliance failure on federal contracts); (b) a *partial* positive on ACC/AB1, AB2, AB4 due to IBM's early disclosures, voluntary remedial measures, and termination/modification of programs at issue, earning DOJ cooperation credit. Net effect: composite materially below the scan-flagged 65.5 (which also appears to be a Gen-2 uniform-score artifact — see methodology note below). **Composite 51.3 vs. published 62.5 = -11.2 delta.**
