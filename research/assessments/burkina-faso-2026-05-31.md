## Summary of cycle ruling

This cycle reweights a **corroborated, severe, recent active-state-perpetration finding** that the published 12.5 baseline (uniform raw 1.5) did not reflect. Human Rights Watch's **April 2, 2026 report** ("Crimes Against Humanity by All Sides" / "None Can Run Away") documents that the Burkina Faso military and allied militias have killed 1,800+ civilians and forcibly displaced tens of thousands since 2023, including **ethnic cleansing of Fulani civilians** and **drone strikes on crowded civilian markets** (Bayraktar TB2 laser-guided munitions) that HRW concludes amount to **war crimes and crimes against humanity**. HRW further finds the junta is "curtailing reporting to obscure the suffering of civilians."

This is the same multi-category active-state-perpetration conduct that anchors the 0.0-floor cases (Myanmar junta airstrikes on civilians; Sudan siege/starvation). Burkina Faso is **not** scored to the absolute 0.0 floor because: (a) the conflict is genuinely multi-sided (JNIM/Al-Qaeda-linked groups are also perpetrating mass atrocities — HRW attributes crimes to "all sides"), so the state is not the sole driver; and (b) limited state–UN humanitarian cooperation persists (the UN $1.16B Food Systems partnership and UNGA co-sponsorship noted in the 2026-05-21 baseline). The result is a meaningful downgrade toward the Critical floor while preserving the distinction from sole-perpetrator and absolute-floor cases.

### Score Difference Analysis
The published uniform 1.5 profile predates HRW's April 2, 2026 determination of government-perpetrated **crimes against humanity and ethnic cleansing**. This is corroborated, Tier-5 independent documentation (HRW field investigation, corroborated by Al Jazeera and JURIST) of multi-category active-state-perpetration conduct — the same category that anchors the 0.0-floor cases. The downgrade concentrates in EQU/ACC/INT/EMP (the dimensions most directly implicated by deliberate state targeting), while AWR/ACT/BND hold marginally above floor because limited UN-partnered humanitarian capacity persists.

### Recommendation
Downgrade published composite 12.5 → 6.3 (Critical → Critical). The April 2 HRW finding is material, corroborated, and not reflected in the published baseline. Distinguished from the 0.0 absolute floor by multi-sided perpetration and residual state–UN humanitarian cooperation.

## Key Findings
- HRW April 2, 2026 documents Burkina Faso military + allied militias committing **crimes against humanity and ethnic cleansing of Fulani civilians**; drone strikes on civilian markets ruled apparent war crimes.
- Junta actively **curtails reporting** to obscure civilian suffering (anti-transparency, anti-accountability).
- Sahel humanitarian catastrophe (2,640 conflict deaths Jan–Mar 2026; 3M+ displaced; 7.5M children needing aid) is the continuing backdrop.
- Multi-sided conflict (JNIM also perpetrating) and residual UN-partnered humanitarian cooperation distinguish this from the 0.0 absolute floor.

## Strongest Dimensions
- SYS (7.5) — only relatively because of climate/food-systems framing that partially offsets; still deep Critical.

## Weakest Dimensions
- EQU, ACC, INT (2.5 each) — driven by deliberate ethnic targeting, atrocity denial, and rhetoric-conduct contradiction.

## Evidence Gaps
- Whether the August/November 2025 drone strikes cited by HRW have continued into the current 2026 window with new dated incidents (the April 2 report is the corroborating anchor; OCHA Apr 29 confirms ongoing mass casualties but does not itemize new state-attributed strikes).

## Recommended Next Steps
- **Critical**: This profile reflects active state-perpetrated atrocity. Carry-forward watch: any new dated state-attributed mass-civilian-casualty event or escalation to sole-perpetrator dynamics would warrant movement toward the 0.0 floor.
