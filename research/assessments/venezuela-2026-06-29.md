# Special Event Assessment: Venezuela — Earthquake Response (Day 5)

**Assessment date:** 2026-06-29
**Composite:** 18.0/100 (CONFIRM — sub-threshold both directions)
**Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
Five days after the June 24 earthquakes, Venezuela's toll stands at about 1,450 dead and 3,150 injured. International aid is flowing: UNICEF's first 20-tonne shipment arrived June 27, a second 48-tonne shipment is due June 30, and the US authorized relief transactions (OFAC General License 60) plus $150M through UN and faith-based channels. The earthquake is a natural disaster, not the government's doing. What we score — the government's response — remains genuinely mixed: it accepts broad aid (positive) but runs a slow, opaque relief effort (negative). The two cancel out below the threshold. The score holds.
