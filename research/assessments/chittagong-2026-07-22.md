## Key Findings

- Why it matters: Record week-long monsoon rains in July 2026 killed 34–43 people, contaminated ~20,000 tube wells, and cut safe drinking water for roughly 800,000 people across Chittagong.
- Chattogram City Corporation is not a pure floor institution: it runs pre-monsoon drain-cleaning and multi-thousand-crore mega drainage projects, and the mayor reported an ~80% waterlogging reduction — real, if contested and delayed, systemic effort.
- The city has lost 47 of its original 104 drainage canals to unplanned urbanisation, and long-delayed projects show commitments eroding under administrative pressure.

## Strongest Dimensions

- Systemic Thinking — large, funded drainage-infrastructure projects and canal restoration target the root cause of recurring floods.

## Weakest Dimensions

- Integrity and Equity — chronic project delays, defensive accountability, and unprotected poor neighbourhoods.

## Evidence Gaps

- Two scan sources (Daily Star, Dhaka Tribune) returned access blocks; scoring relied on TBS and Daily Star coverage of the same events. No independent audit of flood-relief equity was available.
