# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Nigeria (Confirmation)

**Published composite:** 18.0/100 (Critical) — rank 149, countries index.
**Determination:** CONFIRM at 18.0. Continuation of the already-priced man-made northern famine; no materially new own-conduct escalation.

## Within-Window Evidence

The WFP's Jul 2 hunger record — the worst in nearly a decade — remains operative: 36.2M nationally food insecure; 17M+ across nine northern states at crisis/emergency/catastrophe levels (up 2M from prior projections); Borno alone has 3M+ acutely food insecure, 750,000+ in severe hunger, 10,000+ at catastrophe. **WFP can support only 740,000 of 6.2M in need** across three northeast states, leaving 5.5M without lifesaving assistance ([WFP, 2026-07-02](https://www.wfp.org/news/conflict-and-shrinking-humanitarian-assistance-drives-northern-nigeria-hunger-crisis-levels); [GlobalSecurity](https://www.globalsecurity.org/military/library/news/2026/07/mil-260702-wfp01.htm)). No materially new dated escalation beyond the previously-confirmed Jul 2 report.

## Attribution / Screening

The famine is driven by a mix of insurgent conflict, funding shortfalls, and governance failure. The own-conduct component (insecurity, degraded state protection/service delivery in the north) is already priced; the Jun 19 band-relevant crossing already captured the man-made famine. No discrete new own-conduct step-change is evidenced this cycle.

**CONFIRM 18.0 (Critical). No proposal.** Sub-threshold. Downward watch sustained.
