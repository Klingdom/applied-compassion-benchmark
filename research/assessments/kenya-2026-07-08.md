## Assessment Type: Confirmation

Kenya appeared as a flagged NEW-attention entrant on the pre-emptively blocked Saba Saba march. This is a targeted confirmation against the published Developing-band baseline of 35.9.

## Within-Window Evidence (2026-06-24 to 2026-07-08)

- **Saba Saba march pre-emptively blocked (Jul 7, NEW).** Police prevented the planned July 7 procession (from Jeevanjee Gardens to Parliament, petitioning on extrajudicial killings, enforced disappearances, and police accountability) using dawn checkpoints, road closures, plainclothes officers, and unmarked vehicles. The Economic Justice Movement had notified police of a 1,000–3,000-person march. Nairobi Regional Police Commander described the operation as a "success," saying only a handful attempted to gather and 10 people were arrested in Nairobi and environs. Parliament was sealed with barbed wire; CBD largely closed. (Africanews, 2026-07-07; Daily Nation, 2026-07-07; Citizen Digital, 2026-07-07)

## Anti-False-Positive Screening

1. **Baseline provenance.** 35.9 (Developing) reflects a functioning-but-flawed state with active civic space, courts, and a documented pattern of police violence against protesters. Current.
2. **Directionality.** Negative within-window evidence; any move would be downward.
3. **Materiality.** This is a prior-restraint/freedom-of-assembly action — a real negative signal — but materially **less severe** than the 2025 Saba Saba (41 killed). Fewer than 10 gathered; 10 arrested; no reported lethal force. It is consistent with the existing EQU/ACC deficits already priced into the 35.9 baseline and does not approach a 5-point composite move or the Developing/Critical boundary (20.0). Sub-threshold.
4. **Calibration.** Not an outlier.

## Decision

**CONFIRM at 35.9 (Developing).** Downward watch flag maintained given the recurring pattern of protest suppression. No change proposal (sub-threshold). No subdimension sidecar. Conversion trigger: a return to lethal crackdown (mass casualties) or a systemic legal ban on assembly.
