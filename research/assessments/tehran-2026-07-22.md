## Key Findings

- Why it matters: Tehran is governed within one of the world’s most repressive systems. Amnesty International recorded more than 2,100 executions in Iran in 2025 and documented mass arbitrary arrests and politically motivated hangings intensifying into 2026.
- In July 2026 US strikes hit fuel depots near Tehran, causing a “river of fire” and 50+ deaths nationally. That is harm inflicted on the city from outside and is screened out of its own conduct score.
- The score rests on the governing system’s own documented repression — executions, torture, internet blackouts — which keeps Tehran at the very bottom of the Critical band.

## Strongest Dimensions

- Action — only relatively, via functioning basic municipal utilities and transit; still weak in absolute terms.

## Weakest Dimensions

- Accountability and Integrity — executions with impunity and “wartime” cover for intensified repression.

## Evidence Gaps

- Municipal administration is inseparable from the national security state; independent local service data is unavailable under information controls.
