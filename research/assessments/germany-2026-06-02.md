## Cycle Context

Germany was flagged this cycle on genuinely new migration signals: the EU "return hubs" deal agreed June 1, 2026 (Germany among five states planning third-country return facilities); continued deportations to Afghanistan (83 reported) and Syria (1 reported) under non-refoulement objection; and a German court ruling that the government's border-rejection (pushback) policy was incompatible with EU law, with the government continuing the policy. The AfD's designation as a confirmed right-wing extremist organization is a related domestic backdrop.

## Scorability Ruling

The new evidence is **partially scorable but sub-threshold**, and is distinct from the India active-state-perpetration pattern:

- **Return hubs** (June 1 EU deal) are a *policy framework not yet operationalized* — Interior Minister Dobrindt targets third-country agreements "by end of 2026"; no hub exists. Under MATERIALIZATION / ANNOUNCED-NOT-OPERATIONALIZED precedent this is a watch item, not yet a scorable act.
- **Deportations to Afghanistan/Syria** are of *convicted criminals* under frameworks that German courts have adjudicated individually (Federal Administrative Court permitted a Syria deportation; courts review non-refoulement case by case). Human-rights bodies (Amnesty, ECRE) contest these on non-refoulement grounds — a real EQU/universality concern un-priced at the published EQU 3.5 — but the conduct is judicially supervised and limited in scale, not a directed mass-refoulement regime.
- **Border-rejection ruled EU-law-incompatible, government continuing**: this is the most directly scorable signal — a documented values/judicial-defiance concern (INT) and a failure to acknowledge/correct an unlawful practice (ACC). A court (the Berlin administrative court line; Somali asylum-seeker pushback) found the practice unlawful; the executive's continuation is a genuine integrity-under-pressure deficit. It is, however, contested through functioning courts — the opposite of the India "courts overridden" pattern.

Net: a modest, conservative downgrade concentrated in EQU (3.5→3.2: non-refoulement deportations + return-hub framework + asylum-pushback), INT (3.5→3.3: continuing a court-ruled-unlawful border policy), and ACC (3.8→3.6: no acknowledgment/correction of the unlawful pushback). AWR/EMP/ACT/BND/SYS held — Germany's detection, response, and welfare infrastructure remain Established-grade and the rule-of-law check is functioning.
