## What this assessment is

This is Florida's **first evidence-based baseline** under the Compassion Benchmark methodology. Florida already appeared in the published US States index at rank 17 with a composite of 20.3. That entry was a bulk-import placeholder. It had `last_assessed: null` and no evidence behind any of its eight dimension values. It was not a measurement.

So the number below is not a change in Florida's behaviour. It is the first time anyone actually looked.

Florida is a hard test for a benchmark like this one. Reputation predicts what most readers expect the score to be, in both directions. The instruction we followed was to score the record, not the politics, and to let the eight dimensions disagree with each other where the record does. They do disagree, sharply. Florida scores 3.2 on Systemic Thinking and 1.8 on Boundaries. That 1.4-point spread inside a single state is one of the widest in the assessed set.

### Score Difference Analysis

**Accountability, +32.5 points — the largest single correction.** The published 1.5 implies Florida denies harm, hides performance data, and has no functioning oversight. The record does not support that. Florida's constitutional right of access under Article I Section 24 binds all three branches, and any new exemption needs a two-thirds vote in each chamber. OPPAGA and the Auditor General are legislatively funded, structurally independent, and published damaging findings during the assessment window. Most tellingly, a Republican-led Florida House committee investigated the Governor's own signature charity and referred it to a state grand jury. That is an institution scrutinising itself at political cost. None of this makes Florida transparent in practice — more than 1,000 statutory exemptions exist and press organisations describe a default of "ignore, delay, deny" — which is why AB3 sits at 3 rather than 4. But 1.5 was not a measurement of anything.

**Systemic Thinking, +30.0 points.** The published 2.0 implies symptom-only work. Florida instead restructured the cost drivers in a collapsing insurance market rather than subsidising premiums, and the state-backed insurer of last resort then proposed rate reductions after years of increases. Florida also sends emergency management resources out of state — to Texas, Maine and Oklahoma in 2024 — with no in-state benefit.

**Equity, +22.5 points, despite the lowest subdimension score in the assessment.** This is where the dimensions genuinely disagree. Florida scores 1 out of 5 on Universality, the floor, because roughly 388,000 residents sit in the coverage gap and a unanimously passed children's coverage expansion went unimplemented for three years. It also scores 4 out of 5 on Historical Harm Acknowledgment, because it paid $20 million to Dozier School survivors in 2024. Both facts are true of the same state in the same window. The placeholder's 1.5 flattened that into a single low number.

**Boundaries, -5.0 points — the only dimension where research found Florida worse than published.** A federal court established in January 2026 that Florida told roughly 500,000 people their health coverage was ending in letters that failed to explain why. Scope Clarity and Refusal Ethics both score 1. This finding post-dates any plausible date for the placeholder.

### Recommendation

The published composite of 20.3 is **substantially understated** and should be replaced with 38.1. The band does not change: Florida was Developing and remains Developing. The rank should be set to null, since the published index contains only 21 of 51 jurisdictions and rank 17 is an artefact of renumbering after the original extraction lost ranks 9 through 38.

The correction is a data-defect fix. It says nothing about whether Florida improved. Future comparisons should be measured against 38.1.

## Key Findings

**Florida's compassion record splits by domain, not by degree.** The state is competent-to-strong at physical disaster and weak-to-failing at administrative access. It ran nearly 2,600 support missions for Hurricane Milton and rescued about 1,800 people. It also took three years to not implement a children's health expansion its own Legislature passed unanimously.

**The single worst finding is a court ruling, not an opinion.** In January 2026 a federal judge held in a 273-page decision that Florida violated the constitutional rights of people it removed from Medicaid, describing its termination letters as vague, confusing and often incorrect. The state must re-send proper notices to roughly 500,000 people. This is Tier 5 evidence and it drives Boundaries down to 20 out of 100, Florida's only Critical-band dimension.

**The strongest finding cuts against expectation.** Florida paid $20 million in 2024 to living survivors of the Dozier School for Boys, about $50,000 each, for abuse documented as far back as 1901. Concrete, legislated, paid repair for a named historical harm is rare among the states assessed so far.

**Transparency is better than reputation suggests and worse than the constitution promises.** Florida's constitutional access right binds all three branches, which is stronger than most states. But more than 1,000 statutory exemptions now exist. Critically, Florida does NOT have branch-level self-exemption, so the benchmark's AB3 cross-state rule does not apply here — a distinction that separates Florida from Virginia, Massachusetts and Washington.

**Prevention is where Florida is genuinely ahead.** My Safe Florida Home and Elevate Florida commit over $750 million to hardening homes before storms hit, the largest state-level residential mitigation effort in the country. Insurance restructuring produced the first proposed rate cuts from the state-backed insurer in years. Overdose deaths fell 14% in 2024 after 767,000 naloxone kits were distributed.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Systemic Thinking (55/100)** — root-cause work on insurance market structure and overdose prevention, plus standing mutual aid with other states and hundreds of small local governments.
- **Awareness, Action and Accountability (45/100 each)** — a genuinely capable emergency management apparatus, real independent oversight bodies still publishing, and statutory data-publication duties.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Boundaries (20/100, Critical)** — the only Critical-band dimension. Scope Clarity and Refusal Ethics both score 1 out of 5, driven by a federal court finding about how the state communicates denials.
- **Empathy (25/100)** — uniformly 2 across all five subdimensions. No subdimension scores above 2, which is unusual and reflects a consistent pattern rather than one bad domain.
- **Equity (35/100)** — dragged by a floor score of 1 on Universality that a strong 4 on Historical Harm Acknowledgment cannot offset.

## Evidence Gaps

- **B5 Consent Orientation** is scored at low confidence. Evidence on Florida's general consent practice across services was thin; the score rests on the documented chilling effect of the hospital immigration-status question rather than on direct consent-process evidence.
- **The Hope Florida grand jury presentment remains sealed** pending a legal challenge as of February 2026. I4 was scored on the underlying, undisputed fund flows and the confirmed $10 million repayment, not on the sealed findings.
- **No community testimony was gathered directly.** Tier 5 evidence in this assessment comes from courts, peer-reviewed research and university studies, not from residents. That limits E1 and AB5 in particular.
- **Some sources carried only month or year date precision.** Those items are marked `date_precision` in the sidecar rather than given false day-level precision.
- **Corrections vacancy figures** come from a 2024 report and a 2026 bill analysis rather than a current published dashboard, so B1 is medium confidence.

## Recommended Next Steps

Florida sits in the **Developing** band at 38.1, 1.9 points below the Functional threshold. Boundaries is the only dimension whose correction alone would cross that line: raising it from 1.8 to 3.0 returns a composite of 41.9.

- **Developing band**: Consider a [Certified Assessment](/certified-assessments) for a structured improvement roadmap. The highest-leverage single action is redesigning benefit denial and termination communications, which touches B3, B4, E1, E2 and EQ4 simultaneously.
