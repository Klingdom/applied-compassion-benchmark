# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Democratic Republic of the Congo (Near-Floor Confirmation)

**Published composite:** 2.3/100 (Critical, near-floor) — rank 181, countries index.
**Determination:** CONFIRM at 2.3. Near-floor arithmetic forecloses a ≥5pt move; downward watch sustained.

## Within-Window Evidence

The DRC's Bundibugyo-strain **Ebola outbreak reached 702 confirmed deaths and 1,926 confirmed cases** as of Jul 11–12 — up from 600–625 deaths / 1,759 cases just two days earlier (Jul 9–10) — reinforcing Africa CDC's characterization of it as the fastest-growing Ebola outbreak on the continent. WHO's July estimate that the true case count could reach **4,000–8,000** underscores the scale of underreporting ([UN News, 2026-07](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167882); [Al Jazeera, 2026-07-09](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/9/confirmed-ebola-deaths-in-dr-congo-hit-600); [Planet Today](https://www.planet-today.com/2026/07/ebola-outbreak-drc-2026-1759-cases-and.html)). An Ituri healthcare-workers' wage-related walkout continues to threaten response capacity; ongoing armed clashes in North/South Kivu and Ituri continue to hamper containment.

## Attribution (own-conduct vs. externally-inflicted)

The Ebola toll is **largely externally-inflicted** — a natural epidemic, not government conduct. The own-conduct **aggravators** — army redeployment enabling armed-group expansion, blocked/degraded humanitarian access, and unresolved health-worker pay disputes hampering the response — are already priced into the near-floor baseline. No new discrete own-conduct step-change is evidenced this cycle.

## Determination

From a published 2.3, even a move to the harm-flag floor (0.0) is only −2.3 points — a ≥5pt move is arithmetically impossible. The epidemic growth is natural-attribution; own-conduct aggravators are already priced. **CONFIRM 2.3 (Critical). No proposal.** Downward watch sustained.
