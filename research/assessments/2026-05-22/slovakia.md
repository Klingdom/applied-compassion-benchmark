## Methodology-Novel Adjudication: "Dismantler Consistency Across Starting Positions"

The same Liberties.eu 'Dismantler' classification was applied May 21 to Croatia (48.4 mid-Functional, downgraded -7.8 to 40.6) and now to Slovakia (39.1 bottom Developing).

**Question:** Should Slovakia receive the same magnitude as Croatia (-7.8) under symmetric evaluation?

**Ruling:** No, but for principled reasons:

1. **Directional consistency is mandatory.** Same classification, same direction. Confirmed: both downward.
2. **Magnitude is properly anchored to starting position and floor proximity.** Croatia was mid-Functional with room to move within band. Slovakia is already at bottom Developing (0.9 above Critical/Developing boundary at 20.0… wait, 39.1 is well above 20.0). Re-examining: Slovakia floor is 20.1 (Critical boundary). At 39.1, Slovakia has 19.1pt of headroom to Critical. So floor proximity is not yet the binding constraint.
3. **The binding constraint is evidence ceiling.** The Dismantler classification produces an evidence package that is consistent across countries but the *marginal informational content* relative to baseline differs. Croatia was published in Functional, implying baseline scoring credited it with institutional integrity that the Dismantler signal rebutted (large delta). Slovakia is already published in Developing at 39.1, implying baseline scoring already priced significant rule-of-law concerns (smaller incremental delta).

**Decided magnitude:** -5.5 (vs Croatia -7.8). Approximately 70% of Croatia magnitude, calibrated to "baseline already prices most of the concern, EP resolution + Liberties Dismantler adds incremental marginal evidence."

**Precedent established:** When the same Tier-1 institutional classification is applied to multiple entities at different starting compositions, magnitude scales as: full magnitude at baseline that was prima facie inconsistent with the classification; reduced magnitude (60-75%) when baseline already partially priced the concern.
