## Dimension Reasoning — Dual-Signal Weighting

- **ACC (+1.9):** The only positive movement. Microsoft's actions — internal investigation, executive removal, services termination, public acknowledgment of subsidiary opacity — represent the strongest single-company corporate accountability response in the benchmark's recent history. Accountability dimension explicitly rewards this even where root harm is severe.
- **BND (-4.4):** Largest single negative. Microsoft's bright-line policies were not enforced for an extended period; subsidiary structure shielded military use from global oversight. The accountability response repairs going-forward boundaries but the historical breach is material.
- **ACT (-3.7):** Action enabled mass surveillance and lethal targeting over years; current corrective action is too recent to fully credit forward.
- **SYS (-3.1):** Subsidiary-opacity-from-HQ governance gap is a system-design failure regardless of intent.
- **AWR (-3.1):** Awareness that subsidiary was operating outside global ethics framework was apparently lacking until external pressure forced internal investigation.
- **EMP (-1.9), EQU (-1.9):** Modest — Palestinian civilians whose communications were intercepted and whose lives were affected by lethal targeting are the parties harmed. Limited but real EMP/EQU downward.
- **INT (0.0):** Held — integrity dimension reflects values-action alignment; the accountability response is consistent with stated values, partially offsetting the historical inconsistency.

## Methodology-Novel Adjudication: "Harm-Enabled-Then-Accountability-Applied"

This is the first case at this harm-severity level where substantive corporate accountability arrived during the assessment window.

**Question:** How should the benchmark weight enabling-harm (negative) vs. accountability-response (positive) when both are present in a single event arc?

**Ruling:**

1. **Both are scored.** Harm enabled is not erased by accountability applied. Accountability applied is not nullified by harm enabled.
2. **Magnitude asymmetry favors structural change over backward-looking remedy.** A firing without policy change is theater. A firing combined with services termination and acknowledged structural governance gap is structural change. Microsoft did the latter.
3. **Dimension-level signing differs:** ACC moves positive (the response). BND/ACT/SYS/AWR move negative (the historical breach and the governance gap that allowed it). EMP/EQU move modestly negative (harmed parties remain harmed). INT held (values-action alignment partially restored).
4. **Net composite direction is negative.** A company that prevented the harm entirely would be net-positive vs published baseline. A company that enabled harm and then did nothing would be sharply negative. Microsoft sits in between: net -1.9.

**Precedent established:** For enabling-harm with substantive accountability response, net composite delta typically lands in the -1.5 to -3.5 range (sub-threshold). The accountability response must be (a) structural (not just personnel), (b) materially restorative (services terminated/refused, not just apology), and (c) transparent about the underlying governance failure. Microsoft meets all three.

**Comparative anchor:** Amazon (PDX9 worker death, body-on-floor management directive, ongoing federal data-manipulation investigation, EFF: "Amazon did NOT take accountability steps"). Same harm-severity class on EMP/labor dimension; opposite accountability posture; explains why Amazon accumulator remains downward while Microsoft accumulator partially reverts.
