# Assessment: Venezuela — Earthquake Day 10 (Exogenous-Disaster Screen)

**Composite:** 18.0/100 (CONFIRM) | **Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
A major earthquake has now killed an official 2,645 people in Venezuela, with about 50,000 still unaccounted for and $37 billion in damage (UN estimate). The disaster itself is a natural event, not government conduct. Only the government's RESPONSE is scorable. That response is weak — a $200M reconstruction fund against ~$37B in need — but Venezuela is accepting foreign aid (the US committed $300M), which blocks a downgrade. A forensic pathologist told CNN the official toll is "not even a third" of the real figure; that single-source undercount allegation is a forward trigger, not yet verified.
