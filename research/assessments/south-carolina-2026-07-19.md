## Cross-State Consistency: AB3 Transparency Rule (v3)

**Disposition: DECLINED — precondition not met. AB3 scored on the merits at 3.**

The v3 rule caps AB3 at 3 only when *both* limbs are met.

- **Limb (b) is met.** The Legislative Audit Council is still publishing damaging findings nobody forced it to publish, through at least June 2026.
- **Limb (a) is not met.** South Carolina's General Assembly does keep a carve-out for individual legislators' "memoranda, correspondence, and working papers," but this is narrower than a blanket self-exemption, and the 2017 reform *narrowed* it further while confirming that source documents and factual data stay public. No branch has escaped scrutiny wholesale, and the audit council has not been defunded, narrowed, or stripped of independence protections.

Two features were considered and rejected as triggers:

1. **The 2023 execution shield statute** criminalises disclosure of execution supplier and team identities. Under the Idaho refinement, a subject-matter carve-out — however serious — is not a structural branch self-exemption. It is scored on the merits under E4 and AB3, not as a cap trigger.
2. **The 2017 removal of criminal penalties** for records violations, leaving a $500 fine and private civil suits, is a procedural weakness. Under the North Dakota refinement, procedural weakness alone does not trigger the cap.

**Scoring effect:** AB3 = 3 on the merits gives ACC 2.6 and a composite of 37.5. Had the cap been applied and AB3 forced to 2, ACC would fall to 2.4 and the composite to 36.9 — verified output `{"composite":36.9,"band":"Developing","integrationPremium":0}`. The band does not change either way.

## Key Findings

- **Refusing Medicaid expansion is the single largest compassion cost the state carries.** Over 246,000 South Carolinians would gain coverage if the state closed the gap, and roughly 183,000 more were projected to lose exchange coverage in 2026 as enhanced subsidies expired. This one decision drags down Equity, Action and Boundaries at once.

- **South Carolina is one of only two states with no hate crime law — and in April 2026 its Senate refused even to study one.** The bill carries the name of Senator Clementa Pinckney, murdered at Emanuel AME church in 2015. Eleven years after the state took the Confederate flag down at real political cost, the legislative follow-through has not come.

- **The state's watchdog works, and the state does not always act on it.** The Legislative Audit Council keeps publishing findings nobody asked for, including that one suspected disability abuse case went unreported for 82 days. But three years after 101 juvenile justice recommendations, only about half had been implemented.

- **Where South Carolina measures a problem and spends money on it, outcomes move.** Teacher vacancies fell 56 percent in two years, from 1,613 to 706, after pay rises and recruitment work. The same discipline is absent from health coverage and disability waitlists.

- **The state resumed executions in 2024 and killed three people by firing squad in 2025.** An autopsy indicated the squad that executed Mikal Mahdi missed his heart. A 2023 shield law makes it a crime, punishable by up to three years in prison, to reveal who supplies or carries out executions. The state Supreme Court was still deciding the law's reach in June 2026.

## Strongest Dimensions

**Awareness (3.2 raw)** is South Carolina's only dimension above the mid-point, and it rests almost entirely on one institution. The Legislative Audit Council produces structured, damaging, uncompelled findings year after year, and the state's flood resilience plan is genuine forward-looking work. The state sees its problems clearly. It is what happens next that separates Awareness from every other dimension here.

**Accountability, Systemic Thinking and Integrity (all 2.6 raw)** are held up by two real structural corrections — splitting DHEC in 2024 and merging three behavioural health agencies in 2025 — and by the state's willingness to host unflattering monitor reports on its own websites.

## Weakest Dimensions

**Empathy, Equity and Boundaries (all 2.2 raw)** share a single root. South Carolina identifies who is suffering, publishes the disparity, and then declines the structural remedy. Black infants die at more than twice the white rate. Black mothers now die at four times the white rate. Both figures are state-published. Neither has produced a corrective programme.

**Structural Critique (S4) scored 1 of 5**, the lowest single score in this assessment. The *Abbeville* school equity case ran 24 years, produced a finding that the General Assembly had failed its constitutional duty, and was then vacated by a reconstituted Supreme Court in 2017. The enforcement mechanism was removed rather than satisfied.

## Evidence Gaps

- **Current SCDC staffing.** The most recent verified corrections vacancy figure is 40 percent, from April 2023. No 2025–26 figure was confirmed from a primary source, so B1 and I3 were scored conservatively at 3.
- **T.R. v. SCDC compliance.** The 2014 ruling and settlement are well documented, but no public, dated finding on whether South Carolina achieved the required 18 months of sustained compliance was located. This subject therefore carries no score weight of its own.
- **Consent infrastructure (B5).** Almost no direct evidence either way. Marked low confidence.
- **A coordinator-supplied premise was checked and corrected.** A 2025 act extending rights to the Catawba Nation turned out to be a *North Carolina* statute (SL 2025-57), not a South Carolina one. It is excluded. South Carolina's actual 2025 tribal development was an alliance the ten tribes formed themselves, which is scored as community action rather than state action.
- This is a desk-based assessment. No community testimony was independently collected, which caps several subdimensions at 3 by evidence tier alone.

## Recommended Next Steps

South Carolina scores in the **Developing** band. Foundational detection infrastructure exists and works; the structures that convert detection into help do not.

- **Developing band:** Consider a [Certified Assessment](/certified-assessments) for a structured improvement roadmap.
- The fastest realistic route out of the Developing band is either health coverage or civil rights protection. Either alone moves the composite to roughly 40.6 and into the Functional band.
