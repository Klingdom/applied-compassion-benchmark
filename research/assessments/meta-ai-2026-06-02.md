## Cycle Context

New signal: Meta's **Model Capability Initiative (MCI)** — software deployed on US employees' work machines (notified April 2026, deploying mid-2026) capturing keystrokes, mouse movements, clicks, screenshots, and clipboard activity to train AI agents for white-collar tasks. There is **no opt-out** on company-issued devices; EU employees are exempt because GDPR prohibits it. Workers have called it the "Employee Data Extraction Factory" / "very dystopian." This runs concurrent with ~8,000 layoffs.

## Scorability Ruling — CANDIDATE-INTERNAL-CONSISTENCY-SIGNAL (sub-threshold)

The MCI program is a genuine **I3 (internal consistency)** and **B5 (consent orientation)** concern: comprehensive, non-consensual, no-opt-out surveillance of internal stakeholders, with the GDPR-driven EU exemption demonstrating the company itself recognizes the practice would be unlawful in a stricter consent regime. This is real, un-priced labor/privacy harm.

However, against Meta AI's **already near-floor** internal-facing baseline (INT 1.8, ACC 1.5), the signal does not produce a scorable downgrade this cycle:
1. The published INT 1.8 / ACC 1.5 already reflect a poor internal-consistency and accountability posture; the MCI program reinforces rather than reveals a previously-priced-out pattern.
2. The program is **announced/deploying**, not yet independently audited or adjudicated; "privacy regulator attention expected" is anticipated, not filed (symmetric with FILED-BUT-UNADJUDICATED and ANNOUNCED-NOT-VERIFIED treatment).
3. A defensible INT 1.8→1.7 / B5-driven adjustment moves the composite by well under 1 point — below the 5-point proposal threshold and non-band-crossing.

Net: **confirm at 26.3 with an active watch.** If a privacy regulator (FTC, a state AG, or an EU DPA on any in-scope processing) opens a formal action, or an independent audit/credible whistleblower record establishes scope and harm, this converts to a scorable INT/BND downgrade.
