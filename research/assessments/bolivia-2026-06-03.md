## Cycle Focus: Six-week workers' insurrection and state repression

The Paz government faces a sustained workers' uprising (36+ days; road blockades peaking >100 points strangling La Paz). State response: arrest orders against COB executive secretary Mario Argollo and 24 union officials on **terrorism and narco-financing charges** (a court subsequently suspended Argollo's terrorism warrant); La Paz Fejuve secretary Justino Apaza **seized by hooded men in an unmarked, plateless vehicle** and ordered into 6 months' preventive detention. Cabinet reportedly split between dialogue and repression. [Common Dreams](https://www.commondreams.org/news/bolivia-government), [Wikipedia: 2026 Bolivian protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Bolivian_protests), [Latin Times](https://www.latintimes.com/bolivias-cabinet-split-over-mass-protests-between-dialogue-repression-597738)

### Methodology ruling: STATE-REPRESSION-IN-FLUX (scorable-but-sub-threshold)

Terrorism charges against labor leaders, an enforced-disappearance-style seizure, and preventive detention are genuine INT/ACC/BND/EMP pressures. However: (1) the situation is **in flux and partly contested** — a court suspended the Argollo terrorism warrant, and the cabinet is split dialogue-vs-repression, indicating residual rule-of-law friction rather than uniform directed repression; (2) the conduct **reinforces an already-low Developing profile** (ACC 1.8, ACT 1.9, EQU 1.9) rather than revealing a discrete adjudicated mass-harm. Modeled multi-dimension downgrade (ACC→1.6, INT→1.8, BND→2.1, EMP→2.2) reaches only −2.5 — sub-threshold and non-band-crossing. **Confirm-with-watch.** Converts to a scorable downgrade on a documented mass-casualty crackdown, martial law with verified rights violations, or confirmed enforced disappearances.
