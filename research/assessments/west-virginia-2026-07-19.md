## Cross-State Rule Disposition: AB3 Transparency (v3)

**Disposition: DECLINED — the precondition is not met. AB3 scored on the merits at 3.**

The rule caps AB3 at 3 only when a branch of government has escaped the records law or the watchdog itself has been attacked. Neither happened in West Virginia.

House Bill 3412 would have removed the Legislature from the definition of a public body. It passed the House on 2025-04-02 and the Senate on 2025-04-11. It then died, because the House never took up the message to concur in the Senate's changes. No self-exemption is in force.

The oversight body is intact. The Legislative Auditor's Performance Evaluation and Research Division has not been narrowed, defunded or stripped of independence protections, and in January 2026 it published a finding nobody compelled: the Office of Technology had failed for six years to build the cybersecurity framework the Legislature ordered in 2019. That satisfies limb (b), but limb (b) alone does not trigger the cap.

What HB 3412 attempted — removing the liberal-construction presumption, extending the response deadline from 5 days to 14, and adding search and retrieval fees — is a procedural and substantive weakening. Under the North Dakota and Idaho refinements, that is scored on the merits and not collapsed into the structural cap.

**Scoring effect:** none. AB3 is 3 on the merits and would also be 3 under the cap, so the composite is 35.6 either way. Applying the floor of 2 instead would give ACC 2.2 and a composite of 34.4.

## Key Findings

**1. West Virginia is beating the opioid crisis faster than the country is, and that is the strongest thing in this file.** Opioid overdose deaths fell 48.55 percent in the 12 months ending September 2025, from 3,969 to about 2,042. The national decline over the same period was 44.0 percent. The state also leads the nation on settlement spending transparency, with 94 percent of participating local governments filing reports. This drives the two highest subdimension scores in the assessment, S1 Root Cause Orientation and S5 Coalitional Compassion, both at 4 out of 5.

**2. The state repeatedly buys reform and does not install it.** After 14-year-old Kyneddi Miller starved to death in 2024, West Virginia contracted a 560,000 dollar structured intake tool for child protection, paid roughly 223,000 dollars, and cancelled it without ever using it. A state senator on the oversight committee did not know. This is why Systemic Learning scores 2 out of 5 despite genuine reform activity elsewhere.

**3. When money got tight, West Virginia funded the broadest program rather than the neediest people.** The Hope Scholarship voucher opened to all K-12 students in 2026 with 277 million dollars set aside. In the same budget, enacted Medicaid appropriations came in 107.1 million dollars below the Governor's own proposal, and 1,031 people sat on the disability waiver waitlist, 789 of them children.

**4. The state conceals rather than acknowledges when it is the one causing harm.** A federal magistrate judge found in November 2023 that corrections officials intentionally destroyed evidence in a jail-conditions case, and that the decisions came from the highest levels including the Commissioner. In 2024, court documents alleged the state edited the foster care ombudsman's reports. Validation scores 1 out of 5, the lowest score in the assessment.

**5. West Virginia is unlearning its own hardest safety lesson.** The 2014 Elk River spill left 300,000 people without safe tap water and produced a real tank-safety law. In March 2026 delegates voted 58-34 to roll back part of it, after the state's largest gas well owner asked them to. Combined with the absence of any state objection to the indefinite federal delay of the coal-mine silica dust rule, this puts Structural Critique at 1 out of 5.

**6. One thing is genuinely getting better inside the state's own workforce.** Child Protective Services vacancies fell from 27.3 percent in 2023 to 8.5 percent on 2025-09-01, and turnover fell from over 34 percent in 2021 to 15 percent in 2025. That is a structural fix that worked, and it survived a change of governor.

## Strongest Dimensions

- **Systemic Thinking (2.8 raw, 45 scaled)** — carried by the opioid settlement architecture. Money is aimed upstream at prevention and recovery, local governments hold a real share, and downstream demand fell measurably. Held back to 2.8 by Structural Critique at 1.
- **Awareness (2.6) and Action (2.6)** — West Virginia can see and count its problems. It publishes a child welfare dashboard, funds an independent legislative auditor, and mounted a response to the drug crisis that outperformed the national trend.

## Weakest Dimensions

- **Empathy (2.0 raw, 25 scaled)** — the lowest dimension. Driven by Validation at 1 out of 5, where the state destroyed evidence and allegedly edited an ombudsman's reports rather than treat harmed people's accounts as legitimate.
- **Integrity (2.2)** — announced reforms outrun delivered ones, and the state shifts costs onto its own employees while protecting tax cuts.
- **Equity, Boundaries and Accountability all at 2.4** — a consistent pattern of stated commitments that lack follow-through, offset by one genuinely strong equity fact in Medicaid expansion.

## Evidence Gaps

- **B2 Autonomy Preservation and B5 Consent Orientation** were scored at low confidence. No published outcome measurement of whether state help builds independence was found, and no evidence on informed consent practice was found. Both were scored conservatively at 2 rather than assigned a middling default.
- **EQ3 Bias Awareness** rests on outside research rather than state monitoring. No independent state bias audit with published corrections was located.
- **Small-state caution applies.** West Virginia has one statewide nonprofit newsroom pair (West Virginia Watch and Mountain State Spotlight) doing most of the accountability reporting. Thin coverage in a given area is not evidence of good conduct, and several subdimensions rest on fewer independent sources than a larger state would offer.
- **The largest unresolved matter is not yet decided in court.** The foster care class action goes to a bench trial in March 2027. Its outcome could move ACC, EQU and INT materially in either direction.

## Recommended Next Steps

West Virginia scores in the **Developing** band, meaning key structures exist but are inconsistent or reactive. Consider a [Certified Assessment](/certified-assessments) for a structured improvement roadmap. The three highest-leverage areas, based on the score profile:

1. **Close the announce-to-implement gap.** Publish, for every reform announced since 2024, whether it is operating today. Systemic Learning and Non-Performance both sit at 2 because of the same failure mode.
2. **Acknowledge before litigating.** Validation at 1 out of 5 is the single lowest score. The cheapest correction available is a policy that acknowledgment of harm structurally precedes investigation and legal review.
3. **Fund the measured need first.** 789 children on a disability waiver waitlist, next to a 277 million dollar universal voucher, is the clearest proportionality failure in the file.
