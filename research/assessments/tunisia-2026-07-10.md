## Why this matters (plain language)

Tunisia is going backwards. The country that led the Arab Spring and built a Truth and Dignity Commission has, under President Kais Saied, jailed its opposition, dismantled judicial independence, and expelled thousands of Black migrants in life-threatening conditions. On July 8, 2026, a court sentenced Sihem Bensedrine — the former president of that Truth Commission — to 25 years in prison. This assessment is Tunisia's first individual scoring; it lowers the placeholder score of 34.4 to an evidence-derived 23.8, keeping Tunisia in the Developing band but near its lower edge.

### Score Difference Analysis

The published 34.4 is a bulk-import placeholder (`last_assessed: null`) that had never been individually assessed. It applied a near-uniform ~2.375 profile that reads Tunisia as a broadly "some-mechanisms-but-inconsistent" state. Current evidence contradicts that on the accountability and equity axes specifically. The state has: (1) dismantled its own transitional-justice legacy and imprisoned the Truth and Dignity Commission's former president for 25 years (Jul 8, 2026); (2) shut its independent information-access authority; (3) suspended asylum and collectively expelled 12,000+ sub-Saharan migrants in life-threatening conditions with documented torture and sexual violence; (4) run mass "conspiracy" trials producing sentences up to 66 years. These are ACC and EQU floor-adjacent behaviors, not 2.5-level "genuine effort with gaps."

The dimensions that remain near the placeholder (AWR, ACT, BND, SYS at 2.2) reflect that Tunisia is NOT a collapsed or mass-atrocity state: basic health/education services continue, a statistical apparatus functions, and it participates in regional frameworks. This keeps Tunisia in the Developing band rather than Critical.

### Cross-peer calibration note

The May 23, 2026 digest explicitly named Tunisia (with Venezuela) as a "next-risk entity" under the JUDICIAL-REMOVAL/judicial-dismantling category, following Turkey's crossing into Critical at 17.6 for the same pattern. Tunisia's derived 23.8 sits above Turkey (17.6) and China (19.5), which is defensible: Tunisia's repression is smaller in scale and it retains more civil-liberties space (Freedom House 2026: Partly Free, 42/100; Civil Liberties 31/60) than those Critical-band peers. The gap to those peers is now proportionate rather than the ~15-point over-statement implied by the placeholder.

### Recommendation

The published 34.4 is overstated. A downgrade to 23.8 (Developing, no band crossing) is warranted, driven by documented accountability and equity failures. Change proposal generated (downgrade, medium confidence). This is a first-baseline reconciliation, not a peer-calibration relitigation.

## Key Findings

- Tunisia sentenced Truth and Dignity Commission ex-president Sihem Bensedrine to 25 years on July 8, 2026 — a signal that the state is criminalizing its own past accountability work.
- The state suspended asylum and collectively expelled 12,000+ sub-Saharan migrants (Jan-Apr 2025) in life-threatening conditions, with documented torture and dehumanizing sexual violence.
- Mass "conspiracy" trials produced sentences up to 66 years; opposition leader Rached Ghannouchi's sentence was raised to 20 years.
- Independent information-access authority shut down; RSF press-freedom rank fell to 129/180.

## Strongest Dimensions

## Weakest Dimensions
- EQU (1.6) and ACC (1.6): racialized refoulement plus the dismantling of accountability institutions are the two dimensions driving the score down.
