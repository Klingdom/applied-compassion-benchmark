# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: TIAA (Forensic Re-Assessment)

**Entity type:** Company (mutual / nonprofit-origin)
**Sector/Domain:** Finance / Retirement services for education, healthcare, nonprofit sectors
**Assessment date:** 2026-04-23 (forensic re-assessment; prior 2026-04-18 entry was itself a Gen-2-style uniform 4.5×8 artifact)
**Composite score:** 58.6/100
**Band:** Functional

**Trigger:** Designated forensic anchor for F500 Gen-2 audit. Published composite 97.5 (rank 1) = 4.5×8 uniform artifact. Evidence record does not support exemplary status.

**Material evidence against published composite:**
- **$97M SEC enforcement (2021)** — TIAA-CREF Individual & Institutional Services settled charges for "inaccurate and misleading statements" and failure to disclose conflicts to thousands of participants in TIAA retirement plans ([SEC](https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2021-123)). NY AG parallel $97M settlement ([AG James](https://ag.ny.gov/press-release/2021/attorney-general-james-announces-97-million-restitution-tiaa-customers-misled)).
- **Active ERISA class action** — Bryan Byrne v. TIAA alleges self-dealing and mismanagement affecting 28,000+ participants; in-house proprietary fund underperformed benchmark by 186% since 2009; participants charged higher fees than institutional clients ([AARP Foundation](https://www.aarp.org/press/releases/2025-9-4-aarp-foundation-joins-lawsuit-mismanagement-of-retirement-funds.html)).
- **AARP Foundation joined as co-counsel Sept 4, 2025** — extraordinary step; AARP rarely co-counsels litigation ([CPA Practice Advisor](https://www.cpapracticeadvisor.com/2025/09/11/aarp-foundation-joins-lawsuit-against-tiaa-for-mismanagement-of-retirement-funds/168816/)).
- **Whistleblower history** — pension warrior ED Siedle documented multiple whistleblower complaints re: TIAA fund governance ([Siedle substack](https://pensionwarriorsdwardsiedle.substack.com/p/aarp-foundation-joins-lawsuit-against)).
- **RePublic TIAA-deforestation investigation (2021-22)** — investment in Brazilian farmland linked to deforestation; only partially addressed.

**Offsetting positive evidence:**
- Nonprofit-origin mission serving 5M+ participants in mission-aligned sectors; strong historical reputation
- Participant-first governance frameworks
- 2021 settlement monies distributed to affected participants
- Investment stewardship reporting exists, ESG considerations embedded

**Scoring judgment:** The 2021 SEC/NY AG matter and the 2025 AARP Foundation co-counsel move are decisive EQ3/AB1/I2 anchors. Self-dealing fund structures that charged retail participants higher fees than institutional clients (same company, same fund family) fails EQ1 and I3 anchor 1-2. Mission is exemplary in intent; execution has sustained fiduciary-duty disputes spanning 15+ years. Composite lands at upper-Functional band. **Composite 58.6 vs. published 97.5 = -38.9 delta; band shift: exemplary → functional.**
