# Assessment: China — Ethnic Unity Law In Force, Day 4 (Double-Count / Forward-Trigger Screen)

**Composite:** 19.5/100 (CONFIRM) | **Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
China's Ethnic Unity Law took effect July 1. State Council officials confirmed on June 24 that it reaches outside China's borders: people and groups abroad can be held liable for "undermining ethnic solidarity." Amnesty International calls it a "legal blueprint for cultural erasure"; Germany's Foreign Ministry voiced "great concern." But four days in, there is still no documented Article 63 extraterritorial enforcement action. The law codifies an assimilation and transnational-repression policy that China's 19.5 already prices. Germany's statement is a third-party reaction, not new Chinese conduct.
