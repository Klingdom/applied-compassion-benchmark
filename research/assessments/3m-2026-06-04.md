## Assessment Basis — Two Government PFAS Suits in One Month

The 2026-06-04 window brings TWO sovereign PFAS actions against 3M:

1. **Australia, $1.43B (A$2B+), May 28 2026** — the largest claim ever brought by the Australian government, over firefighting-foam PFAS contamination at 28 military bases. The complaint accuses 3M of **withholding information about the risks** posed by the foam. Australia has already spent A$1.3B on impacts and paid A$408M in community settlements.
2. **Minnesota MPCA, filed May 2026 (Washington County District Court)** — alleging ongoing PFAS discharge from the Cottage Grove facility, with PFOS at ~310,000 ppt vs a 0.05 ppt state standard, and that a 2020 3M-disclosed discharge "relate[s] to discharges that continue to this day." Seeks up to $30,000 per violation per day.

### Methodology ruling: FILED-BUT-UNADJUDICATED (new harm) + ADJUDICATED-PRIOR-REPARATION

The two new suits are **newly-filed civil complaints with no liability finding or settlement** for the alleged new harm — placing the *new* allegations in the filed-but-unadjudicated tier (consistent with the cycle's treatment of UnitedHealth/CVS). Two factors keep the movement disciplined and sub-threshold:

- **Sovereign-enforcement density is real and reinforcing.** Two government plaintiffs (a sovereign state + a US state agency) in one month, plus 3M's **military-contractor-immunity defense** (it asked to move the MN case to federal court and disclaimed liability for military-sought products), is an AB1 deflection signal and an AB4 systemic-recurrence signal ("discharges that continue to this day" despite a 2018 $850M Minnesota settlement). This REVEALS un-priced correction-willingness failure.
- **But 3M has ALREADY made adjudicated/settled reparation** — the 2018 $850M Minnesota natural-resource settlement and A$408M Australian community settlements are reparative payments (AB5), which sit ABOVE the absolute-floor anchor and do NOT support flooring ACC to 1.0. The entity is not an active-perpetrator of mass casualty; the harm is environmental/historical with ongoing-discharge recurrence.

Modeled disciplined downgrade (ACC 1.5->1.25, INT 1.5->1.25, EMP 2->1.75 for liability-contesting deflection): **20.3 -> 18.0, delta -2.3, which would cross Developing -> Critical but is SUB the 5-point change-proposal threshold.** A maximally aggressive read (ACC/INT floored to 1.0) reaches -6.2 but is not defensible given the adjudicated prior reparation. **Confirm at 20.3 with boundary watch; no change proposal.**
