## Cycle Focus: UK CMA world-first AI Overviews publisher opt-out ruling (June 3 2026)

Under the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024, the UK CMA imposed a **world-first conduct requirement** ordering Google to:
- Let UK publishers withhold content from AI Overviews, AI Mode, Gemini and Vertex AI at directory and page level **without losing search ranking**;
- Allow opt-out from content use for model fine-tuning;
- Properly attribute publisher content with clear links in AI-generated results.
Google has **9 months to implement** and must file regular compliance reports. The ruling follows documented publisher traffic loss from AI Overviews. [Press Gazette](https://pressgazette.co.uk/platforms/google-regulation-uk/), [Washington Times](https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2026/jun/3/uk-orders-google-allow-publishers-opt-ai-scraping-search-summaries/), [TechHQ](https://techhq.com/news/google-ai-search-regulation-cma-conduct-requirement/)

### Methodology ruling: net effect of the CMA ruling

**Net-neutral; confirm at 40.0.** This is the only assessed entity this cycle where a NEW signal could plausibly drive *upward* movement, so it was scrutinized closely. Two offsetting reads:

- **Positive (future):** mandated publisher opt-out + attribution is a genuine EQU/BND/ACC-relevant remedy that, *if delivered*, improves Google's treatment of low-power content creators (signal-amplification, scope honesty).
- **Negative / discount:** the remedy is **COMPELLED, not self-initiated**. Under the methodology, creditable correction (AB2 / I2) is self-initiated *before* external pressure; a regulator-imposed order that follows an adverse finding (Google's AI Overviews harmed publishers until forced to stop) is the inverse pattern. It also has a **9-month runway with zero delivered effect yet** (ANNOUNCED-NOT-OPERATIONALIZED), and is jurisdiction-limited to the UK.

The compelled-remedy positive and the adverse-finding-disclosure negative offset. Modeled even a generous ACC 2.8 / BND 2.9 credit yields composite 40.9 (+0.9) — sub-threshold and non-band-crossing.

**BOUNDARY CASE FLAG:** Alphabet sits exactly at the **40.0 / 41 Developing-Functional boundary**. A verified, delivered publisher-opt-out remedy (post-implementation, ~9 months) plus EU/other-jurisdiction replication could push a future cycle across into Functional. Conversely, an adverse AI-Overviews antitrust finding elsewhere could pressure downward. Carry-forward, no proposal.
