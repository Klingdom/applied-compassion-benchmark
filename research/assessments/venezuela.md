## METHODOLOGICAL NOTE — REGIME-CHANGE RESET BASELINE

**This assessment treats post-Jan 3, 2026 Venezuela as a materially new governing entity.** On Jan 3, 2026, US special forces captured Nicolás Maduro, ending 12 years of authoritarian rule. Acting President Delcy Rodríguez and a transitional cabinet have taken office. Human Rights Watch and WOLA both formally characterize this as a "governance transition with uncertain trajectory" rather than continuity of the Maduro state. Per the scan's data-quality note and standard benchmark methodology for regime-change events, the pre-Maduro-capture score (4.4 composite) is **NOT** used as a prior. This is a **fresh baseline** anchored on Q1 2026 evidence, with structural/historical components still reflecting the Venezuelan state's durable institutional damage.

The score is expected to move materially over the next two quarters as: (a) the amnesty/prisoner-release program matures or stalls; (b) free elections are or are not scheduled; (c) the "repressive architecture" HRW flags is dismantled or preserved.

## Reset-Baseline Evidence Cluster (Jan 3 – Apr 19, 2026)

1. **Jan 3, 2026 — Maduro captured** by US special forces; Delcy Rodríguez sworn in as Acting President. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_United_States_intervention_in_Venezuela))
2. **Feb 19, 2026 — Amnesty law** passed covering political violence from 1999–present. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/19/americas/venezuela-political-prisoners-amnesty-law-latam-intl))
3. **Mar 8, 2026 — 659 political prisoners confirmed released**; hundreds still detained. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_political_prisoner_release_in_venezuela))
4. **Jan–Feb 2026 — El Helicoide detention/torture site ordered closed.** ([WOLA](https://www.wola.org/analysis/two-months-without-maduro-in-venezuela-democratic-transition-or-authoritarian-adaptation/))
5. **Jan 7, 2026 — HRW warns US intervention risks new rights disaster;** "repressive architecture remains largely intact." ([HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/01/07/venezuela-us-risks-rights-disaster))

**Net directional effect:** Large positive movement on ACC (AB1–AB5), EQU (EQ5), INT (I4), AWR (A1–A3). Still-Critical-band reflects that: (a) institutional damage from 12 years of Maduro misrule is structural; (b) the transition is fragile and reversible; (c) SEBIN, DGCIM, and colectivos remain operational per HRW; (d) no elections have been scheduled; (e) economic collapse, 7.7M+ refugees, and humanitarian emergency persist.

### Score Difference Analysis

The +13.6 delta reflects the Jan 3, 2026 regime change. ACC and INT show the largest upgrades (+21.9 each) because the Maduro-era score correctly captured zero accountability (anchor 1 across both dimensions), whereas the transitional government has taken three concrete 3-anchor actions (harm acknowledgment, course correction, values-aligned amnesty) in 3.5 months. AWR, EMP, SYS upgrades are smaller because structural apparatus (SEBIN, DGCIM, colectivos) remains intact. BND and ACT/EQU remain near Critical-floor levels because the humanitarian/economic emergency is durable.

### Recommendation

**Upgrade, band-change flagged for review.** Proposed composite (18.0) remains in Critical band (≤20) but is within 3 points of the Developing boundary. Confidence is **medium** due to transition fragility. If Q2 2026 sees: (a) free elections scheduled, (b) SEBIN/DGCIM formal dissolution, or (c) UN transitional-justice mechanism, expect band crossover to Developing. If transition reverses, expect return toward published baseline.
