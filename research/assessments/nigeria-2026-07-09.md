## Within-Window Evidence (2026-06-25 to 2026-07-09)

- **Worst northern hunger in nearly a decade.** 36.2M nationally food insecure; 17M+ across nine northern states at crisis/emergency/catastrophe IPC levels (up ~2M since last projections). Borno: 3M+ acutely food insecure, 750,000+ in severe hunger, 10,000+ at catastrophe level. WFP can support only 740,000 of 6.2M in need across three northeast states — 5.5M without lifesaving assistance; WFP needs $89M over six months. (WFP, 2026-07; GlobalSecurity; Daily Post, 2026-07-09)

## Analysis

This is a worsening of the structural, decade-plus northeast famine crisis already captured in the Jun-19 band-crossing downgrade (21.9 → 18.0). The ~2M faster-than-projected deterioration deepens the existing ACT/SYS/ACC deficits but is a continuation, not a new discrete own-conduct step-change. Much of the acute driver is conflict-and-access-related; the state-attributable governance failure is already priced. Sub-threshold.
