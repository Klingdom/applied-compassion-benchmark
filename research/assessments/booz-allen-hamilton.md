## Trigger — Sovereign-Counterparty Trust Revocation + Active Civil Litigation

1. **Treasury cancellation (January 26, 2026)**: U.S. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent announced cancellation of all 31 Treasury contracts with Booz Allen ($4.8M annual / $21M total obligations), citing the firm's 'past insider threat incident' (Charles Edward Littlejohn, 2018-2020 IRS contractor) ([Treasury press release](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0371); [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/26/trump-tax-records-treasury-cancels-booz-allen-contracts.html); [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/26/nx-s1-5689010/treasury-cancels-booz-allen-contracts-irs-leak)). Stock fell more than 10% on the announcement.
2. **April 2026 federal court ruling**: Maryland federal judge refused to dismiss class action against Booz Allen and IRS over Littlejohn breach affecting 406,000 taxpayers ([Law360](https://www.law360.com/articles/2461238/judge-keeps-irs-booz-allen-in-lawsuit-over-tax-data-leak)).
3. **March 24, 2026 supplementary suit**: Senator Rick Scott (R-FL) filed separate civil action against Booz Allen ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/24/rick-scott-booz-allen-littlejohn-tax-leak-trump)).

This is a **first-of-kind sovereign-counterparty governance signal**: not a settlement, not a fine, but the principal U.S. Treasury severing all contracts after multi-year IRS-CI/DOJ investigation. Tier-1 evidence directly applicable to INT and ACC.
