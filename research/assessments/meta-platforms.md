## Reassessment Context

Meta was assessed Apr 15 at composite 10.9. Three major legal developments concentrated in Mar 24 – Apr 13, 2026 post-date that assessment and warrant a small downward revision:

1. **Mar 24: New Mexico $375M jury verdict.** First state to prevail at trial against a major tech company for child sexual exploitation harms. Jury found Meta violated state consumer protection law, engaged in "unconscionable" trade practices, concealed what it knew about child sexual exploitation, and was negligent — thousands of violations, each counting separately ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/jury-reaches-verdict-in-meta-child-safety-trial-in-new-mexico.html), [NM DOJ](https://nmdoj.gov/press-release/new-mexico-department-of-justice-wins-landmark-verdict-against-meta/)). A second phase begins May 2026.
2. **Apr 13: Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court ruling.** Section 230 does not shield Meta from state lawsuit over Instagram's harmful design features targeting children ([Insurance Journal](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/east/2026/04/13/865454.htm)).
3. **California MDL trial** against Meta, TikTok, Snapchat, and Google (YouTube) began January 2026; 2,465 total claims pending.

Cumulative effect: two simultaneous successful judicial findings (NM trial, MA appellate ruling) that Meta engaged in deceptive and harmful conduct toward children. These materially strengthen the "harm acknowledgment failure" pattern (AB1), "validation refusal" pattern (E4), and "values misalignment" (I4) already in the Apr 15 assessment.
