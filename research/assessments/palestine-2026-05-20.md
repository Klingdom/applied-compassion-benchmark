## Methodology Question Under Human Review

- The Compassion Benchmark scoring framework measures **how the entity treats those it serves**.
- For Palestine, the entity's ability to serve its population is fundamentally constrained by occupation, blockade, and external aggression — not by domestic compassionate-governance failures comparable to floor-state perpetrators (Russia, Israel-as-perpetrator, North Korea, Myanmar, Sudan).
- The RS=20.0 floor designation would treat Palestine as comparable to floor-state perpetrators; the INDEX=25.0 Developing designation treats it as a constrained-capacity state.

**The Microsoft Azure finding strengthens the case for INDEX=25.0:** it provides further evidence that Palestine is a target of mass-surveillance conduct rather than a perpetrator of it.
