# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Microsoft

**Assessment date:** 2026-06-07
**Composite score:** 65.3/100 (CONFIRM — no scorable movement)
**Band:** Established

## Recommendation: CONFIRM at 65.3 (positive-watch)

This cycle's only genuinely novel analytical case. The scanner flagged the June 8, 2026
announcement that Microsoft will tighten human-rights reviews for national-security customers
after an internal inquiry into the Israeli military's use of Azure found violations of
Microsoft's own human-rights standards, terminated Unit 8200 access, and will create a new
Human Rights Review Board with mandatory pre-contract human-rights impact assessments.

### Methodology ruling: COMPELLED-REMEDY-NOT-SELF-CORRECTION (sub-threshold positive)

The decisive question under the methodology is whether this is genuine self-correction
(AB2 anchor 5: "self-initiated correction before external pressure") or correction under
pressure (AB2 anchor 2–3). The evidence is clear that it is the latter:

- The review and the Unit 8200 termination resulted **directly from the Guardian / +972 /
  Local Call investigative exposé of August 2025**, which revealed Unit 8200 storing intercepted
  recordings of millions of Palestinian phone calls on Azure. Microsoft President Brad Smith
  confirmed services were disabled after the reporting surfaced evidence supporting it.
  (NBC News; 972mag; Business & Human Rights Resource Centre.)
- The Covington & Burling external review **"never looked at what was in the military data,"**
  relying solely on business records, citing customer privacy — a deliberate scope limitation
  that constrains the depth of the remedy. (the-decoder.com.)
- The action followed staff departures and sustained employee/advocacy pressure, not a
  self-initiated ethical audit.

This places the conduct at **AB2 anchor 3** ("≥1 significant course correction based on harm
evidence") rather than the floor — a genuine, materially costly contract termination and a new
governance structure — but **not** anchor 4–5 (self-initiated, structurally-prior correction).
It is a modest, real improvement on a compelled basis, narrower than the headline implies.

### Why no proposal (sub-threshold)

The strongest defensible upgrade is ACC 3.8 → 3.9 (course correction + standing up a Human
Rights Review Board with pre-contract HRIA) and INT 3.0 → 3.1 (a publicly unflattering action
taken — I2 — albeit reputation-pressured). Modeled via the canonical formula:

- Published ACC 3.8 / INT 3.0 → **65.3**
- ACC 3.9 only → 65.6 (+0.3)
- ACC 4.0 / INT 3.1 (most generous) → 66.6 (+1.3)

All scenarios remain inside Established and far below the 5-point proposal threshold. The
remediation is real but (a) compelled, (b) scope-limited (no review of the underlying data),
and (c) a governance promise whose execution is unverified. The June 8 board is a forward
commitment, not yet a demonstrated structural change with outcomes. Scoring conservatively,
confirm at 65.3.

## Key Findings
- The Azure-Israel remediation is **compelled (post-exposé), scope-limited (no data review),
  and partly prospective** — a real but modest AB2/AB3 positive, not self-initiated correction.
- Strongest defensible move is +1.3 (ACC 4.0 / INT 3.1) — **sub-threshold**; confirm at 65.3.
- Convert to scorable UPGRADE only on **verified execution** of the Human Rights Review Board
  with a published HRIA that demonstrably blocks/exits a harmful contract (anchor 4–5 conduct).

## Evidence Gaps
- No assessment of the underlying surveilled data; remedy adequacy to harmed Palestinians is
  unaddressed (AB5 floor). Board outcomes not yet observable.

## Recommended Next Steps
Functional/Established: Advisory Support could translate the new governance commitments into
verifiable, audited human-rights due-diligence with outcome reporting.
