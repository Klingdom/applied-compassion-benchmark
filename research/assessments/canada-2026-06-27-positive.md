# Special Upward Assessment: Canada

**Composite:** 84.6/100 (CONFIRM — no upgrade warranted)
**Band:** Exemplary

## Why it matters (plain language)
Canada's Carney government has pledged billions for Indigenous health, education, and clean drinking water, and named the first Indigenous Minister of Indigenous Services. These point the right way on Canada's weakest area — equity for Indigenous peoples. But the money is announced, not yet spent, and the appointment and new council came before this window. Announced money is not realized care.

## Symmetric-rigor screens
- **Correct baseline:** Deep-scan lists "79.6"; the published composite is **84.6** (rank 3, exemplary). Headroom and delta use 84.6.
- **Realization (DECISIVE):** The $4B+ health and $4.6B First Nations water figures are Spring-Update *pledges* — not yet disbursed. Under the realized-own-conduct standard, undisbursed pledges are not credited.
- **Window:** First Indigenous Minister (Mandy Gull-Masty) appointed ~April 2026 and the National Council for Reconciliation established March 2026 are both out-of-window. Within-window is the June 21 National Indigenous Peoples Day *statement* — words, not a realized act.
- **Magnitude:** Even taken at face value the scan estimates +2 to +4 — below threshold — at a country already in the exemplary band.

## Disposition
CONFIRM at 84.6. Delta 0.0. No change proposal. **Watch:** if the First Nations water and health funds are actually disbursed and reach communities, re-test EQU (the lowest dimension, 3.5) in a future cycle — disbursement would be realized own-conduct.
