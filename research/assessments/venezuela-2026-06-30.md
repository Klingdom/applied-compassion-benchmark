# Special Event Assessment: Venezuela — Earthquake Day 6 (Response Screen)

**Composite:** 18.0/100 (CONFIRM — net sub-threshold both directions)
**Band:** Critical

## Why it matters (plain language)
Day 6 of the June 24 twin earthquakes (Mw 7.2 + 7.5). The toll rose to **1,719 dead** (from 1,450), **46,600 reported missing**, 3,150+ injured; NYT warns of a "substantial undercount." The quake itself is a natural disaster — exogenous, not own-conduct. Only the government's **response** is scorable, and it remains genuinely mixed.

## Baseline provenance (§3e-bis(1))
- Live baseline **18.0**, rank **151**, Critical. The 18.0 was set/confirmed across the Jun 25–29 earthquake-response cycle. The June 28 cycle already corrected a stale cache (−13.6pt) to the live 18.0. Baseline verified live, not assumed.

## The scorable question: response conduct, not the quake
- **Mild positive (blocks downgrade):** broad aid acceptance continues. UNICEF first 20-tonne shipment (Jun 27) and a second 48-tonne shipment due today; US OFAC General License 60 authorizing relief, with the **US commitment now doubled to $300M** (from $150M). The government is not blocking aid at the source — this is what separates Venezuela from the floor cluster.
- **Negative (blocks upgrade):** slow/opaque domestic relief, access restrictions, and a fast-rising missing-persons figure (46,600) against possible undercount allegations. These remain capacity/transparency concerns within the 14-day window, not yet documented deliberate obstruction.

## §3e-bis screening (both directions blocked)

- **(3) Directionality — NO upgrade:** surfaced on a negative-theater event; rising US aid pledges are donor conduct, not Venezuelan compassion conduct.
- **(4) Double-count / threshold — NO downgrade:** mixed response nets sub-threshold; no documented deliberate obstruction/diversion clears 5 points.
