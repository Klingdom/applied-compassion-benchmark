## Cycle Focus: Two NEW state-AG enforcement actions

This cycle introduces two genuinely new sovereign-enforcement signals against UnitedHealth:

1. **Massachusetts AG $100M Medicaid (MassHealth) fraud suit** (filed Suffolk Superior Court, May 29 2026). AG Campbell alleges a **decade-long systematic upcoding scheme** (2015–2025) in the Senior Care Options (SCO) dual-eligible program: classifying members as behavioral-health "Level 2" without diagnosis; representing members as needing daily skilled-nursing services they did not receive; coaching nurses to assess patients' "worst days." Treble damages would reach ~$300M. UnitedHealth calls it "meritless." [Mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov/news/ag-campbell-sues-united-healthcare-for-defrauding-masshealth-out-of-100-million), [STAT](https://www.statnews.com/2026/05/29/united-healthcare-sued-massachusetts-ag-alleges-100-million-upcode-fraud-medicaid/)

2. **Arizona AG algorithmic price-fixing cartel suit** (filed June 1 2026) naming MultiPlan + 8 insurers including UnitedHealthcare, alleging a shared out-of-network repricing algorithm fed by confidential claims data suppressed provider payments — disproportionately harming rural and mental-health providers. [AZ AG](https://www.azag.gov/press-release/attorney-general-mayes-sues-multiplan-and-major-health-insurers-alleged-price-fixing), [Healthcare Dive context](https://www.healthcaredive.com/news/massachusetts-sues-unitedhealthcare-medicaid-fraud-upcoding/821594/)

### Methodology ruling: does this cross from filed-but-unadjudicated to scorable?

**No — but it materially raises sovereign-enforcement density.** Both actions are newly-filed civil complaints (not adjudicated, no liability finding, settlement, or injunction). Under the established **FILED-BUT-UNADJUDICATED** rule, allegations are not independently scorable. Two distinguishing features were weighed:

- These are **state-AG (sovereign) enforcement actions**, a higher evidentiary tier than the private class litigation already in the file, and they **converge with the already-priced DOJ civil+criminal Medicare-Advantage billing probe**. The MA suit alleges a documented decade-long *pattern*, not an isolated act.
- However, they **reinforce rather than reveal** the near-floor profile: ACC is already 1.125 (3.1 scaled — effectively floor), EMP 1.25, EQU 1.25. The harm class (upcoding seniors / suppressing provider payments) is the same conduct family the published score already prices at near-floor.

Modeled downgrades confirm sub-threshold: even an aggressive multi-dimension move (ACC→1.0, EMP→1.125, INT→1.25, EQU→1.0) yields composite 8.2 (−2.0), below the 5-pt threshold and non-band-crossing (stays deep Critical). A single-dimension ACC→1.0 move is −0.4.

**ANNOUNCED-NOT-VERIFIED re-affirmed:** the ~50-insurer prior-authorization reform pledge remains announced/in-progress, not verified for delivered effect — not creditable as an upgrade.

**Net: confirm at 10.2 with watch.** Converts to a scorable downgrade only on adverse adjudication/settlement of the DOJ probe or either AG suit. No change proposal.
