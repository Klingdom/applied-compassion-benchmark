## Key Evidence (within-window)

- Cuba's national grid collapsed on 2026-07-14, the third nationwide blackout in under 10 days and the fifth total outage of 2026, leaving roughly 10 million people without power. Daily blackouts in July average 20 to 24 hours, with some areas exceeding 72 consecutive hours. Source: https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/14/cubas-power-grid-collapses-again-triggering-third-blackout-in-10-days

- The collapse is driven by an oil blockade imposed by the United States that deepened fuel shortages; oil imports fell to effectively zero in January 2026 for the first time since 2015. Source: https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2026/jul/14/cuba-plunged-3rd-nationwide-blackout-2-weeks-fuel-runs-low/

- Human Rights Watch's count of 700+ political prisoners and Prisoners Defenders' record of 1,306 as of 2026-07-09 were both reaffirmed this cycle. No new record count beyond the Jul 9 figure already logged in the 07-16 cycle.

- Scattered protests continued across Havana, with residents banging pots and pans and shouting "turn on the lights."
