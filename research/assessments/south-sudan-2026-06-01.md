## Summary of cycle ruling
**Floor confirmation at 0.0.** South Sudan is already at the absolute floor; the IPC Phase 5 escalation cannot move it lower, and the evidence reinforces the floor. IPC April-July 2026 projection: 7.8M (56% of the population) in IPC Phase 3+; **73,300 in IPC Phase 5 Catastrophe — a 160% increase**; credible famine risk in four counties across Upper Nile and Jonglei; 2.2M children acutely malnourished (700,000 projected severe). This is NOT a pure natural-disaster overlay: the IPC and HRW (May 6, "civilians starving in South Sudan's conflict areas") attribute the catastrophe substantially to escalating SAF/SPLM-IO conflict cutting communities off from food, markets and services, with constrained humanitarian access — i.e. conflict-driven starvation with state culpability, consistent with the active-perpetration floor (Sudan, Myanmar). Luakpiny/Nasir County at famine risk; 142,000 displaced from Akobo; 100,000 fled to Ethiopia. WFP requires $266M urgently.
