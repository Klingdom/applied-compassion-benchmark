## Methodology Note — Dismantler Magnitude Calibration

Per the precedent established by Croatia (May 21, -7.8 from mid-Functional 48.4) and Slovakia (May 22, -5.5 from bottom-Developing 39.1):

> When the same Tier-1 institutional classification is applied to multiple entities at different starting compositions, magnitude scales as: full magnitude at baseline that was prima facie inconsistent with the classification; reduced magnitude (60–75%) when baseline already partially priced the concern.

Bulgaria published 35.9 is mid-Developing — closer to Slovakia's position (39.1 bottom Developing) than Croatia's (48.4 mid-Functional). Apply -5.5 by analogy to Slovakia: published 35.9 already partially prices rule-of-law concerns, but the formal Liberties.eu Dismantler classification adds marginal evidence. Final magnitude: -5.5 (parity with Slovakia).
