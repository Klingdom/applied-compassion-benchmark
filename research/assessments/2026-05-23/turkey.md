# Compassion Benchmark Assessment: Turkey (2026-05-23)

**Trigger:** Turkish appeals court annulled the CHP party congress on May 21, removing opposition leader Özgür Özel and the entire CHP leadership. HRW issued a named condemnation May 22. On May 23, Turkish police detained 13 people in a probe of the opposition congress. Istanbul stock market dropped 6% (circuit breaker triggered).

## Triggering Evidence Window

- **2026-05-21:** Turkish appeals court annulled the CHP party congress that elected Özgür Özel. Effectively removed the entire main opposition party leadership through judicial action. CHP deputy parliamentary group chair characterised it as "a coup carried out through the judiciary."
- **2026-05-21:** Borsa Istanbul circuit breaker triggered after 6% drop on the news. Capital markets pricing the event as a structural governance shock.
- **2026-05-22:** Human Rights Watch issued a specific institutional statement ("Türkiye: Court Removes Leadership of Main Opposition Party"), explicitly naming the action as an attack on multi-party democracy.
- **2026-05-23:** Turkish police detained 13 people in a probe of the opposition congress that chose the ousted leader. Suggests downstream criminal enforcement of the judicial annulment.
- **Carry-forward context:** Imamoglu (the AKP's principal 2028 presidential rival) imprisoned March 2025 → April 2026 X account block → CHP X account block → CHP leadership judicially removed. The arc is now structural, not episodic.

## Methodology-Novel Adjudication: "Judicial Removal of Opposition Party Leadership"

The scanner flagged this as a methodology-novel event type. I rule as follows:

**Ruling — distinct weighting convention warranted.** Judicial removal of an opposition party's governing structure is qualitatively distinct from:
- Individual political arrests (Imamoglu) — affects a person, not the party's leadership succession.
- Electoral manipulation — affects an event, not the party itself.
- Speech restrictions (X account blocks) — affects expression, not institutional capacity.

It is the procedural inverse of a free and fair election: rather than the electorate choosing the opposition leadership, the judiciary chooses the *absence* of an opposition leadership. The closest precedent in the benchmark is the Dismantler classification (Croatia -7.8, Slovakia -5.5), which captures a sustained EU-classified rule-of-law dismantling pattern. The Turkey event is comparable in magnitude but more acute (a single discrete judicial act vs. an accumulated pattern).

**Applied weighting:** Treat as a primary GOV-dimension event with bright impact on BND (boundary against single-party capture), ACC (no accountability for judicial overreach), INT (judicial instrument used to entrench AKP power — values inversion), and secondary impact on AWR/EMP/ACT/SYS via the systemic-attack-on-multi-party-democracy framing.

**Precedent established:** Judicial removal of opposition party leadership warrants -4 to -6 composite delta when (i) the judiciary's action is institutionally final (no successful appeal pathway), (ii) the government does not move to restore the status quo ante, and (iii) downstream criminal enforcement (police detentions) follows. All three conditions are met for Turkey as of 2026-05-23.
