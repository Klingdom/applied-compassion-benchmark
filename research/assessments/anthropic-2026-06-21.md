## Assessment basis & attribution

Window event (Tier 4): On Jun 12, the US Commerce Dept (BIS) issued an export-control directive forcing Anthropic to disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all foreign nationals, including its own non-citizen employees, citing a Mythos 5 jailbreak. Anthropic received the directive at 5:21pm, disagreed with its scope, but complied; it warned the standard "would essentially halt all new model deployments for all frontier model providers." Less-powerful Claude models (incl. Opus 4.8) were unaffected. Separately, Jun 17 G7: Amodei and DeepMind's Hassabis called for a US-led AI coalition.

**Attribution:** The model withdrawal was an action taken BY the US government ON Anthropic. Per the victim/perpetrator rule, this government action is not scored against Anthropic. What is scorable is Anthropic's OWN conduct in response: it complied with a national-security directive while transparently publishing its disagreement and articulating industry-wide risk (anticipatory awareness, structural critique, transparency). This conduct is broadly neutral-to-mildly-positive.

**Why confirmation.** Nothing in the window reveals new adverse Anthropic conduct toward those it serves. The published 59.1 (high-Functional, rank #13) already reflects an AI lab with comparatively strong safety governance, transparency, and harm-anticipation practices. The episode marginally supports AWR/A5 (anticipatory) and SYS/S4 (structural critique) but within the existing range. Score confirmed at 59.1.
