## Key Findings

- Why it matters: Boston Dynamics builds humanoid and mobile robots and now sits inside Hyundai. On a suffering-reduction framework its record is defined by safety engineering and its prior refusal to weaponize general-purpose robots, offset by looming job-displacement questions.
- Hyundai completed its full buyout on July 16, 2026, and plans up to 30,000 Atlas units a year at its Georgia plant from 2028 (GoodCarBadCar, July 2026) - a commercial move, not a compassion harm.
- The genuine compassion-relevant risk is worker displacement from mass humanoid deployment, which slightly lowers Systemic Thinking but is prospective, not a realized in-window harm.
- The May proposal's ~25-point drop on partnership grounds is not substantiated; the rigorous review reproduces the established-band baseline.

## Strongest Dimensions

Awareness (AWR, 4.0) and Integrity (INT, 3.8): Boston Dynamics has a documented anticipatory-safety engineering culture and a public stance against weaponizing general-purpose robots that has held under commercial pressure.

## Weakest Dimensions

Systemic Thinking (SYS, 3.2) and Equity (EQU, 3.0): large-scale humanoid deployment raises unresolved worker-displacement and distributional questions the company has not publicly addressed.

## Evidence Gaps

As a robotics R&D firm, Boston Dynamics has a thin direct 'people it serves' surface; several dimensions are inferred from engineering-safety and ethics conduct rather than served-population testimony. Its no-weaponization pledge is well documented historically but was not re-anchored with a fresh in-window primary source; the July Ulsan union strike over Atlas is Hyundai-workforce conduct, not Boston Dynamics' own conduct, and was excluded per screening rules.
