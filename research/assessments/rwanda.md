### Score Difference Analysis

The most significant downward drivers are the March 2026 US sanctions on senior RDF officials for supporting M23 rebels in DRC, the UN Fact-Finding Mission findings of crimes against humanity, and the immediate violation of the Washington Accords. The published score likely reflected Rwanda's development achievements without adequately weighing the cross-border military operations and their humanitarian consequences (7+ million displaced in DRC). Freedom House and OHCHR evidence on press freedom and political repression also drives significant downgrades in empathy and boundaries.

### Recommendation

The published score of 41.8 appears overstated by approximately 12 points given current evidence. Rwanda's development achievements remain genuine but the scale of documented harm in DRC, sanctions by multiple governments, and the systematic suppression of domestic dissent warrant a significant downgrade from Functional to Developing band.

## Key Findings
- US Treasury sanctioned 4 senior RDF officials in March 2026 for supporting M23 rebels; UN found reasonable grounds for crimes against humanity
- Rwanda signed Washington Accords then immediately violated them; M23 captured Uvira days after signing
- Genuine development achievements (poverty reduction from 60% to 30%, near-universal health insurance) offset by severe political repression and cross-border military aggression
- Press freedom severely restricted; 99.2% election result demonstrates absence of genuine political consent
- 7+ million people displaced in DRC due to Rwanda-backed M23 operations
