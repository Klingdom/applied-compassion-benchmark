## Context: In-Window Evidence (Federal Executive Conduct)

Two in-window federal actions reinforce the already-Critical published score (17.5):
1. **ICE death-reporting cessation (4–5 June 2026):** Acting ICE Director David Venturella issued a memo eliminating the requirement to report deaths occurring within 30 days of release from custody — reversing a Biera-era policy that required reporting and investigation. This is a direct transparency/accountability regression amid 18–19 detainee deaths in custody in the first five months of 2026 (49 since the current administration began).
2. **Medicaid work-requirement restrictions (3 June 2026):** Last-minute narrowing of the medical-frailty exemption that CBPP projects could push eligible people off coverage; up to 7 million could lose coverage by 2028.

These reinforce rather than newly establish the degraded state captured by the published Critical score. Composite moves only -1.3, below the change-proposal threshold. **Confirmation.**
