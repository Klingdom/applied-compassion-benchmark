## Context for this cycle

The scanner flagged Anthropic as the cycle's most material new development: on **June 12, 2026** the US Commerce Department issued an export-control directive requiring Anthropic to suspend its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models for all foreign nationals (including foreign-national Anthropic staff), citing national security and a reported jailbreak. Anthropic disabled both models for all customers on June 12-13 to ensure compliance and published a transparent explanation.

**Methodological treatment:** This is a *government-mandated* shutdown, not a failure of Anthropic's own compassion conduct. The benchmark scores the entity's behavior — recognizing, responding to, and reducing suffering — not external sentiment about a regulatory action. Anthropic's *conduct* in the event (prompt public disclosure, an apology to customers, disclosure of the government's stated reasoning, a stated effort to restore access) is mildly positive on Accountability/Transparency. The event does not constitute evidence of reduced compassion and therefore does not drive a downgrade.
