### Recommendation

The delta of 5.0 points is at the threshold for a change proposal. The FTC settlement for deceptive pay practices represents significant new Tier 5 evidence (federal enforcement action) that was not available when the published score was set. The forced nature of the correction (10-year monitoring, annual FTC reporting) indicates the published scores for Action and Accountability were too generous. Recommend marginal downgrade.

## Key Findings
- FTC and 11 states reached $100M settlement for deceptive pay practices in Spark Driver gig delivery program
- Walmart showed drivers inflated base pay and tip amounts, then quietly reduced pay after acceptance
- Falsely claimed 100% of customer tips went to drivers
- Deceptive practices persisted since 2021 (5 years) without internal correction
- 10-year FTC compliance monitoring and annual reporting now required

## Strongest Dimensions
- Awareness (31.3), Action (31.3), Boundaries (31.3), Systemic Thinking (31.3), Integrity (31.3): Moderate developing-band scores reflect genuine associate programs offset by gig worker exploitation

## Weakest Dimensions
- Empathy (25.0) and Equity (25.0): Gig workers treated as expendable; two-tier system with vulnerable workers receiving worst treatment
- Accountability (25.0): Correction only under federal enforcement; no voluntary harm acknowledgment

## Evidence Gaps
- Independent employee satisfaction data beyond ESG self-reporting
- Spark Driver outcomes post-settlement not yet available
- Supply chain labor conditions not independently verified in this assessment
