## Triggering Evidence Window (last 14 days)

- April 6, 2026: 46-year-old "tote runner" collapsed and died on the second level of the loading dock at PDX9 Troutdale OR fulfillment center. Employee accounts (anonymized, multiple) indicate management ordered employees to continue working around the body for more than an hour (TechCrunch / Western Edge / WSWS / Yahoo Finance / Moneywise).
- April 13: Death broken publicly by Western Edge after one week of non-disclosure.
- Oregon OSHA classified incident as "not work-related" for reporting purposes — OSHA explicitly noted this classification covers reporting requirements only, not causation or whether workplace contributed.
- Documented contributing factors: tote runner staffing recently reduced (increased physical burden), soundproof curtains installed limiting airflow (heat speculation per employee testimony).
- Historical context: 2019 Reveal investigation found PDX9 had the worst injury rate of 23 major Amazon distribution centers analyzed (2018 OSHA data: >25% of workers injured on the job).
- March 2026 (partially-offsetting positive): Teamsters union forced Amazon to formally honor right to strike via settlement.
- EFF May 7 joint letter explicitly named Amazon (alongside Microsoft and Google) demanding human-rights accountability steps.
