## Assessment basis & attribution

Window event (Tier 2-3, pre-adjudication): Apple's Towson, MD store (its first unionized retail location) closed Jun 20 as planned. The IAM filed an NLRB unfair-labor-practice charge alleging Apple denied unionized workers transfer rights that it provides at non-union store closures; Apple disputes the charge. 54 Congressional Labor Caucus members demanded an NLRB investigation; the Maryland Governor criticized Apple. The NLRB investigation is proceeding; any remedy would follow months later. This is Apple's second consecutive cycle on the I3 (internal consistency) watch.

**Attribution:** This is Apple's OWN conduct toward internal stakeholders (retail workers). Scorable on INT/I3 and ACC. However, the NLRB charge is an **allegation that is not yet adjudicated**.

**Why confirmation.** Per pre-adjudication discipline, an unadjudicated ULP charge does not by itself move a score below threshold. The conduct reinforces a watch on INT/I3 but does not produce a ≥5-point composite movement, and there is no new adjudicated finding this cycle. Apple's published 59.4 already reflects a strong-Functional profile with a known labor-relations discount on ACC/EQU (both at 3.0). Score confirmed at 59.4; I3 watch flag continued (2nd consecutive cycle — escalate to a focused INT review if a third adverse cycle occurs or the NLRB issues a finding).
