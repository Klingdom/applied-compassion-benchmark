Thermo Fisher Scientific was surfaced by the 2026-07-20 scan on a reported layoff announcement and a broader pattern of WARN Act notices. ## Key Findings

- Thermo Fisher is closing sites steadily, and workers have little protection. A WARN notice covers 421 permanent separations at Weaverville, North Carolina, in a workforce with "no union representation or employee bumping rights available."
- The company does give long notice, which matters. Layoffs run from 2026-12-31 through 2027-12-31, giving affected staff roughly one to two years of warning rather than the legal minimum.
- Its community work is real and upstream. Thermo Fisher reached more than 89,500 students through STEM programming, logged over 120,000 colleague volunteer hours, and works on health equity in more than 100 low- and middle-income countries.
- The scan's specific trigger was wrong. The Middleton, Wisconsin layoff attributed to Thermo Fisher appears to belong to Sun Nuclear Corp; the restructuring pattern is real but the cited site is not.
- The 12.1 point gap is a measurement artifact. The placeholder gave a flat 3.5 across all eight areas; genuine review found normal variation from 2.8 to 3.4.

## Strongest Dimensions

Systemic Thinking (raw 3.4) and Action (raw 3.2). Root Cause Orientation, Long-Term Impact and Coalitional Compassion each score 4 of 5. Thermo Fisher invests in the scientific talent pipeline it depends on and extends diagnostic access into low- and middle-income countries through public-private partnerships rather than pure commercial channels.

## Weakest Dimensions

Boundaries (raw 2.8), Empathy (raw 2.8) and Integrity (raw 2.8). Self-Sustainability, Perspective-Taking and Internal Consistency each score 2 of 5. Repeated closures across multiple states, decided on the basis of "aligning with current customer demand" and executed without worker representation, indicate decisions made about staff rather than with them.

## Evidence Gaps

No employee-review aggregate or engagement survey result was verified. Thermo Fisher's CSR figures come from its own reporting and, apart from the CDP submission, are not independently assured, so Transparency (AB3) is credited at 4 on disclosure breadth rather than on external verification. Fourteen of forty subdimensions carry low confidence.

## Calibration Note

SCAN TRIGGER CORRECTION plus MEASUREMENT GAP. First, a data-quality note the reviewer needs: the 2026-07-20 scan attributed a Middleton, Wisconsin layoff to Thermo Fisher Scientific. That appears to be a misattribution — the Middleton closure in that period belongs to Sun Nuclear Corp., a Mirion Medical company. The scan's underlying claim about a Thermo Fisher restructuring pattern is nonetheless correct, just sourced to the wrong site; the verified events are Weaverville/Asheville NC (421 employees), Franklin MA, and Princeton NJ (113 employees). Second, Thermo Fisher has never been individually assessed and its published 60.9 is a bulk-import placeholder with a near-flat 3.5. The -12.1 gap is largely that arithmetic difference, consistent with the documented scrutiny bias. The verified conduct — repeated site closures with 12 to 24 months of WARN notice, alongside a substantial CSR programme — does not describe an institution that fell 12 points. Recommendation is flag-for-review for cohort calibration, not a conduct-based downgrade.
