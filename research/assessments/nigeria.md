## Context: In-Window Review
The Cadre Harmonisé projects 34.7 million Nigerians (27 states + FCT) food-insecure June–August 2026, with over 15,000 people in Borno State (Dikwa, Kaga, Kalabalge) at risk of famine (Phase 5). Drivers: insurgency/banditry, weakened rural economies from food imports, floods/dry spells, and displacement. The government's Permanent Secretary called the data "a wake-up call for coordinated and sustained action" and points to the National Agricultural Growth Scheme and National Food Security and Nutrition Emergency Plan. The underlying analysis (Cadre Harmonisé, Nov 2025; FAO Sahel outlook Jan 2026) predates the strict 14-day freshness window, so it confirms rather than moves the published Developing score. **Confirmation, no proposal.**
