## Assessment Basis

The 2026-06-04 evidence is a severe but **continuation** picture: ~34.7M projected Crisis (IPC Phase 3+) in the June–Aug lean season; ~3.5M IDPs (≈10% of all African displacement); 5.9M in need in the NE (Boko Haram/ISWAP); widespread NW banditry (2,938 kidnapped Jul-2024–Jun-2025, Zamfara worst); Middle-Belt farmer/pastoralist conflict; Lake Chad Basin crisis (UN News Jan 2026; ACAPS; HRW World Report 2026).

### Methodology ruling: PRICED-CONTINUATION / NON-STATE-DRIVEN

The dominant drivers are **non-state armed groups** (Boko Haram, ISWAP, bandit networks) and chronic structural fragility — conditions already encoded in the 21.9 Developing baseline (EQU 1.5 / EMP 1.75 / SYS 1.75 laggards reflecting state inability to protect/serve at-risk regions). The June 2026 figures confirm sustained pressure but do not REVEAL discrete new state-attributable mass-harm beyond the priced fragility. No documented deliberate state obstruction of humanitarian access of the Sudan/South Sudan kind; the harm is insurgency- and criminal-violence-driven against a low-capacity state. This is PRICED-CONTINUATION — confirm.

Modeled downgrade for the lean-season deterioration is sub-threshold and would not be defensible as state-attributable revelation. Confirm at 21.9 with boundary watch (1.9pt above Critical).
