## Methodology Adjudication — Decision: SUB-THRESHOLD ACCUMULATOR (Tier C)

**Reasoning:**

1. **House passage is a precursor signal, not yet law.** OBBBA still requires Senate passage and presidential signature. Per scored-event methodology, the strongest signal would be enactment. House passage at 215-214 indicates a credible legislative path but is not itself a welfare-reduction event.

2. **CBO-projected 7.6M coverage losses is a *forecast*, not a realized harm.** If the bill becomes law, the loss is enforceable; until then, projected losses are accumulation evidence, not realized events.

3. **DOGE $175B savings figure is contested/political; not yet operationalized as a measurable benefit-reduction.** Many DOGE-cited "savings" reflect contract cancellations, not direct beneficiary impact. Treat as accumulation, not apply trigger.

4. **Sub-threshold accumulator approach:**
   - Record OBBBA House passage as sub-threshold accumulator weight (~-1.5 directional).
   - Record DOGE $175B + $9B rescission request as additional accumulation weight (~-1.0 directional).
   - Combined directional accumulator: -2.5, sub-threshold (5pt apply threshold).
   - **Promote to formal apply when Senate passes OBBBA OR when bill is signed into law.**

5. **Boundary-proximity discipline.** US at 25.0 sits 5pt above Critical boundary. A premature -5 apply on House passage alone could force band-crossing on what is not yet realized law. Sub-threshold accumulator preserves room for the formal Senate/enactment-trigger apply.

**Classification: Tier C — sub-threshold accumulator, documented hold.**
