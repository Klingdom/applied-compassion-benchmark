## Candidate 1: LinkedIn-to-Entity-Page Conversion Bridge

**Priority Score: 14**
Impact 5 + Strategic 5 + Learning 3 + Confidence 4 − Effort 2 − Risk 1

### Problem / Opportunity

The founder is already generating organic engagement on LinkedIn by sharing daily update excerpts — entity names, score changes, band movements, and evidence. The engagement proves there is an audience for this content. But the current LinkedIn post has nowhere specific to send readers. If a reader sees "OpenAI fell from 60.8 to 30.5 in three nights" and wants to know more, the logical destination is a page that shows OpenAI's score, the evidence behind it, and a clear path to buying the AI Labs index report. That page does not exist. The site has index pages (e.g., /ai-labs) that display the ranking table, and the updates page that shows the daily briefing in full, but there is no entity-level landing page that serves as a shareable URL for a single company or country.

Over four nights the pipeline has assessed 59 entities. The daily briefing already contains the entity slug (e.g., "openai", "meta-platforms", "ford-motor") as a data field. The content is there; the destination is not.

### Proposed Experiment

Build static entity detail pages at predictable URL patterns — for example `/company/openai`, `/country/iran`, `/city/new-york-city`. Each page surfaces:
- The entity's current published score and band
- The two most recent assessment headlines and evidence bullets from the daily JSON (already structured in the `scoreChanges` and `confirmations` arrays)
- The relevant index's Gumroad purchase CTA ("See Ford Motor's full dimension breakdown — Fortune 500 Index, $195")
- Newsletter signup

Start with the 20 highest-profile entities that have already been assessed (OpenAI, Amazon, Meta Platforms, Johnson and Johnson, Boeing, Alphabet, xAI, Ford Motor, Target, CVS Health, UnitedHealth Group, Israel, Iran, United States, Russia, Ukraine, Sudan, NYC, Finland, Denmark). These are the names most likely to be Googled after a LinkedIn post or a news cycle.

In LinkedIn posts, replace the generic site link with the entity-specific URL. This converts the post from a broadcast into a traceable, measurable funnel.

### Traffic / Revenue Mechanism

- **Traffic:** "Target compassion score" and "OpenAI accountability rating" are zero-competition search queries. A static page optimized for the entity name + "compassion score" or "compassion benchmark" would rank within days for navigational searches driven by LinkedIn exposure.
- **Revenue:** The entity page is the tightest natural handoff to a Gumroad purchase. A reader who came from a post about OpenAI is already pre-qualified for the AI Labs Index ($195). The CTA on the entity page can be direct: "This assessment is drawn from the Top 50 AI Labs Index. Full dimension scores, methodology, and all 50 labs — $195."
- **Newsletter:** The entity page is also the right signup moment — "Get notified when OpenAI's score changes" is a concrete subscription offer, not a generic newsletter pitch.

### Evidence Basis

- Daily JSON already contains `slug`, `headline`, `evidence[]`, `publishedScore`, `assessedScore`, `band`, and `index` fields — all the data needed to render the page.
- Four daily files cover 59 distinct assessed entities. The content engine will produce more every night.
- APPLIED_CHANGES.md shows 20 applied changes, each with an entity name and index reference, ready to be the first wave.

## Candidate 3: LinkedIn Flywheel — "Most Surprising Finding" Weekly Format Post

**Priority Score: 12**
Impact 4 + Strategic 4 + Learning 4 + Confidence 4 − Effort 2 − Risk 2

### Problem / Opportunity

The founder has organic traction on LinkedIn but the format and cadence are described as "daily update excerpts" — that is, derivative from the research output rather than crafted for the channel. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards posts that generate saves and comments in the first 60 minutes. A raw daily digest excerpt may get reads but may not trigger the engagement signals that expand reach. The four daily digests in the repo contain a recurring section called "insights" that is analytically sharper than the score changes themselves — these are the structural findings that make the benchmark credible and quotable. The Apr 18 insight about the 18:1 downgrade-to-upgrade ratio is a standalone finding worth a post. The Apr 17 insight about the Accountability dimension being the most consistently weak dimension across all assessed entities is another. These are the ideas that get shared and discussed.

### Proposed Experiment

Run a weekly LinkedIn format test for four weeks. Each Friday, publish one "Most Surprising Finding This Week" post structured as:

- Hook: one counterintuitive number or result ("We assessed 59 institutions this week. 58 received downgrades or no change. Here is why that ratio matters.")
- Insight: the structural finding in 3-4 sentences drawn directly from the `insights` array in the weekly digest
- Evidence anchor: two entity examples with score and evidence (drawn from that week's `scoreChanges` data)
- CTA: one link — either the weekly briefing page (`/updates`) or the relevant index page, depending on the dominant story

Compare engagement (LinkedIn impressions, saves, comments) for this format against the daily excerpt format over 4 weeks. The variable being tested is whether a once-weekly synthesized insight outperforms daily raw-findings posts.

### Traffic / Revenue Mechanism

- **Traffic:** A single high-performing LinkedIn post can drive 500-2,000 site visits for a niche B2B account with early traction. "Most Surprising Finding" format is designed to maximize saves (which LinkedIn treats as a strong engagement signal) because the insight is worth returning to.
- **Revenue:** Each weekly post links to a specific page. If the week's dominant story is about AI labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI), the post links to /ai-labs or /purchase-research, with the AI Labs Index as the featured product. The post is the top of a three-step funnel: LinkedIn post → index page or updates page → Gumroad checkout.
- **Learning value:** This test establishes whether LinkedIn is a meaningful acquisition channel before investing in more sophisticated content operations. Four weeks of controlled comparison between two formats produces a decision-ready answer.

### Evidence Basis

- The `insights` array in each daily JSON already contains the synthesized structural findings. The Apr 15 insight on "structural accountability deficit" and the Apr 18 insight on "18:1 downgrade-to-upgrade ratio" are strong standalone LinkedIn content without additional research work.
- The daily update pipeline produces new insights nightly. The raw material cost of running this experiment is near zero.

## Candidate 4: "Right to Context" Institutional Outreach to Scored Entities

**Priority Score: 8**
Impact 4 + Strategic 3 + Learning 4 + Confidence 2 − Effort 3 − Risk 2

### Problem / Opportunity

The benchmark has now applied material score changes to high-profile institutions: OpenAI (−30 points in 3 days), J&J (−20.9), CVS Health (−18.7), Target (−10.0), Ford Motor (−5.9), Amazon (band change to Critical), United States (−10.5), Israel (−19.0). Some of these entities have PR and government affairs teams who will eventually find the benchmark — either through LinkedIn posts that mention them, Google indexing, or referrals from journalists or researchers who cite the scores.

The independence policy (CLAUDE.md) clearly prohibits score changes or suppression for payment. What it does not prohibit is a paid "right to context" service — a structured mechanism for an entity to submit evidence that may not have been in the research scan, which is reviewed according to the benchmark's published methodology and either incorporated (if it meets evidentiary standards) or rejected (with documented reasoning). This is distinct from purchasing a score change. It is purchasing access to the review process.

The revenue model would be a flat engagement fee ($5,000-$15,000) for a formal evidence review cycle. The outcome is independent — the score may improve, stay the same, or decline further based on the submitted evidence. The service is sold as transparency access, not outcome purchase.

### Proposed Experiment

Draft a one-page "Institutional Evidence Review" service description. Position it as: "Your score is based on publicly available evidence. If you believe material evidence was missing from our scan, this is the formal channel to submit it." Publish it at a URL (e.g., /evidence-review or as part of /services). Do not actively cold-email entities yet — instead, add a subtle "Are you this entity? Submit evidence for review" link on each entity page (see Candidate 1) or the entity's row in the index. Let inbound interest arrive organically.

Track whether any inquiries arrive within 90 days of publishing entity pages.

### Traffic / Revenue Mechanism

- **Revenue:** A single institutional engagement at $5,000-$15,000 exceeds the revenue from dozens of individual Gumroad sales. For the institutional buyer motion the founder has identified as the highest-revenue target, this is the most direct path to that revenue tier.
- **Traffic:** The service description page adds an indexable page that may rank for queries like "dispute compassion benchmark score" or "compassion benchmark evidence review." Low volume but high commercial intent.
- **Learning value:** Whether any inbound inquiries arrive, and from which entities or sectors, reveals which corners of the benchmark are most commercially sensitive and most likely to generate institutional interest in a subscription or ongoing relationship.

### Evidence Basis

- APPLIED_CHANGES.md shows 20 material changes to well-resourced entities with active PR operations. Multiple have band changes (OpenAI, J&J, CVS Health, Israel, Amazon).
- The independence policy is clear and protects against the main risk. The service is explicitly not selling score outcomes.
- PENDING_CHANGES.md flagged several entities (Alphabet, UnitedHealth, Amazon) for imminent further downgrades driven by pending legal events. Institutional awareness of incoming score pressure is a motivation to engage with the evidence review process before the next assessment cycle.
