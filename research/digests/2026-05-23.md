## Impacts and Insights

This cycle's most analytically significant output is structural rather than entity-specific: the benchmark has now established three distinct methodology categories in a single week (Dismantler magnitude-scaling, dual-signal weighting, JUDICIAL-REMOVAL) plus two new promotion protocols (FORWARD-TRIGGER-FIRED, confirming-event uplift). The methodology is doing something important — it is becoming specific enough to handle edge cases that no generic rubric could anticipate, while remaining consistent enough to produce comparable outputs across different event types and entity classes.

The Turkey and Vanuatu cases together make a single analytical point: governance quality is not a fixed property of an entity's region, religion, or economic development level. Turkey is a G20 economy, a NATO member, and a Council of Europe state. Vanuatu is a small Pacific island nation with a population of roughly 340,000. They cross band boundaries in opposite directions on the same day based on evidence of what their governing institutions actually do — not on what category of entity they are. This is the benchmark's core methodological commitment expressed in the most concrete possible form.

The EU Dismantler cohort sweep (Croatia, Slovakia, Bulgaria in four nights) demonstrates a different point: when a Tier-1 institutional source produces a classification covering multiple entities, the benchmark can systematically apply the classification with consistent methodology across the cohort. The result is not mechanical — magnitude still depends on starting position — but it is systematic. The benchmark is now producing sector-level analytical output that rivals what formal rule-of-law monitors produce over full annual cycles.

The Amazon finding surfaces a practical concern about the relationship between published scores and assessed positions. Amazon's published composite (17.8) has been below its assessed position (14.1, then 12.8) for two consecutive cycles before the formal apply. The gap between published and assessed positions — when accumulators are active but sub-threshold — is a structural tension in the methodology that becomes most visible when confirming events arrive. The confirming-event promotion path is the right resolution: it prevents single-event over-sensitivity while still applying when patterns are genuinely documented across multiple independent sources.
