## Impacts and Insights

- **Tonight is a useful counterpoint to 2026-07-20's finding that flat placeholders systematically understate risk.** Four placeholder entities assessed for the first time tonight (Azerbaijan, Algiers, Ibadan, Tucson) all held close to their published scores, unlike five Fortune 500 placeholders on 2026-07-20 that moved 10+ points. This suggests the placeholder-understatement problem may be sector- or band-specific (concentrated in never-assessed Fortune 500 entities carrying flat mid-band scores) rather than universal — worth testing with a larger sample before drawing a firm conclusion.
- **Screening rule 3 discipline is visibly working, not just theoretically sound.** Two of tonight's ten countries (Iran, Palestine) were surfaced on negative within-window evidence about harm done to them by external actors, and in both cases the assessor correctly declined to move the score. This is the cleanest same-night demonstration of the rule operating in both directions (a state actor and a non-state territory) seen in recent digests.
- **Municipal governments are being assessed as institutions, not as proxies for a single incident.** Algiers, Delhi, Ibadan, and Tucson each had one dated tragic or contentious event in the window (a fire, a protest crackdown, an ethnic clash, a mass shooting), and in each case the assessment explicitly weighed that single data point against the city's broader institutional capacity (civil-protection systems, welfare infrastructure, Housing First programs) rather than letting the incident dominate the 40-subdimension profile. This is the intended design and is worth naming as a positive pattern.
- **Evidence gap:** Iran's near-floor score continues to rest primarily on standing documentation (2,159 executions in 2025) rather than fresh within-window own-conduct evidence, since the only within-window Iran event was the US strike campaign, which is not scoreable against Iran. Pakistan's Operation Shaaban toll (91 by Jul 16) remains reported only by security sources, now well into its confirmation streak without independent verification.
