## Second Finding — New Methodology Rule Articulated: Day-3 Sustained-Conduct Dimensional-Credit-Under-Sub-Threshold-Composite Rule

**Methodology version 1.2 is now in the record.**

## Impacts and Insights

**The ceasefire strategic-format-exploitation finding expands the methodological vocabulary for scoring non-kinetic bad-faith conduct.** Prior to this cycle, the bad-faith-INT category was applied primarily to documented false denials (claiming compliance while conducting strikes). The ISW stockpile-window finding adds a structural dimension: using a harm-reduction format to increase harm capacity. This distinction matters for scoring future ceasefires, prisoner exchanges, and other nominally humanitarian formats where the dominant actor may comply superficially while exploiting the format's constraints on the other party.

**The Day-3 Sustained-Conduct rule resolves a real-world measurement problem that was previously handled inconsistently.** Before v1.2, an entity that produced consistent moderate improvement — never crossing the 5-point materiality threshold in any single cycle — would accumulate no scoring record of that improvement. The carry-forward mechanism means that three cycles of +2.5 dimensional pressure are now equivalent to one cycle of +7.5 evidence, which should be correctly recognized. For entities like Ukraine (active conflict with variable ceasefire behavior) this is particularly important: the 3-day breach-free record is a real behavioral signal that deserves preservation in the scoring record even when it does not produce a composite upgrade.

**Two new boundary-watch entities (Comoros and Vanuatu) emerge from the Southern Africa / Pacific rotation cluster.** Combined with the Pacific boundary cluster (Timor-Leste, Kiribati, Marshall Islands at 39.1) and Pakistan's ongoing boundary watch (20.3), the pipeline now has five entities simultaneously near band thresholds. This is a structural feature of the rotation queue reaching the mid-range of the countries index — many entities in the 20-40 composite range have genuine uncertainty about their correct band placement, and first-baseline assessments at medium confidence should be expected to generate boundary-watch designations at above-average rates in this phase.

**The backfill completion reveals a structural observation about pipeline throughput.** Across 4 days, 49 first-baselines were completed alongside high-priority monitoring for the ceasefire and inauguration windows. The daily capacity of 10-15 entities is sufficient to maintain rotation for 1,160 entities at approximately a 90-120 day full-rotation interval, but only if emergency re-queues remain a small fraction of total capacity. If the May 15 Anthropic assessment generates follow-on monitoring requirements (as the Pentagon AI complex suggests it might), the backlog risk is primarily in the high-priority queue, not in the rotation queue.
