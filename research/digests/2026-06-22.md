## Methodology Note — Bolivia False Positive: Baseline-Provenance + Directionality Screening (Cycle of June 22)

**What was proposed:** Bolivia upgrade from 6.3 (Critical) to 15.9 (Critical), delta +9.6. The proposal's premise was that 6.3 predated the Nov-2025 democratic transition and that the new Paz government's conduct warranted a regime-change reset analogous to the Venezuela special case.

**Why it was rejected:**

The baseline-provenance check establishes that the 6.3 was NOT inherited from the prior MAS administration's collapse — it was driven DOWN to 6.3 under the elected Paz government by Paz's own conduct:

- **Jun 8, 2026**: Paz government enacted a codified-impunity law authorizing military force against protesters.
- **Subsequent to Jun 8**: 10+ protesters killed, 365+ arrested, terrorism charges brought against union leaders under Paz's authority.
- **Jun 20, 2026**: 90-day military state of emergency declared.
- **Within window (Jun 22)**: 17 deaths including 7 from blocked medical access; Air Force relief-flight crash killing 6.

Within-window directionality is uniformly negative — no positive countervailing conduct that would anchor an upgrade. The Venezuela analogy fails because Venezuela's transition government (Rodríguez) actually released 659 political prisoners; the Bolivia transition government enacted new impunity law and deployed the military against protesters.

**Anti-false-positive discipline applied:** Under screening rule 3e-bis (baseline-provenance check), a proposal that asserts "the current score reflects the prior regime's conduct" must be verified against the actual scoring history. Here, the score was driven down by the current regime's documented conduct. The proposal fails that verification.

**What this means for Bolivia's status:** Bolivia remains at 6.3. The score is accurately calibrated to the Paz government's conduct. The Jun 8 codified-impunity law and the 90-day military state of emergency are the primary anchors. If the Paz government reverses the impunity law, lifts the state of emergency, and releases detained union leaders, a re-assessment would be warranted. Until then, 6.3 is accurate, not stale.
