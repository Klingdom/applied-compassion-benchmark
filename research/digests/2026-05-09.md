## Impacts and Insights

**Hungary illustrates the discontinuous-jump scoring pathway.** Incremental institutional reform under a stable government tends to produce sub-threshold deltas that accumulate slowly toward band crossings. A government transition that installs a demonstrably different administration on compassion-relevant dimensions can produce a multi-point delta in a single assessment cycle — because the accumulated gap between the prior administration's posture and a new standard closes abruptly at the transition point rather than gradually. The +9.4 Hungary result is not an anomaly; it is the expected output of the pipeline applied to a day-1 inauguration event after a contested multi-year prior-administration period.

**The Senegal math-discipline catch has methodological implications.** Two consecutive cycles have now surfaced reconstruction discrepancies that changed the scoring outcome: Pakistan (rotation-state 15.3 vs. canonical 20.3 — prevented using an incorrect base score) and Senegal (staging 40.6 vs. reconstruction 37.5 — converted a near-boundary sub-threshold result into a clean band crossing). The pattern suggests that staging formulas and rotation-state composites should be treated as provisional inputs, not authoritative values, until dimension-level reconstruction validates them. The assessor's math-verification discipline is functioning as a quality gate that the pipeline design depends on.

**The dual floor-conduct documentation cycle (Russia + Israel) raises a structural observation about the floor cluster's evidentiary function.** The floor cluster was originally conceived as an ongoing conduct record for entities where the score cannot move further downward. The accumulation of conduct documentation across multiple cycles serves a different function: it builds an evidentiary basis for floor-exit criteria. An entity that exits the floor will face a challenge to the authenticity of any positive signals given the prior record. The richer and more recent the negative conduct record, the harder it is to argue for floor exit on the basis of a single positive signal. Russia and Israel are both accumulating conduct categories rapidly enough that floor-exit criteria — even if genuinely met at some future point — would face a high evidentiary burden.

**Five consecutive rotation first-baselines confirm at Functional band.** Cook Islands, Cyprus, Croatia, São Tomé and Príncipe, and Nepal all land at 48.4-50.0. Combined with yesterday's five (Palau, Bhutan, Monaco, Namibia, Samoa at 50.0-53.1), the pipeline has now assessed ten consecutive rotation first-baselines that all confirm at the Functional band midpoint. This is not coincidence — the rotation queue at ranks 52-57 contains countries selected by the published composite ranking, which placed them in the 48.4-53.1 range. The confirmations validate both the published composites for this cohort and the pipeline's ability to reconstruct cleanly on first-baseline assessments of small and mid-sized states with limited in-window news coverage.
