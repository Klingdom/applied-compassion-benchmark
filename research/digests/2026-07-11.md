## Methodology Notes

### Proposal Reaffirmation vs. Proposal Duplication — Tunisia, Day 2

When new within-window evidence corroborates an already-open change proposal without altering the proposed figure, the correct handling is reaffirmation (logged in the proposal file's `reaffirmation_log`), not a duplicate proposal. Tonight's Tunisia case is the first test of this mechanism: the HRC 62nd session close (Jul 8) and further Bensedrine sentencing detail (a ~$600M fine alongside the 25-year term) are both genuinely new, dated facts, and both are fully consistent with the existing 23.8 basis. The published score remains 34.4, unchanged, pending founder review.

### Calibration Flag vs. One-Off Reset — Bolivia (NEW) and Mali-Burkina Faso (18th Day)

A calibration flag is distinct from a scoreable change: it identifies a possible cross-peer inconsistency for coordinator/meta-level review rather than resolving it unilaterally within a nightly assessment. Bolivia's flag (6.3 may sit too low for an electoral democracy in an austerity crisis relative to mass-atrocity-adjacent Critical-band peers) is raised tonight for the first time; it joins the Mali-Burkina Faso flag (Mali 12.5 vs. Burkina Faso 6.3 for comparably documented state-collapse conduct), open 18 consecutive days with no resolution timeline set.

### Reversed Positive Retraction — Yemen

When a previously logged positive-direction watch flag fails to materialize, it must be explicitly retracted, not silently dropped. Yemen's largest-ever prisoner exchange (logged as an upward-watch in the 2026-07-10 digest) collapsed on its scheduled start day; tonight's assessment explicitly retracts the upward-watch designation rather than simply omitting mention of it, so the public and internal record accurately reflects that the anticipated positive event did not occur.

### Near-Floor Arithmetic Ceiling — Iran

Iran's published composite (2.5) sits only 2.5 points above the absolute floor (0.0). Even severe within-window intensification of an already-priced pattern (a multi-decade-high execution rate) cannot produce a ≥5-point move, because the maximum arithmetically possible downward move is 2.5 points. This is distinguished from a substantive judgment that the executions "don't matter" — the near-floor ceiling is a structural limitation of the scoring mechanism, not an evidentiary one.

### Rotation-State Integrity Correction — Afghanistan

Afghanistan's rotation-state rank (189) had drifted from the published index rank (182); corrected tonight to mirror the published value (composite/band already matched at 0.0/critical). This is the same bug class previously documented for El Salvador, Mali, Uganda, Myanmar, Tunisia, and Somalia — rotation-state drift affecting rank only, with composite/band already correct.
