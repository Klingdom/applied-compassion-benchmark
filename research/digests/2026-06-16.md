## Impacts and Insights

Tonight's cycle is structurally unusual: a single positive lead in a landscape of Critical-band confirmations and floor reinforcements. Bangladesh's upgrade illustrates the benchmark's asymmetric evidence standard in both directions. Positive credit requires verification through an independent international monitoring body (ITUC here), structural gap closure (the 20-worker threshold change), and consistency with the monitoring body's own retained assessment of limitations (Rating 5 is not a clean pass — it's a verified partial improvement). This three-part standard is why positive upgrades are rarer than confirmations: they require more specific evidence than ongoing harm documentation.

The allegation-vs-adjudication three-case confirmation is analytically the most instructive finding for the AI-labs and Fortune 500 methodology. The framework is tested most cleanly when three cases land in the same cycle at different evidence tiers: pre-adjudication investigation (OpenAI), pre-adjudication charge (Apple), adjudicated finding (Amazon). The three-case pattern will be referenced in future methodology documentation as the definitional illustration of the evidence hierarchy.

Bolivia's confirmed 6.3 is the cycle's quiet structural signal. Applied June 14, confirmed June 16 — the two-cycle confirmation closes the proposal arc and opens the floor-designation conversion watch. The June 16 assessment finds no new military deployment incident, which is the expected pattern after a threshold-crossing application: the law was enacted June 9, the score was applied June 14, and the subsequent cycle confirms stability unless a new deployment event occurs. Bolivia now sits in the deep-near-floor cluster alongside DRC (2.3) and CAR (4.7).

The Sahel freshness-rule architecture's limitation is most visible during the lean-season peak: four countries with worsening humanitarian conditions are confirmed at prior-cycle scores because no country-specific in-window conduct evidence has arrived. This is the methodology working correctly, but it creates a structural lag. The Nigeria Phase 5 situation is the most acute: 15,000 people at catastrophic food insecurity, a WFP funding gap, and a 1.9pt boundary margin are all present simultaneously — and the freshness rule is holding the score because no Nigerian government-specific conduct event has occurred within the 14-day window to trigger a new assessment.
