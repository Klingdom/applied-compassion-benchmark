## Impacts and Insights

**The math-hygiene cluster's cross-band extension changes how the discrepancy pattern should be interpreted.** As of May 4, the hypothesis was that composite derivation issues were concentrated in the upper band — a systematic compression in how Exemplary/Established composites were originally calculated. The May 5 addition of two Critical-band entities with inverse discrepancies (published lower than reconstruction) complicates this framing. There are now at least two distinct discrepancy types: upper-band published-higher-than-reconstruction, and lower-band published-lower-than-reconstruction. The data integrity question is no longer "was the Exemplary/Established composite formula applied differently?" but "were multiple composite formula variants applied across different bands or different entity cohorts?" The answer to that question requires direct inspection of the original scoring database, not cycle-by-cycle assessment inference.

**The floor cluster's qualitative deepening this cycle illustrates a general pattern in the pipeline: what floor entities accumulate over time is not composite decline (they cannot go below zero) but evidentiary specificity.** Each documentation cycle adds precision to the floor-exit criteria record and raises the evidentiary threshold for floor removal. Israel's floor record now includes four distinct conduct categories with documentary sourcing from Amnesty International, Al Jazeera, Democracy Now, and formal state diplomatic protests from two G20 member countries. Myanmar's record now includes a quantified 4-year airstrike trajectory from a UN Special Rapporteur final report — arguably the highest-credibility international human rights documentation mechanism. The evidentiary bar for floor removal is not static; it rises with each documentation cycle.

**The competing-ceasefire pattern in Ukraine represents a novel diplomatic structure that does not fit cleanly into prior scoring categories.** A coordinated cessation of hostilities — both parties agreeing to the same window and terms — would warrant a positive composite signal. A unilateral declaration by one party would be scored as a political gesture without humanitarian weight. A dual-unilateral declaration with adversarial framings — each party declaring its own ceasefire on different terms at overlapping windows — is something different: it is the form of ceasefire diplomacy without the coordination that gives ceasefire periods operational humanitarian meaning. The bad-faith-ceasefire rule correctly captures the scoring logic, but the May 9-10 verification will determine whether either window produced a de facto cessation at the field level, which would have practical humanitarian weight regardless of political framing.

**The AI labs safety-floor cluster — DeepSeek, Mistral, xAI/Grok — represents a methodologically distinct entity type from the rest of the AI labs index.** Most AI labs entities in the index are scored across all eight dimensions with varying profiles. These three entities share a common structural characteristic: the documented absence of functional safety safeguards in one or more categories is not a dimension-level weakness but a systemic-level failure. DeepSeek's 0% harmful prompt blocking is not a "low BND score" — it is an absence of the safeguard function entirely. The methodology may warrant a formal sub-category distinction for entities with documented systemic safeguard absence versus entities with measured but weak safeguard performance.
