## Methodology Innovation — 'external-accountability-reversal': The Framework's First Positive-Conduct Category

**Every conduct category introduced into the Compassion Benchmark framework prior to tonight documented harmful conduct. Tonight's new category, applied to Hungary, documents positive conduct. This is a methodology evolution moment.**

All prior new categories — strategic-format-exploitation (May 11), post-format-offensive-surge (May 12), drone-warfare-as-primary-civilian-kill-mechanism (this cycle), and earlier categories for state capture, denial engineering, and systematic access restriction — were negative. They added to floor-entity records or reinforced downgrade proposals.

'External-accountability-reversal' is structurally different. It applies when an institution actively reverses a prior posture of non-compliance with or withdrawal from an established external accountability mechanism. Three criteria must be met:

1. A prior documented non-compliance or withdrawal posture
2. An enacted policy reversal (not merely stated intent)
3. A practical effect on the accountability mechanism's operational reach

Hungary meets all three. The prior posture: Orbán's refusal to enforce ICC warrants during Netanyahu's April 2025 Budapest visit, followed by Hungary's ICC withdrawal announcement. The enacted reversal: Magyar's government policy that ICC-warranted leaders will be arrested if they enter Hungary — an institutional policy posture as of inauguration. The practical effect: Hungarian territory is now operationally closed to Netanyahu and Gallant. ICC enforcement footprint contracts by one EU member state.

**The pairing rule:** When applied to a country with ICC obligations, external-accountability-reversal generates secondary positive credit on the INT dimension (credited in Hungary's proposal, producing the ACT and INT dimensional uplift). When the reversed accountability mechanism is the ICC, the reversal also generates collateral evidence in the floor records of entities subject to outstanding ICC warrants — not as a scoring adjustment, but as an external accountability signal documented in the INT-dimension conduct record.

This category can be applied in the future to any institution that reverses non-compliance with a multilateral accountability mechanism: sanctions compliance, human rights treaty reporting, environmental monitoring cooperation. The Hungary/ICC case is the triggering application and the definitional anchor.
