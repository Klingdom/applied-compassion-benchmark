The three-floor simultaneous monitoring-suppression pattern (Gaza/UNRWA, Russia/OVD-Info, Sudan/funding gap) is a methodological observation rather than a scoring event. But it points to a structural challenge: the benchmark's evidence methodology depends on independent monitoring capacity. At the absolute-floor level, the entities being scored are precisely those most actively suppressing that capacity. This is not a solvable problem within the current methodology, but it is worth naming as an ongoing limitation in the assessment of floor-designated entities.
