The practical implication: the pipeline is working correctly, but the zero-proposal count overstates the degree to which the institutional landscape is stable. The most important action item from tonight is not a score change — it is the South Sudan hard-deadline escalation and the methodology-note decision for the floor-limitation cluster.

### Theme 3 — Floor-Limitation Cluster: Methodology Decision Now Overdue

Five entities (xAI 2.2, Palantir 6.6, Sudan 0, South Sudan 0, Israel 8.8) are accumulating new material evidence each night with zero expressive composite movement. The cumulative gap between what has been documented and what the published scores can express has been growing for 7 consecutive nights.

The three methodology options proposed by the assessor:

(a) **Permanent-floor designation framework**: Formally designate entities at floor with a distinct status marker (e.g., "CF" for confirmed floor) that signals the score is a ceiling, not a measurement.

(b) **Cumulative evidence-tier weighting**: Allow sub-score expression below 0 for harm-scale metrics (deaths, detained persons, documented atrocities) as a secondary data layer alongside the composite.

(c) **Sub-score for harm-scale beyond the 0-100 dimensional scale**: Introduce a harm-severity qualifier that does not change the composite but appears on the entity record and is searchable.

Any of these approaches would address the current transparency gap. The absence of a decision is itself a methodology statement — it implies the current scores adequately represent these entities' situations. They do not.
