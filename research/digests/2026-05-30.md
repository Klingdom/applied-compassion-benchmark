**The pre-committed protocol is the key governance innovation this cycle.** Hungary's +0.2 follows directly from a three-path pre-commitment documented May 27. The assessor did not make a discretionary upgrade judgment tonight — it recognized a trigger condition and fired the pre-documented path. This is how the benchmark maintains consistency across politically sensitive governance events: the analysis happens before the trigger fires, not after.

**The "exogenous shock" vs "state incapacity" distinction matters at near-floor baselines.** The NATURAL-DISASTER-OVERLAY ruling for DRC applies specifically to entities already priced for systemic state incapacity. A 2.3-composite entity's inability to respond to an Ebola outbreak does not produce a new downgrade because the incapacity is already reflected in the baseline. This is analytically important: it prevents double-counting institutional failure, while preserving the ability to score deliberate obstruction if documented.

**Math-hygiene drift is a v1.2 transition artifact.** Two of the last three cycles have included formula-reconciliation applies (Turkey, DeepMind/Google). Both follow the same pattern: stored composite predates the v1.2 canonical formula, dimensions reconstruct cleanly to a lower number. This is a known artifact of the v1.2 integration-premium formula that was not retroactively applied to all stored composites. The validate-indexes warning gate is functioning correctly.

**The Fortune 500 rotation boundary cluster (4 entities near two boundaries without triggers) is an index calibration signal.** When multiple rotation entities cluster near boundaries without entity-specific events, the boundaries themselves may need index-level review rather than individual entity re-scoring.
