## New Conduct Category Articulation — post-format-offensive-surge

**First application: Russia, May 11-12, 2026.**

Definition: A conduct category applied when an entity conducts offensive military operations in the period immediately following the expiry of a declared ceasefire or truce format, where the post-expiry operational tempo measurably exceeds the pre-format baseline.

Distinguishing features:
- Requires comparison to pre-format baseline (not merely to in-format tempo)
- Applies to the post-expiry window, not the expiry moment itself
- Is distinct from "strategic-format-exploitation" (which documents conduct during the format)
- Together with strategic-format-exploitation, enables documentation of the full bad-faith-format cycle

Pairing rule: When both categories are applied to the same format event (as in the May 9-12 Russia case), they should be cross-referenced in the floor-conduct record as constituting a "complete bad-faith-format cycle."

## Impacts and Insights

The Pakistan band crossing illustrates the boundary-watch protocol operating correctly across a 4-cycle arc. The first assessment (May 8) was intentionally conservative — a low-confidence first baseline at 0.3 above the Critical floor is exactly the situation where holding for more evidence is methodologically correct. The HRCP annual report provided the comprehensive primary-source documentation that justified moving. This is the right outcome: a band crossing supported by a primary-source national human-rights institution's comprehensive annual report, not by a single news event.

The Russia three-phase conduct documentation has implications beyond Russia's own floor record. It provides the first fully documented template for how a state actor can use a ceasefire format to maximize harm capacity — before, during, and after. The "complete bad-faith-format cycle" framing is analytically useful because it shows that the format itself becomes an instrument of harm when exploited systematically. For future ceasefires involving any actor, scanners should be pre-configured to look for all three phases: pre-format-collapse, during-format-exploitation, and post-format-surge.

The healthcare Fortune 500 cluster surfaces a structural observation about how accountability mechanisms function in regulated industries. The regulatory actions now underway against UnitedHealth, CVS, and J&J did not emerge from voluntary transparency — they emerged from federal audits, whistleblowers, congressional investigations, and mass litigation. The ACC dimension is particularly weak across this cluster because none of the material conduct was self-disclosed before legal obligation. This is a sector-level ACC-dimension finding, not merely individual company findings.

The Day-3 Sustained-Conduct Rule (v1.2) continued to operate correctly this cycle: Ukraine's +3.1 carry-forward was reaffirmed, Vanuatu received +1.6 carry-forward, and Mongolia received +1.5 carry-forward — all sub-threshold dimensional pressures preserved for future cycles. The rule is proving durable across varied entity types (sovereign states, small island states) and different evidence types (conflict conduct, climate leadership, human-rights law enactment).
