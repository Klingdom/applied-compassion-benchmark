## CYCLE HEADLINE

Tonight's cycle is anchored by a single scored movement: Burkina Faso downgraded from 12.5 to 6.3 (delta -6.2, Critical -> Critical, no band crossing). The finding is methodologically durable. Human Rights Watch's April 2, 2026 report — which documents Burkina Faso military and allied militia crimes against humanity, ethnic cleansing of Fulani civilians, and drone strikes on crowded civilian markets ruled apparent war crimes — was not reflected in the published baseline of 12.5. The baseline was a uniform 1.5 across all eight dimensions, set before the HRW report landed. The reweight corrects that gap.

The result is a new standing rule: ACTIVE-STATE-PERPETRATION-REWEIGHT. Where a corroborated Tier-5 independent finding documents government-perpetrated multi-category atrocity conduct that the baseline did not price, reweight toward the Critical floor. The entity is NOT scored to 0.0 when the conflict is genuinely multi-sided and limited state-UN humanitarian cooperation persists. Myanmar and Sudan are scored to 0.0 because the state is the dominant or sole perpetrator with no residual cooperation signal. Burkina Faso is scored to 6.3 — the same active-perpetration category, but calibrated by the JNIM's parallel atrocities and the junta's residual UN $1.16B Food Systems partnership.

The remaining nineteen assessments are confirmations, ruling re-affirmations, and carry-forwards. ## Impacts and Insights

**The ACTIVE-STATE-PERPETRATION-REWEIGHT ruling adds a new calibration layer to the Critical band.** Prior to tonight, the Critical band's internal structure was largely uniform (the uniform-1.5 baseline at 12.5 was the most common Critical entry point). Burkina Faso's 6.3 creates a documented distinction within the band: entities that are committing crimes against humanity but are not sole perpetrators land materially lower than the uniform baseline, but above the absolute floor. This is the methodology rendering a judgment about proportionality within the worst category — the kind of discrimination that distinguishes a benchmarking system from a pass/fail filter.

**The CVS Health FILED-BUT-UNADJUDICATED ruling has cross-entity implications.** The logic — that an already near-floor entity with institutional-plaintiff suits against it is not further downgraded until adjudication — will apply to any future case where large organizational plaintiffs file against a Fortune 500 entity with existing ACC/EQU deficits. The $250M 340B diversion case and the TN FAIR Rx challenge both involve major institutional plaintiffs with documentary audit records (TN audit found a 16,000% affiliated-pharmacy reimbursement gap). These are among the stronger allegation packages in the Fortune 500 assessment record. The standing rule protects against premature scoring while ensuring the watch is formally documented.

**Published scores are systematically undercounting active-perpetration states where baseline assessments predate independent findings.** Burkina Faso is the second case in the current cycle series (after Turkey) where a published baseline score was materially too high not because of a methodological disagreement but because a major corroborated finding postdated the baseline. This is a structural evidence gap: baselines are set at a point in time, and independent organizations like HRW publish findings months after events occur. The scanner's priority-queue mechanism is the primary safeguard; tonight's cycle demonstrates it working.
