## Methodology Notes

### Contested, Unimplemented Agreements and the Forward-Trigger Doctrine

The Lebanon-Israel framework (Jun 26-27) is the first test in this cycle series of the forward-trigger doctrine applied to a signed diplomatic agreement. The benchmark treats forward triggers as conditions that, if met, would produce scored own-conduct — not as scored conduct themselves. A signed agreement that is immediately rejected by a key governing-coalition actor (Hezbollah) and that has not changed the military situation is a forward trigger, not a realized outcome. The scoring sequence: (1) framework signed = diplomatic step logged; (2) framework implementation = scorable positive own-conduct; (3) framework failure under current military conditions = no negative Lebanese own-conduct (the failure is the war, not Lebanon's government).

### Policy Intent vs. Realized Harm — The Olmstead Memo

The DOJ Olmstead memo is the clearest 2026 example of the benchmark's distinction between policy intent and realized harm. The Supreme Court's 1999 Olmstead ruling requires states to integrate people with disabilities into the community rather than institutions. The DOJ memo questions that mandate — a legal-opinion document signaling policy direction. No one has been re-institutionalized yet. No enforcement has been withdrawn. The benchmark scores realized harms, not policy statements. The watch flags on EQU and ACC are the appropriate response: the conversion trigger is a formal DOJ action or documented state conduct.

### Eleven False Positives — Three-Rule Compound Screens (New Pattern)

Two entities triggered three §3e-bis rules simultaneously tonight: Lebanon (directionality + attribution + pre-adjudication/forward-trigger) and the United States (stale-baseline-rejected + double-count + forward-trigger). This is the first night in the June 2026 series where two entities required three-rule compound screens simultaneously. Each rule in a compound screen would be sufficient on its own to prevent a proposal; running all three confirms that the surface evidence is genuinely ambiguous across multiple dimensions, not just one.
