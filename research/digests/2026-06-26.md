### The Floor Designation and New Evidence Categories

The UN COI genocide finding (Jun 23) raises a methodology question: when a floor-designated entity (score = 0.0) receives a new category of adverse finding — not more of the existing evidence, but a qualitatively new legal-category determination — does the floor designation absorb it automatically, or should it be logged as a new dimension sub-anchor even if the composite cannot move?

The current methodology treats the floor as absorbing all categories while the entity remains at 0.0. The genocide finding is logged as evidence-tier reinforcement under AWR/EMP/EQU/ACC/INT. A methodology-evolution question about whether the genocide sub-anchor should be formalized as a distinct category in the v1.3 candidate methodology set remains open.

### Attribution Rule — Lebanon Illustration

The Lebanon case this cycle is the clearest illustration of the attribution rule in a conflict setting. The event (Amnesty June 2026: Israel's mass evacuation orders in 29 towns = war crime of unlawful transfer) is well-documented and significant. But the rule is: we score the entity that took the action. Israel took the action; Israel is scored for it. Lebanon's score reflects Lebanon's own conduct toward its population. The absence of new negative Lebanese own-conduct evidence is not positive evidence for Lebanon — it is simply neutrality.
