## Impacts and Insights

**The Established band is now empty in AI labs.** Before Apr 30, Anthropic (61.6) was the sole Established-band AI lab after DeepMind/Google's downgrade. If tonight's proposal is approved at 59.7, the entire AI labs index will sit below the Established/Functional boundary. This is not merely a compositional observation — it signals that no AI lab currently assessed by the benchmark has demonstrated the consistent, broad-scope institutional compassion that the Established band requires. The nearest entity (Anthropic, whether at 61.6 or 59.7) occupies a posture that is structurally different from the others, but the Mythos disclosure gap reflects a real limitation in its transparency systems.

**Transparency under pressure is the dominant dimension gap across the pipeline.** INT and ACC are the dimensions most frequently driving proposals this cycle. Tonight's Anthropic downgrade is an INT/ACC event. The Apr 30 DeepMind/Google downgrade was an INT/BND event. Turkey's Apr 30 downgrade was an ACC/INT event. This convergence is methodologically significant: institutions that maintain strong stated values but fail to apply them to their own operational failures consistently score lower than institutions with more modest stated values but consistent application. The benchmark is, in practice, a test of applied consistency, not stated aspiration.

**Rotation-state drift is a systematic risk, not an isolated occurrence.** The 14 reconciliations applied tonight — following 12 on the prior cycle — represent the pipeline's largest combined drift-correction event. The root cause is architectural: rotation-state.json is updated manually by agents when they apply proposals, but it is not automatically validated against live-JSON files before each cycle begins. The ChangeProposalSchema/IndexFileSchema work in progress is designed to enforce upstream consistency; until it is wired into the score-updater, a pre-cycle validator run is the recommended interim control.

**The floor cluster is stabilizing as a confirmed categorical designation.** Six nights ago, the floor cluster was a pending methodology question. Tonight, all six entities complete their respective confirmation cycles (Myanmar its first, others their second or subsequent). The exit-criteria framework is functioning as designed: no entity has met any of the four criteria (independent investigation, structural reform, treaty-body compliance, verifiable behavioral change). The cluster is no longer a methodology edge case — it is a durable feature of the benchmark requiring a visible public communication layer, which the `floorDesignation` payload and entity-page banner now provide.
