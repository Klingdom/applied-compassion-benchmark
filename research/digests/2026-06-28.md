## Methodology Notes

### Natural Disaster vs. Own-Conduct — The Venezuela Earthquake Protocol

The Uganda natural-disaster-overlay precedent (Jun 6) established the baseline rule: natural disasters are exogenous events; the benchmark scores only the government's response. Venezuela's twin magnitude 7.2 and 7.5 earthquakes (June 24) triggered the first application of this protocol to a Category 1 mass-casualty event within the June 2026 series. The scoring question was not "did an earthquake happen?" but "what did the government do about it?" The four-rule §3e-bis compound screen produced the most analytically rigorous single-entity determination to date.

### Aid Acceptance as the Critical Differentiator — Floor Calibration

The Venezuela determination formalizes a distinction that has been implicit in prior Critical-band assessments: accepting international humanitarian aid is a meaningful positive own-conduct signal that blocks an upgrade (not strong enough alone) but also blocks a downgrade (because it differentiates the entity from states that block aid). Yemen/Houthis blocked aid entirely (floor). Venezuela accepted 17 flights, 1,600+ rescuers, and international funds. The existence of meaningful aid access is what keeps Venezuela's score at 18.0 rather than approaching the floor. Domestic delivery opacity and access restrictions are watch-flag-level findings in the context of a government that does not block aid at the border.

### The Lebanon-Israel 48-Hour Arc — Forward-Trigger Doctrine Under Stress

The June 26-28 Lebanon-Israel arc tested the forward-trigger doctrine in its most compressed form. A diplomatic agreement passed through three stages — creation, rejection, and collapse — in 48 hours. The benchmark produced a determinate outcome at each stage without waiting for evidence to settle: (1) framework signed → forward trigger logged, no score change; (2) Hezbollah rejection → forward trigger suspended, no score change; (3) Israel resumed strikes → forward trigger closed, no score change. The doctrine held without modification.
