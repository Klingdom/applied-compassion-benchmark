## Impacts and Insights

Tonight's findings suggest that AI-driven capital allocation is now producing a structural bifurcation in Fortune 500 workforce conduct: entities with more mature HR infrastructure and established corporate culture (Meta's sector-leading severance) are navigating the AI pivot with meaningfully better treatment of displaced workers than entities that appear to be optimizing purely for cost (Oracle's 6 a.m. email + algorithmic targeting). This is not a surprise finding, but the benchmark now has concrete dimensional evidence to document the bifurcation systematically. The new methodology categories established this cycle (algorithmic-targeting-of-stock-option-holders, liability-waiver-as-severance-condition) will enable consistent comparative assessment of future AI-pivot layoff events.

The floor-band methodology is under productive stress. The dual-signal framework (IHL-compliant gesture + IHL-violating conduct same day) required a novel documentation structure — the gesture needed to be acknowledged as evidence of IHL-protocol capability without allowing it to produce the uplift that would be inconsistent with the simultaneous harm. This sets a precedent for how the benchmark handles actors who exhibit selective IHL engagement: document the selectivity, confirm the floor, note the integrity-inconsistency. This is more analytically honest than either ignoring the gesture or allowing it to offset the harm.

Published scores for seven of the sixteen assessed entities are systematically understated relative to assessor-reconstructed composites (the math-hygiene cluster). Until the Open Bionics formula audit resolves and propagates through the cluster, any comparative analysis within the robotics-labs index that includes Apptronik, 1X Technologies, or Aleph Alpha should carry a confidence caveat.
