## Impacts and Insights

The five-cycle EU Dismantler sweep (Croatia through Italy) produces the most systematic sector-level analytical output in the benchmark's operating history. Five entities with different starting positions, different governance profiles, and different national democratic contexts received calibrated assessments from a single institutional source. The results are not identical — they range from -4.0 to -7.8 — but they are internally consistent and externally verifiable. Italy's referendum offset (-4.0 net vs. -6.0 base) is the most methodologically sophisticated output: it acknowledges the Dismantler classification while crediting the specific democratic-check evidence that the classification itself was designed to detect. This is the benchmark behaving as a precision instrument rather than a rule-to-apply.

The Turkey arc illustrates the opposite end of the methodology's scope. Two consecutive formal applies in 48 hours, two new methodology categories in two consecutive cycles, and a comprehensive accountability vacuum at the EU/multilateral level. The second-event-at-floor convention prevents the arc from consuming the entire scoring scale in a single week, but it does not suppress the evidence. Both the event documentation and the conservative magnitude calibration are now permanently on record.

The convergence of the EU Dismantler sweep completion with the Turkey arc in the same 48-hour window is analytically coincidental but structurally revealing: the benchmark is simultaneously documenting the systematic erosion of democratic institutions within the EU's own member states and the outright collapse of democratic governance in a major EU candidate/partner state. These are not the same phenomenon, but they are responses to the same underlying question — how institutional actors recognize, respond to, and reduce the suffering caused by governance failure.

The Amazon bargaining order finding illustrates a third pattern: positive counter-evidence emerging from the same evidence arc that produced the most recent downgrade. The NLRB action is structurally significant as a positive ACC event; the second-event-at-floor convention appropriately holds the score while documenting the positive. This is the methodology's accumulation logic working in both directions simultaneously.
