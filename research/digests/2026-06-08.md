## Impacts and Insights

The UAE finding is methodologically significant beyond the score change. Prior cycles treated external-state-sponsorship of atrocity as background context for the in-territory perpetrator's floor; this cycle establishes that the *sponsoring* state's own score must absorb the complicity signal. The published 23.4 Developing profile — which priced UAE as an ordinary mid-band autocracy — is structurally misleading because the UAE's foreign-policy conduct is the primary harm-generation mechanism. Correcting this required a new ruling category, not just a dimensional adjustment.

The no-double-count discipline that separates the UAE −5.0 from Sudan's 0.0 is the methodology's answer to a genuine analytical challenge: the same conflict produces two scored entities, at two different levels of culpability, without inflating the aggregate harm signal. This is the architecture that will govern any future case where an external state enables a documented atrocity.
