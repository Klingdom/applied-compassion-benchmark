## Impacts and Insights

The June 20 cycle is notable for what it does not contain: a proposal. After back-to-back proposal cycles (Bangladesh June 16, Humana June 18, Figure AI + Nigeria June 19), tonight's zero-proposal outcome is driven by a methodological principle, not an absence of adverse evidence. The evidence tonight was substantial — DOJ criminal probe expansion, Senate Medicare-gaming finding, RSF drone strikes, Ebola burial-team hostage, Towson store closure — but every piece falls into a category where the methodology prevents composite movement: (a) near-floor limitation (UHG, Nigeria, Sudan, DRC, Iran, Bolivia, Israel); (b) pre-adjudication discipline (UHG, Apple); (c) victim/perpetrator attribution (Ukraine, universities).

The University Index's first nightly cycle is methodologically instructive in a specific way: it demonstrates that the victim/perpetrator rule scales to an entire sector. The federal government's 2026 campaign against universities — whatever its merits as policy — is external action inflicted on institutions that the benchmark evaluates as compassionate-or-not in their own conduct toward students, workers, and communities. By attributing the funding freeze, grant clawbacks, and admissions reviews to the federal government (the perpetrator), the benchmark preserves the universities' assessment integrity and avoids penalizing institutions for choices made by others. The same principle applies in reverse: a university that proactively imposed funding cuts on its own vulnerable populations (rather than having them imposed externally) would be scored differently. Harvard's layoffs and salary freeze are a case in point — they are internal-consistency findings that stay in the assessment, unlike the DOJ action, which does not.

The UHG evidence accumulation pattern raises a structural question for the near-floor methodology: at what point does the density of pre-adjudication evidence — across Medicare Advantage, Optum Rx, and physician reimbursement simultaneously — constitute a floor-designation case even without a formal charge? The current methodology is clear: adjudication is the trigger. But the volume and specificity of the pre-adjudication evidence base around UHG is now qualitatively different from what existed when the 10.2 score was first assembled. This is a candidate for methodological discussion in the v1.3 notes: whether "scope of documented probe" constitutes a separate evidence-tier pathway distinct from adjudicated findings.
