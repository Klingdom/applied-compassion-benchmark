## Impacts and Insights

**The bad-faith ceasefire encoding establishes an important methodological precedent.** The Myanmar junta's pattern — declaring ceasefires while conducting concurrent documented attacks — could recur in other conflict contexts. The formal rule is now: ceasefire declarations are inverted signals when concurrent attacks are documented by independent sources (UN, HRW, Fortify Rights). This is a tighter evidentiary standard than 'conflict is ongoing' — it specifically addresses the instrumental use of humanitarian language.

**The AI labs sector governance divide is now documented across five consecutive cycles.** DeepMind/Google, OpenAI, Microsoft, Palantir AI, xAI/Grok, and Amazon (AWS) are all within the Pentagon classified deployment or defense-infrastructure cluster. Anthropic is the lone exception at material economic cost. The INT and BND dimensions are under structural downward pressure for the entire cohort. No positive governance reorientation event has emerged from any cohort member in the current phase. This is a sector configuration, not a series of episodic events.

**Botswana's upgrade is analytically significant beyond its size.** The Penal Code repeal is the only case in the current pipeline where a country has enacted structural legislative reform of a colonial-era human-rights violation within a single assessment cycle. President Boko's pre-presidency litigation history and in-office legislative delivery represent the rare case where INT dimension evidence is fully corroborated across the private, campaign, and governing phases. This is a reference case for how institutional compassion translates from leadership orientation to enacted change.

**The conflict-cluster floor is not monolithic.** Sudan, South Sudan, Myanmar, and Israel share the floor designation but differ in evidence character, international-attention profile, and trajectory signals. DRC's marginal positive trajectory (Switzerland talks) is precisely what keeps it above the floor despite severe documented harm. Future assessments should be explicit about which floor-exit criteria are open and which are absent — 'floor confirmed' without characterization conceals meaningful differences in the severity, character, and trajectory of institutional harm.
