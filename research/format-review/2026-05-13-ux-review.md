# Daily Briefing UX Review — May 13, 2026

Scope: `updates/[date]/page.tsx`, `updates/page.tsx`, `DailyBriefing.tsx`,
`TopSignals.tsx`, and JSON feeds for May 4, 11, 12.

---

## 1. Information Architecture

### Current section order (rendered top-to-bottom)

1. Archive banner (conditional)
2. Hero — masthead, h1, CTA cluster, lead callout, coverage panel, About panel
3. Top Signals (sectorAlerts / fallback from scoreChanges)
4. Floor Designations panel (always present, drawn from entity registry)
5. Score Movements (full dossier cards)
6. Inline newsletter nudge
7. Source Record (sectorAlerts sources, if present)
8. Purchase CTA
9. Confirmed Positions (table)
10. Today's Analysis (highlights array)
11. Newsletter signup
12. Sector Findings (sectorTrends)
13. Risk Signals (emergingRisks)
14. Analytical Notes (insights)
15. Today's Coverage grid (recentAssessments)
16. End-of-page CTA

### What is missing from the render entirely

- `signals` array (forward triggers, dated upcoming events, CRITICAL flags)
- `holds` array (deferred entities with resume dates)
- `mathHygiene` object (cluster count, criticalFlag, per-entity severity table)
- `floorEntities` array from the daily JSON (conduct documentation episodes like Russia post-ceasefire, Myanmar school strike, South Sudan famine)

The `FloorDesignationsPanel` renders a static grid from the entity registry but never surfaces the day's conduct documentation entries from `floorEntities[]`. This means the most significant floor events — Russia completing a complete bad-faith-format cycle, Myanmar killing 22 students — appear nowhere on the page.

### Recommended section order

1. Cycle-signal strip (1 line: cycle type, entities scanned, band changes, new floors, math-hygiene status)
2. Lead Finding (current: buried in hero as small inline callout)
3. Top Signals (keep position)
4. Score Movements (keep position)
5. Floor Cluster — conduct documentation for this cycle (currently missing)
6. Forward Signals and Holds (currently missing)
7. Math-Hygiene status (currently missing)
8. Confirmed Positions
9. Methodology callouts (new rules, new conduct categories — currently missing)
10. Sector Findings / Risk Signals / Analytical Notes
11. Newsletter / CTA

Rationale for moving Lead Finding above Top Signals: the current hero buries the most consequential finding (Pakistan band crossing) as a small pill inside a column next to a large "Daily Briefing" h1 and three CTA buttons. A reader skimming on a phone sees the h1 and buttons first, then must hunt for the actual news. The lead should be the headline, not a navigation aid.

## 2. Scannability

A reader doing 30 back-to-back cycles needs to answer three questions in under 30 seconds per cycle: (a) anything move? (b) floor activity? (c) anything coming up?

### Problems

- The confirmation table has 8 columns including a free-text "Finding" column with headline-length prose. On a 14-column-wide 390px phone it wraps into an unreadable horizontal scroll. The headline text is too dense to scan in a table cell.
- Top Signals cards are long. A HIGH-severity signal with "What happened", "Why it matters", "What's next", affected entities, and sources routinely takes 400px of vertical space. Five signals = roughly 2,000px of scroll before Score Movements begin.
- The "Continue to" anchor nav at the bottom of TopSignals is the only within-page navigation. There is no sticky section nav, no jump-to-section list in the hero, and no back-to-top affordance.
- Score movement cards contain both a headline (bold, left-bordered) and a full evidence record (numbered list with sources). On a high-signal day (3 proposals + 14 confirmations) a reader cannot tell what changed vs. what was confirmed until they read the card header.
- Pending vs. confirmed proposals render identically. The `status: "pending"` field on scoreChanges is not surfaced visually. A reader does not know whether Pakistan's band crossing is applied or still under review.

### Recommendations

- Add a cycle-signal strip immediately below the archive banner (or at the very top on current cycle). Single row: `BAND CROSSINGS: 1 | PROPOSALS: 3 | CONFIRMATIONS: 14 | FLOOR DOCS: 3 | MATH FLAGS: 13`. Tappable segments link to the relevant section. This single row answers question (a) in 3 seconds.
- Add a collapsible toggle to Top Signals cards. Default: show severity + sector headline + "What happened" only. Expand to show full card. This reduces first-pass scroll height by ~60%.
- Replace confirmation table with a scannable card-list on mobile, or add a mobile-only collapsed view (entity name + band + delta, tappable to expand headline). The 8-column table should only render at md+ breakpoints.
- Badge `status: "pending"` score-change cards with a visible "Pending review" pill in orange (#fb923c). Badge `status: "confirmed"` or `status: "applied"` with a check in green. This lets a reader immediately distinguish a proposal from a realized change.

## 3. Continuity Across Cycles

Multi-day arcs are a core part of what the briefing communicates. The Hungary arc (proposed May 9, re-pinned May 10, confirmed May 11, re-assessment June 9) and Pakistan boundary watch (4 days, resolved May 12) are the institution's intellectual product. Neither is visible as a thread in the current UI.

### Problems

- No entity carries a "previous cycle" link. If a reader lands on May 12 and sees Pakistan's band crossing, there is no UI path to May 8-11 where the boundary watch was first opened.
- The `boundaryWatchResolution` field on Pakistan's scoreChange ("Band crossing. Boundary watch active since May 8.") contains the arc narrative, but it renders as unstructured prose inside the headline block. There is no visual distinction between "this is a multi-day arc resolution" and "this is a one-cycle event."
- The `signals` array (which contains the forward leg of arcs: Hungary June 9 re-assessment, Vanuatu May 20 UNGA vote) is not rendered at all. The backward leg (prior proposals, re-pins) is not rendered either.
- The `openWatches` field on score-change entries is not rendered. Pakistan's open watches (monsoon impact, HRCP state response, Ahmadi protection) exist in the JSON but are invisible in the UI.

### Recommendations

- Add an "Arc" badge and timeline strip to score-change cards when `boundaryWatchResolution`, `openWatches`, or any multi-cycle context field is present. The strip should show: watch opened → [date] → resolved, with links to the prior cycle page. Implementation: `boundaryWatchResolution` already contains the opening date in prose — parse it, or add a structured `watchStartDate` field to the JSON schema.
- Render `openWatches[]` at the bottom of each score-change card as "What's being watched." These are the forward leg of the current arc.
- Render the `signals` array as a "Forward calendar" section. Group by entity. Each entry: entity name, date, signal summary, priority badge. Link the entity name to the entity profile page. The CRITICAL Open Bionics math flag should render here with a red border.

## 4. Visual Hierarchy

### What is currently well-differentiated

- Score changes: red/green gradient backgrounds correctly signal direction.
- Top Signals: severity color system (red/orange/yellow/blue) is consistent and meaningful.
- Band components: the five-band color system is applied throughout.
- Floor Designations: rose border distinguishes the cluster from everything else.

### What is not differentiated enough

- Conduct documentation (floor entities) vs. score changes: currently not rendered at all, but when they are added, they need a distinct visual treatment. Floor entities are not moving — they are at 0.0 and the documentation is a record of continued pattern. Use a flat charcoal card with a rose left-border, not a gradient card, to distinguish conduct docs from active proposals.
- Math-hygiene: a CRITICAL flag (10 cycles, -10 point discrepancy on a published 97.5 score) has the same visual weight as background analytical notes. It needs a distinct amber card with a "Data integrity" label, not the same "Note" badge as insight strings.
- Methodology callouts: when a new rule is articulated (Day-3 Sustained-Conduct Rule; post-format-offensive-surge conduct category), it is not surfaced at all. These should appear as a "Methodology update" card with a neutral-slate border, immediately after Score Movements. They are not scoring events but they are significant intellectual records.
- Pending proposals: identical visual to applied changes — see Scannability section above.

## 5. Evidence Presentation

### Current behavior

- Score-change cards: evidence renders if an `evidence[]` array is present. The May 12 JSON uses a `keyEvidence: string[]` field (not `evidence[]`), so the evidence block does not render for Pakistan, CVS, or UnitedHealth. This is a schema mismatch — `keyEvidence` and `evidence` coexist across cycles with no normalization in the render code.
- TopSignals: sources render as domain-only links (3 max), collected under "Primary sources." This is appropriate at the signal level.
- Floor entities: not rendered at all, so their `sources[]` and `conductCategories[]` are invisible.

### Recommendations

- Normalize `keyEvidence` alongside `evidence` in the score-change render block. Either coerce `keyEvidence: string[]` into the numbered evidence list, or add a `keyEvidence` branch. This is a one-line change in the normalization logic. Without it, the three highest-impact cards in the May 12 briefing render with zero evidence.
- Evidence should remain inline (not footnotes or modal). The numbered list with domain attribution is the right pattern — it matches the institution's "grounded in primary-source evidence" claim. Keep it, just fix the field name mismatch.
- For floor conduct documentation, render `conductCategories[]` as a numbered list with the same treatment as evidence. Sources render as domain links underneath. Do not collapse — floor conduct docs are the primary artifact for that cluster.

## 6. Floor Cluster

### Current behavior

`FloorDesignationsPanel` renders a static grid of all floor-designated entities (from the entity registry). It appears between TopSignals and Score Movements on every cycle, including cycles with no floor activity. It links to entity profile pages but shows no cycle-specific conduct.

The `floorEntities[]` array in the daily JSON (Russia, Myanmar, South Sudan on May 12) is never read by the render code.

### Recommendations

- Move the static floor-designation grid to a persistent sidebar or collapsible footer panel. It does not need to appear at full height between TopSignals and Score Movements on every cycle.
- Add a "Floor conduct documentation" section that renders `floorEntities[]` when present. Each entry: entity name + floor date + conduct categories list + sources. Use a flat rose-tinted card, not a gradient.
- On cycles with no floor conduct documentation, the section is suppressed (same pattern as scoreChanges).
- The static "9 entities at 0.0" grid should show a count of how many have conduct documentation this cycle ("3 of 9 documented this cycle") rather than appearing identical every day.

## 7. Methodology Callouts

### Current behavior

New rules and conduct categories (Day-3 Sustained-Conduct Rule v1.2 on May 11; post-format-offensive-surge category on May 12) appear only in the `lead` prose string, which renders nowhere in the UI. The `pipeline.newMethodologyRules` count on May 11 (`"newMethodologyRules": 1`) is not surfaced.

### Recommendations

- Add a `methodologyUpdates[]` field to the JSON schema (or parse it from `pipeline.newMethodologyRules > 0` as a trigger).
- When present, render a "Methodology record" card immediately after Score Movements. Card style: neutral slate border (`--color-line`), "Methodology" category badge in slate (#94a3b8), rule name as h3, full rule description as body. This is an archival record — it should not look like a scoring event.
- The card should link to `/methodology` with a "Read the methodology" anchor.

## 8. Forward Signals

### Current behavior

`signals[]` array (11 entries on May 12, spanning May 13 through June 9) is not rendered anywhere. This includes CRITICAL items (Open Bionics formula audit) and HIGH-priority dated triggers (Anthropic May 15, OpenAI May 21 Musk verdict, Hungary June 9 re-assessment).

`holds[]` array (Anthropic, OpenAI, Microsoft, Google) is also not rendered.

### Recommendations

- Add a "Forward calendar" section after the confirmed positions table. Render `signals[]` grouped by date in chronological order. Each entry: date chip + entity link + signal summary + priority badge. CRITICAL signals render with a red border. HIGH signals render with an orange border.
- Render `holds[]` as a compact list at the bottom of Forward Calendar. Each entry: entity name + hold reason + resume date. This answers "why isn't [entity] in this cycle?" directly.
- Calendar export: add a single `.ics` download link for the forward signals array. Static export means this must be pre-generated at build time. The `.ics` file can be generated by the same script that produces `manifest.json`. Each signal becomes a `VEVENT` with `DTSTART`, `SUMMARY` (entity + priority), and `DESCRIPTION` (signal text). This is a low-implementation-cost feature with high retention value.
- Per-signal subscription is not feasible without a server. The calendar export covers the same user need for the current static architecture.

## 9. Empty States and Sparse Cycles

### Current behavior

May 4 has zero score changes and 19 confirmations. The hero renders identically to May 12 (3 proposals, band crossing). The `leadChange` logic falls back to `null` when no band change or ≥10-point delta exists, so the lead callout is suppressed — but nothing else signals that this is a quiet cycle. The "Today's coverage" panel says "1 score change" only when `proposalsGenerated > 0`, but on May 4 it says nothing about the 19 confirmations.

### Recommendations

- The cycle-signal strip (recommended in Scannability section) solves this. `BAND CROSSINGS: 0 | PROPOSALS: 0 | CONFIRMATIONS: 19` immediately communicates cycle character.
- On confirmation-only cycles, the h1 "Daily Briefing" section should show a subdued callout: "No material score changes this cycle — 19 positions confirmed." This is not a failure state; it is a meaningful signal about stability. Treat it as such.
- TopSignals already has a fallback that synthesizes signals from score changes when sectorAlerts is empty. Extend this: when both are empty, render a "Stability cycle" notice in the TopSignals slot. Do not render an empty section or skip to confirmations silently.

## 10. Mobile Experience

### Specific problems

- Confirmation table: 8 columns including free-text "Finding" will overflow on any phone. The table has `overflow-auto` but the Finding column truncation at `max-w-[380px]` still forces horizontal scroll on 390px viewports. On mobile, render a card list instead of a table, or hide the Finding column and show it only on row expand.
- Score-change card header: the entity name (h3 at 1.4rem) and the score display (`publishedScore → assessedScore` at 1.5rem bold) are laid out `flex items-start justify-between`. On narrow viewports this pushes the score display to wrap below the entity name, but the flex direction does not change, causing the score to appear cramped against the right edge with no breathing room.
- Date navigation tabs in the hero: 5 tabs at `px-3.5 py-1.5` will exceed the viewport width on phones. They are `flex-wrap` so they wrap, but wrapping 5 tabs produces two rows of tabs that visually crowd the masthead. On mobile, consider a `<select>` or a horizontal scroll strip with `overflow-x-auto` and `whitespace-nowrap`.
- The "Continue to" anchor nav at the bottom of TopSignals is text-only and will also wrap awkwardly on narrow viewports. A small pill-row with overflow-x-auto scroll is more mobile-appropriate.
- TopSignals cards: `p-6` padding on a 390px viewport leaves ~340px of content width. This is acceptable. The "Dimensions implicated" chip row wraps cleanly. No critical layout break here.
- The hero CTA cluster (3 buttons: "Read today's brief", "Subscribe to weekly briefing", "View research reports") will stack to three full-width rows on mobile. This is fine layout-wise, but "View research reports" as the third button in a briefing hero is a conversion ask at the wrong moment. Consider dropping it from the hero on mobile.

## 5 Specific Component-Level Changes

### 1. Normalize `keyEvidence` in score-change render (highest data-integrity impact)

In `DailyBriefing.tsx`, the evidence block renders only if `change.evidence` is present. The May 12 JSON uses `keyEvidence: string[]` for all three proposals. Add:

```
const evidenceItems = change.evidence ?? change.keyEvidence ?? [];
```

Then use `evidenceItems` in the render. Without this fix, the three highest-impact entries in the May 12 briefing show zero evidence.

Location: `DailyBriefing.tsx`, the `(change.evidence as unknown[])?.length > 0` block around line 472.

### 2. Add `signals` and `holds` to `DailyBriefing.tsx` destructuring and render a Forward Calendar section

Current destructuring (line 131) does not include `signals` or `holds`. Add both, add a new section after confirmed positions. This surfaces the 11 forward triggers currently invisible on May 12, including the CRITICAL Open Bionics flag.

### 3. Add `floorEntities` render block

Add `floorEntities = []` to destructuring. Render a "Floor conduct documentation" section when `floorEntities.length > 0`. Each card: flat charcoal background, rose left-border, entity name, `conductCategories[]` as a bulleted list, `sources[]` as domain links. Position: after score movements, before confirmed positions.

### 4. Add cycle-signal strip to hero

Add a single-row strip between the date navigation tabs and the masthead. Content drawn from `pipeline`: band changes, proposals, confirmations, floor docs, math-hygiene flag count. Each segment is a tappable anchor to its section. Renders on all cycles including sparse ones. On sparse cycles, "PROPOSALS: 0" itself communicates cycle character without requiring the reader to scroll to find no score-change cards.

### 5. Add status badge to score-change cards

Score changes carry `status: "pending" | "confirmed" | "applied"`. Add a badge in the card header row:
- `pending`: orange pill (#fb923c bg-opacity-10, orange border, "Pending review")
- `confirmed` / `applied`: green pill (#86efac bg-opacity-10, green border, "Applied")

This is a 5-line addition to the card header block. Without it, a reader cannot distinguish a proposal that has not been applied yet from one that has been confirmed and published.

## Single Highest-Impact UX Change

**Render the `signals` and `floorEntities` arrays.**

These two fields contain the most consequential content in high-signal briefings and are currently invisible. On May 12: three floor conduct documentation entries (Russia completing a complete bad-faith-format cycle, Myanmar school airstrike killing 22 students, South Sudan famine) and 11 forward triggers (including a CRITICAL data-integrity flag) exist in the JSON and render as zero pixels on the page. A reader of the May 12 briefing sees Pakistan's band crossing, three score-change proposals, 14 confirmations — and nothing about Russia, Myanmar, or South Sudan, and no awareness that Anthropic is being assessed May 15 or that OpenAI's hold expires on the Musk verdict.

The floor conduct entries and forward signals together represent roughly 40% of the editorial content of a high-signal briefing. Making them visible is not a design enhancement — it is making the product work as intended.

## Assumptions

- The static-export constraint (no server-side rendering) means any calendar export must be pre-generated at build time. Per-signal email subscriptions require a server and are out of scope for the current architecture.
- `keyEvidence` vs. `evidence` field naming is an upstream JSON authoring inconsistency, not an intentional schema split. The fix belongs in the render normalization layer.
- The `formatDateLabel` export from `DailyBriefing.tsx` is the canonical date formatter across the updates subsystem. New date rendering should use this function.
- Floor conduct documentation cards should not use the same gradient-card treatment as score movements. The floor is not changing; the conduct is being documented. Visual parity between "score changed" and "conduct documented at 0.0" would be misleading.
