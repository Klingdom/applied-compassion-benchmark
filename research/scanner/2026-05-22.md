**1. Hungary EU reform pushback (May 22) — "reforms-contested-not-abandoned" pattern**

This is distinct from abandoning reforms. The canonical upgrade was anchored on "reforms-announced-not-enacted" — the new evidence is "reforms-contested-on-conditionality-specifics-not-principle." This should be logged as a methodology note for future cases where reform governments negotiate reform conditions rather than reject them.

**2. Microsoft Azure/Unit 8200 — "harm-enabled-then-accountability-applied" dual-signal**
The Microsoft case presents a methodological test: an entity that enabled severe harm (200M hours of Palestinian surveillance supporting lethal operations) over an extended period, but then responded with meaningful accountability (GM fired, access terminated, investigation completed). Standard methodology would weight the enabling-harm dimension negatively and the accountability response as a partial offset. The net question is whether accountability partially restores the GOV/ACC dimension or whether the enabling-harm dimension dominates. This is the first documented case at this harm-severity level with a substantive corporate accountability response. Assessor should establish a precedent ruling on the weighting.

**3. Slovakia "Dismantler" classification paralleling Croatia**

Croatia started at 48.4 (well within Functional); Slovakia starts at 39.1 (bottom of Developing, 0.9 from boundary).
