## 1. Problem Definition

### Reader question this index answers
"Which universities genuinely protect and support the people who study and work within them — and which ones hide behind prestige while tolerating preventable harm?"

### Who has the problem
- **Primary:** Prospective students and their families making high-stakes enrollment decisions with no independent welfare signal — only marketing materials and prestige rankings.
- **Secondary:** Graduate students, adjunct and contingent faculty, and staff who bear the most institutional risk and have the least information.
- **Tertiary:** Institutional procurement officers, foundation officers, and accreditors who want a third-party benchmark without commissioning custom research.
- **Buyer (commercial):** University administrators who want a gap analysis; journalists and policy researchers who want a citable comparative dataset.

### The pain
Existing rankings (THE, QS, ARWU) measure research output, faculty citations, employer reputation, and internationalization. None measures how the institution treats the humans inside it. A university can rank #1 for research and simultaneously have an active Title IX investigation, chronic graduate student housing insecurity, adjunct poverty wages, and suppressed whistleblower complaints. Students have no way to compare on these axes before they commit.

### Why now
- Post-COVID mental health data at universities is now publicly reported and machine-readable at more institutions than ever before.
- The US DOE Title IX and Clery Act enforcement database is queryable.
- Graduate student unionization produced publicly filed labor agreements at 40+ major research universities — actual contract terms are now citable evidence.
- Several high-profile campus harm events (2022–2025) have increased public demand for an independent welfare signal.
- The Compassion Benchmark has already established credibility with 7 indexes; universities are a natural next category and carry strong SEO and citation potential.

## 2. MVP Scope

### In scope
- Top 100 universities worldwide, scored on the standard 8-dimension / 40-subdimension framework
- Composite 0–100 score, band assignment (Critical / Developing / Functional / Established / Exemplary), rank 1–100
- Institution-level scoring only (the legal entity / accredited institution as a whole)
- Index page at `/universities` following the established RankingTable + IndexHero page pattern
- JSON data file at `site/src/data/indexes/universities.json` following the `meta` + `rankings[]` schema
- Per-entity public score file at `public/data/scores/<slug>.json` (generated by existing prebuild script)
- Score Watch subscription compatibility (entity slugs follow existing badge endpoint pattern)
- Per-entity `confidence` flag (see section 4) on each ranking entry

### Explicitly out of scope — MVP
- Per-campus or per-school-within-university scoring (no "Harvard Law vs. Harvard College")
- Department-level scoring
- Individual faculty or administrator scoring
- Student satisfaction survey integration (not independently verifiable at scale)
- Annual update automation (v1 is a point-in-time 2026 snapshot; refresh cadence is a post-launch decision)
- Paid partnerships with universities (independence policy: prohibited permanently, not just for MVP)
- US-only or research-university-only subset indexes (post-launch consideration)
- University self-assessment tool (separate product; see BACKLOG)

### Assumption flags
- ASSUMPTION: Benchmark-research agent compiles the 100-entity list; PM does not pre-select entities.
- ASSUMPTION: Scoring is done by benchmark-research agent using public evidence; PM defines the signal map (section 5).
- ASSUMPTION: The existing `extract-rankings.mjs` and `export-public-data.mjs` scripts require no modification to handle a new index JSON file in the standard schema.
- MISSING: No baseline traffic or conversion data for university-adjacent queries on the site today. Success metrics use absolute targets, not lift targets.

## 3. Entity Selection Criteria

### Method
The benchmark-research agent compiles the 100-institution list using a **composite of three defensible, citable, annually-updated global rankings**:

| Source | Publication | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2026 | THE | Research, teaching, citations, industry income, international outlook |
| QS World University Rankings 2026 | QS | Academic reputation, employer reputation, faculty-student ratio, citations |
| Academic Ranking of World Universities (Shanghai) 2026 | ARWU/ShanghaiRanking | Alumni/faculty Nobel & Fields, HiCi researchers, Nature/Science papers, PCP |

### Deduplication rule
Take the top 50 from each source. Deduplicate by legal institution name. If fewer than 100 unique institutions result (likely, given overlap in top 30), extend each list downward until 100 unique institutions are selected. Rank by average position across sources where an institution appears in multiple; single-source institutions ranked below multi-source ones.

### Rationale for this method
- All three sources are independently produced, have published methodologies, and are updated annually.
- Using three sources guards against single-source bias (THE is European-weighted; QS is employer-survey-weighted; ARWU is research-output-weighted).
- The composite is defensible to any institution that questions inclusion: "You appear in the top tiers of at least one of the three most-cited global university rankings."

### Known bias — geographic and English-language-evidence skew
Above approximately rank 80 in the composite list, institutions from non-Anglophone countries (particularly those in East Asia, Latin America, and the Middle East) have less public English-language documentary evidence available for scoring. This is a scoring-quality problem, not an inclusion problem.

**Mitigation:** Each ranking entry carries a `confidence` field (see schema, section 4):
- `"high"` — substantive public English or translated evidence exists for all 8 dimensions
- `"medium"` — substantive evidence for 5–7 dimensions; 1–3 dimensions estimated from proxies
- `"low"` — fewer than 5 dimensions have direct public evidence; significant estimation

Scores for `"low"` confidence entities are published with an explicit caveat on the index page and entity page. The index page displays a confidence filter. Benchmark-research must not assign a composite above 60 to any `"low"` confidence entity without a written evidence note.

## 4. Per-Entity Metadata Schema

Mirror the `ai-labs.json` pattern (`hq`, `sector`) and the `global-cities.json` pattern (`country`, `region`). Add university-specific fields.

### Required fields per ranking entry

```jsonc
{
  "rank": 1,
  "name": "University of Cambridge",          // Official English-language name
  "slug": "university-of-cambridge",          // kebab-case, URL-safe, unique
  "country": "United Kingdom",                // Country of primary campus / legal domicile
  "region": "WEur",                           // 6-char region code matching existing scheme
  "type": "public" | "private",              // Legal/funding type
  "scores": {
    "AWR": 3.8,
    "EMP": 3.2,
    "ACT": 3.5,
    "EQU": 3.0,
    "BND": 2.8,
    "ACC": 3.1,
    "SYS": 3.6,
    "INT": 3.4
  },
  "composite": 67.4,
  "band": "established",
  "confidence": "high" | "medium" | "low"
}
```

### Optional fields (add if available without significant research overhead)
- `founded`: integer year (e.g., `1209`). Include only if readily available; do not hold scoring for it.
- `studentBody`: approximate enrollment (nearest 1,000, from institution's own published figure). Useful for normalization context in research products, not used in scoring.

### Fields explicitly excluded from MVP schema
- `endowmentUSD`: high variance in reporting standards internationally; exclude until post-launch normalization method is defined.
- `researchRank`: this is a compassion index, not a prestige index; including prestige-rank data risks conflating the two signals in reader interpretation.
- `accreditation`: too jurisdiction-specific to normalize across 100 global institutions.

### Region code mapping
Use the same 6-char region codes as `global-cities.json`. For reference (not exhaustive):
- `NEur` — Northern Europe
- `WEur` — Western Europe
- `SEur` — Southern Europe
- `EEur` — Eastern Europe
- `NAm` — North America
- `LAm` — Latin America
- `EAsia` — East Asia
- `SAsia` — South Asia
- `SEAsia` — Southeast Asia
- `MidEast` — Middle East & North Africa
- `SSAf` — Sub-Saharan Africa
- `Oceania` — Australia, New Zealand, Pacific

## 5. Eight-Dimension Signal Map for Universities

For each dimension, 2 primary public evidence signals are listed. Benchmark-research scores each subdimension (0–5 per the anchors in `dimensions.ts`); these signals guide which public sources to consult.

### AWR — Awareness
*Does the university proactively detect student and staff distress before it becomes crisis?*

| Signal | Source type |
|---|---|
| Student mental health early-warning systems: documented triage protocols, student-to-counselor ratios published or reportable under FOIA/equivalent | Counseling center annual reports; accreditation self-studies; student newspaper investigative records |
| Faculty and staff wellbeing monitoring: formal HR wellbeing data collection beyond EAP existence; disaggregated by employment type (adjunct vs. tenured) | HR reports; union contracts; accreditor findings |

**Subdimension emphasis:** A1 (Suffering Detection), A3 (Blind Spot Mitigation) — are contingent workers and international students included in monitoring, or only full-time domestic students?

---

### EMP — Empathy
*Do students, staff, and faculty feel genuinely cared about rather than processed?*

| Signal | Source type |
|---|---|
| Ombudsperson or student advocate office: existence, independence from administration, published caseload summaries | Institution website; annual reports; student government publications |
| Documented case of institutional communication that acknowledged emotional harm before legal review (e.g., presidential letter to survivors that preceded settlement) | News archives; institutional communications; student newspaper |

**Subdimension emphasis:** E4 (Validation) — does the university structurally acknowledge harm reports before they require legal pressure? E1 (Affective Resonance) — do students from non-dominant groups describe feeling genuinely seen?

---

### ACT — Action
*Does identified need result in proportional, timely, effective response?*

| Signal | Source type |
|---|---|
| Student mental health waitlist data: average days to first counseling appointment, documented against peer benchmarks | CCMH (Center for Collegiate Mental Health) annual data; institution reports |
| Emergency financial aid program: existence, disbursement speed (same-week vs. same-semester), documented utilization | Financial aid office reports; student government advocacy records; accreditation materials |

**Subdimension emphasis:** AC1 (Responsiveness) and AC4 (Resource Mobilization) — is mental health staffing funded at the level need data indicates, or is it chronically underfunded?

---

### EQU — Equity
*Is institutional care distributed fairly across students and workers regardless of identity or background?*

| Signal | Source type |
|---|---|
| Access and completion gaps: Pell Grant (US) or equivalent low-income enrollment share vs. general population; documented first-generation student completion rates vs. overall; for non-US institutions, equivalent published access-program data | IPEDS (US); HESA (UK); institution equity reports; OECD education database |
| Adjunct and contingent faculty compensation: documented pay differential vs. tenure-track for equivalent credit hours; access to benefits, office space, professional development | AFT/AAUP data; union contracts; institution salary schedules; investigative journalism |

**Subdimension emphasis:** EQ1 (Universality) and EQ5 (Historical Harm Acknowledgment) — does the institution acknowledge and act on its history with excluded groups?

---

### BND — Boundaries
*Does the institution protect workers and students from exploitation and depletion?*

| Signal | Source type |
|---|---|
| Graduate student labor conditions: stipend against local cost of living, health insurance coverage, documented workload caps in teaching assistantship agreements | Graduate student union contracts; NAGPS surveys; institution TA policy documents |
| International student consent and financial risk: documented pre-enrollment disclosure of full cost of attendance including visa/housing; refund policy; cases of deceptive enrollment practices | Enrollment agreements; state AG investigations; consumer protection findings; student media |

**Subdimension emphasis:** B1 (Self-Sustainability) for graduate students and adjuncts; B5 (Consent Orientation) for prospective students and international students in particular.

---

### ACC — Accountability
*Does the university acknowledge its failures and make genuine repair?*

| Signal | Source type |
|---|---|
| Title IX / Clery Act compliance record (US) or equivalent jurisdiction findings: open OCR investigations, resolution agreements, Clery Act violations, documented retaliation findings | US DOE Office for Civil Rights database; Clery Center data; equivalent EU/UK regulator findings for non-US institutions |
| Institutional response to high-profile harm events: did the university acknowledge harm before external legal compulsion? Were harmed parties involved in designing remediation? | Case law; news archives; settlement documents; victim advocate statements |

**Subdimension emphasis:** AB1 (Harm Acknowledgment) and AB5 (Reparative Action). A history of OCR resolution agreements is not itself penalized — the scoring turns on whether the university self-disclosed, cooperated fully, and made substantial repair.

---

### SYS — Systemic Thinking
*Does the university address root causes of student and worker suffering, not just surface symptoms?*

| Signal | Source type |
|---|---|
| Structural mental health investment: evidence that counseling center funding has grown in proportion to enrollment and documented need over 5+ years — not only crisis response | Budget documents (FOIA-obtainable); accreditation self-studies; student government budget analyses |
| Advocacy for structural change in higher education access: public institutional positions on federal student loan policy, state higher ed funding, or immigration policy affecting enrolled students that carry institutional cost or political risk | Presidential statements; legislative testimony; amicus briefs; coalition memberships with documented resource contribution |

**Subdimension emphasis:** S1 (Root Cause Orientation) and S4 (Structural Critique) — is the university willing to publicly advocate for structural changes that reduce its own enrollment revenue or donor relationships?

---

### INT — Integrity
*Is the university's stated commitment to student and worker welfare genuine and consistent under pressure?*

| Signal | Source type |
|---|---|
| Endowment investment vs. stated values: documented cases of endowment holdings in sectors the university's stated values explicitly oppose (fossil fuels vs. climate commitments; private prison vs. criminal justice reform commitments); and documented response when this contradiction was raised publicly | DivestInvest filings; AASHE data; shareholder records; institution investment committee reports |
| Administrative compensation vs. student-facing resource investment: ratio trend of administrator compensation growth to counseling center / financial aid growth over 5 years | IRS Form 990 (private US); FOIA salary data (public US); institution budget documents |

**Subdimension emphasis:** I4 (Values Alignment) — does endowment and budget behavior match stated mission? I2 (Non-Performance) — are commitments maintained when there is no reputational upside?

## 6. Acceptance Criteria

### Data completeness
- [ ] All 100 institutions have a value for every required schema field: `rank`, `name`, `slug`, `country`, `region`, `type`, `scores` (all 8 dimensions), `composite`, `band`, `confidence`
- [ ] No two institutions share the same `slug`
- [ ] `composite` is derived from the standard 8-dimension formula (including integration premium); it is not manually overridden
- [ ] `band` assignment matches the `composite` value against the canonical BANDS thresholds in `dimensions.ts` (Critical 0–20, Developing 20–40, Functional 40–60, Established 60–80, Exemplary 80–100)

### Evidence standards
- [ ] Every dimension score for every entity is traceable to at least one cited public source in the benchmark-research working file
- [ ] No dimension score relies solely on an institution's own marketing materials as primary evidence
- [ ] Every `"low"` confidence entity has a written evidence note explaining which dimensions were estimated and why
- [ ] No entity is scored above composite 60 with `"low"` confidence without PM sign-off on the evidence note

### Independence
- [ ] No institution was contacted for input prior to publication of v1 scores
- [ ] No institution was offered early access to scores in exchange for any consideration
- [ ] No dimension score was modified in response to legal threat, PR outreach, or institutional relationship
- [ ] Benchmark-research lead has signed the weekly independence audit log before publication

### Site integration
- [ ] Index page renders at `/universities` with the standard RankingTable and IndexHero components
- [ ] Confidence filter is visible and functional on the index page (filters to High / Medium / Low or any combination)
- [ ] Each entity's Score Watch badge endpoint returns the correct composite for university entities
- [ ] `public/data/scores/<slug>.json` files are generated for all 100 entities by the prebuild script without modification to the script (if a script modification is required, it must be documented as a separate engineering task)
- [ ] The index appears in site navigation under the existing indexes menu
- [ ] OG image is generated for the index page and for each entity page

### Edge cases
- [ ] Institutions that have changed names since 2023 use current legal name; prior name is noted in evidence file but not displayed
- [ ] Institutions under active regulatory investigation are scored on evidence available; the `confidence` field reflects evidentiary uncertainty, not investigation status
- [ ] If an institution has multiple campuses scored separately by a source ranking, the entry covers the flagship campus / legal parent entity only; this is documented in the entity evidence note

## 7. Success Metrics

### Launch metric (within 30 days of publication)
- 500 unique visitors to `/universities` index page
- 50 unique visitors to individual university entity pages (aggregate, not per-entity)
- 1 inbound media inquiry or citation referencing the University Index

### Post-launch metrics (90 days)
- 2,000 unique visitors to `/universities`
- 10% of university index visitors click through to a purchase or Score Watch sign-up (leading indicator of commercial intent)
- 3 external citations (journalism, academic, or policy) referencing a specific university's score

### Leading indicators (weekly, first 8 weeks)
- Search Console impressions for university + compassion / welfare / wellbeing query combinations
- Score Watch subscriptions for university entities (target: 25 within 60 days)
- Social shares of individual university score pages

### Baseline
No university-query traffic baseline exists on the site today. The 500 / 30-day launch target is set by analogy to the AI Labs index launch (nearest comparable by entity count and audience specificity). If the AI Labs launch data is available, that replaces this proxy.

### Success is incomplete if
- No citation or media pickup occurs within 90 days — this indicates the entity selection or scoring did not produce a newsworthy finding; the issue to diagnose is either score compression (too many entities in Functional band with undifferentiated scores) or insufficient PR distribution.
- Score Watch adoption for university entities is below 10 subscribers at 60 days — this indicates the audience (students, families) does not yet trust or find the product; the issue to diagnose is messaging clarity on the index page, not the index itself.

## 8. Independence Safeguards

These are not MVP features — they are non-negotiable constraints that apply permanently.

| Safeguard | Implementation |
|---|---|
| No paid inclusion | Universities are selected by composite of public global rankings only. No university can pay for inclusion, exclusion, or score reconsideration. |
| No paid score improvement | Commercial products (whitepapers, Score Watch, institutional reports) support access and interpretation only. They do not change scores. |
| No endorsement relationships | The benchmark does not endorse, certify, or partner with any ranked university. No university may display a "Compassion Benchmark Certified" badge or equivalent. |
| No pre-publication review by ranked entities | Universities do not receive draft scores before publication. If an institution contacts the benchmark before publication, the contact is logged in the independence audit trail and the score is not changed in response to the contact. |
| Post-publication corrections | If a university provides verifiable public evidence that a cited source was in error, benchmark-research reviews the evidence and may update the score in the next scheduled review cycle. The correction is documented publicly. Score changes driven by evidence are not hidden; they are announced. |
| Legal threat response | If a university sends a legal threat regarding its score, the benchmark publishes the existence of the threat (not the full legal text) in the independence audit log. No score changes in response to legal pressure. Legal counsel is engaged for response. |

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Litigious / PR-active institutions challenge scores publicly | High (several top-100 institutions have active legal and PR infrastructure) | Reputational; could distract from launch | Evidence standards (section 6) ensure every score is traceable to public sources. Legal threat response protocol (section 8) is prepared before publication. All evidence notes are retained permanently. |
| Evidence unevenness above rank ~80 produces score compression or inaccuracy for non-Anglophone institutions | High | Credibility; potential bias criticism | Confidence flag (section 3, section 4) makes uncertainty visible. The `"low"` confidence category is published openly, not hidden. Scoring agents must document estimation explicitly. |
| Score compression: too many institutions cluster in Functional band (40–60) | Medium | Reduces differentiation value; reduces media interest | Benchmark-research reviews pre-publication distribution. If >50% of entities fall in a single band, scoring agent reviews anchors against the dimension rubrics to confirm scores are calibrated rather than defaulted. |
| Graduate student or adjunct advocacy groups challenge the framing of BND scores | Medium | Community trust; reputational | BND signal map (section 5) sources labor conditions from union contracts and AAUP data — third-party sources, not institution self-reporting. Methodology is published. |
| Institutions with very little public evidence receive scores that appear authoritative but are not | Medium | Credibility | `"low"` confidence flag and published evidence notes address this directly. Consider excluding entities where fewer than 3 of 8 dimensions have any direct public evidence (treat as "insufficient evidence" rather than scoring at 0). |
| University administrators attempt to leverage Score Watch subscriptions for score influence | Low | Independence | Score Watch is a notification product. The CRM does not distinguish institutional subscribers from individual subscribers for scoring purposes. The scoring process is isolated from subscriber data. |
| Index launch timing conflicts with an active campus harm event involving a ranked institution | Low (unpredictable) | PR sensitivity | No timing change required. The benchmark scores on public evidence, not on current news. If an event occurs during scoring, benchmark-research notes the event in the evidence file but waits for official findings before adjusting relevant subdimensions. |
