# Nonprofit Simplification Review — Product/Scope Lens

**Version:** 1.0 · **Date:** 2026-07-12 · **Role:** Product Manager
**Status:** Proposal for founder review — no code changed. Companion to architecture/UX/engineering simplification reviews for the same transition.
**Trigger:** Compassion Benchmark is transitioning from a commercial/monetized model to a non-profit model. This review proposes scope cuts that reduce commercial surface and ongoing maintenance while preserving the mission (independent measurement of institutional compassion) and the daily research pipeline that produces it.

---

## 1. Framing — what a nonprofit benchmark institution's MVP surface actually is

A nonprofit benchmark institution's product is trust in a number, not a sales funnel. The thing being sold today — a 15-page commercial surface (pricing, services, contact-sales, enterprise, data-licenses, advisory, certified-assessments, assess-your-organization, purchase-research, api-access, ai-evaluation-suite, prompting-suite-for-humans, score-watch, supporters, thank-you) — was built for a $10k-MRR direct-sales motion (`docs/SALES_PLAN.md`, `docs/REVENUE_MODEL.md`) run by a solo founder. That motion is optimized for revenue extraction from data users. A nonprofit's job is the opposite optimization: maximize public trust and citation reach per unit of maintenance effort, and fund itself through donations/grants/modest earned income rather than an outbound sales cadence.

**The nonprofit MVP surface is five things:**

1. **The rankings** — 8 published indexes (Countries, Fortune 500, AI Labs, Robotics Labs, US States, US Cities, Global Cities, Universities), entity detail pages, and history pages. This is the actual product.
2. **The methodology** — `/methodology`, dimension/subdimension framework, evidence hierarchy, independence policy. This is the credibility layer.
3. **The daily pipeline output** — `/updates`, archive, forward-watch, special briefings, RSS/JSON feeds. This is what makes the institution feel alive and citable daily, and it is already the thing the sales plan itself calls "the outreach engine" — it is more valuable as free, citable, press-ready output than as a lead-gen mechanism.
4. **A single funding ask** — donate/support (already exists at `/supporters`, underbuilt and not live).
5. **A single "who we are and how we're funded" page** — `/about` + independence policy, expanded to carry the nonprofit funding-transparency story that funders and press will look for.

Everything else on the current site is either a redundant restatement of #4 (7 near-duplicate commercial pages), a monetization mechanism whose maintenance burden (Gumroad + Worker webhook + Listmonk transactional pipeline, per `docs/PRD_MONETIZATION.md`) is disproportionate to what it earns, or a product line (AI Evaluation Suite, Prompting Suite for Humans) that is adjacent to but not the mission.

## 3. DO NOT CUT — core to mission, credibility, or independence

- **The daily research pipeline** (overnight scanner → assessor → digest → score-updater) and its audit trail (`research/change-proposals/*.json`, `research/alert-deliveries/`, `research/integrity-reports/`). This is the actual product; every other page is a window onto it.
- **All 8 published indexes and their entity detail + history pages** (Countries, Fortune 500, AI Labs, Robotics Labs, US States, US Cities, Global Cities, Universities). This is the ranking itself.
- **`/methodology`** — the 8-dimension/40-subdimension framework, evidence hierarchy, band definitions, and the recently-hardened accuracy fixes (per `SYSTEM_HEALTH.md` Iteration 9). This is the credibility layer a nonprofit lives or dies on.
- **The independence policy statement itself** ("entities never pay for inclusion, score changes, or suppression of findings") — must appear at least as prominently after the transition as it does today, not less. If anything, strengthen it: a nonprofit's independence claim is more scrutinized, not less, because donors and grantors will ask the same "how do I know this isn't biased" question investors do today.
- **`/updates`, `/updates/archive`, `/updates/forward-watch`, `/updates/special`, and the RSS/JSON feeds.** This is the free daily distribution layer that makes the institution feel alive and gives press something to cite continuously — keep it fully free, do not consider gating any part of it.
- **The integrity audit tooling** (`research/scripts/integrity-check.mjs`, the weekly independence audit) — this is the mechanism that makes the independence claim verifiable rather than asserted, which matters more, not less, once "trust us, we're a nonprofit" replaces "trust us, we don't sell scores."
- **`/self-assessment`** as a free public tool — keep it, but re-point its post-assessment CTAs away from any cut commercial page (Certified Assessments, Advisory) toward `/methodology` and `/supporters` instead of deleting the tool itself. It is genuine public-interest utility with no entity-payment risk.
- **Citation/press support** (some consolidated form of `/media`'s citation formats and data feeds) — a nonprofit's primary growth channel is being cited, not being sold to; this must survive any consolidation in proposal #8.

## 4. Open questions for the founder

1. **Tax/legal status timing:** Has 501(c)(3) (or equivalent) status been filed or granted yet? This determines whether `/supporters` can legally use "donate" / "tax-deductible" language now or must wait, and affects the wording of proposal #7.
2. **Funding runway:** Do any current revenue lines (Score-Watch subscribers, Gumroad report sales, any closed Sales Plan deals) currently offset real operating costs (VPS, Gumroad fees, Listmonk, Cloudflare Worker)? Proposals #3 and #4 remove the only two SKUs with actual sales history — is there a donation/grant runway to replace that before they're cut, or should they be sunset on a timer (e.g., honor existing subscribers through their paid term, stop selling new ones) rather than cut immediately?
3. **Earned income tolerance:** Many nonprofits retain modest earned-income lines (data licensing to newsrooms/academics, e.g.) without compromising status. Should a *reduced, firewalled* version of data licensing or advisory survive proposal #2 — e.g., one plain "institutional data request" contact path — rather than disappearing entirely? This review recommends consolidation, not necessarily zero earned income.
4. **Existing paying customers:** Are there any current Score-Watch subscribers or closed data-license/advisory clients who need an explicit transition, refund, or grandfathering plan if proposals #3/#4 proceed?
5. **AI Evaluation Suite / Prompting Suite for Humans disposition:** Given real past investment in these (large embedded content libraries), should they be spun out as a separate for-profit product/entity rather than simply retired, or folded into free published research under the nonprofit?
6. **Governance/legal sign-off:** Does a board member or legal reviewer need to approve the removal of Certified Assessments and the broader commercial-surface consolidation before execution, given it touches the independence policy directly?
7. **Nav/brand consequence:** Once `footerLinks.services` and `mainNav` are simplified (proposals #2, #7), what replaces "Services" as a nav label — is "Support" or "Donate" the right primary-nav term, and does that need updating everywhere the old commercial ladder is referenced (e.g., `/about`, entity-page CTAs)?

## 5. Handoff summary (for downstream agents)

- **Problem:** The current commercial surface (15 pages, a tiered sales ladder, an outbound sales cadence, and one entity-facing paid product) was built for a for-profit revenue target and is disproportionate to what a small nonprofit team can or should maintain — and one product (Certified Assessments) directly conflicts with the independence claim the whole institution is built on.
- **Target users (post-transition):** data users (researchers, journalists, academics, ESG/impact analysts) as free/citation users; individual donors/supporters as the funding base; no scored entity should ever be a commercial counterparty.
- **MVP scope:** rankings + methodology + daily updates/feeds + self-assessment + about/independence + a single, working donate/support surface. Everything else in the table above is a candidate for cut or consolidation.
- **Acceptance criteria for "done":** nav and footer link counts under `services`/`community` collapse from 12 combined links to roughly 2–3; zero pages route to a paid, entity-facing assessment product; `/supporters` checkout actually completes (currently broken); independence-policy language is present and unchanged (or strengthened) everywhere it appeared before.
- **Success metrics:** cannot be fully specified without founder answers to open questions #1–#4 above (funding runway, legal status, and existing-customer handling directly determine what "success" looks like in the first 90 days). Recommend as leading indicators once scoped: `/supporters` conversion rate, donation MRR, and zero support tickets referencing a removed paid product without a stated transition path.
