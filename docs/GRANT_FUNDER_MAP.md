# Grant-Funder Map — Compassion Benchmark

**Prepared:** 2026-06-22 · **Role:** nonprofit grants / development strategist · **Status:** working strategy doc

## What this is

A prioritized, evidence-grounded map of REAL funders for Compassion Benchmark (compassionbenchmark.com) — an independent accountability/measurement institution that scores how institutions recognize, respond to, and reduce suffering across 8 indexes (~1,256 entities), with daily evidence-based research, a public methodology, and a strict independence policy.

Every funder below was verified via web research in June 2026. Where a fact (open-round status, exact grant size, fee %) could not be pinned to a primary source, it is flagged **[verify]** with the source to check. No funder names, programs, deadlines, or grant sizes were invented.

### How to position Compassion Benchmark to funders (read first)

The work reads to different funders as different things. Lead with the framing that matches the funder's mandate:

- **To AI-governance funders:** "an independent third-party measurement / evals / accountability institution for AI and robotics labs" (lead with the AI Labs Index + Robotics Labs Index).
- **To accountability/transparency funders:** "an independent comparative index that holds governments and corporations accountable" (lead with countries, US states, Fortune 500).
- **To data-for-good / public-interest-tech funders:** "open public-interest data infrastructure measuring institutional response to suffering."
- **To journalism funders:** "accountability journalism — daily evidence-based briefings and special investigations" (lead with the daily research + special briefings, NOT the index).

**The single strongest proof point to open any pitch:** the Patrick J. McGovern Foundation funded Full Fact to build an "AI Trust Benchmark" — concrete precedent that a top-tier funder backs exactly this "independent benchmark institution" model.
Source: https://www.mcgovern.org/2025-press-release/ · https://www.mcgovern.org/grants/

### Two hard constraints that shape everything

1. **Structure gate.** Compassion Benchmark is a founder-led project, not a registered nonprofit. Most large foundations require a US 501(c)(3) or a fiscal sponsor. The realistic near-term plan is: secure a **fiscal sponsor first** (weeks), which unlocks the widest set of funders, while pursuing the handful of EA/responsible-tech funders that fund individuals/unincorporated projects directly. See §3.
2. **Independence gate.** The brand promise is "entities never pay for inclusion, score changes, or suppression of findings." Grant funding must not compromise that. Avoid funders who would have a scored entity in scope as a co-funder of the same work, take only **unrestricted or independence-compatible** funding, disclose all funders publicly, and never let a grant deliverable dictate or constrain scores. See §6.

## 3. Structural prerequisites (eligibility — how to get fundable fast)

**Bottom line: fiscal sponsorship, not your own 501(c)(3).** A fiscal sponsor can make Compassion Benchmark eligible to receive tax-deductible grants in **weeks** (sometimes days). A full IRS Form 1023 determination takes **6–10+ months**.

### Fiscal sponsorship — the fast path

- **Typical fee:** 5–15% of funds raised (most commonly 5–10%); government/international grants often higher (up to ~14%).
- **Break-even vs running your own 501(c)(3):** roughly $150k–$300k/yr in revenue. Below that, a sponsor is cheaper than the cost + founder time of maintaining your own nonprofit.
- **Funder perception:** generally positive and normalized; often seen as *more* credible than a brand-new 501(c)(3) because an experienced sponsor vets and stewards funds. Caveat: some government/foundation programs require an independent 501(c)(3) and won't fund a sponsored project — verify per grant.
- **Model A (comprehensive):** project is an internal program of the sponsor; sponsor holds legal/financial control + handles admin. Best for maximum hand-holding.
- **Model C (grantee/pre-approved):** you are a separate legal entity (e.g., LLC); sponsor re-grants to you; you keep operational control. Often reserved for vetted/established projects.

### IRS Form 1023-EZ (if/when you want your own entity)

- **Eligibility:** gross receipts ≤ $50k/yr (each of past 3 + projected next 3) AND assets ≤ $250k. Must incorporate at state level + get EIN first.
- **Cost:** $275 (Pay.gov). **Timeline:** ~2–4 weeks IRS processing.
- **Cons:** the <$50k cap is a poor fit if chasing a meaningful grant (one large grant blows the threshold → forces full Form 1023); you take on all compliance (990s, state registration, board governance).

### Recommended structure decision

**Two-track:** Start under a fiscal sponsor now for immediate eligibility → spin out into your own 501(c)(3) once you approach the ~$150k–$300k break-even or hit funders that require an independent entity. Plan 6–12 months of overlap. Ashgro, Rethink Charity, and New Venture Fund are explicitly built to incubate toward independence.

**Independence note:** choose a sponsor whose membership/editorial-independence standards reinforce (not dilute) the benchmark's no-pay-for-inclusion brand, and confirm the sponsor structures grants to avoid "mere conduit" treatment so private foundations can give safely.

## 5. 90-day grant-pursuit sequence

**Earliest realistic dollars:** Manifund/LTFF in weeks 2–6. Pulitzer/SFF in 2–4 months. Institutional (McGovern, Coefficient, Humanity AI, FMF) in 4–9 months. Plan fast money to bridge to slow money.

## 6. Risks & honesty notes

**Confidence levels.** Tier-1 AI-governance funders and fiscal-sponsor facts are **high confidence** (primary-sourced). Items flagged **[verify]**: open-round status for data.org / Knight / GNI; exact fees for PPF, NVF, RPA, Rethink Charity; current open windows for OSF and the MacArthur democracy open-call mechanics; whether Multiplier/HCB consider an AI-governance benchmark in-scope. **Verify all "open" pathways on the funder's live site before applying** — policies change fast (Mott closed its open door in Jan 2025; the AI Governance RFP closed Jan 2026; Craig Newmark exited journalism funding Jan 2026).

**Must be verified before relying on a funder:** (1) does the specific grant permit a fiscally-sponsored applicant? (2) is the round actually open? (3) does any co-funder of that grant pool include an entity Compassion Benchmark scores (independence conflict)?

**Independence / brand risk (the most important risk).**
- **Conflict of interest:** several strong AI-governance funders (FMF AI Safety Fund) are backed by AI labs that Compassion Benchmark scores. Taking money from a pooled fund whose backers you rate could undermine the "entities never pay for inclusion or score changes" promise. Mitigations: prefer funders with no scored entity in the relevant pool; take only **unrestricted** funding; never accept a deliverable that constrains or dictates scores; **publicly disclose every funder** and recuse funders' scores from any influence. For the FMF fund specifically, weigh whether any acceptance is worth the perception cost on the AI Labs Index — it may be safer to decline.
- **Restricted funding drift:** large institutional grants often come restricted to a program. Restricted money that pushes scope away from the independent core (e.g., a funder wanting only "their" sector measured) is a mission-creep risk. Prefer general operating / core support (Luminate, OSF, Coefficient can give unrestricted).
- **Earned-revenue scrutiny:** the project sells digital research assets. Independence-focused funders will scrutinize commercial revenue + conflicts. Have a clean, documented firewall between commercial services (access/interpretation only) and scoring, consistent with the existing independence policy.
- **Fiscal-sponsor independence fit:** pick a sponsor whose standards reinforce editorial independence; ensure the sponsor isn't a "mere conduit."

**Dependency risk.** EA/AI-safety funders (Manifund, LTFF, EAIF, SFF, Open Phil) are the warmest near-term sources but cluster in one ideological ecosystem and one funder (Open Phil) anchors much of it. Diversify deliberately into accountability/transparency (Luminate, OSF, Wallace Global, Oak) and the benchmark-funder leads (CIFF, Generation, Aviva) so the institution isn't single-ecosystem-dependent — which also protects the independence brand.
