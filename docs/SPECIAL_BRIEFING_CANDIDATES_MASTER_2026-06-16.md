## 1. The Equity Tax — the one dimension that drags everyone down
- **Thesis:** Equity (EQU) is the single most diagnostic dimension in the record — it is the (tied) weakest dimension for **1,046 of 1,156 entities (90%)** and the lone sub-band score for **62 of 64 exemplars**. The institutions that get everything else right still fail the most vulnerable.
- **Scope:** all 7 indexes (the EQU-weakest cohort), with the exemplar-equity-gap as the twist.
- **Lens convergence:** Data #1 ("Equity Floor") + Knowledge #4 ("Equity Tax") — both independently flagged it as *the* number.
- **Teaches:** what a dimension is + how a single weak dimension caps the integration premium. **Methodology tension:** SBQ-8.
- **Why now / why a good read:** the most data-backed, cross-cutting finding the benchmark has; "even the best fail the vulnerable" is a strong, defensible headline.

## 2. The Middle of the Scale — how to read the 92% that are neither monstrous nor model
- **Thesis:** The published briefings spent the extremes (floor + exemplars); but almost every entity a reader looks up lives in the **Developing/Functional middle**. What does a 50 actually *mean*, and why is the boring middle the hardest band to read?
- **Scope:** the Developing + Functional bands across indexes (the bulk cohort the extremes skipped).
- **Lens:** Knowledge #1 (the top reader-value pick) — the largest comprehension gap.
- **Teaches:** the 5-band vocabulary + base-score-vs-integration-premium (the most under-taught mechanic). **Pairs as a "How to Read a Score" on-ramp for newcomers.**
- **Why now:** highest search relevance (mid-band entities are the most-searched); turns the franchise from "extremes only" into a usable reading guide.

## 3. What the Product Is For — robotics & AI at the harm frontier
- **Thesis:** In the AI + robotics indexes the product's **purpose predicts the score**: defense / surveillance / weapons cluster at the floor; healthcare / accessibility at the ceiling. CB is the **only institution that scores robotics labs at all** — no comparator exists.
- **Scope:** 50 robotics labs + 50 AI labs (by category/sector), e.g. the defense→floor vs healthcare→ceiling gradient.
- **Lens convergence:** Data #2 ("What the Product Is For") + Competitive #5 ("Robotics at the Harm Frontier").
- **Teaches:** how purpose/end-use flows into Boundaries/Integrity/Accountability. **Methodology tension:** SBQ-1/SBQ-7.
- **Why now:** humanoid/automation scaling is the fastest 2026 deployment story; a genuinely unoccupied citation lane.

## 4. State of Exception — when governments codify impunity
- **Thesis:** A cluster of governments is converting emergency law into codified impunity, cratering Accountability + Integrity through *legal* mechanisms, not single atrocities. **Bolivia's 28.4 → 6.3 collapse across cycles is the benchmark's only "predicted-trigger-realized" sequence.**
- **Scope:** ~12–15 Critical/backsliding countries (+ the US-states/cities "compassion-buffer" angle).
- **Lens convergence:** Data #3 ("State of Exception") + Competitive #3 ("Democratic Backsliding") — and the earlier unbuilt proposal, now stronger.
- **Teaches:** the "authorized-but-not-yet-implemented" ruling + civil-society-suppression as a floor trigger. **Methodology tension:** debuts v1.3 rulings.
- **Why now:** rides the V-Dem / Freedom House spring-report citation window; cross-govt/state/city is novel vs both.

## 5. Allegation, Indictment, Ruling — how the benchmark scores accusations vs proof
- **Thesis:** **42 state attorneys general couldn't move OpenAI's score; one documented severance clause moved Oracle into Critical.** A deep-dive on the pre-adjudication discipline (and the compelled-conduct doctrine) that separates noise from evidence — the rule that keeps the benchmark credible.
- **Scope:** a curated cross-index set (OpenAI, Oracle, UnitedHealth, Anthropic, Microsoft, Hungary) — allegation vs adjudication vs compelled action.
- **Lens convergence:** Knowledge #3 ("Allegation, Indictment, Ruling") + Knowledge #2 ("Why a Score Doesn't Move") + Data #8 ("Compelled-Conduct Doctrine").
- **Teaches:** evidence tiers, the pre-adjudication ceiling, freshness, conduct-vs-coercion. **Methodology tension:** SBQ-10/16/17.
- **Why now:** a trust/authority read that turns the methodology rigor into a compelling story — exactly what earns citations from skeptical journalists + researchers.
