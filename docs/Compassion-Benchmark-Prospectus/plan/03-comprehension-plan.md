# 03 — Comprehension & Teaching Plan

**Owner:** Knowledge / Comprehension Architect
**Scope:** How a sophisticated but non-expert funder comes to genuinely understand the standard across Parts I–III. This is a teaching plan, not manuscript. It governs the *order, depth, and device* by which hard concepts are introduced, plus the accuracy guardrails authors must not breach.

**North star:** after Part II a funder can explain, unprompted, (1) what the benchmark measures, (2) how a 0–100 score is built from behavior, and (3) why a paper claim does not move a score. After Part III they can defend the standard against the two questions every reviewer asks: "isn't compassion subjective?" and "can a lab buy a better score?"

## 4. Worked-example strategy

Thread **two running illustrative institutions** through the explanation chapters so the abstract scoring becomes concrete, and reuse the same two whenever a mechanism needs a face.

1. **Primary (AI lab) — a clearly-labeled composite illustration, NOT a named live score.** Build a fictional but realistic frontier AI lab (e.g., "a frontier AI lab") to demonstrate the dimension wheel, the 0–100 math, the integration premium (give it a spiky 90/40 profile so it under-earns), and the pressure test. Framing must be explicit: "an illustrative profile, not a published score." This protects independence and avoids stating a number that is not in source_notes.

2. **Secondary (healthcare, real and sourced) — UnitedHealth Group, composite 10.2, Critical.** This *is* in source_notes §4/§8 and is the canonical real example. Use it once, in Ch13/Ch15, to make near-floor limitation and attribution concrete (the DOJ-probe expansion logged as a tier upgrade with no composite move). Cite it as a real published score because it is documented.

**Rule:** any *number presented as a fact* must trace to source_notes (UHG 10.2; Harvard 52.3; university mean 46.2; zero Exemplary universities). Every *invented* number must be visibly flagged illustrative. Never blur the two. Reuse the same two entities across chapters so the reader's mental model compounds instead of resetting.

## 5. Accuracy guardrails — the must-not-get-wrong list

Authors of Parts I–III check every draft against this before submission.

1. **Entity count is 1,256, never 1,155.** The grant docs say 1,155; that figure is stale. Use 1,256 (8 indexes: Countries 193, F500 448, Global Cities 250, AI 50, Robotics 50, US States 21, US Cities 144, Universities 100). "Scanned ~1,260" only where copy literally means the scanner's broader sweep.
2. **Scoring is v1.2, never the legacy /80 + 20.** Base = `((mean of 8 dims − 1)/4)×100`; integration premium caps at **+10**, not +20. Bands are 0–20 / 20–40 / 40–60 / 60–80 / 80–100 (no 21/41/61/81 boundaries).
3. **Keep automated and certified assessment distinct.** The 1,256 public scores come from the **automated nightly pipeline (BUILT)**, human-gated at the score-updater. The **7-session Human Assessment Battery is ASPIRATIONAL** and underpins a future paid certification. Do not imply humans hand-assess 1,256 entities.
4. **Do not overclaim the integrity audit or Score Watch as fully operational.** The weekly integrity-check is **spec/stub**, describe as designed/in-rollout. Score-Watch alerts are sold but automated *delivery* is partial. No "weekly automated audits already run" language.
5. **Public methodology transparency is the selling point, lead with it.** Published rubric, weights, sources, and the reproducibility test (a successor can rebuild any score) are the differentiator versus capturable/paywalled indices. The independence statement is load-bearing prose, not an appendix footnote.
6. **Impact is forward-looking.** Baseline external citations are 0; KPIs are targets. Never present projected revenue or citation counts as achieved.
7. **A score is reconstructable.** Every published composite must be shown as derivable from its 8 dimension scores — say so wherever the formula appears; it is the honesty proof.
