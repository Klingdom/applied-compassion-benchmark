# Layout and Design Direction

## Design Goal

The publication should feel serious, calm, credible, and human.

Not startup.

Not corporate ESG.

Not academic PDF wall-of-text.

Not glossy philanthropy brochure.

Aim for the feel of a flagship public-interest publication that could sit comfortably beside a Gates Foundation annual letter, Stanford HAI report, or high-quality nonprofit research prospectus.

## Page Rhythm

Use variation.

A strong 60 to 70 page publication should include:

- full-page chapter openers
- two-column narrative spreads
- full-page graphics
- callout pages
- short case examples
- tables
- timelines
- diagrams
- pull quotes
- occasional dense methodology pages
- spacious reflection pages

Do not make every page the same.

## Typography

Recommended style:

- clean serif or humanist sans serif for body text
- restrained sans serif for headings
- generous line spacing
- strong hierarchy
- minimal decorative fonts
- avoid overly futuristic AI-style typography

## Color Palette

Use a restrained palette.

Suggested direction:

- warm off-white background
- deep charcoal text
- muted blue, green, and gold accents
- limited use of red for active harm or risk
- one color family for the eight dimensions, with subtle differentiation

Avoid neon gradients, generic AI purple, and overly bright startup colors.

## Cover

The cover should not show abstract AI graphics, glowing networks, or generic business hands.

Use a human-centered institutional image:

- public service waiting room
- clinician and patient
- teacher and student
- humanitarian aid context
- city street with ordinary civic life
- quiet institutional setting with human presence

The image should imply that institutions touch lives.

## Eight-Dimension Icon System

Create simple icons for:

- Awareness: signal, listening, detection
- Empathy: perspective, human experience
- Action: response, hand, movement
- Equity: balance, distribution
- Boundaries: guardrail, consent, sustainability
- Accountability: repair, record, answerability
- Systems Thinking: roots, network, structure
- Integrity: pressure, alignment, consistency

Icons should be simple line icons, not decorative illustrations.

## Core Graphics

Include graphics for:

1. Institutional decision map
2. Measurement evolution timeline
3. Existing frameworks comparison matrix
4. Eight dimensions wheel
5. Evidence hierarchy pyramid
6. Assessment lifecycle
7. Score-to-improvement cycle
8. Pressure test principle
9. Maturity gate timeline
10. AI and robotics accountability map
11. Theory of change
12. Impact framework
13. Governance model
14. Financial sustainability model
15. Funding opportunity menu
16. Five-year roadmap

## Pull Quotes

Pull quotes should be specific and sharp.

Avoid motivational quotes that could belong anywhere.

Good pull quote style:

"At institutional scale, compassion is not a feeling. It is a pattern of decisions."

"Mission statements are not evidence."

"High performance when it is easy is not evidence of institutional character."

"The issue is not whether institutions care. The issue is whether care survives process, pressure, scale, incentives, and time."

## Sidebar Style

Sidebars should clarify, not decorate.

Use sidebars for:

- definitions
- short examples
- funder implications
- methodology notes
- cautionary limitations
- how a reader might use the benchmark

## Tables

Use tables when comparing:

- existing frameworks
- evidence tiers
- funding packages
- output vs outcome vs impact
- governance safeguards
- chapter roadmap

Keep tables readable. Avoid spreadsheet density.

## Image Principles

Images should:

- show real people and real institutional contexts
- avoid poverty porn
- avoid staged corporate collaboration imagery
- avoid generic AI visuals
- respect dignity
- support the chapter's argument

## PDF Production Notes

The manuscript should be layout-ready, not design-complete.

Keep Markdown clean.

Use bracketed layout notes only in `layout-notes.md`, not in the final manuscript unless explicitly desired.

Design should strengthen credibility, not compensate for weak writing.
