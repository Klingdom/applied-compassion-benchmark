# Quality Gates

Do not mark the project complete until every gate is passed or explicitly documented as unresolved.

## Gate 1: Strategic Coherence

The manuscript must move naturally from:

problem

to measurement gap

to standard

to public benefit

to institutional readiness

to funding opportunity.

If the reader encounters funding before understanding the public need, restructure.

## Gate 2: Human Voice

Reject any chapter that sounds generated.

Warning signs:

- uniform paragraph length
- overuse of one-line paragraphs
- generic observations
- artificial profundity
- repeated phrasing
- over-explaining
- empty transitions
- interchangeable nonprofit language

If detected, rewrite.

## Gate 3: Specificity

Every chapter must contain at least one insight or example specific to Compassion Benchmark.

If another nonprofit could use the chapter with a simple search-and-replace, it fails.

## Gate 4: Reader Momentum

For each chapter ask:

Would a busy, skeptical, intelligent reader voluntarily continue?

If not, rewrite the opening, sharpen the argument, or cut the chapter.

## Gate 5: Funder Readiness

The manuscript must answer:

- Why this matters
- Why now
- Why this organization
- Why philanthropy
- What changes if funded
- How independence is protected
- How impact will be measured
- How the work becomes sustainable

These answers should be embedded naturally, not bolted on.

## Gate 6: Evidence Discipline

Flag unsupported claims.

Do not overstate:

- market size
- legal conclusions
- nonprofit tax treatment
- impact already achieved
- funder interest
- adoption likelihood
- uniqueness
- platform readiness
- regulatory relevance

Use careful wording.

## Gate 7: Brand Discipline

Check every file for:

- no public-facing "ACB"
- no public-facing "Applied Compassion Benchmark"
- consistent use of "Compassion Benchmark"
- no em dashes
- no generic brand drift

## Gate 8: Structural Completeness

Confirm:

- all chapters exist
- all appendices exist
- all required output files exist
- source notes complete
- graphics plan complete
- layout notes complete
- funder summary complete
- claims-to-verify complete

## Gate 9: Layout Readiness

Every chapter must have:

- visual recommendation
- pull quote candidate
- layout note
- sidebar or engagement point when useful

The manuscript should be ready for a designer.

## Gate 10: Final Acceptance Test

The manuscript is complete only when all are true:

- A professional editor would believe it was written by a single experienced author.
- A foundation program officer would continue beyond the Executive Summary without feeling they are reading a grant proposal.
- A journalist could quote individual passages without rewriting them.
- Every chapter contains at least one memorable insight specific to Compassion Benchmark.
- The document establishes Compassion Benchmark as a new category of public-interest institution rather than another mission-driven startup.
- If every occurrence of "Compassion Benchmark" were replaced with another organization's name, the manuscript would no longer make sense.
