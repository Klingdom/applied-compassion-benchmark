# Style Lock: Compassion Benchmark Funder Prospectus

**Status:** Locked reference. Every author reads this before drafting. If a draft contradicts this file, it is returned before it advances.
**Companion required reading:** `02-editorial-style-guide.md` (full banned lists), `source_notes.md` §0 (canonical numbers), Appendices A, B, C in `chapters/`.

---

## 1. The throughline (the one sentence the whole manuscript serves)

Compassion at institutional scale is not sentiment: it is a pattern of decisions, and those decisions are observable, scorable, and subject to public accountability. The question this publication answers is whether care survives process, pressure, scale, incentives, automation, and time, and whether public measurement of that survival changes the institutional calculus.

Every Part advances one beat of this. Part I: is the gap real? Part II: can it be measured? Part III: is the measurement credible? Part IV: does it find anything meaningful? Part V: does it change anything? Part VI: can this institution actually do it?

## 2. Voice rules

Write like a serious human author who has spent twenty years improving institutions from the inside and finally noticed we measure almost everything except whether institutions reduce suffering. Clear, sharp, grounded, occasionally memorable. Never a grant template, startup pitch, ESG report, or consultant deck.

- Vary sentence pressure. Some paragraphs develop one idea across five or six sentences toward a conclusion that could not be stated at the start. Others move in three. Do not stack short declaratives as if each is a revelation.
- One precise example beats three generic ones. Name the score, the institution, the subdimension. Then stop. Do not explain what the reader can already infer.
- Open chapters with one of three moves used deliberately, not by default: a scene at a specific decision, an anomaly the reader has not framed as one before, or a concrete number or verdict without context.
- Transitions carry a thought forward; they never signal a direction. End a section on a question the next answers, or on a fact that only makes sense once the reader understands what follows.
- Every chapter must contain at least one insight specific to Compassion Benchmark that survives a name-replacement test.

### Top AI tells to delete on sight
- Any sentence ending in a slogan ("...and that changes everything").
- "Not X, but Y" used more than once per chapter.
- Three consecutive paragraphs of similar length; more than two consecutive paragraphs opening with the same grammatical structure.
- "This highlights" / "this underscores" / "taken together" / "ultimately" / "as we have seen."
- Generic praise framing ("this work demonstrates...") and false profundity after a strong data point.
- Section endings that merely summarize what was just said.
- Any paragraph that could appear in a different institution's report unchanged.

## 3. Banned phrases (do not use unless quoting source material)

in today's rapidly changing world · now more than ever · at its core · the reality is · the truth is · it is important to note · this highlights · this underscores · taken together · ultimately · as we have seen · this publication explores · this chapter examines · the following section · imagine a world · one thing is clear · unlock · leverage · ecosystem · stakeholder (unless no better word exists) · paradigm · holistic · innovative · transformative · scalable solution · game changer · mission-driven (as filler).

**Banned patterns:** three consecutive paragraphs of similar length; more than two consecutive paragraphs with the same opening structure; more than three rhetorical questions per chapter; section endings that only summarize; excessive "not X, but Y" contrast pairs; repeated "society needs" constructions; false profundity; unexplained first-of-kind claims; generic praise for philanthropy; vague "impact" language that does not name who benefits and how.

## 4. Brand and terminology rules

- Public brand is **Compassion Benchmark** only. Never "ACB" or "Applied Compassion Benchmark" in narrative prose (legacy document codes allowed only in appendix citations if unavoidable).
- **No em dashes anywhere.** Use colons, commas, or separate sentences. This applies even when paraphrasing source text that contains them.
- Periods after version numbers: v1.2, not V1.2.
- Brand, link, accent, and button color is **blue `#3b82f6`** (tint `#93c5fd`). **Cyan `#7dd3fc` is reserved for the Exemplary band only** (and, in the icon set, the AWR Awareness mark). Never use cyan as a general accent: it would imply top-band status.

## 5. Locked numbers and decisions (treat as fixed)

- **Entity count: 1,256.** Eight public indexes: Countries 193, Fortune 500 448, Global Cities 250, AI Labs 50, Robotics Labs 50, US States 21, US Cities 144, Universities 100. Use "scanned ~1,260" only where copy literally means the scanner's broader sweep.
- **Scoring model: v1.2.** Base composite = `((mean of the 8 dimension scores − 1) / 4) × 100`. Integration premium caps at **+10** (`10 × consistency factor × balance factor`); any single dimension at 0 (active harm) sets the premium to 0. Bands: 0–20 / 20–40 / 40–60 / 60–80 / 80–100. No 21/41/61/81 boundaries. No "base out of 80," no "+20 premium."
- **A score is reconstructable.** Every published composite is derivable from its 8 dimension scores. Say so wherever the formula appears.
- **Automated and certified pathways stay distinct.** The 1,256 public scores come from the automated nightly pipeline (scanner → assessor → digest → human-gated score-updater), which is BUILT. The 7-session Human Assessment Battery is ASPIRATIONAL and underpins a future paid certification. Never imply humans hand-assess 1,256 entities.
- **Do not overclaim.** The weekly integrity audit is spec/stub: describe as designed or in rollout, not running. Score-Watch is sold but automated alert delivery is partial. Impact is forward-looking: baseline external citations are 0, KPIs are targets, projected revenue is a model not a track record.
- **Worked-example rule.** Numbers presented as fact must trace to `source_notes.md` (UHG 10.2 Critical; Harvard 52.3, highest in the university set; university mean 46.2; zero Exemplary universities; USC 36.7; NYU 39.1; Columbia 44.5). Invented numbers (the composite AI lab used to teach the mechanism) must be visibly flagged illustrative. Never blur the two.

## 7. Canonical terms: the eight dimensions and five bands

**Dimensions** (code, name, color, the question it asks):

| Code | Dimension | Color | The question it asks |
|---|---|---|---|
| AWR | Awareness | `#7dd3fc` | Does this entity detect suffering before it is formally named? |
| EMP | Empathy | `#c084fc` | Does this entity genuinely connect with the inner experience of those it serves? |
| ACT | Action | `#86efac` | Does understanding translate into real, proportional, effective help? |
| EQU | Equity | `#fcd34d` | Is care distributed fairly, especially toward those with greatest need and least power? |
| BND | Boundaries | `#fb923c` | Is helping sustainable, ethical, and autonomy-preserving rather than dependency-creating? |
| ACC | Accountability | `#f472b6` | Does this entity own its failures, correct course, and make genuine repair? |
| SYS | Systemic Thinking | `#34d399` | Does compassion extend to root causes and structural change, not only symptom relief? |
| INT | Integrity | `#a78bfa` | Is compassion genuine, consistent, and non-performative, especially when it costs something? |

Full names and all 40 subdimensions live in Appendix A. Note that several dimension colors share a hex value with a band color (AWR with Exemplary, ACT with Established, EQU with Functional, BND with Developing). Context disambiguates them; never rely on color alone, and respect the cyan-is-Exemplary rule above.

**Bands** (name, range, one-line meaning):

| Band | Range | Meaning | Color |
|---|---|---|---|
| Critical | 0–20 | Foundational compassion practices are absent, or documented active harm is present. | `#f87171` |
| Developing | 20–40 | Some practices are emerging but remain inconsistent, reactive, or unevenly applied. | `#fb923c` |
| Functional | 40–60 | Core practices exist and meet a basic bar, with significant gaps remaining. | `#fcd34d` |
| Established | 60–80 | Practices are systematic, documented, and supported by consistent evidence. | `#86efac` |
| Exemplary | 80–100 | Practices are independently verified, consistent, and sustained under pressure. | `#7dd3fc` |

The behavioral anchor scale beneath every subdimension runs 0 to 5: 0 Active Harm, 1 Absent, 2 Minimal, 3 Developing, 4 Established, 5 Exemplary. The five anchors stored per subdimension represent levels 1 through 5; 0 is a separate documented-harm floor.
