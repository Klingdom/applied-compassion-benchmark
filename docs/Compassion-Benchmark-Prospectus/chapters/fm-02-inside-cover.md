# Inside Cover

Compassion Benchmark is an independent benchmark institution. It measures how institutions recognize, respond to, and reduce suffering, and it publishes the scores.

This is a funder prospectus. It sets out the measurement gap the institution exists to close, the standard it uses, the evidence behind that standard, what the standard finds when applied to real institutions, and the work that philanthropic capital would fund. It is written to be argued with. Where the work is built, it says so. Where the work is planned or aspirational, it says that instead.

What this document is not: a marketing brochure, a finished impact report, or a claim of results not yet earned. The benchmark has no external citations to point to yet, and no institution has visibly changed because of a published score. Those limits are stated here and throughout, because a measurement institution that overstates its own evidence forfeits the only thing that makes it worth funding.

One rule governs everything that follows, and it comes first:

> Entities never pay for inclusion, score changes, or suppression of findings. Commercial services support access, interpretation, and institutional use only. This separation must be preserved in all content and code.
