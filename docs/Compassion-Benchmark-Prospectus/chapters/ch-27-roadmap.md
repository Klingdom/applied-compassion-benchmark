# Chapter 27: Roadmap

The hardest single date on this roadmap is August 8, 2026, the deadline for the Schmidt Sciences proposal, and it is hard for a reason worth stating up front: the sequence here is built so that credibility comes before scale. The temptation in any plan like this is to chase the largest grant first. The discipline this roadmap imposes is the opposite. Stand up the legal and governance structure, win the fast accessible money that does not require it to be finished, publish work an outsider can cite, and only then approach the funders who move in millions but only on a track record. Each phase earns the right to the next.

The gating constraint, before any milestone, is people. A 501(c)(3) cannot be filed with credibility until at least three independent directors have committed in writing, and recruiting them is the slowest and least delegable task in the whole plan. It starts on day one and runs underneath everything below.

**Months 0 to 3: stand up the structure and win the fast money.** The legal path opens with a Model C fiscal sponsor, an existing charity that re-grants funds to Compassion Benchmark and lets it receive tax-deductible grants within weeks rather than waiting on the IRS. With the sponsor signed, the Articles of Incorporation are drafted with the independence clause embedded, the bylaws and conflict-of-interest policy are adopted, the board is constituted, an EIN is obtained, and the full Form 1023 is filed. The full form, not the short one, is the deliberate choice: it lets the organization pre-clear its earned-revenue model and its independence safeguards with the IRS rather than leaving them ambiguous. In parallel, the first grant applications go out, because the Tier 1 funders accept fiscally sponsored applicants and do not wait for a determination letter. The Survival and Flourishing Fund speculation grant and the EA Long-Term Future Fund go in first and in parallel, both capable of deciding in days to weeks. The Schmidt Sciences multi-agent safety proposal goes in against its August 8 deadline. The first quarterly impact report publishes in this window, covering only what the pipeline can already count.

**Months 3 to 6: build track record and field presence.** The Fast Forward accelerator application goes in during its window, which runs July 30 to September 8, 2026; the value there is as much the mentorship and funder network as the twenty-five thousand dollars. One or two citable artifacts publish in this phase, a methodology paper or a special briefing substantial enough that another researcher or a journalist can point to it. The external methodology advisory board is constituted and announced, which strengthens every subsequent application. If the timeline holds, the IRS determination letter arrives somewhere in this window or the next, converting the fiscal-sponsor bridge into a standalone charity. The mid-size applications begin: Knight on its open calls, Sloan via the two-page letter of inquiry that precedes any full proposal there. The weekly integrity audit, partly built today, is brought to full automated operation in this phase, which is the milestone that lets the organization claim it as running rather than designed.

**Months 6 to 12: convert progress into a fundable record.** The target for the full year is one hundred fifty to three hundred thousand dollars in total grants secured across two to four funders. Four quarterly impact reports complete the year. The automated alert product, sold but not yet fully delivering, reaches reliable automated fulfillment in this phase. Cultivation of the relationship-gated funders begins, not as cold applications but as logged contact: an unsolicited concept note to Open Philanthropy, program-officer conversations at the foundations that only find organizations through their published work. A track-record packet is assembled, the grant wins and delivered metrics and any early citations compiled into the document that every Year 2 conversation will start from.

Two milestones in that twelve-month span deserve to be called out as the ones a funder should watch, because they are the difference between intention and institution. The first is the IRS determination letter, which turns the independence rule from founder policy into enforceable law. The second is the first documented outside citation of a score, which today stands at zero and is the leading indicator of whether the theory of change in Chapter 22 is beginning to hold. Neither can be willed into existence on a fixed date, which is exactly why they are the honest measures of progress rather than the flattering ones.

The horizon past the first year is sketched deliberately lightly, because a roadmap that claims precision two and three years out is not being candid. The Year 2 funding target is three hundred to six hundred thousand dollars from a wider and more diversified set of funders, including renewals of any Year 1 wins and the mid-size foundations that needed a track record to engage. The structural decisions that wait for scale wait on purpose: a first full-time research hire is triggered by sustained grant funding above roughly four hundred thousand dollars a year, and a wholly-owned taxable subsidiary for the commercial work is evaluated only if earned revenue grows beyond what a related educational program comfortably covers, not at formation. Scope expansion of the indexes past 1,256 entities is a Year 2 question, not a Year 1 promise, because maintaining the credibility of the existing scores matters more than inflating the count.

The far end of the roadmap is not a milestone at all but a condition, and Chapter 30 names it: a measure cited until citation becomes habit, contested until the contest itself signals the measure matters, embedded until an institution that expects to be measured maintains its conduct the way it now maintains its compliance. That is a decade-scale outcome and it cannot be scheduled. What can be scheduled is everything that makes it possible, and that is what the twelve months above lay out: a legal home, an independent board, a verified integrity audit, the first citable research, the first dated grants. The instrument runs tonight. The institution is what these months build around it.
