# Appendix E: Example Funding Packages

This appendix turns the ask into something a funder can read in a minute: named packages, each sized, each mapped to the specific work it pays for and the metrics it moves. The packages are deliberate groupings of the budget into fundable units. They are not separate products and not separate restricted accounts. The core scoring work is always funded by general-operating or unrestricted grants, per the firewall policy in Appendix D, so the packages describe what a given level of support sustains, not a menu of earmarks.

Everything here is a target. The ask sizes trace to the grant model; the deliverables and the metrics are commitments the institution intends to hold itself to, not results already booked and not funder commitments already secured. The annual cost base the packages cover runs roughly $155k to $300k: founder compensation $120k to $180k, pipeline compute and API $20k to $45k, hosting and infrastructure $5k to $10k, a part-time research contractor $0 to $60k, legal, accounting, and external integrity review $5k to $30k, a fiscal-sponsor fee on grants, and directors-and-officers plus media-liability insurance $3k to $10k.

## Package 1: Seed, $150k, 12 months

The floor that keeps the institution running and converts it from a solo venture into a governed nonprofit. This is the fast-track ask: the funders that fit it decide in days to weeks and accept fiscally-sponsored applicants.

**What it funds.** Founder compensation at the lower band; the nightly pipeline compute and API; hosting and infrastructure; and the governance and formation work, meaning the Form 1023 filing, fiscal-sponsorship fee, and the board and advisory-board recruitment that the conversion depends on. No contractor at this level.

**Deliverables.** All 1,256 scores maintained across the eight indexes; the nightly pipeline held at 90 percent uptime or better; one or two citable artifacts published (a methodology paper or a special briefing); four quarterly impact reports; the nonprofit formation underway with a fiscal sponsor in place.

**Metrics it funds.** Entities scored 1,256 maintained; pipeline uptime 90 percent or better; score-change accountability events documented 12 or more in the year; quarterly impact reports 4 of 4.

**Funder fit.** Survival and Flourishing Fund Speculation Grant ($50k to $100k), EA Long-Term Future Fund ($50k to $100k), and the Fast Forward accelerator ($25k plus capacity). The realistic route to $150k is the first two in parallel, with Fast Forward adding non-cash capacity.

---

## Package 2: Core, $300k, 12 months

Everything in Seed, resourced to grow rather than only to hold. This is the size ask aimed at the single best thematic fit in the near-term calendar.

**What it funds.** Founder compensation at the full band; the part-time research and editorial contractor phased in at Month 3 to 6; index expansion beyond the current eight indexes; platform hardening (the score-update tooling, the badge and subscriber API, and bringing the weekly integrity audit from partial implementation toward fully running); and an external methodology review on top of the weekly audit.

**Deliverables.** Everything in Seed, plus measurable index expansion, a hardened platform with the integrity audit moving into routine operation, and one external methodology review completed in the year.

**Metrics it funds.** The Seed metrics, plus external citations 10 or more; Score Watch subscribers 100 or more; external sites or tools using the badge or API 5 or more; documented entity responses to a score 3 or more.

**Funder fit.** Schmidt Sciences "Scaling AI Safety for a Multi-Agent World," Tier 1 at $150k to $300k plus compute and API credits that directly offset the pipeline line by an estimated $10k to $20k a year. The robotics-lab scoring maps precisely to multi-agent oversight. Note the hard deadline of August 8, 2026.

---

## Package 3: Multi-year, $500k and up, 24 to 36 months

The package that buys permanence rather than a runway. It funds the institution past the point where it depends on any single person or any single grant cycle.

**What it funds.** Sustained operations across two to three years; a first full-time research hire as grant funding crosses roughly $400k a year; a validation study of the methodology, meaning external review and inter-rater reliability work that strengthens scoring defensibility; scaled coverage beyond 1,256 entities; and a fully constituted independent board and external methodology advisory board with the supermajority independence bylaw enacted.

**Deliverables.** Coverage expanded past the current count; the validation study published; the board and advisory board fully seated; the independence bylaw in force; a track record assembled across multiple grant cycles.

**Metrics it funds.** The Year 2 targets: external citations 25 or more; Score Watch subscribers 500 or more; sites or tools using the badge or API 15 or more; documented entity responses 10 or more; and the establishment of a longitudinal baseline for tracking score improvement across a cohort.

**Funder fit.** This is relationship-gated, pursued in Year 2 and beyond: Open Philanthropy (unsolicited concept note, $200k to $500k a year), DRK Foundation (up to $300k over three years, requiring a path-to-lives-impacted framing), Knight Foundation and Sloan Foundation (open calls and a two-page letter of inquiry respectively, $75k to $150k each). These funders are pull, not push; they are reached by being citable, not by cold applications.

## Year-1 and Year-2 envelope

The packages are not additive line by line, since each higher tier absorbs the work of the one below. They describe the same institution funded at three levels of ambition.

| Horizon | Total ask | Funders | Source of the work |

## Flagged for verification

- **[VERIFY] The package decomposition is an authoring construct.** The three packages are a presentation of the budget, built from the grant model's recommended ask sizes and budget narrative and the organization plan's financial model. The named tiers ($150k Seed, $300k Core, $500k-plus Multi-year) follow the prospectus graphics plan. The work groupings (formation, index expansion, platform hardening, validation study) are drawn from the budget lines and activities; they are not separately costed line items in the source.
- **[VERIFY] Year-2 envelope variance.** The grant model states a Year-2 target of $300k to $600k from 3 to 6 funders; `source_notes.md` §10 cites $300k to $750k blended across 2 to 3 funders; the organization plan's KPI table cites $300k to $600k. Reconcile to a single figure before publishing. This appendix uses $300k to $600k.
- **All figures are targets, not commitments.** Ask sizes, deliverables, and metrics are forward-looking. No funder commitment is implied, and external citations baseline at 0 reported. Do not present projected revenue or metrics as achieved.
- **Schmidt deadline and eligibility.** The August 8, 2026 deadline is fixed; eligibility of a fiscally-sponsored project as a nonprofit research organization is flagged for confirmation in the funder research before proposal time is invested.
