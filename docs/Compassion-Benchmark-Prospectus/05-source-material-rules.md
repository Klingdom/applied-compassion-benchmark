# Source Material Rules

## Source Materials to Ingest

Study all project files available in the working folder, including:

- Organization plan
- Business plan
- operating plan
- website copy
- benchmark methodology
- assessment battery
- self-assessment questionnaire
- platform build report
- grant model
- foundational quotes and research notes
- prior prospectus drafts
- any notes from the founder

## Brand Rules

Use "Compassion Benchmark" in all public-facing prose.

Do not use "ACB" in narrative sections.

Do not use "Applied Compassion Benchmark" unless referring to legacy file titles or document codes in appendices.

If source material uses older branding, translate it into Compassion Benchmark unless doing so would misrepresent a formal document title.

## Use of Source Material

Source documents are raw material, not prose to paste.

Extract:

- facts
- decisions
- concepts
- frameworks
- evidence hierarchy
- governance logic
- funding rationale
- technical capabilities
- methodology details

Then rewrite in a coherent public-facing voice.

## Claims Discipline

Separate every claim into one of four categories:

1. Established in source materials
2. Plausible but needs verification
3. Aspirational future plan
4. Unsupported and should not be used

Do not present future plans as completed accomplishments.

Do not present aspirations as proof.

Do not use market claims without flagging citation needs.

## Nonprofit Conversion Material

If nonprofit conversion is part of the source materials, present it carefully.

Do not give legal advice.

Do not make definitive tax claims.

Use language such as:

- "The organization is evaluating..."
- "One recommended path is..."
- "Legal review should confirm..."
- "A fiscal sponsorship bridge may allow..."

The funder-facing point is that governance, independence, and public benefit are being designed intentionally.

## Earned Revenue Material

Frame earned revenue as sustainability support, not mission.

Acceptable framing:

"Earned revenue can help sustain the public-interest infrastructure, but it cannot substitute for philanthropic capital during the early build-out because open methodology, public indexes, independent validation, and governance are public goods."

Avoid framing Compassion Benchmark as a consulting company.

## Assessment and Certification Material

Be precise.

The standard includes:

- 8 dimensions
- 40 subdimensions
- evidence hierarchy
- human assessment battery
- self-assessment questionnaire
- 0 to 100 composite score
- maturity gates
- pressure test principle
- public directory
- assessment platform

Explain these in plain language before technical language.

## Platform Material

The platform exists to support:

- evidence management
- score calculation
- assessment workflow
- public directory
- data access
- alerts
- longitudinal tracking
- research publication

Do not let technology become the hero.

The hero is accountable institutional behavior.

## AI and Robotics Material

Use AI and robotics as urgency accelerators.

The point is not that AI is uniquely bad.

The point is that automated systems can affect more people, faster, with less visibility, making independent measurement more important.

## Evidence Hierarchy

Preserve the hierarchy:

1. Independent external audit
2. Verifiable outcome data
3. Community testimony
4. Policy and process documentation
5. Entity self-report

In public prose, explain why this matters:

Mission statements are not evidence. Public claims are not evidence. Self-report is useful but weak. People affected by institutions must have evidentiary weight.

## Maturity Gates

Explain maturity gates plainly.

The highest recognition should not be available after one good assessment.

Character requires consistency under pressure across time.

This is one of the strongest credibility features. Use it well.

## Pressure Test Principle

Use this principle prominently.

High performance when it is easy is not evidence of institutional character.

A meaningful standard must examine what happens when compassion is costly, inconvenient, legally risky, unpopular, or in tension with institutional incentives.

## Public Benefit Material

Always connect the work to public benefit:

- journalists gain a citable standard
- researchers gain structured data
- policymakers gain comparative evidence
- communities gain visibility
- institutions gain improvement pathways
- funders support public infrastructure
- the public gains a clearer way to distinguish evidence from reputation
