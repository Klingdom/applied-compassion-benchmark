## Spot 1: The origin

**Current shippable text (opening two paragraphs):**

> I want to tell you why this exists, and I want to be honest about what it cannot yet do.
>
> The idea did not arrive as a thesis. It arrived as a question I could not answer. We can find out, in an afternoon, whether a company passed its financial audit, whether a building met fire code, whether a model cleared a safety benchmark. We cannot find out, by any comparable public measure, whether the institution on the other side of those systems noticed that a person was in distress and did something proportional about it.

**Optional personal detail to add:** One small, exact scene, a single situation, case, or decision you personally witnessed or lived through that made the measurement gap concrete and impossible to set down. A single moment, not a thesis. Insert it as a short paragraph between the two above, so "The idea did not arrive as a thesis" becomes the reflection that follows the scene. Keep it specific: a person, a place, a decision, and what it did to someone.

## Spot 2: The hardest decision

**Current shippable text:**

> Then came the hardest decision.
>
> I made independence a property of the architecture rather than a line in a policy. The systems that score the world cannot see who pays us. The systems that handle payment cannot touch a score.

**Optional personal detail to add:** Your own account of choosing to firewall assessment from revenue at the level of system design, and the moment you committed to it. What did that choice cost or rule out (a faster revenue path, an easier product, a class of customer you turned away)? One or two sentences after "Then came the hardest decision" naming what you gave up would turn a design statement into a decision with a price.

## Spot 3: The doubt

**Current shippable text:**

> There was a doubt to get past first, the oldest objection to this whole idea: that compassion is too soft, too subjective, too interior to measure, and that any attempt to score it would be performance dressed as rigor.
>
> What resolved it for me was specificity. When UnitedHealth Group scores 10.2 out of 100 and lands in our Critical band, that number is not a mood or a verdict handed down.

**Optional personal detail to add:** The moment you personally doubted this could be done, or feared the whole effort risked being performative, and the specific thing that resolved it for you. The current text names the doubt in general terms; a sentence on when you felt it and what turned it, ideally the first time a reconstructable score changed your own mind, would make the resolution yours rather than the argument's.

## Spot 4: The commitment to a first funder

**Current shippable text:**

> What I am asking for is the capital to turn a working instrument into a durable institution: an independent board, legal formation, the first genuinely citable research, the integrity audit finished and running on its own. To a first funder, I commit to the discipline I ask of every institution I score: quarterly reports that state plainly what moved and what did not, the independence indicators published intact at every interval, and no projection dressed up as a result.

**Optional personal detail to add:** A single personal promise in your own words about what you will do for a first funder over the first year, beyond the reporting discipline already stated. What will you personally guarantee: a standing line of contact, a specific milestone you will hit or forfeit, the first citable artifact by a named point? One concrete, personal commitment reads stronger than a general one.
