## Executive Summary

The path to $10k MRR runs through two buyer segments — ESG/impact investors and investigative newsrooms — contacted directly via personal outreach before inbound traffic exists. Both segments buy information they cannot get elsewhere, today, because of things that just happened (Humana downgrade, UHG DOJ expansion, OpenAI 42-state AG subpoena, Figure AI whistleblower downgrade, University Index launch). The daily research engine is not just the product — it is the outreach engine. Every score that moves is a warm call to make.

This plan is direct-outbound only. No waiting on SEO. No ad spend. The goal is 3–5 closed deals in 30 days producing $5–8k of initial revenue and a pipeline that sustains $10k MRR by day 60.

## 1. Lead Buyer Segments

### Primary: ESG and Impact Investors

**Who:** Portfolio analysts and ESG/sustainability officers at venture capital firms, private equity impact funds, ESG-integrated asset managers, and family offices tracking healthcare, tech, and education sector exposure. 

**Specific titles:** ESG Analyst, Director of Responsible Investment, Portfolio Risk Analyst, Impact Investment Associate, Head of ESG Integration.

**Firm types to prioritize:** Healthcare-sector PE/VC (direct UHG/Humana/insurer analog), AI-focused VC (direct OpenAI/Figure AI/xAI exposure), impact-first asset managers with education portfolio (direct University Index use case), family offices managing endowment-adjacent capital.

**Why they buy:** They carry fiduciary duty to flag material non-financial risk. The Humana −5.4 downgrade (Functional → Developing on an OIG finding) and UHG near-floor Criminal designation are exactly the signals their compliance teams need documented before a quarter closes. No other public source produces a scored, evidence-linked, daily-updating view of institutional conduct-toward-people across 447 Fortune 500 companies and 50 AI labs simultaneously.

**Urgency driver:** AI governance risk is the fastest-growing ESG sub-category in 2026. The OpenAI 42-state AG subpoena and Figure AI whistleblower downgrade are live undocumented risks in investor portfolios today. EU AI Act enforcement begins August 2026; AI Labs Index has 50 labs scored right now.

**Conflict-free confirmation:** These buyers are data users, not scored entities. No independence question exists.

**Offer fit:** Data license + API feed ($1–2k/month) for portfolio-wide monitoring. Score-Watch Alert ($79/year/entity) as the entry-level self-serve. Custom deep-dive report ($2.5–5k) for a specific holding. Advisory retainer ($2–5k/month) for ongoing quarterly briefings.

**Path to $10k MRR:** 3 data licenses at $1.5k average = $4.5k. 2 advisory retainers at $2.5k average = $5k. Total: $9.5k recurring. This is a realistic 60-day target from a standing start, with the first two closes possible by day 30.

**Why secondary, not primary:** Newsrooms close slower, budgets are smaller, and the data license sale requires a procurement cycle. However, a single STAT News or Bloomberg citation produces inbound investor leads and is worth taking at break-even in month one for the compounding effect. Target 1 newsroom deal in 30 days; treat it as a reference account.

## 3. List Building

**Method: named, public-source, no scraping.**

### For ESG/Investors

1. **PRI (Principles for Responsible Investment) signatories list** — public at unpri.org. Filter for US-based asset managers in the healthcare, technology, and education sector. Each signatory firm has a named primary contact published. 100+ named firms in 30 minutes.

2. **US SIF member directory** — public at ussif.org/members. Sorted by firm type. ESG analyst names often linked to firm profiles. 50 named contacts in 15 minutes.

3. **LinkedIn search: "ESG Analyst" + healthcare OR "impact investing" + AI.** Use first-degree connections first. Filter to venture capital, private equity, asset management industries. Build a spreadsheet of 50 named people with public contact info (or reachable via LinkedIn InMail).

4. **Healthcare PE firms with public portfolios:** Identify 20 firms via Bain Healthcare PE report (public, annual). Find portfolio companies that map to entities already in the Fortune 500 index. The fund analyst who covers that company is the contact.

5. **AI-sector VC:** Crunchbase top 20 AI-sector investors (public). Find named ESG/impact partners at each firm.

**Target: 75 named investor contacts in 3 days. Prioritize anyone at a firm with documented healthcare or AI portfolio exposure.**

### For Investigative Newsrooms

1. **Direct masthead reading** — STAT News, The Markup, ProPublica, KFF Health News all publish staff lists with beats on their websites. Read each masthead and identify 2–3 reporters covering health insurer accountability, AI regulation, or university policy. This is 3 hours of reading, producing 20–30 named reporters with public email formats.

2. **Byline search on existing hook stories** — Search "UnitedHealth Medicare investigation reporter" or "OpenAI attorney general" on Google News. The reporters who wrote these stories are the contacts. Byline → publication → Twitter/LinkedIn → email format.

3. **IRE (Investigative Reporters and Editors) member directory** — partial public access. Health and tech beats are heavily represented.

4. **Chronicle of Higher Education and Inside Higher Ed editorial staff** — masthead published on both sites. Reach data journalists and senior editors directly.

**Target: 30 named journalist contacts. Quality over quantity here — one right reporter is worth 20 wrong ones.**

## 5. Close Motion

### Call Structure (20 minutes)

**Minutes 1–5 — Confirm the problem.** Ask: "What's your current process for tracking this kind of conduct risk on portfolio companies?" Listen. Do not pitch.

**Minutes 5–12 — Show the relevant data.** Pull up the entity detail page(s) relevant to their portfolio. Show the score, the dimension breakdown, the evidence trail, the change history. Let the data speak.

**Minutes 12–17 — Offer fit.** Say: "There are three ways firms use this. Some want the full Fortune 500 data feed under a license for their own analysis tools. Some want a custom report on a specific sector or portfolio slice. Some want the daily alert so they're notified the moment a watched entity moves. Which of those maps closest to what you're describing?"

**Minutes 17–20 — Scope and next step.** If license: "I can send you a one-page scope and pricing in 24 hours — we'd want to know roughly how many entities you're tracking and whether you need the API or flat file." If report: "What's the deadline? I can have something in front of you in [X] days." If advisory: "What would a monthly briefing need to cover to be useful for your team?"

### Price Anchoring

Lead with the institutional data license framing ($1–2k/month) before the report ($2.5–5k one-time), even if you suspect they will buy the report. The license framing sets the expectation of ongoing value and monthly investment. If they flinch at $1.5k/month, the $3k one-time report feels like a relief, not a concession.

If asked for a "quick version" or "trial," offer Score-Watch at $79/entity as the genuine self-serve entry point. Frame it as: "The individual alert subscription is self-serve and starts at $79 per entity per year. Most institutional buyers move to the data license once they've seen a couple of cycles. But if you want to start tracking one or two specific entities today, that's the path."

### Handling the Objectivity Question

You will be asked: "How do I know this isn't biased? Are you selling to the companies you score?"

**The answer:**

"We have a hard independence rule. Entities never pay for inclusion, score changes, or score suppression. Our only commercial products are sold to people who use the data — investors, journalists, researchers — not to the entities being scored. The research pipeline is structurally isolated from the commercial system at the code level. If a company wanted to pay us to move their score, we would decline, and our architecture makes it impossible to do so quietly — the scoring source code and the change-proposal audit trail are both version-controlled. The independence is the point; without it the data is worthless to you."

Do not be defensive. State it matter-of-factly. Then ask: "Does that answer the question, or is there a specific part of the methodology you'd want to walk through?"

## 6. On-Site Fixes That Unblock Revenue

These are not blocking launch but they are blocking close. Fix them in Week 1.

### Fix 1: Offer and Pricing Page

There is currently no page that states what the institutional products cost and how to buy them. A prospect who receives your outbound email and visits the site cannot find a price, a scope, or a booking link.

**Action:** Create `/pricing` or enhance `/purchase-research` with three tiers stated explicitly:
- Data License / API Feed: "$1,000–$2,000/month — contact for scope"
- Custom Deep-Dive Report: "$2,500–$5,000 one-time — contact for scope"
- Advisory Retainer: "$2,000–$5,000/month — limited availability"

Add a Calendly (or equivalent) booking link labeled "Book a 20-minute data walkthrough." This link should live on the pricing page and in the nav.

**Why this matters:** A prospect who looks you up after getting your email and cannot find a price or a booking link will not reply. The absence of pricing signals either "we're not ready" or "we will waste your time." Both kill reply rate.

### Fix 2: Install Plausible Analytics

Zero analytics are installed. You cannot measure whether outbound is generating site visits, which pages they land on, or whether people who receive the templates are clicking through.

**Action:** Install Plausible (privacy-first, no cookie banner required, compatible with static Next.js export). Add 3 custom events in Week 1:
- `pricing_page_view` — did they get there?
- `booking_link_click` — did they try to book?
- `gumroad_outbound_click` — did they go to checkout?

This costs $9/month and gives you the data to debug outreach conversion within 5 days.

### Fix 3: Booking Link in Every Outbound Email

The templates above include no booking link. Add a P.S. to every email: "If easier: [Calendly link] — I have 15-minute slots available this week." This removes one reply-step from the prospect's path.

### Fix 4: Score-Watch Gumroad Flip (PRD P0-A)

Per the PRD, Score-Watch still routes to contact-sales. This blocks self-serve conversion for anyone who receives an outbound email and wants to start immediately with the low-ticket option. Flip `SCORE_WATCH.useGumroad = true` as part of Week 1 — this is a one-line config change plus Gumroad product creation.

## 8. Metrics

### Leading Indicators (track daily)

| Metric | Target Range | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|
| Outreach emails sent (cumulative) | 40 by day 7, 105 by day 30 | Volume is the base. If you are under pace, everything else is moot. |
| Reply rate (replies / emails sent) | 5–12% | Below 5%: hook is wrong, list is wrong, or both. Above 12%: you have product-market fit on messaging — scale the list. |
| Calls booked | 2–4 per week by Week 2 | If reply rate is acceptable but calls are not booking, the call-to-action in your reply handling is the problem. |
| Proposals sent | 1–2 per week by Week 3 | If calls happen but proposals don't follow, either the scope was unclear on the call or you are not asking for the next step. |
| Proposals to close rate | Target 25–40% | Below 25%: proposal is too complex, price is too high, or urgency is absent. |

### Lagging Indicators (track weekly)

| Metric | Week 4 Target |
|---|---|
| Closed revenue (one-time) | $3,000–$8,000 |
| New MRR (recurring contracts) | $1,500–$4,000 |
| Gumroad self-serve transactions (from outbound spill) | 5–10 |
| Score-Watch subscriptions (from outbound email links) | 3–8 |

### Decision Rules

**If reply rate < 3% by day 10:** The hook is not working. Switch from company-name hooks (Humana, UHG) to sector hooks ("managed-care sector map") or the prestige-compassion narrative. Do not change the list — change the message first.

**If calls book but no proposals follow:** Add a specific ask to the call close: "Can I send you a one-page scope this week?" If the answer is consistently "not yet," the buying motion requires a second stakeholder. Ask who else needs to see this and address them directly.

**If proposals go dark after send:** The price is too high or the urgency is absent. Test a smaller entry point: "Would a single-entity Score-Watch for three of your holdings make sense as a starting point?" The goal is to get a dollar in, then expand.

**If nothing closes by day 21:** Do not pivot to inbound or paid acquisition. Diagnose the calls. Ask one non-converting prospect directly: "Can you tell me what got in the way?" Their answer is worth 20 email tests.
