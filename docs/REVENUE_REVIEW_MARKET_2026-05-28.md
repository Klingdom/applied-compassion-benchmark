## 2. Per-SKU Pricing Verdict

### SKU 1 — Watcher ($79/yr per entity)

**Verdict: UNDERPRICED for professional buyers. Correctly priced for individual/prosumer buyers.**

Evidence:
- Owler Pro ($99/yr) is the only per-entity alert subscription at this price tier — and Owler delivers raw news with no scoring layer. Score-Watch delivers a normalized score delta with methodology-backed evidence citations. That differentiation supports a meaningful premium over Owler.
- No institutional ESG monitoring product sells a per-entity alert below $1,000/yr. Score-Watch at $79 is accessible to individuals who cannot reach a $15K/yr platform.
- Factiva individual news alerts: $700–$2,500/yr. Score-Watch delivers a more structured signal (scored, not just news-flagged) at $79 — positioned below its functional value for professionals.
- A compliance officer at a fund paying $15,000–$30,000/yr for Refinitiv WorldCheck would not blink at $500–$1,000/yr for a scored, evidence-backed entity signal.
- Recommended action: Retain $79/yr as individual/retail entry. The "professional upgrade" to a scored signal at this per-entity rate is inherently the Observer and Analyst bundle tiers — the tiering handles the professional/individual split without splitting the Watcher SKU.

**Pricing experiment already specified (PRICING_STRATEGY §4 Experiment 1):** Test $59/$79/$99 over 30 days. The evidence above suggests $99 may convert as well for professional buyers — run the experiment before locking in permanently.

### SKU 2 — Observer ($249/yr, 5-entity bundle)

**Verdict: CORRECTLY PRICED. Possibly underpriced for the compliance-officer persona.**

Evidence:
- Owler Teams: $199/seat/yr for unlimited entities (raw news, no scoring). Observer at $249/yr for 5 scored-entity alerts is within range of the only direct structural comp.
- Crunchbase Pro: $348/yr for unlimited company alerts (data enrichment, no scoring). Observer sits 28% below Crunchbase Pro for 5 entities — justified given CB is earlier-stage with smaller coverage.
- For a compliance officer tracking 5 suppliers who already pays $5,000–$15,000/yr for EcoVadis or Refinitiv supply-chain monitoring, $249/yr is effectively free. This persona has much higher WTP but is not the primary launch buyer.
- The $249 price point is strong because it creates a natural upsell pressure: 4 Watcher subscriptions = $316/yr vs. Observer at $249/yr. The arithmetic drives a 5-entity upgrade without a sales conversation.
- No action required at launch. After 90 days of data, test $299/yr.

### SKU 3 — Index Snapshot Individual ($99–$195 one-time)

**Verdict: SLIGHTLY UNDERPRICED for the AI Labs and Robotics SKUs. Fortune 500/Countries/Cities correctly priced.**

Evidence:
- Morningstar individual company report: $50–$200. Index Snapshot covers 50–447 entities in one purchase — per-entity equivalent is $0.33–$3.90 per entity at current pricing. This is dramatically below any comparable per-entity research cost.
- Statista single report: $149–$999. A Fortune 500 Snapshot at $149 sits at Statista's floor, appropriate for a newer benchmark.
- Gartner Magic Quadrant individual: $1,495–$2,495 for ~15–20 vendors. AI Labs Snapshot at $195 for 50 labs is priced far below the Gartner analogy — which is the closest structural comp (niche vendor landscape, professional research buyer, governance framing). The AI Labs and Robotics SKUs could support $249–$295 without evidence of price resistance.
- IBISWorld sector report: $1,000–$1,500 for a general industry. Not a direct comp (CB is more specific and data-dense), but sets a $1,000+ ceiling for the commercial/team license tiers.
- Recommended action: Price AI Labs and Robotics Snapshots at $249 (test $195 vs. $249 per PRICING_STRATEGY Experiment 5). Retain $149 for Fortune 500/Countries/Cities. Retain $99 for US States given partial coverage (21 of 51).

### SKU 4 — Briefing Archive ($99/yr)

**Verdict: UNDERPRICED relative to comparables. Defensible as a low-friction acquisition product.**

Evidence:
- Stratechery: $150/yr for one writer's analysis newsletter archive. Briefing Archive at $99/yr for daily intelligence across 1,155 entities is cheaper than a single-author newsletter.
- Axios Pro: $599/yr per vertical. Briefing Archive covers 7 "verticals" (indexes) in one subscription at $99 — 83% below Axios Pro per vertical.
- Morningstar Premium (individual): $250–$750/yr. The Archive gives daily scored-movement data that Morningstar does not provide at any individual price point.
- The $99 price is a deliberate acquisition tool: it is low-friction enough for a daily reader to convert on impulse (the "I might need to look back at that" moment). Raising it to $149–$199 is viable once 50+ subscribers prove the product has value retention.
- Recommended action: Launch at $99/yr as specified. After 50 archive subscribers, test $149. The Briefing Archive's strategic value is as a subscriber acquisition and daily engagement tool, not as a primary revenue line — low price supports that goal.

## 3. Most Underserved High-WTP Segment

**The ESG/governance analyst at a small-to-mid asset manager or ESG consultancy.**

Characteristics:
- Pays $10,000–$80,000/yr for MSCI ESG, Sustainalytics, or RepRisk platforms.
- Has a real multi-entity monitoring workflow covering 20–100 entities per analyst.
- Needs a signal that goes beyond what MSCI/Sustainalytics covers — specifically: governments, AI labs, and robotics labs are not in any ESG platform at any price.
- Has institutional procurement budget but no self-serve path into Compassion Benchmark today. The Watcher/Observer self-serve tiers are too small for their workflow; there is no Analyst or Institutional tier live yet.
- The gap: MSCI ESG starts at $25,000/yr and does not cover governments or AI labs. Compassion Benchmark covers both. There is no institutional data product in the $3,000–$10,000/yr range that covers these entity classes with scored, evidence-backed methodology. This segment cannot buy what CB uniquely offers at any price today.

Why they are underserved:
- No Analyst tier ($999/yr, 25 entities + briefing archive) exists yet — this tier fits their workflow at a negligible cost compared to their current stack.
- No institutional sales path exists yet — no demo, no contract, no API.
- Entity-page CTAs and Score-Watch marketing are written for individual buyers, not professional teams.
- LinkedIn distribution has likely already brought professional buyers to entity pages; the current product offers them nothing beyond a $79 per-entity subscription that does not scale to their workflow.

**Second-ranked underserved segment: AI safety and AI governance researchers** (academic, NGO, policy institute). No ESG platform covers the AI Labs index. The AI Labs data is unique and non-substitutable. WTP evidence: Gartner Magic Quadrant for a narrow tech category sells at $1,495/report, suggesting $249–$500+ WTP from this segment for the AI Labs Snapshot and a dedicated AI-labs alert product. Regulatory tailwind: EU AI Act enforcement and U.S. AI governance policy activity are structurally increasing demand for AI-labs comparative intelligence.

### Candidate 3 — Briefing Archive paywall as a free-to-paid conversion funnel anchor

**Type:** Improvement

**Problem with market evidence:**
The Briefing Archive ($99/yr) is correctly specified in the PRD and PRICING_STRATEGY but its launch is framed as secondary to Score-Watch. The market evidence suggests this is the reverse of the optimal sequencing: the Briefing Archive is the highest-volume, lowest-friction acquisition product in the lineup, and its value as a subscriber pipeline anchor for Score-Watch upgrades is understated in the current documents.

Evidence: Stratechery ($150/yr) built its subscriber base almost entirely via free content distribution, then converted habitual readers when they hit the paywall. The Briefing Archive product is structurally identical to this model: the `/updates` daily briefing already has repeat-reader behavior (it exists as daily content per recent git commits), the gate is technically simple (client-side only, per PRD §2 SKU 4), and the $99/yr price is low enough that the decision is a "of course" for any regular reader.

The current PRD targets 10 Briefing Archive subscribers in 90 days. This is likely conservative: if the `/updates` page has even 30–50 unique daily visitors who return more than once, conversion to $99/yr among readers who hit a gated post will measurably exceed 10. However, that conversion only happens if the free window is calibrated correctly and the paywall prompt is visible and frictionless.

The evidence gap from PRICING_STRATEGY Experiment 4 (7-day vs. 14-day free window) is worth running, but the more important improvement is ensuring the Briefing Archive launches on Day 1 alongside Watcher — not as a "second product after Score-Watch validates."

**Proposed change:**
(a) Launch Briefing Archive $99/yr simultaneously with Watcher, not after. (b) Ensure every daily briefing page has an inline opt-in (free list) + archive CTA visible below the fold, regardless of paywall status. (c) Add a "you've read this free for X days" counter as a soft nudge (browser-side cookie, no backend required) — this is the mechanism Stratechery and The Athletic have used to convert habitual readers before they hit a hard wall. (d) Run the 7-day vs. 14-day free window experiment starting week 2.

**Expected revenue benefit:**
If the Briefing Archive converts 50 subscribers in 90 days at $99/yr (vs. the PRD's 10-sub target), that is $4,950 ARR vs. $990 — a $3,960 difference. The free opt-in list generated by the Briefing Archive's lower-friction path is the primary feeder for Score-Watch conversions: subscribers who already have their email on file and have experienced the product value have materially higher conversion rates to paid tiers (Stratechery model: newsletter → paywall hit → subscribe → upgrade).

**Independence-policy check:** PASS. Paywall gates archive access, not score data. Scores remain public.

**Type:** New SKU (Phase 2 pull-forward)

B Lab, Just Capital, Freedom House, and Good Jobs First all give their data away for free to researchers. The strategic value is not the $249 — it is the academic citations that flow from researcher access, which are the primary demand signal for institutional buyers considering a $10,000–$50,000/yr data license.

Market evidence for citation-to-enterprise pipeline: MSCI, Sustainalytics, and RepRisk all trace institutional pipeline growth to academic and NGO coverage. Sustainalytics explicitly provides free data access to university researchers as a pipeline strategy (Morningstar academic access program). The citation trail is how institutional buyers discover and gain internal credibility for a new data vendor.

The Researcher License as currently scoped (PRD Phase 2, trigger: first academic citation OR 15 Index Snapshot sales) has the causality backwards: citations are the output of researcher access, not the trigger for offering it. The license should be available from Phase 2 launch, not gated on an outcome it needs to generate.

**Proposed change:**
Create the Researcher Data License ($249/yr individual, $799/yr institutional) as a Phase 2 Day 1 product. No additional engineering beyond the Index Snapshot JSON/CSV export already required for other SKUs. Add a "For researchers and academics" section to the /pricing page with explicit non-commercial terms and attribution requirements. If 5 institutional leads convert at Institutional Entry ($3,500/yr minimum), the researcher pipeline is worth $17,500/yr in institutional ACV — 3.5x the direct license revenue. ### Candidate 5 — Sector Deep-Dive Report ($295): AI Labs 2026 as the first product, funded by pre-orders

**Type:** Experiment

**Problem with market evidence:**
The Sector Deep-Dive Report is scoped as Phase 2, triggered by AI Labs Score-Watch validation. The market evidence supports a different sequencing: the report itself is the demand validation mechanism, not the output of it. Gartner Magic Quadrant individual reports at $1,495–$2,495 demonstrate that governance-framed, niche-vendor analysis has documented WTP from professional buyers at price points far above the $295 current target. Morningstar sector reports at $300–$800 are the floor comp. The AI Labs 2026 report at $295 is priced 80% below the Gartner equivalent.

A pre-order mechanism (standard in newsletter and research publishing) would: (a) validate demand before writing effort, (b) provide capital to fund the editorial work, and (c) create a launch list of buyers who are signal-quality leads for Score-Watch and Observer conversions. Substack and Gumroad both support pre-orders natively. The editorial effort (15–25 pages) is the primary cost; if pre-orders do not reach a threshold (e.g., 10 pre-orders at $295 within 30 days), the writing effort is not justified and the signal is clear.

**Proposed change:**
Launch an AI Labs 2026 Report pre-order on Gumroad at $295 (individual). Set a minimum threshold of 10 pre-orders before writing begins; communicate this transparently to pre-order buyers ("We will write this report once we reach 10 pre-orders. You will be charged only when the report is delivered"). If threshold reached: write and deliver. If not: full refund, zero writing effort lost, and the failed pre-order is itself a demand signal (this specific framing does not convert at this price). Announce the pre-order via the existing briefing/newsletter audience and one targeted LinkedIn post to AI governance researchers.

**Expected revenue benefit:**
10 pre-orders at $295 = $2,950 minimum. 25 pre-orders = $7,375. More importantly, a successful AI Labs 2026 pre-order validates $295+ WTP for Sector Deep-Dives across all 7 indexes — the replication value is $295 × 7 indexes = $2,065 per annual report cycle at minimum. Failed pre-order costs zero writing effort.

**Independence-policy check:** PASS. Report editorial findings are driven by existing methodology pipeline, not by pre-order buyer identity or commercial commission. Pre-order buyers do not influence what the report concludes.
