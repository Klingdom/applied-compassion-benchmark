## 3. The Post

---

Two of the highest-profile AI labs in the country are backing competing liability bills in the same Illinois legislative session. The committee votes on both April 24.

SB 3261, backed by Anthropic, would require frontier AI companies to maintain mandatory safety plans and assigns liability for child harm from AI systems.

SB 3444, backed by OpenAI, would exempt AI firms from liability for mass-casualty events — including CBRN scenarios — where deaths exceed 100 or damages exceed $1 billion. Legal experts have characterized the liability threshold as "markedly weak."

These are not position papers or values statements. They are active lobbying choices in a live legislative session, on opposite sides.

The Compassion Benchmark scores both entities. The divergence shows up most clearly in the ACC (Accountability) and SYS (Systemic Thinking) dimensions, where the assessment evidence directly reflects each organization's posture toward institutional liability and structural harm prevention. Anthropic sits at 62.2, Established band — the highest-scoring frontier AI lab in the index. OpenAI sits at 31.3, Developing band. The 31-point gap between them is not primarily about product capability. It is about governance philosophy made visible in public choices.

If you are an investor, policy analyst, compliance officer, or board advisor with AI exposure, the April 24 vote narrows the range of ambiguity about where each lab stands on regulatory accountability.
