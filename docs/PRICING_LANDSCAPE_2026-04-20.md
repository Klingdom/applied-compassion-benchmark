## 3. Score-Watch Positioning Check — $79/yr/entity

**Where it lands:** $79/yr/entity sits at the Owler/prosumer floor — below every institutional ESG monitoring product by 2–3 orders of magnitude, and in line with basic news-alert subscriptions (Owler Pro: $35–$99/yr).

**The gap this fills:** No institutional ESG product sells per-entity monitoring at below $1K/entity/yr. Score-Watch at $79 is accessible to individuals (journalists, policy analysts, retail investors, NGO researchers) who cannot justify or access a $15K/yr enterprise platform. It is priced as a low-friction first-purchase, not as an institutional tool.

**Is it under-priced?**
- For retail/individual buyers: reasonable. Owler is the benchmark; $79 is defensible.
- For professional buyers: significantly under-priced. A compliance officer who pays $15K/yr for WorldCheck would not blink at $500–$1,000/yr per entity for a scored, evidence-backed signal.
- Recommended: retain $79/yr as the individual/retail SKU. Create a professional tier.

**Best analog for prospect comparison:** Owler Pro ($99/yr) — same mechanism (email alert when something changes), but Owler is raw news without a scored rating. Score-Watch delivers a normalized score delta with evidence. That differentiation supports a 3–5x price premium over Owler for professional buyers.

**Per-portfolio SKU estimate:**
- If an investor watches 10–20 entities, $790–$1,580/yr at current per-entity pricing.
- A portfolio tier bundling unlimited entities for one index at $500–$1,500/yr would be attractive to retail/prosumer. Enterprise portfolio (multi-index) likely supportable at $3K–$8K/yr based on comp set.

**Per-team-seat SKU estimate:**
- A 3-seat compliance team paying $249–$499/seat/yr, covering 10–20 entities per seat, would be reasonable based on comp from Owler Teams (~$199/seat/yr) and Klue (~$1,200/seat/yr for richer intel). The right range for Score-Watch team seats given the depth of signal: $300–$600/seat/yr.

## 6. Market Demand Signals

**M&A / acquisition signals (WTP rising):**
- Morningstar acquired Sustainalytics for ~$170M in 2020 — signaled that ESG ratings are a durable revenue category, not a niche. [Morningstar press release, April 2020]
- FactSet acquired Truvalue Labs (AI-driven ESG signal) in 2021 — real-time ESG intelligence validated as a product category worth acqui-hiring. [FactSet press release, November 2021]
- MSCI acquired RCA (Real Capital Analytics) and multiple ESG data firms 2019–2022 — ESG data consolidation ongoing.
- S&P Global acquired IHS Markit for $44B in 2022; ESG data integration was explicitly cited. [S&P press release]

**Revenue growth signals:**
- MSCI ESG & Real Assets segment grew ~14% YoY in 2023 (reported in MSCI 2023 Annual Report — public). ESG was cited as the fastest-growing sub-segment.
- Sustainalytics revenue estimated at $150M+ annually by 2024 (embedded in Morningstar reporting — M).
- RepRisk reported 30%+ subscription revenue growth 2021–2023 (stated in press interviews — M).

**Regulatory tailwind (WTP structural driver):**
- EU CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) — effective 2024–2025 — mandates third-party ESG data for ~50,000 companies. Creates procurement pressure across compliance teams in Europe and multinationals. This is the single largest structural demand driver. [EU Official Journal, December 2022]
- SEC climate disclosure rules (U.S.) — proposed 2022, partially finalized 2024 — increased demand for third-party ESG verification among U.S. fund managers.
- U.S. DOL ESG investing rules (ERISA) — 2022 rule allows ESG consideration in pension fund investing, increasing institutional demand for ESG data.

**Headwinds:**
- ESG "backlash" in U.S. political discourse (2024–2025) has caused some asset managers to rebrand ESG products. This is a labeling risk, not a demand-destruction signal — underlying compliance and reputational monitoring demand is unchanged or growing.
- Greenwashing scrutiny (EU, SEC) increases the bar for what constitutes credible ESG data — creates opportunity for independent, methodology-transparent benchmarks.

**Compassion-specific signal:** No direct comparator exists in the market. The closest framing is "ethical behavior / stakeholder accountability benchmarking" — which is a white space between ESG ratings (environment/governance heavy) and reputational monitoring (news-based, unscored). The compassion framing differentiates from both, though it introduces a buyer-education requirement that pure ESG products do not face.
