## 1. Journey Maps

### Persona A — Analyst landing from a LinkedIn post

The analyst arrives mid-funnel, typically linked to a specific index or entity page. If they land on an index page (e.g., `/fortune-500`), they encounter a well-formed IndexHero with band distribution and a purchase CTA, followed immediately by the ranking table. Navigation friction begins here: dimension column headers (`AWR`, `EMP`, `ACT`, etc.) carry no tooltips or labels, so the analyst sees 8 unlabelled abbreviations and has no way to decode them without leaving for `/methodology`. There is no inline column legend. Clicking a company name does link to that company's entity detail page (entityKind is set), but the entity page has no "previous/next entity by rank" navigation, no "similar-band peers" list, and no sector-scoped comparison view. The analyst who wants to benchmark a company against its sector peers must manually return to the index and search. After reading the entity page they reach a Score-Watch CTA and a newsletter signup — both are appropriate — but there is no link back to the specific date's `/updates` briefing except through the `latestChange` panel (which appears only when there is a recent change). Net friction: dimension abbreviation opacity, no peer navigation, no score history chart.

### Persona B — Journalist looking up one entity

The journalist arrives directly at an entity slug (e.g., `/company/microsoft`). The page is clean: breadcrumb to Indexes → Fortune 500 Index → Microsoft, composite score box, band chip, 8 dimension bars. The dimension bars show a description one line long and a numeric score, which is good. The problem is granularity: the journalist sees "Accountability: 2.4 of 5.0" but the page does not show subdimension breakdown (5 subdimensions per dimension, scored 0–5). The full 40-subdimension standard is defined in `dimensions.ts` but EntityDetail only renders the 8 dimension bars with no drill-down. The journalist cannot cite a specific finding ("they scored 1.0 on Harm Acknowledgment") because that granularity is invisible. The evidence citation trail on the entity page itself is also absent — evidence lives only in the `/updates` feed as point-in-time cards. Additionally, if the journalist arrived via Google, the breadcrumb is functional but there are no "share" or "cite this page" affordances to support quick editorial use. Score change history appears only as the most recent change (a single `latestChange` panel) — there is no visible list of historical changes. Net friction: subdimension opacity, no evidence trail on entity pages, no score history, no cite/share mechanism.

### Persona C — Investor exploring an index

The investor lands on `/indexes` or a specific index page. They can sort, search, and filter the RankingTable. The score column renders a single composite number; the 8 dimension columns render raw 0–5 sub-scores with no band colouring. The investor comparing two companies must hold both rows in memory — there is no comparison mode and no facility to export or copy a row's data. The "go deeper" service cards (Purchase Research, Data Licenses, Advisory, Enterprise) appear below the fold after key findings, with appropriate CTAs. However, the `/purchase-research` and `/data-licenses` pages are not linked directly from the index hero or the table itself, only from the below-fold service section. The purchase CTA inserted every 50 rows in the table is appropriate in principle but the copy ("Get the Complete Benchmark Report") is generic; it does not name the specific index the investor is currently viewing. The Score-Watch product is mentioned only in the footer nav from index pages — there is no Score-Watch mention on any index page itself, which is the highest-intent context for that product. See also `MONETIZATION_REVIEW_2026-04-20.md` for revenue implications. Net friction: no comparison mode, generic CTA copy, Score-Watch absent from index pages, no inline legend for dimension codes.

### Persona D — Prospect considering Score-Watch

The prospect is typically an investor, executive, or trust-and-safety professional. Their journey is: find an entity they care about → see the entity page → decide whether the Score-Watch Alert is worth $79/year. The entity page does show the Score-Watch CTA prominently (a large panel above the fold on the lower section), with the entity's name interpolated into the headline ("Be first to know when Microsoft's score changes"). This is strong. However, the journey has two gaps. First: the `/score-watch` product page, which the prospect may land on directly from the footer nav, routes subscription to `/contact-sales` (not to a Gumroad checkout), while the entity-page CTA conditionally routes to Gumroad if `SCORE_WATCH.useGumroad` is true — creating an inconsistent purchase path depending on entry point. Second: the prospect who wants to evaluate the product before committing has nowhere to see a sample alert. The "How it works" panel on `/score-watch` describes the alert content but does not show a visual example. No sample email or sample alert card exists. Net friction: inconsistent purchase routing, no sample alert preview, no trust signal at the score-watch purchase moment that score-buying is impossible (the independence note is one card among six — it does not lead the page).

## 2. Navigation Audit

**Affected entities in `/updates` sectorAlerts are not clickable.** In `DailyBriefing.tsx` (line 339–348), `affected_entities` slugs render as static `<span>` chips styled in accent blue. They look interactive but are not links. A user reading a sector alert about 5 affected companies cannot click any of them to reach an entity page. This is the same class of problem as the score-movement-to-entity-card fix being implemented in parallel, and is arguably more egregious because the chips are visually styled like interactive badges.

**Assessed entity cards on the updates page link to the index, not the entity.** In `DailyBriefing.tsx` (line 581–583), `recentAssessments` cards link to `/${a.publishedIndex}` if the index slug contains no spaces — meaning clicking "Microsoft" in the "Assessed entities" section takes the user to `/fortune-500`, not to `/company/microsoft`. The card shows the entity name, composite score, band, and delta, strongly implying it is a link to that entity's page.

**Dimension labels in the RankingTable are opaque abbreviations.** Column headers `AWR`, `EMP`, `ACT`, `EQU`, `BND`, `ACC`, `SYS`, `INT` appear without tooltips or a legend row. A user unfamiliar with the framework cannot decode them without navigating to `/methodology`. The dimensions page has no anchor links per dimension, so there is no way to deep-link from a column header to its definition.

**Entity pages have no "previous/next in rank" navigation.** The footer nav at the bottom of `EntityDetail.tsx` has only two links: "Back to [index]" and "Read the methodology". A user who wants to compare rank #47 with rank #48 must return to the index, scroll to find rank #48, and click through again.

**Entity pages have no "same sector" or "same band" peer links.** An investor viewing a Fortune 500 company has no way to see other companies in the same sector without returning to the full index and filtering.

**Methodology dimensions are not individually linkable.** The 8 dimension cards on `/methodology` are rendered as plain `<Card>` elements with no `id` attributes and no anchor links. A link from the RankingTable column header or from an entity's dimension bar to the methodology cannot be made specific — it can only point to `/methodology` at the top.

**Score change history is invisible on entity pages.** `EntityDetail.tsx` renders at most one `latestChange`. An entity may have 5 changes in the updates archive, but the entity page shows only the most recent. There is no "View all changes for this entity" link to a filtered view of `/updates`.

**Updates page and Methodology changelog are not cross-linked.** The methodology version log on `/methodology` has a button linking to `/updates` ("See every approved score change"). The `/updates` page does not link back to `/methodology`. A user reading the briefing and wondering about methodology assumptions has no in-page link — they must use the nav bar.

**Breadcrumbs return to the index, not to the user's prior search/filter state.** This is an inherent limitation of static exports, but it is worth flagging: a user who filtered the Fortune 500 table by sector "Healthcare" and clicked an entity will find the breadcrumb returns them to the unfiltered index.

**`/score-watch` "Browse entities" CTA links to `/indexes`.** The more helpful destination is a specific index page with the table visible, so the user can immediately find the entity they want to watch. `/indexes` is a directory page, adding one more click.

## 3. Comprehension Gaps

**Dimension abbreviations have no persistent legend.** Users who do not know that `AWR = Awareness` or `BND = Boundaries` are left to guess across the entire ranking table. The `IndexHero` band distribution panel is well-executed; the same treatment does not exist for dimension abbreviations.

**Score bands are defined in the hero but not repeated at the table level.** Band color-coding is used throughout the table (the `Band` component renders consistently), but the five bands and their score ranges appear only in the `IndexHero` panel. Once a user scrolls past the hero into the table, the legend is gone. A user who sees "Developing" on row 300 must scroll back up or open `/methodology` to understand what that means.

**The delta display on the homepage score-movement cards is ambiguous.** On `page.tsx` (line 157–163), the score transition is rendered as plain text: `{change.publishedScore} → {change.assessedScore}`. The arrow character used is `→` (a right-arrow entity), and the delta is shown separately as `+/-N` in a large colored number. This works, but the meaning of the direction — published-to-assessed — is not labeled. A first-time reader could mistake it for previous-quarter to current, or for a target vs. actual comparison.

**"Composite", "dimension", and "subdimension" are used without a first-use definition.** Every entity page and index hero assumes the user understands these terms. The methodology link is present, but it is a full reference document, not a glossary tooltip. First-time visitors from social media or search have no lightweight entry point to the vocabulary.

**Entity pages show no score change history.** A user viewing an entity wants to know: has this score been stable, rising, or falling? The page shows only the most recent change in a panel. A reader of the 40-subdimension framework in `dimensions.ts` might reasonably expect to see historical trend data for each dimension. This gap is also a data model constraint (no entity-specific history array is hydrated to the page), so resolving it requires a data layer change — noted as a dependency below.

**"Pending review" vs "Applied" status on score cards is not explained.** In `DailyBriefing.tsx` the status badge renders either "Applied" (green) or "Pending review" (yellow). What "pending review" means in practice — that a human must approve before the published score changes — is explained in a methodological callout elsewhere on the page, but there is no inline tooltip or link at the badge level. A user who sees "Pending review" on a large delta may not know whether the published score has moved.

**The integration premium is invisible to most users.** The composite score of an entity is computed using an integration premium of up to +10 points. A score of 72.4 might be significantly shaped by this premium. The entity page shows only the composite, not a decomposition that would show the dimension base vs. the integration adjustment.

## 4. Conversion-Depth Gaps

**Score-Watch is absent from all 7 index pages.** Index pages (`/fortune-500`, `/countries`, `/ai-labs`, etc.) contain below-fold service cards linking to Purchase Research, Data Licenses, Advisory, and Enterprise — but not to Score-Watch. An investor browsing the Fortune 500 table and spotting a company whose score concerns them has no Score-Watch CTA in context. They would need to click into the entity page, which adds a step and assumes they know to do so. Score-Watch is the highest-frequency, lowest-friction revenue product and should appear on every index page. See `MONETIZATION_REVIEW_2026-04-20.md` for revenue modeling of this gap.

**Newsletter CTA is absent from `/methodology` and `/purchase-research`.** Both pages attract high-intent visitors (researchers, buyers). Neither page has a newsletter signup. The methodology page ends with three navigation panels. The purchase-research page ends with a Callout + two CTA buttons. Adding a compact newsletter signup to each is low effort and directly captures users who have read enough to trust the benchmark.

**Every index page's purchase CTA is generic.** The RankingTable injects a CTA every 50 rows using a static `ctaText` prop. On the Fortune 500 page this reads "Get the Complete Benchmark Report." There is nothing in the text identifying it as the Fortune 500 report. A user who has opened multiple tabs might not know which report they are being asked to buy.

**Entity pages lack a "Buy the full index this entity belongs to" shortcut in the Score-Watch panel.** The Score-Watch panel is well-placed, but the adjacent purchase CTA panel (which does name the index and price) is the only cross-sell. There is no "View all Fortune 500 companies" quick link from within an entity page — only the breadcrumb at the top.

**The `/updates` page purchase CTA ("Get the full benchmark report") is generic and links to `/purchase-research`.** A user who just read about a score change for an AI lab is most likely interested in the AI Labs report, not the generic catalog page. The updates page does not have the context to know which index the user cares about (without entity-level purchase CTA scoping), but the sectorAlerts could at least link to the appropriate index report.

**External Gumroad links use `target="_blank"` with `rel="noopener noreferrer"` (correct).** Evidence citations in `DailyBriefing.tsx` (line 357–363) also use `rel="noopener noreferrer"`. No pull-back mechanism exists (i.e., users who follow a source citation leave the site). This is expected behavior for academic/journalistic sources — it is not necessarily a problem, but it is a conversion leak for users who were on their way to purchase.

**The `/score-watch` page CTA routes to `/contact-sales`, not Gumroad.** If `SCORE_WATCH.useGumroad` is false at the page level, the score-watch landing page directs to a contact form. This introduces sales friction for what is intended to be a self-serve $79 product. The entity-page CTA conditionally uses Gumroad if the flag is true, creating an inconsistent user experience depending on how the product routing is configured. This is also flagged in `MONETIZATION_REVIEW_2026-04-20.md`.

## 5. Ranked Backlog — Top 15 UX Improvements

Priority score = Impact + Confidence − Effort. Confidence is 1–5 (how certain the fix will move the needle).

---

### Item 1: Wire `affected_entities` chips on `/updates` to entity detail pages

**Pages affected:** `/updates`, `/updates/[date]` — `DailyBriefing.tsx` line 339–348

**Problem:** Sector alert chips render as styled but inert spans. Users cannot navigate from an alert to the entity page. This is the same navigation-depth problem as the score-movement card fix being done in parallel.

**Proposed change:** Replace the `<span>` wrapping each slug in `affected_entities` with a `<Link>` component. Build a slug-to-route resolver (a `Map<slug, route>` built from existing entity data) to produce the correct route per entity kind. Display the entity's common name (not the raw slug) if a name lookup is feasible, or at minimum convert the slug to title case.

**Impact:** 4 — Every affected_entity chip that becomes a link is a navigation path that currently does not exist.
**Effort:** 2 — Slug-to-route lookup is the same pattern used in `DailyBriefing.tsx` for `recentAssessments` links, but needs a cross-index resolver.
**Priority score:** 4 + 4 − 2 = **6**
**Dependency:** Needs a cross-index entity lookup utility. The `getEntityBySlug` function in `data/entities` handles per-kind lookup; a kind-agnostic version is needed.

### Item 2: Fix assessed-entity cards to link to entity detail, not index

**Pages affected:** `/updates`, `/updates/[date]` — `DailyBriefing.tsx` line 581–583

**Problem:** `recentAssessments` cards currently link to `/${a.publishedIndex}` — the index page — instead of the entity detail page. The card visually implies it is an entity link (shows entity name, score, band, delta).

**Proposed change:** Use the entity's `slug` and `publishedIndex` fields to construct the correct entity detail route (e.g., `/company/{slug}`, `/country/{slug}`) using the `ENTITY_ROUTE` map already defined in `RankingTable.tsx`. Wire the card `href` accordingly.

**Impact:** 4 — Every assessed entity card is a broken navigation path at the moment.
**Effort:** 1 — The route construction pattern is already implemented in `RankingTable.tsx`.
**Priority score:** 4 + 5 − 1 = **8**
**Dependency:** None — all required data fields are present in the `recentAssessments` payload.

### Item 4: Add dimension abbreviation tooltip or legend to RankingTable

**Pages affected:** All 7 index pages — `RankingTable.tsx`

**Problem:** Column headers `AWR`, `EMP`, `ACT`, `EQU`, `BND`, `ACC`, `SYS`, `INT` are unexplained. New users cannot decode the framework from the table alone.

**Impact:** 4 — Comprehension of the core data product.

### Item 15: Add a "cite this page" or "copy score" affordance to entity pages

**Pages affected:** All 5 entity detail variants — `EntityDetail.tsx`

**Problem:** Journalists and researchers need a citeable form of an entity's score. The page has no "copy citation" button, no structured data export, and no permalink-to-score format. The entity page URL is already canonical (and carries JSON-LD), but there is no user-facing affordance to copy it or to get a formatted citation string.

## 7. Independence-Policy UX Safeguards

The independence policy must appear as a trust signal on every page where money changes hands. Current state:

| Surface | Independence Signal Present? | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage | Yes — "Independence policy" panel in the body | Strong: bullet list, explicit rules |
| `/indexes` | Yes — paired "Independence policy" panel | Strong |
| `/purchase-research` | Yes — "Commercial integrity rules" panel | Strong: explicitly names what purchasing does NOT do |
| `/score-watch` | Partial — one card among six ("Independence-preserving") | Weak: buried in a feature grid, does not lead the section |
| `/fortune-500` (and other index pages) | No dedicated independence callout | Missing from the purchase CTA area |
| Entity pages (Score-Watch CTA) | No independence signal adjacent to the CTA | Missing |
| `DailyBriefing.tsx` purchase CTA | No independence signal | Missing |

**Specific issues:**

The `/score-watch` page's independence signal appears as one of six feature cards in a grid. It does not lead the purchase consideration section. A user who arrives at the Score-Watch page wondering "does paying this affect the score?" must read all six cards to find the answer. The signal should move to the hero section of the page, ideally as a short sentence directly beneath the price: "Subscribing does not affect any score. Entities cannot pay to change their position."

Entity pages present the Score-Watch CTA with interpolated entity name and strong purchase framing, but the "How it works →" link (which goes to `/score-watch`) is the only signal that independence information exists. There is no inline independence note adjacent to the CTA itself. Adding a single line — "Score-Watch is an observation product. Subscribing does not influence this entity's score." — directly below the CTA price on the entity page closes this gap.

Index pages show the Gumroad purchase button in the hero and in the RankingTable inline CTA, neither of which carries an independence note. A brief note adjacent to the purchase button on each index page ("Public rankings are independent. Purchasing delivers access, not ranking influence.") would complete the coverage. This is already present on the `/indexes` hub page but is absent from the individual index pages.

The `/methodology` independence policy section is the most thorough of all pages (five rule statements with explanations in a structured grid) and is the correct reference document. The UX gap is that this page is several clicks removed from any purchase moment. The fix is not to replicate the full policy everywhere, but to ensure every monetization page has a one-sentence signal with a link to the methodology section.
