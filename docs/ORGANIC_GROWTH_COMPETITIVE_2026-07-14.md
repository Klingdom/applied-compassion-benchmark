## Nonprofit / independence positioning vs the ESG-ratings space (evidence-backed)

- **MSCI** — documented conflict-of-interest research (Columbia/Emory); ~60% of revenue from indexing; ESG ratings move with performance.
- **Sustainalytics** — issuer-pay model in Sustainable Finance Solutions (rated company is a paying customer).
- **Ethisphere** — self-submission + paid Ethics Quotient survey; honoree list only from those who opt in and pay.
- **JUST Capital** — measures Americans' stated priorities via survey, not verified behavior.
- **EU ESG Rating Regulation (effective 2026)** — regulators now require ESG raters to disclose methodology + manage conflicts — market itself recognizes the trust gap.

**Recommended repeated on-page claim:** *"Unlike issuer-pay ESG ratings and self-submission ethics awards, no entity we score can pay for inclusion, a higher score, or suppression of findings — every Fortune 500 company is scored, not just those who apply."* True, and directly answers a live regulator-validated objection → strong AI-citation candidate for "are ESG ratings biased / pay to play".

## AEO/GEO structural notes (how to win citations, not just rankings)

- AI engines weight the **first ~200 words** for direct answers; pages with structured lists/quotes/stats see 30-40% higher AI-response visibility → every new page must lead with a direct quotable answer before narrative.
- Google AI Overviews prioritize strong organic rank + E-E-A-T + structured data. The site already emits per-entity JSON at `site/public/data/scores/<slug>.json` — a structural advantage most competitors lack.
- **Durable technical edge:** none of Ethisphere/RepTrak/JUST/WBA/Princeton Review expose scrapable per-entity structured-data pages. Build every new cluster to expose the same JSON/schema pattern (per-robotics-lab ethics page, per-AI-lab safety page).
