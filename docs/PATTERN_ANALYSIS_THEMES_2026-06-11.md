## 2. Cross-Cutting Meta-Patterns

**M1 — A synchronized global democratic recession is the signature of the period.**
The single loudest signal is not any one entity but the *clustering* of band-crossings into Critical among formerly-Developing democracies: United States, India, Turkey, Bolivia, China — plus the Liberties.eu "Dismantler" cohort (Croatia, Slovakia, Bulgaria, Italy) sinking within Developing, and Ethiopia's one-party consolidation. The countries critical-band count rose 39→45 over the window; developing fell correspondingly. Hungary is the conspicuous *counter-trend* (a sustained multi-cycle upgrade 37.5→50.2 under the Magyar government), which makes it the natural control case for "what reversal looks like." This is a *standing institutional finding*, not a collection of incidents — and it is the strongest candidate for a flagship recurring Special Briefing.

**M2 — Three structurally distinct failure modes by entity type, each with its own adjudication threshold.**
- **States** fail by *active perpetration or codified impunity* — and are scored on attested conduct (lethal force, signed impunity laws, judicial findings), reaching an absolute 0.0 floor. The scoring question is *floor entry vs. priced continuation*.
- **Corporations** fail by *accumulated unadjudicated enforcement* — and are explicitly held NOT scorable until a merits adjudication/settlement (FILED-BUT-UNADJUDICATED). The scoring question is *enforcement density vs. adjudication*. This produces the persistent corporate boundary cluster jammed just above 20.0.
- **AI labs** fail by *governance posture and contracting* — and are scored net-neutral on announcements, pending regulatory enactment or court rulings. The scoring question is *posture vs. operative conduct*.
This three-way split is itself a publishable thesis: the same word ("failure") means three different evidentiary bars across the three indexes.

**M3 — The methodology is maturing faster than the scores.**
The volume of *new rulings* (≈25+ named categories in the window) vastly exceeds the volume of *applied score changes*. Most cycles are "zero-scored-proposal confirmation cycles" whose entire analytic output is a ruling that resolves a scorability question (usually *against* scoring: duplicate-suppression, pre-adjudication, repression-within-pattern, overlay-non-double-count). The institution's real product in this period is a *jurisprudence of restraint* — a documented, conservative theory of when evidence does and does not move a number. The recurring `dailyOpeningQuestion` field is the public-facing edge of this: nearly every one poses a methodology-boundary tension (reversal-evidence thresholds, magnitude caps, compelled-remedy credit). That jurisprudence is an under-exploited asset worth a standing Special Briefing of its own (see SB-3).

**M4 — Cross-entity-type events break the single-index frame.**
UAE↔Sudan (external sponsor scored separately from in-territory floor), Microsoft Azure↔Israel (corporate contract scored under Microsoft, not the sovereign floor), and the AZ AG eight-insurer cartel suit (one adjudication that could simultaneously reprice eight Fortune 500 health insurers) all show that the most consequential signals increasingly *span indexes*. The daily format, organized by index, structurally under-serves these.

## 3. What's Under-Covered

Verified by content sweep of all 30 June public briefings: `us-states`, `global-cities`, and `robotics-labs` appear **zero times**; `us-cities` and `exemplary` entities appear only incidentally (NYC and Target rotation confirms; Apptronik in May). The corpus is ~90% countries + fortune-500 + ai-labs, and overwhelmingly downside-only.

- **Positive / high-compassion exemplars.** The benchmark publishes Exemplary and Established bands but the briefings almost never feature them except as rotation confirms (Target 92.8). There is no recurring "what good looks like" coverage. Upgrades exist (Hungary, Mongolia, Vanuatu, Marshall Islands, Timor-Leste, Botswana, Germany reconciliation) but are scattered and never synthesized. **Gap:** the only sustained *upside* lens is COMPELLED-REMEDY (which by design rarely converts) — leaving genuine exemplary conduct invisible.
- **US States (21 of 51 scored) and US/Global Cities (144 / 250).** Entire indexes effectively absent from the narrative. NYC surfaces only as a rotation line. Sub-national governance — likely where US federal backsliding either propagates or is checked — is a blind spot directly adjacent to the period's biggest meta-pattern (M1).
- **Robotics labs (50).** Zero June coverage. Last movements were May (Ghost Robotics →0.0, Agility/Apptronik boundary). Embodied-AI safety is a quiet-deterioration risk with no monitoring cadence.
- **Quiet sector deterioration without a date-certain trigger.** The pipeline is event- and deadline-driven, so slow drifts (DEI-disclosure decline; North Korean forced labor in EU supply chains; supply-chain transparency under CSDDD) surface only as low-priority emergingRisks and never get a dedicated synthesis.
- **Internal methodology integrity (T11).** Math-hygiene reconciliations, assessor overrides, and the multi-cycle Open Bionics audit hold are real institutional-discipline work that the public never sees — a missed *independence/credibility* signal.

## 4. Special-Briefing Concepts (7)

Each: theme · trigger · entity scope · research-criteria modification vs. a normal daily (entity selection, evidence filter, methodology lens) · cadence. All preserve the independence policy — these are editorial syntheses over published assessments, never paid.

### SB-1 — The Democratic Recession Report *(flagship)*
- **Theme:** The synchronized global slide of democracies from Developing into Critical (M1, T1).
- **Trigger:** Recurring; also fires on any new country band-crossing into Critical driven by governance (not conflict/famine).
- **Entity scope:** Countries only, filtered to entities that crossed *downward* on a democratic-governance ruling in the trailing period, plus the Dismantler cohort and the Hungary counter-case.
- **Criteria modification:** Entity filter = governance-driven crossings (exclude conflict/famine floors); evidence filter = courts/opposition/press/franchise events only; lens = JUDICIAL-REMOVAL, CODIFIED-IMPUNITY, ADJUDICATED-UNLAWFUL-CONDUCT, EU-CONDITIONALITY rulings; explicitly include *reversal* arcs (Hungary) so the report is symmetric.
- **Cadence:** **Monthly**, event-triggered on each new crossing.

### SB-2 — Filed but Unadjudicated: The Corporate Enforcement-Density Watch
- **Theme:** Corporations clustered just above the 20.0 floor under multiplying-but-unadjudicated sovereign actions (T3, M2).
- **Trigger:** Recurring; fires on any date-certain corporate trigger (WARN windows, MDL deadlines, AG-suit milestones).
- **Entity scope:** Fortune 500 within ~1.0pt of the Critical boundary + entities with ≥2 concurrent sovereign actions (UnitedHealth, CVS, Cigna, Oracle, 3M, Elevance, Amazon, Boeing).
- **Criteria modification:** Entity filter = boundary-distance ≤1.0pt OR enforcement-density ≥2 actions; evidence filter = adjudication-stage only (filed / discovery / settlement / verdict), with a "conversion trigger" column; lens = FILED-BUT-UNADJUDICATED, COORDINATED-INVESTIGATION-STILL-PRE-ADJUDICATION, THIRD-PARTY-REPARATION-ATTRIBUTION-BOUNDARY.
- **Cadence:** **Monthly**, plus event-triggered on any merits adjudication (the AZ AG eight-insurer suit is a single high-value trigger).

### SB-3 — The Jurisprudence of Restraint: Methodology in Review
- **Theme:** The benchmark's accumulating theory of when evidence does *not* move a score (M3) — the institution's distinctive credibility asset.
- **Trigger:** Recurring; also one-off when a new named ruling reaches its 2nd–3rd application (anchor-case status).
- **Entity scope:** All indexes; organized by ruling, not entity.
- **Criteria modification:** Not entity-driven at all — it's a *ruling registry* synthesis: every ruling promoted to active/anchor in the period, its anchor case, its conversion trigger, and the open `dailyOpeningQuestion` tensions it leaves unresolved. Evidence filter = the rulings ledgers (`PENDING_CHANGES`/`APPLIED_CHANGES`), not the scanner.
- **Cadence:** **Monthly** (or quarterly), with one-off editions when a major new category appears (e.g., ACTIVE-COMPLICITY-BY-PROXY).

### SB-4 — Floors That Cannot Fall: The Active-Perpetration Cohort
- **Theme:** The 0.0 / near-floor cohort and the precise conditions for floor *exit* vs. *entry* (T2, T5, M2).
- **Trigger:** Event-triggered — a new floor designation, a near-floor entity revealing a *second* dominant harm category, or a documented perpetration-cessation (exit) signal.
- **Entity scope:** Israel, Russia, Sudan, Myanmar, North Korea, Afghanistan (0.0); Iran, DRC, Haiti, Ethiopia (near-floor); UAE as external-sponsor edge case.
- **Criteria modification:** Entity filter = composite ≤5.0; evidence filter = perpetration *cessation* signals and *new harm category* signals only (not volume of continuing harm, which is priced); lens = ACTIVE-PERPETRATION-FLOOR-REINFORCEMENT, single-dominant-extremity hold, ACTIVE-COMPLICITY-BY-PROXY. Frame around the conceptual question "how does a floor entity ever move?"
- **Cadence:** **Event-triggered**, with a standing quarterly cohort review.

### SB-5 — What Good Looks Like: The Exemplar & Reversal Briefing *(fills the biggest gap)*
- **Theme:** High-compassion exemplars and genuine upward reversals — the systematically under-covered upside (Gap §3, M1 counter-case).
- **Trigger:** Recurring; fires on any sustained multi-cycle upgrade or any verified COMPELLED-REMEDY conversion.
- **Entity scope:** Exemplary/Established-band entities + confirmed upgraders (Hungary, Mongolia, Vanuatu, Marshall Islands, Timor-Leste, Botswana, Germany, Target, Apptronik) + positive-watch entities (Microsoft, Alphabet).
- **Criteria modification:** Inverts the normal downside filter: entity filter = positive delta in trailing period OR composite ≥60; evidence filter = *verified operative* improvements (executed reforms, blocked contracts, abolitions) — explicitly excluding announcements; lens = COMPELLED-REMEDY-NOT-SELF-CORRECTION (to separate real self-correction from compelled posture), POSITIVE-DEMOCRATIC-CHECK, FORWARD-TRIGGER-RESOLUTION-CONFIRMED-FIRED.
- **Cadence:** **Monthly** — deliberately counter-programs the downside-heavy daily flow.

### SB-6 — Cross-Index Atrocity Chains
- **Theme:** Events that span entity types and break the single-index frame (M4) — external sponsorship, corporate-to-sovereign contracting, sector-wide cartel adjudications.
- **Trigger:** Event-triggered — whenever one evidence base implicates entities across ≥2 indexes.
- **Entity scope:** Cross-cutting: UAE↔Sudan; Microsoft Azure↔Israel/Unit 8200; AZ AG suit ↔ eight health insurers; AI-lab military contracts ↔ sovereign militaries.
- **Criteria modification:** Entity selection is *relationship-driven*, not index-driven — trace the supply/sponsorship/contract chain and show how no-double-count discipline allocates the harm; lens = ACTIVE-COMPLICITY-BY-PROXY, OCCUPATION-ATTRIBUTION-NON-DOUBLE-COUNT, MILITARY-AI-BY-CONTRACT-GOVERNANCE.
- **Cadence:** **Event-triggered** (one-off per chain).

### SB-7 — The Sub-National & Quiet-Deterioration Briefing
- **Theme:** The structurally invisible indexes and slow drifts (Gap §3) — US States, US/Global Cities, Robotics Labs, and trigger-less sector drifts (DEI disclosure, forced-labor supply chains).
- **Trigger:** Recurring on a fixed cadence (it has *no* event trigger by design — that's the point).
- **Entity scope:** us-states, us-cities, global-cities, robotics-labs; plus index-wide drift signals.
- **Criteria modification:** Force-rotate the indexes the daily pipeline skips; evidence filter = slow-moving structural indicators (budgets, ordinances, audit obligations, supplier disclosures) rather than date-certain events; lens = candidate/quiet-deterioration framing, CSDDD/forced-labor supply-chain transparency, math-hygiene/integrity disclosure (T11) as a credibility chapter.
- **Cadence:** **Monthly** (or alternating months between sub-national and robotics).
