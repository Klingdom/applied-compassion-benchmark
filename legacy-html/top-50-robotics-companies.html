Methodology summary

Scores use publicly available evidence including safety research, labor displacement disclosures, military and weapons partnerships, worker retraining commitments, accessibility and healthcare applications, open research culture, governance transparency, geographic access equity, regulatory cooperation, and alignment between stated mission and actual deployment choices.

Composite scores are normalized to 0–100 with an integration adjustment: +5 if all dimensions score 4 or above, +3 if all score 3 or above, -2 if any dimension scores 1.5 or below, and -5 if any dimension scores 1.0 (active harm override).

Industry-level finding

The industry mean of 56.2 — solidly Functional — masks extreme variance: 2 labs reach Exemplary while 3 score Critical. Healthcare-first mission is the single strongest predictor of high scores. Weaponization is the clearest failure mode — and it is self-declared. The 88.1-point gap between Open Bionics (92.5) and Paladin AI (4.4) reflects a fundamental split in the industry's purpose.

Key findings

What the 2026 data reveals about the global humanoid robotics industry.

Healthcare-first mission predicts exemplary scores

Open Bionics and Ottobock top the index by design, not accident. Both built their entire business model around restoring human capability. The Healthcare/Accessibility category averages 87.1 — the highest of any category in the index.

Weaponization is the clearest failure mode

Ghost Robotics and Paladin AI score 4.4 — the lowest in the entire Compassion Benchmark index. Both explicitly build for lethal military applications. The line is visible, crossable, and recoverable — if organizations choose recovery.

The augmentation narrative is the integrity test

INT scores average 3.2/5.0 — revealing widespread divergence between stated mission and actual business model. When a company deploys humanoids in warehouses without any published retraining commitment, the augmentation narrative is integrity theater.

European and Nordic labs dominate through structural values

UK average 78.2, Norway 71.0, EU mean exceeds 74 — versus US mean of 49.4. European AI Act compliance, Nordic labor ethics, and publicly-funded research mandates produce a measurable compassion premium.

Equity scores lowest — the global access gap is structural

EQU averages 2.77/5.0 — the lowest dimension in the index. The most advanced humanoid robots are being built almost exclusively for wealthy-market deployment. Global South access is near-zero.

Eldercare and rehabilitation: robotics at its most compassionate

Cyberdyne's HAL, ReWalk's paraplegic mobility device, Ekso Bionics' stroke rehabilitation system, and Kinova's JACO arm all score Established. These applications represent robotics restoring the most fundamental human capability — physical autonomy.

Category & country analysis

Average compassion score by application category and country, highlighting structural patterns in the global industry.

Top categories

Healthcare/Accessibility leads at 87.1 average, followed by Education (80.0) and Healthcare/Rehab (72.4). When the machine's purpose is to restore human capability, every dimension of compassion improves by design.

Labor and industrial categories

Labor/Industrial averages 56.1 and Industrial categories cluster around 46–50. These segments show basic safety obligations met but persistent gaps in accountability, labor transition planning, and displacement acknowledgment.

Defense and security: the floor

Defense/Security averages 9.0 — the lowest category in the index. Three labs here represent the clearest documented harm in the entire benchmark. Mission determines score; defense-primary mission produces defense-primary outcomes.

European and Nordic premium

UK (78.2), France (78.0), Spain (76.4), Norway (71.0), and Germany (68.3) all outperform the global mean. EU AI Act compliance and Nordic labor ethics produce measurably higher scores — this is regulatory infrastructure, not cultural sentiment.

US mean: Functional band

18 US labs average 49.4 — Functional. The US produces both exemplary labs (Diligent, Ekso, Apptronik) and the index's worst scores (Ghost Robotics, Paladin AI). Mission variation within the US is extreme.

Dimension averages

AWR 3.25 · EMP 3.35 · ACT 3.24 · EQU 2.77 · BND 3.24 · ACC 3.22 · SYS 3.24 · INT 3.20 — EQU lowest, EMP highest. Equity remains the hardest dimension to improve without fundamental business model change.
