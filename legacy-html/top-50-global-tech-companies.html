Key findings

Seven headline findings from the 2026 Top 50 Global Technology Companies Index.

Buy Full Report
1Enterprise Software Outperforms Consumer Tech Dramatically
The top five companies — Microsoft, Salesforce, Workday, ServiceNow, and Atlassian — are all enterprise software firms. No major consumer platform appears in the top 10. The pattern is structural: when end users are also customers with meaningful exit power, compassion outcomes improve. When users are the product, they are treated accordingly.
The gap between the top enterprise software company and the bottom consumer platform is 71.0 points.
2Chinese Technology Companies Score Uniformly Critical
All seven Chinese companies in the index score in the Critical band. The report frames this as a governance architecture finding rather than a cultural one: mandatory censorship, surveillance requirements, and structural inability to prioritise user welfare when it conflicts with state directives shape the scores.
The average Boundaries score for Chinese companies is 1.21 — the lowest of any national grouping.
3Twitter/X Records the Lowest Score in the Entire Index
Twitter/X scores 0.8, the lowest of any company in the 50-company benchmark. The report cites mass layoffs, the dismantling of trust and safety functions, moderation collapse, and governance tied to a single owner's ideological preferences as a concentrated example of institutional compassion collapse.
4Meta Represents a High-Stakes Integrity Failure
Meta scores 21.4 overall. The report highlights suppressed internal research on teen mental health, the Myanmar genocide context, the $5B FTC privacy settlement, and Cambridge Analytica as a long-running pattern of misalignment between stated values and demonstrated behavior.
5Indian IT Services Show That Scale and Compassion Can Coexist
Infosys, TCS, Wipro, HCL Technologies, and Capgemini all score Functional, outperforming many larger consumer platforms. The B2B service model, employee welfare infrastructure, and lower public harm exposure contribute to relatively better outcomes.
6Boundaries Is the Most Revealing Dimension
Boundaries is the lowest-scoring dimension overall at 2.25. The central question is what a company will refuse to do for revenue. The report points to Palantir, Meta, Amazon, Cisco, and Uber as examples of a sector-wide pattern of treating ethical limits as negotiable when financial incentives are large enough.
7No Company Achieves Exemplary Status
The highest scores in the sector are Microsoft and Salesforce at 71.8, both in the Established band. The sector has not yet produced a genuinely exemplary compassionate institution. The report attributes this to shareholder primacy, regulatory capture, and incentives to grow faster than the capacity to understand and mitigate harm.
The mean score of 34.7 places the sector as a whole in the Developing band.

Methodology summary

Scores use publicly available evidence only and evaluate corporations as institutions rather than the character of individual employees or founders. The benchmark applies eight science-grounded compassion dimensions derived from the four-component model of compassion.

Evidence sources include annual reports, regulatory filings, ESG reports, investigative journalism, consent decrees, labour board filings, employee satisfaction signals, whistleblower disclosures, litigation records, third-party audits, and peer-reviewed research.

Composite formula: ((avg dimension − 1) / 4) × 100 + integration adjustment. Adjustments include +5 if all dimensions are 4.0 or above, +3 if all are 3.0 or above, −2 if any are 2.0 or below, −2 if any are 1.5 or below, and −5 if any are 1.0 or below.

Companies on upward trajectories

Microsoft shows credible movement toward a higher band through Responsible AI standards, AI safety investment, accessibility leadership, and housing commitments, though workplace culture and AI surveillance questions remain.

Accenture shows structural progress on gender balance and AI ethics practice, with room to move upward if client work integrates human rights due diligence more deeply.

Netflix shows improving access thinking in global content reach and affordability tiers, but worker equity in production remains a core gap.

Companies facing deterioration risk

Meta faces likely declines as moderation and fact-checking guardrails are rolled back and governance aligns more directly with political priorities than safety priorities.

Tesla faces concentrated governance risk from executive control, union suppression, documented safety concerns, and political polarization of the brand.

Oracle faces continued downside risk from extreme executive-worker inequality, data broker settlement history, return-to-office impacts, and Cerner workforce integration concerns.
