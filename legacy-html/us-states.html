Methodology summary

States were scored 1–5 on eight dimensions using publicly available evidence including healthcare coverage and Medicaid expansion status, uninsured rates, poverty and child poverty rates, education funding equity, incarceration rates, LGBTQ+ civil rights protections, voting rights expansion or restriction, environmental justice, worker protections and minimum wage, paid family leave, housing policy, maternal mortality, gun violence rates, government transparency indices, corruption records, and social spending per capita.

Composite scores are normalized to 0–100 with an integration adjustment: +5 if all dimensions score 4 or above, +3 if all score 3 or above, -2 if any dimension scores 1.5 or below, and -5 if any dimension scores 1.0.

National finding

The average score across all 51 states and DC is 48.9, placing the United States in the Functional band but close to the Developing threshold. The gap between the highest-scoring state (Vermont, 92.5) and the lowest (Mississippi, 7.5) is 85.0 points.

Published findings

Major structural patterns identified in the 2026 U.S. index.

Geographic divide

New England and the West Coast dominate the top tier. The Deep South anchors the bottom. New England averages 80.2 while the Southeast averages 23.2.

Medicaid expansion

41 states expanded Medicaid and 10 did not. Non-expansion states have the highest uninsured rates, worst maternal mortality, and more preventable deaths.

Equity scores lowest

Equity is the hardest dimension for states to master, with racial wealth gaps, education funding inequities, and gerrymandering suppressing scores.

Integrity gap

Post-Dobbs abortion bans are associated with dramatically lower integrity scores and materially worse maternal mortality outcomes.

Minnesota effect

Minnesota’s 2023 trifecta — free school meals, paid family leave, and free college for low-income students — moved it into the top tier.

Voting restrictions

States that enacted post-2020 voting restrictions cluster in the Developing or Critical bands, with boundaries and accountability scores deteriorating together.
