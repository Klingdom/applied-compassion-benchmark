Methodology summary

Scores rely on publicly available evidence including SEC filings, sustainability and ESG reports, investigative journalism, regulatory actions, litigation records, employee satisfaction signals, independent audits, labor board filings, consumer protection settlements, environmental violation records, and peer-reviewed academic research.

Composite scores are normalized to 0–100 with an integration adjustment: +5 if all dimensions score 4 or above, +3 if all score 3 or above, -2 if any dimension scores 1.5 or below, and -5 if any dimension scores 1.0.

Market-level finding

The average Fortune 500 score of 39.0 places large-company America at the boundary of Developing and Functional. More than half of the most powerful corporations in the country sit in the bottom two bands, and 97 companies show active documented harm in at least one dimension.

Key findings

What the 2026 data reveals about corporate America.

Equity is the hardest dimension

EQU averages 2.25/5.0, the lowest of all eight dimensions. Public commitments have often not translated into measurable structural change.

Accountability failures are near-universal

ACC averages 2.62/5.0. The default response to harm remains denial, delay, or correction only under pressure.

Scale does not create compassion

Revenue rank and compassion rank are effectively uncorrelated. Extreme scale often increases the distance between decision-makers and affected people.

Employee ownership predicts stronger performance

Companies with employee ownership structures repeatedly outperform sector peers across awareness, equity, and accountability.

Healthcare is highly bifurcated

The sector spans the full range from exemplary mission-driven actors to critical extractive models with denial and access concerns.

The harm override matters

97 companies receive the harm override, separating performative CSR from active, documented institutional harm.

Sector analysis

Average compassion score by sector, highlighting the strongest and weakest structural patterns.

Top sectors

Steel, Industrial/Science, and Industrial/Water lead the index at 87.4 average, showing what operational accountability and mission alignment can look like in practice.

Middle sectors

Technology, logistics, food, and many financial sectors cluster in the Functional band, showing partial progress but persistent structural gaps.

Bottom sectors

Coal, corrections, aerospace/defense, and some extractive energy segments anchor the bottom due to concentrated documented harm and weak accountability.

Healthcare split

Healthcare spans from exemplary device and mission-driven firms to critical denial-driven and extractive models, making it one of the most morally bifurcated sectors.

Ownership matters

Employee-owned or mission-shaped firms consistently outperform peers in worker treatment, long-term thinking, and integrity.
