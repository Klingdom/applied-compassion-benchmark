Methodology

The Compassion Benchmark Framework — Applied to AI

The framework is science-grounded and measures institutional compassionate behavior across eight dimensions derived from the four-component model of compassion. Applied to AI, it asks whether the organizations building frontier systems actually reduce suffering — or accelerate it.

Scoring draws on published safety research, governance disclosures, whistleblower accounts, product harm documentation, open-source commitments, labour practices, independent audits, regulatory filings, and peer-reviewed research. No company paid to be included.

Scoring formula: composite = ((avg_dimension − 1) / 4) × 100 + integration adjustment. Adjustment: +5 if all dimensions ≥ 4.0, +3 if all ≥ 3.0, −2 if any ≤ 2.0, −2 if any ≤ 1.5, −5 if any ≤ 1.0.

Interpretation

What the sector data shows

AI Safety/Research, Open Source, and healthcare-oriented AI use cases cluster near the top. Creative AI, consumer companionship AI, surveillance AI, and government enforcement infrastructure cluster near the bottom.

Open-source access and genuine safety infrastructure correlate with stronger Equity, Accountability, and Integrity scores. Consent-free training-data practices, law-enforcement deployment, and engagement-maximizing consumer systems correlate with lower Boundaries and Accountability scores.
